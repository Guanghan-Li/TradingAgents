## DLR Scenario Frame

DLR screens as an AI-infrastructure beneficiary with solid free cash flow, but the setup is balanced by rich valuation, very high leverage for a rate-sensitive REIT, and execution risk around the announced Singapore/Asia AI buildout. The current macro backdrop is mixed-to-constructive for the base case: policy has eased from late-2025 levels, the yield curve is positively sloped, and volatility is contained, but long-duration yields remain elevated enough to cap multiple expansion.

**Bull case: 35%**. AI-driven leasing demand accelerates, the Asia expansion improves DLR's strategic scarcity value, and analyst target revisions continue to validate upside. This scenario needs proof that new capacity is pre-leased or leased at attractive returns, with funding costs staying manageable.

**Base case: 45%**. DLR remains a quality data-center REIT, but upside is more incremental than explosive. Leasing stays healthy, the market rewards the AI narrative, but elevated long-end rates and a full multiple keep appreciation tied to execution rather than re-rating.

**Bear case: 20%**. The stock de-rates if yields move higher again, hyperscaler/enterprise leasing cadence disappoints, or the Singapore program raises capital intensity before earnings power catches up. With debt-to-equity elevated and forward EPS below trailing EPS, DLR has less room for execution misses than a cheaper REIT.

### Key signposts
- Quarterly leasing bookings, backlog conversion, and utilization trends.
- Funding mix for Asia expansion and whether incremental projects are pre-leased.
- Long-end Treasury direction, since 10Y/30Y yields remain relevant for REIT valuation.
- Any evidence that AFFO/EPS growth is re-accelerating rather than being diluted by capex and financing costs.

### Thesis invalidation logic
The constructive thesis weakens if DLR fails to translate AI demand into visible lease monetization, if balance-sheet metrics worsen materially to fund growth, or if higher-for-longer rates compress REIT multiples faster than operating growth can offset.
