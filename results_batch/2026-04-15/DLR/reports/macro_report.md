Base case for `DLR`: no sharp macro deterioration, but also no fast valuation relief from falling long yields unless inflation cools again.

Cross-asset implications for `DLR`: equities tied to secular infrastructure demand can still work, credit should remain stable if growth holds, but long-duration real assets are vulnerable if the bond market keeps repricing inflation risk. The main macro risk is a higher-for-longer rates regime driven by sticky services inflation and pass-through from producer prices. A secondary risk is that a still-healthy economy keeps the curve steep for the wrong reason, via term premium rather than real growth optimism, which would also pressure REIT valuations including `DLR`.

Net macro takeaway for `DLR`: fundamentals backdrop is better than a recession scare would imply, but the rates backdrop is not clean enough to become aggressively bullish on valuation expansion. Macro contribution is mildly constructive on business demand, neutral-to-cautious on multiple expansion, and sensitive to any renewed upside surprise in inflation or the 10Y yield.
