## macro_report
Macro regime for `EQIX`: the backdrop reads as a soft-landing / mid-cycle expansion rather than recession. Labor is still holding up, payrolls are growing, unemployment ticked down to 4.3%, VIX is contained, and the Treasury curve is positively sloped with a 2Y-10Y spread of +50 bps. That combination argues against an imminent recession signal and supports continued enterprise, cloud, and network infrastructure demand, which is fundamentally favorable for `EQIX` occupancy, interconnection activity, and pricing power.

The complication is inflation and rates. CPI and PPI both accelerated sequentially in the latest print, while the Fed has paused after several cuts, leaving effective fed funds at 3.64%. That suggests policy is no longer restrictive in the same way it was in late 2025, but it is also not on a clean easing glidepath. For `EQIX`, this is a mixed macro setup: growth is supportive, but the long end of the curve is still elevated at 4.26% on the 10Y and 4.87% on the 30Y. As a capital-intensive, rate-sensitive data center REIT, `EQIX` benefits from stable growth and a normal curve, but valuation multiples and incremental development economics remain vulnerable to any higher-for-longer repricing in long-duration yields.

Base case: modestly constructive macro for `EQIX` fundamentals, but not an all-clear on valuation. The most likely policy path is a Fed hold with an easing bias that is contingent on renewed disinflation. If inflation stays sticky, the next macro impulse for `EQIX` is likely to come more from bond yields than from changes in growth expectations. In cross-asset terms, this favors quality growth and infrastructure exposure over deep cyclicals, but within REIT-like duration assets it argues for selectivity because long-end rates can still cap upside.

Concrete risks the committee should incorporate for `EQIX`:
- A bear steepening move, where 10Y and 30Y yields rise further even without stronger growth, would pressure `EQIX` valuation and raise the hurdle rate for new capacity.
