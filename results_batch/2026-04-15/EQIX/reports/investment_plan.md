My decision is Buy EQIX, but only as a staged, risk-budgeted buy rather than an aggressive chase. That matters more than the freeform Hold language. The base case at 50% argues against over-sizing, but it does not justify abandoning a positive-skew, quality compounder with strong momentum, improving operating metrics, and fresh AI infrastructure catalysts.

The bull side’s strongest argument is that EQIX has real operating support behind the premium narrative. Revenue grew from $2.261B to $2.420B year over year, gross profit rose from $1.065B to $1.222B, operating income improved from $405M to $507M, and normalized EBITDA increased from $941M to $1.090B. Citi and Jefferies raising targets to $1,200 and $1,220 reinforces the market’s willingness to underwrite EQIX as an AI infrastructure compounder.

The bear side’s strongest argument is valuation and timing. EQIX is expensive, technically extended, and rate-sensitive. The stock is around 1052.98, well above the 50-day and 200-day averages, with RSI near 75.7. Debt has risen meaningfully, net debt/EBITDA is about 4.2x, capex is heavy, and free-cash-flow data is messy. Those are real risks, especially with the 10Y Treasury near 4.26% and the 30Y near 4.87%. The bear is right that this is not a clean full-size entry after a sharp run.

But the bear case does not quite get me to Sell or even a pure Hold. The evidence points to a premium business with execution momentum, positive sentiment, and more upside probability than downside probability. The mistake to avoid here is treating overbought technicals and high multiples as automatic sell signals in a high-quality infrastructure winner. The better lesson is to separate direction from sizing: EQIX deserves exposure, but not reckless exposure.

The plan for the trader is to initiate or maintain a 2.0% EQIX position now, with a maximum portfolio loss budget of 0.6%. Do not immediately move to the 4.0% target weight at current levels. Add only if either of two conditions occurs: first, EQIX pulls back in an orderly way into roughly the 1028 to 1035 support zone and holds; second, EQIX closes decisively above the 1057 to 1066 recent high/upper-band zone with stronger volume confirmation. If either confirmation arrives, scale toward 3.0%, then toward the 4.0% target weight if earnings, rates, and price action remain supportive.

Risk control should be strict. If EQIX closes below the 10-day EMA and momentum starts rolling over, stop adding. If it loses the 1006 to 1016 breakout shelf, reduce exposure materially because that would suggest the setup has shifted from bullish digestion to broader mean reversion. Also reassess immediately if the next earnings update disappoints on revenue, EBITDA, FFO/AFFO, leasing demand, interconnection growth, or capex returns. A material rise in long Treasury yields from the current 4.26% 10Y reference should also tighten risk controls.

So the actionable recommendation is Buy EQIX with discipline: start at 2.0%, scale only on pullback support or breakout confirmation, cap loss at 0.6% of portfolio value, and require earnings or adoption evidence before treating the AI thesis as fully de-risked.
