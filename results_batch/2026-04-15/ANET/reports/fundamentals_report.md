Fundamental report for ANET as of 2026-04-15.

ANET remains one of the highest-quality large-cap infrastructure names in the Technology sector on the provided data. The core fundamental picture is: high growth, very high profitability, very strong liquidity, minimal balance-sheet stress, strong cash generation, and a premium valuation that already discounts continued execution. Over the last week, ANET also held its relative strength, rising +6.38% versus +6.08% for XLK, which suggests the market is still rewarding the company’s earnings quality rather than rotating away from it.

From a business-quality standpoint, ANET’s trailing profile is exceptional. Revenue is $9.01B TTM, gross profit is $5.77B, EBITDA is $3.93B, and net income is $3.51B. That translates into a gross margin of roughly 64%, EBITDA margin of roughly 44%, net margin of 38.99%, and operating margin of 41.52% from the summary snapshot. Return on equity is 31.4% and return on assets is 14.39%, both strong signals that ANET is converting capital into profits efficiently. For traders, this matters because names with this combination of growth and margin resilience usually keep valuation support longer than lower-quality hardware peers.

The latest reported quarter for ANET, 2025-12-31, was strong on both a sequential and year-over-year basis. Revenue was $2.488B, up 7.8% quarter over quarter from $2.308B and up about 28.9% year over year from $1.930B. Gross profit was $1.564B, up about 27.0% year over year. Operating income was $1.033B, up about 29.2% year over year. Net income was $955.8M, up about 19.3% year over year. Diluted EPS was $0.75 versus $0.62 a year earlier, up about 21.0%. These are not signs of a business that is decelerating sharply.

There is a mild nuance inside the margin structure for ANET. Gross margin in the latest quarter was about 62.9% ($1.564B on $2.488B revenue), down from about 63.8% in 2024-12-31. That is still excellent, but it suggests some mix or cost pressure at the gross level. ANET offset that with scale and expense discipline: operating income still expanded faster than revenue year over year. Research and development spending rose to $348.4M from $285.0M a year ago, which is constructive because ANET is still investing while preserving elite profitability.

The balance sheet for ANET is a major strength. Total assets were $19.45B at 2025-12-31, versus $14.04B a year earlier. Stockholders’ equity rose to $12.37B from $9.99B. Working capital rose to $11.01B from $9.18B. Current ratio is 3.048, which indicates ample short-term liquidity. Cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments were $10.74B, up from $8.30B a year earlier. That gives ANET a large buffer for supply-chain management, internal investment, acquisitions, or buybacks.

A particularly important fundamental signal for ANET is deferred revenue growth. Current deferred revenue was $4.003B at 2025-12-31 versus $1.727B at 2024-12-31. Non-current deferred revenue was $1.370B versus $1.064B. Combined deferred revenue was roughly $5.37B versus $2.79B a year earlier. That is one of the strongest indicators in this dataset because it points to substantial billed business and improved revenue visibility. For traders, this supports the view that ANET’s growth is backed by real commercial momentum rather than only cyclical order timing.

Working-capital components deserve close attention because they show both strength and risk. Inventory rose to $2.247B from $1.835B year over year, up about 22.5%. Receivables rose to $1.887B from $1.141B, up about 65.4%. Payables rose to $651.7M from $381.1M. Inventory growth is understandable in a scaling hardware platform, but receivables growth materially outpaced revenue growth, so traders should monitor whether this reflects larger enterprise deals, billing timing, or any early collection slippage. At the moment, the deferred revenue expansion offsets much of that concern, but it is still a line item worth watching in the next print.

Cash-flow generation for ANET remains very strong. In 2025-12-31, operating cash flow was $1.262B and free cash flow was $1.225B, versus $1.031B and $1.019B respectively in 2024-12-31. That is about 22.4% year-over-year growth in operating cash flow and about 20.3% growth in quarterly free cash flow. Capital expenditure was only $37M in the quarter, which means ANET’s business model remains highly cash generative and not capital intensive. Free-cash-flow conversion remains strong relative to net income, which is a positive signal for earnings quality.

Capital allocation is also supportive. ANET repurchased $620.1M of stock in 2025-12-31, after $195.9M in 2025-06-30 and $787.1M in 2025-03-31. Ordinary shares outstanding fell modestly to 1.2565B from 1.2613B a year earlier, and diluted average shares also edged down. The buyback is not transforming the share count, but it is still additive and signals management confidence. Importantly, ANET is buying back stock from a position of balance-sheet strength rather than leverage stress.

Valuation is the main constraint on the ANET fundamental story. The stock trades at 56.12x trailing earnings, 36.10x forward earnings, and 15.68x book value, with a market cap of about $194.35B. On TTM revenue of $9.01B, the implied price-to-sales multiple is roughly 21.6x. Those are premium multiples even for a high-quality infrastructure leader. The valuation can be justified only if ANET sustains elevated revenue growth, preserves operating margins near current levels, and keeps converting growth into cash. If any of those weaken, the multiple leaves room for a sharp de-rating.

The forward-versus-trailing earnings setup is notable. TTM EPS is $2.75 and forward EPS is $4.27. That implies the market is pricing in a large step-up in profitability. This is bullish if ANET keeps landing large AI and cloud networking demand and monetizes that demand at scale. It is risky if expectations have moved ahead of fundamentals. For traders, that means ANET is a strong company but not a low-expectation stock.

Relative performance supports the bullish fundamental interpretation. ANET is ranked 1 of 9 peers over the last 3 months, up +18.78% versus +2.74% for XLK, a +16.04% alpha spread. YTD, ANET is up +15.52% versus +4.29% for XLK, a +11.23% alpha spread. Over 6 months, however, ANET has slightly lagged XLK by 1.04 percentage points. The pattern suggests recent re-acceleration in sentiment, not a straight-line momentum trade. Traders should interpret the last week’s +6.38% move as confirmation of strength, but not as uniquely extreme relative to the sector.

Macro conditions are mixed but still generally supportive for ANET. The effective Fed funds rate is 3.64%, down from late-2025 levels, and the yield curve is upward sloping with the 10-year at 4.26% and the 2-year at 3.76%. That backdrop is usually healthier for high-quality growth names than an inverted curve would be. VIX at 18.36 suggests moderate market stress, not panic. The main macro caution is inflation: CPI and PPI both moved higher in the latest reading window. For ANET, that matters because a hardware-oriented business can face input-cost or supply-chain pressure. So far, the gross margin profile suggests ANET is managing that reasonably well.

There are also two data-quality nuances traders should keep in mind. First, the summary snapshot lists free cash flow for ANET at $3.388B, while summing the four reported quarterly free-cash-flow figures gives roughly $4.252B. That likely reflects vendor methodology differences, but it means traders should not anchor on a single FCF figure without checking the next filing. Second, the summary debt-to-equity figure is 0.732, but the balance sheet shown does not indicate a traditional debt-heavy capital structure; much of the liability growth appears driven by deferred revenue and operating liabilities rather than obvious borrowing. That makes the raw leverage label look worse than the economic reality.

Actionable takeaways for traders from the fundamentals alone:

1. ANET is fundamentally strong enough to justify continued institutional sponsorship. Revenue growth near 29% year over year, operating margin above 40%, and large deferred-revenue expansion are hard to find together.
2. The clean balance sheet and $10.74B liquidity position reduce downside tied to financing stress. ANET does not appear dependent on external capital to fund growth.
3. The biggest bullish proof point is deferred revenue. If that remains elevated in the next quarter, it would reinforce that ANET’s order pipeline is real and sticky.
4. The biggest near-term accounting watch item is receivables growth. If receivables keep rising faster than revenue, traders should test whether collections are stretching.
5. The biggest trading risk is valuation compression, not business fragility. ANET is priced for continued excellence.
6. The latest week’s price action looks like confirmation of strong fundamentals rather than a disconnected speculative spike, but after a strong 1-month and 3-month run, ANET may need another clean execution print to expand further.
