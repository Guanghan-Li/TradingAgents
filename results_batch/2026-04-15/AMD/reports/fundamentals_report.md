AMD fundamental report as of 2026-04-15

Advanced Micro Devices, Inc. (`AMD`) is a large-cap semiconductor company in the Technology sector with a market capitalization of about $420.8B. The current fundamentals snapshot shows a business that has moved back into strong top-line and earnings expansion, is producing solid free cash flow, and is being rewarded with major relative outperformance versus both peers and the Technology ETF benchmark. The main fundamental question for traders is not whether `AMD` is improving operationally, because it is, but whether the current valuation already discounts a large part of that improvement.

## Company profile and market positioning
`AMD` operates in semiconductors and is typically evaluated as a high-beta, cycle-sensitive, AI and compute infrastructure name. The stock’s beta is 1.963, which means price action is likely to remain more volatile than the broader market. That fits the recent tape: `AMD` gained 11.35% in 1 week, 31.31% in 1 month, and 16.81% in 3 months, materially ahead of `XLK` and ahead of most listed peers in the provided set. On a 3-month basis, `AMD` ranked 1 out of 8 peers.

This relative strength matters fundamentally because markets usually do not sustain this degree of outperformance without a clear earnings and revenue acceleration story. The financial statements support that story.

## Core valuation snapshot
The current multiples show a market paying up for future growth:

- TTM P/E: 99.28
- Forward P/E: 23.83
- Price to book: 6.68
- TTM EPS: 2.60
- Forward EPS: 10.83

The gap between TTM and forward P/E is the key point. The market is pricing `AMD` on forward earnings normalization and growth, not trailing earnings. That usually implies two things:

1. Traders are expecting the latest earnings ramp to continue.
2. Any revenue, margin, or AI demand disappointment can create sharp multiple compression.

## Expense structure and margin implications
Operating expenses are rising alongside growth:

- R&D rose from $1.71B on 2024-12-31 to $2.33B on 2025-12-31
- SG&A rose from $792M on 2024-12-31 to $1.20B on 2025-12-31

This indicates `AMD` is investing aggressively to support growth. That is not automatically negative. For a semiconductor growth name, higher R&D can be a positive signal if revenue and gross profit are scaling faster than opex over time. The 2025-12-31 quarter suggests that this operating leverage is beginning to appear again.

Gross margin also improved meaningfully in the latest quarter. At 2025-12-31, gross profit of $5.58B on revenue of $10.27B implies gross margin of roughly 54.3%. That compares favorably with roughly 50.7% at 2024-12-31 ($3.88B on $7.66B). This is an important sign that mix and pricing likely improved.

The stated debt-to-equity ratio in the prefetched fundamentals is 6.359, but the detailed balance sheet suggests a much more conservative picture when comparing total debt ($3.85B) against equity ($63.00B). That implied ratio is far lower. This looks like a methodology mismatch in the headline snapshot rather than an actual balance sheet stress signal. Traders should rely more heavily on the raw balance sheet here.

Important positives:

- Liquidity improved sharply through 2025.
- Working capital rose from $11.77B at 2024-12-31 to $17.49B at 2025-12-31.
- Cash and short-term investments more than doubled from $5.13B at 2024-12-31 to $10.55B at 2025-12-31.
- Equity base continued to expand.

Important watch item:

- Goodwill and other intangible assets remain very large at $41.83B, with goodwill alone at $25.13B.

That means a large portion of the balance sheet still depends on the long-term value of prior acquisitions and purchased intangibles. There is no immediate impairment sign in the provided data, but the scale is significant enough that traders should watch for any future writedowns if end-market assumptions weaken.

## Working capital and inventory analysis
Inventory increased materially:

- 2024-12-31: $5.73B
- 2025-03-31: $6.42B
- 2025-06-30: $6.68B
- 2025-09-30: $7.31B
- 2025-12-31: $7.92B

That is a notable rise. It can mean one of two things:

1. `AMD` is building inventory to meet expected demand, which is bullish if sell-through remains strong.
2. Channel inventory is rising ahead of demand, which becomes bearish if revenue growth slows.

Because revenue also rose strongly into 2025-12-31, the current evidence leans more toward demand preparation than inventory distress. Still, this is one of the most important forward risk indicators in the report. If a future quarter shows slowing revenue while inventory remains elevated, the market could react negatively.

Receivables were also elevated but broadly consistent with a growing business:

- Accounts receivable at 2025-12-31: $6.32B

No immediate red flag appears, but traders should monitor whether receivables grow faster than revenue in later periods.

## Key risks

- TTM valuation is rich, and the stock is priced for continued execution.
- Inventory has increased materially and must be monitored for signs of overbuild.
- ROE and ROA are still modest relative to the market value being assigned.
- Large goodwill and intangible asset balances create longer-term impairment sensitivity.
- High beta means even modest disappointments can produce large price swings.

## Actionable trader takeaways

1. Fundamentally, `AMD` is in an earnings acceleration phase. That supports bullish positioning only if a trader believes the 2025-12-31 quarter is sustainable rather than peak-like.
2. The best-quality bullish evidence is the combination of revenue growth, margin recovery, and free cash flow expansion occurring at the same time.
3. The main reason to stay cautious is valuation. A 99.28 TTM P/E means the stock is already discounting a lot of future improvement.
4. Inventory growth is the most important non-valuation watchpoint. If future revenue growth slows while inventory stays high, that would weaken the fundamental case quickly.
5. For near-term trading, `AMD` currently screens more like a high-momentum growth compounder than a deep-value semiconductor name. Traders should frame setups around expectations risk, not around cheapness.

## Bottom line
`AMD`’s current fundamentals are strong and getting stronger. The company has real revenue acceleration, materially better quarterly profitability, strong liquidity, manageable absolute debt, and healthy free cash flow. The stock’s recent outperformance is supported by the fundamentals in the dataset. The main constraint is valuation: the market is no longer waiting for the turnaround, it is already paying for continuation. That makes `AMD` fundamentally attractive but execution-sensitive.
