FINAL TRANSACTION PROPOSAL: **HOLD**

For `BND`, the most relevant indicator set in this macro regime is: `close_10_ema`, `close_50_sma`, `close_200_sma`, `macd`, `rsi`, `boll_ub`, `boll_lb`, and `atr`. That mix is appropriate because it covers short-, medium-, and long-term trend, momentum confirmation, overextension/range location, and volatility/risk sizing without wasting slots on redundant variants like `macds`, `macdh`, or `boll`.

The technical picture has improved meaningfully since the late-March washout, but it still looks more like a carry-friendly recovery than the start of a major duration rally. `BND` closed at `73.88` on `2026-04-15`, above the `close_10_ema` at `73.69`, above the `close_50_sma` at `73.80`, and well above the `close_200_sma` at `72.92`. That alignment says trend structure is constructive across all major horizons. The problem is magnitude: price is only about `0.11%` above the 50-day average, so the medium-term breakout is shallow, not decisive. The long-term trend is still positive because the 50-day sits well above the 200-day, but the instrument remains below the late-February high near `74.69`, so the recovery is incomplete.

Momentum has turned better. `macd` moved from `-0.276` on `2026-03-27` to `+0.043` on `2026-04-15`, which is a clean improvement and effectively a shift from negative to mildly positive momentum. `rsi` rose from `36.7` on `2026-03-27` to `58.8` on `2026-04-14`. That matters because it shows the rebound has gone from oversold repair into moderate bullish momentum, but not into overbought territory. In other words, upside is still possible, but the market is no longer washed out enough to offer a deeply asymmetric entry if inflation or yields reprice higher.

The Bollinger structure argues for restraint rather than aggression. On `2026-04-14`, price at `73.98` was just under `boll_ub` at `74.07`, and `boll_lb` was `72.77`. That places `BND` in the upper part of its recent range, not at the beginning of a volatility expansion. With sticky inflation, a positively sloped curve, and the 10-year Treasury at `4.26%` and 30-year at `4.87%` as of `2026-04-14`, this upper-band positioning looks more like a test of resistance than a clean trend acceleration. If a true duration rally were forming, I would want to see price press through the upper band and then hold above the February resistance cluster; the evidence does not show that yet.

`atr` falling from about `0.317` on `2026-03-30` to `0.274` on `2026-04-15` reinforces the same conclusion. Volatility is compressing while price grinds up. For `BND`, that usually fits a range-trade or slow income-driven advance, not an impulsive move. In the stated macro regime, that makes sense: Fed policy is no longer tightening, which supports carry and stabilizes the front end, but CPI and PPI reacceleration reduce the odds of a fast easing cycle and keep long-duration upside capped.

Actionably, the setup favors `HOLD` over fresh aggressive buying. Existing long exposure still has support from the improving trend stack and positive carry. New buying is more attractive on pullbacks into the `73.70` to `73.80` zone around the 10 EMA and 50 SMA than on strength near the upper band. A confirmed upside continuation would require a sustained move through roughly `74.07` to `74.10`, with room afterward toward the late-February supply area around `74.31` to `74.54`, then the prior peak near `74.69`. On the downside, a failure back below the 10 EMA and 50 SMA would weaken the recovery, and a break toward `72.77` to `72.86` would suggest the late-March low zone is back in play.

The macro overlay keeps the bullish case limited. In a true risk-on equity regime, breakouts can extend, but `BND` is not primarily a beta instrument here; it is still dominated by duration and term-premium behavior. That means soft-landing conditions help credit carry, but sticky inflation and a steep curve still constrain NAV upside. So the technical read is: better, but not liberated. Constructive enough to keep, not strong enough to chase.
