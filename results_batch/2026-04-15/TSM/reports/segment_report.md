## TSM Segment View

TSM is best understood as a single foundry franchise with multiple demand platforms rather than a diversified conglomerate. The core business-mix point is that **revenue durability is increasingly tied to AI/HPC demand and leading-edge process leadership**, while smartphone and broader consumer-related volumes remain the main swing factor for utilization outside the AI cycle. The prefetched quarterly income statement shows strong top-line acceleration from **TWD 868.5B in 2024-12 to TWD 1,046.1B in 2025-12**, with gross profit rising from **TWD 512.4B to TWD 652.0B** and EBIT from **TWD 451.3B to TWD 595.4B**. That indicates favorable mix, pricing, and utilization, which is consistent with stronger leading-edge/AI exposure.

From a segment-quality perspective, the highest-value economic engine is the advanced-node franchise and the attached advanced packaging ecosystem used by AI accelerators and high-performance compute customers. Recent news flow in the provided set reinforces that AI hardware demand remains the primary external catalyst, while the ASML/China headlines highlight a second-order risk: geopolitical controls can reshape regional demand and equipment availability even if they also raise barriers to entry. Mature-node foundry remains important for breadth and utilization stability, but it is strategically lower margin and more exposed to cyclical pricing pressure. The key concentration risk is that TSM’s economics are increasingly dependent on a relatively narrow set of very large HPC/AI customers and on sustaining its technology lead at the frontier.
