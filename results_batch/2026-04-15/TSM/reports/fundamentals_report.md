TSM fundamental report as of 2026-04-15.

All statement figures below appear to be in TSM’s local reporting currency from the feed, likely TWD. The supplied data does not show a new weekly filing event; the most relevant 1-week signal is price-relative performance, while the core fundamental picture remains dominated by TSM’s very strong 2025 operating trajectory.

TSM remains one of the strongest large-cap semiconductor fundamentals in the dataset: market cap about 1.95T, TTM revenue about 3.81T, gross profit about 2.28T, EBITDA about 2.61T, and net income about 1.72T. Profitability is exceptional, with profit margin 45.1%, operating margin 53.9%, ROE 35.1%, and ROA 16.6%. Liquidity is also strong, with a current ratio of 2.62. The main valuation tradeoff is that the stock is no longer cheap on trailing numbers: TTM P/E is 36.0x and price/book is 57.4x. However, forward P/E drops to 20.3x and forward EPS rises to 18.43 from 10.41 TTM, which implies the market expects another large earnings step-up rather than flatlining.

The income statement trend is the clearest bullish fundamental signal for TSM. Quarterly revenue rose from 839.3B in 2025-03-31 to 1,046.1B in 2025-12-31, a roughly 24.6% sequential build across the year. Q4 2025 revenue was up about 20.5% versus Q4 2024. Margin expansion accompanied that growth, which matters more than revenue alone. Gross margin improved from about 59.0% in Q4 2024 to about 62.3% in Q4 2025. Operating margin improved from about 49.0% to about 54.0%. Net margin improved from about 41.4% to about 48.3%. Net income rose from 359.8B in Q4 2024 to 505.7B in Q4 2025, roughly 40.6% YoY growth. That combination of accelerating revenue plus expanding margins usually indicates pricing power, favorable mix, and high utilization rather than low-quality growth.

Earnings quality also looks strong. EBITDA stayed above 600B in each of the last five reported quarters and reached 757.5B in Q4 2025. EBIT was 595.4B in Q4 2025 against interest expense of only about 3.0B, while TSM also generated positive net interest income of 26.5B. That means the balance sheet is strong enough that financing costs are not meaningfully diluting operating performance.

The balance sheet is a major support to the bull case in TSM. At 2025-12-31, cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments were about 3.13T, versus total debt of about 1.06T. Even using total debt, TSM still sits in a large net cash position. Stockholders’ equity increased from 4.29T at 2024-12-31 to 5.42T at 2025-12-31. Working capital improved from 1.82T to 2.36T over the same period. Total debt increased only modestly year over year while cash expanded materially, so leverage did not fund the growth story in an unhealthy way.

Asset quality and operating support metrics also look clean. Inventory was 288.1B at 2025-12-31, almost flat versus 287.9B a year earlier, even though revenue was substantially higher. That is a favorable sign because it does not suggest unsold buildup. Receivables were 282.1B, also manageable relative to quarterly revenue above 1.0T. TSM continues to convert sales into cash without obvious working-capital stress.

Cash flow is strong, but traders should notice one data-normalization issue. Quarterly operating cash flow was 625.6B, 497.1B, 426.8B, and 725.5B through 2025, while quarterly free cash flow was 292.5B, 197.5B, 138.4B, and 364.0B. That implies very large internal cash generation even during a heavy investment cycle. Q4 2025 free cash flow of 364.0B covered dividends of 129.7B by about 2.8x. Across the four 2025 quarters, simple summation of reported quarterly free cash flow is about 992.4B, while the snapshot field shows free cash flow of 643.4B. Traders should normalize on one vendor methodology before building a valuation model, because the discrepancy can materially alter FCF yield assumptions.

Capex remains the main strategic and valuation balancing factor in TSM. Capital expenditure was 333.1B in Q1 2025, 299.6B in Q2, 288.4B in Q3, and 361.5B in Q4. Net PPE rose from 3.28T at 2024-12-31 to 3.74T at 2025-12-31, and construction in progress was still very high at 1.52T. This is fundamentally positive if demand remains strong because it supports future capacity and node leadership, but it also means TSM needs continued high utilization and mix strength to keep returns elevated. If semiconductor demand, especially AI-related demand, slows materially, the market will start focusing on underutilization risk and the valuation multiple could compress quickly.

Capital allocation is conservative. TSM is paying a dividend yield of 0.92%, so the equity story is still primarily growth and execution rather than income. There is no meaningful buyback signal in the cash flow data. Dividend payments are steady and well covered by operating cash flow and free cash flow, which lowers downside risk relative to more aggressively levered semiconductor names.

On market behavior, TSM gained 2.51% in the last week, underperforming XLK at 6.06% and lagging several peers such as AMD, AVGO, and NVDA over that short window. That is worth noting because it suggests some near-term digestion after a strong run. Over longer horizons, the trend is much stronger: TSM is up 10.56% in 1 month, 13.57% in 3 months, 24.52% in 6 months, and 17.69% YTD. It ranked 2/9 in its peer set on 3-month performance and generated strong alpha versus XLK over 1 month, 3 months, 6 months, and YTD. Read-through: the market has already recognized the fundamental acceleration, so the debate is shifting from “is the business strong?” to “how much of the strength is already priced?”

The macro backdrop is mixed but not hostile for TSM. The Fed funds rate is 3.64% and stable, unemployment is 4.3%, and payrolls are still growing, which is broadly supportive for demand and growth multiples. However, CPI and PPI both moved higher sequentially in the latest print, and the long end of the Treasury curve is elevated, with the 20-year at 4.84% and the 30-year at 4.87%. For TSM, that means the operating backdrop is still good, but a higher-rate long end could limit further multiple expansion even if earnings remain strong.

Most actionable read-through for traders in TSM:
- The core business trend is bullish: revenue, margins, and net income all improved materially through 2025.
- The balance sheet gives TSM unusual resilience for such a capex-heavy company; this is not a fragile growth story.
- The main risk is not weak operations today, but high expectations embedded in valuation and the need to keep fabs highly utilized.
- Short-term underperformance versus peers in the last week looks more like consolidation than a fundamental breakdown.
- If upcoming data confirms continued margin strength and cash conversion, the forward P/E near 20x can still look reasonable; if growth normalizes abruptly, trailing valuation metrics leave little room for disappointment.
