**ARM Fundamental Report As Of 2026-04-15**

ARM remains a fundamentally strong but richly valued semiconductor name. The latest prefetched snapshot shows a business with exceptional gross margins, rising revenue, large cash reserves, and a balance sheet that is still net-cash positive even as investment spending ramps. The main tension for traders is that the operating business is improving, but the stock already reflects very aggressive growth expectations: ARM trades at about 209.7x TTM earnings and 74.3x forward earnings, with a 21.7x price-to-book multiple and a high 3.338 beta.

The core fundamental story is straightforward. ARM is not a commodity chip maker; it is an IP and royalty platform. That business model shows up clearly in the numbers. TTM revenue is $4.671B, gross profit is $4.554B, and implied gross margin is roughly 97.5%. That is extremely high even for quality semiconductor-adjacent software/IP businesses. EBITDA is $1.084B, net income is $801M, profit margin is 17.1%, operating margin is 15.4%, ROE is 11.3%, and ROA is 5.9%. Those returns are solid, but not yet at a level that fully explains the current valuation on their own. In other words, the business quality is real, but the multiple still requires a strong future growth path to be justified.

**Company Profile And Operating Quality**

ARM operates in Technology, within Semiconductors, and sits at a market cap of about $169.2B. The financial profile matches a licensing-heavy model rather than a capital-intensive manufacturer. Cost of revenue is minimal relative to sales, while the real battleground is operating expense discipline, especially R&D. That means the key fundamental questions for traders are not about factory utilization or commodity pricing; they are about royalty growth, design wins, AI/datacenter exposure, product mix, and whether operating leverage can eventually emerge despite heavy reinvestment.

The quality of revenue also looks reasonably supported by deferred revenue and liquidity trends. Total deferred revenue was about $1.058B at 2025-12-31, up from about $938M at 2024-12-31. That is a favorable signal for revenue visibility. Meanwhile, current ratio is 5.427, which indicates substantial short-term financial flexibility.

**Income Statement Trend**

The latest five quarterly revenue prints were:

- 2024-12-31: $983M
- 2025-03-31: $1.241B
- 2025-06-30: $1.053B
- 2025-09-30: $1.135B
- 2025-12-31: $1.242B

That sequence matters. ARM exited calendar 2025 with quarterly revenue back at the prior peak level, and the latest quarter grew about 26.3% year over year versus 2024-12-31. Sequentially, revenue also increased 9.4% from $1.135B to $1.242B. This is a constructive top-line pattern and supports the view that ARM is still in an expansion phase.

Gross profit remained consistently strong:

- 2024-12-31: $955M
- 2025-03-31: $1.213B
- 2025-06-30: $1.023B
- 2025-09-30: $1.106B
- 2025-12-31: $1.212B

Gross margin stayed near the high-90% range throughout, which reinforces that ARM's commercial model remains structurally attractive.

The more nuanced story is below gross profit. Operating income was:

- 2024-12-31: $175M
- 2025-03-31: $410M
- 2025-06-30: $114M
- 2025-09-30: $163M
- 2025-12-31: $191M

The March 2025 quarter was unusually strong on an operating basis, while the following two quarters normalized lower. The latest quarter improved sequentially, but not enough to establish a clean operating-margin expansion trend. Net income also shows mixed conversion from revenue growth into bottom-line growth:

- 2024-12-31: $252M
- 2025-03-31: $210M
- 2025-06-30: $130M
- 2025-09-30: $238M
- 2025-12-31: $223M

Despite strong revenue growth in the latest quarter, net income on 2025-12-31 was down about 11.5% year over year versus 2024-12-31, and diluted EPS fell from $0.24 to $0.21. That is a meaningful point for traders: ARM's revenue momentum is currently stronger than its earnings momentum.

A major reason is expense growth. R&D spending increased sharply across the same period:

- 2024-12-31: $533M
- 2025-03-31: $546M
- 2025-06-30: $650M
- 2025-09-30: $691M
- 2025-12-31: $737M

That is a roughly 38% increase year over year in quarterly R&D. SG&A also rose from $247M to $284M over the same span. This tells traders two things. First, management is still investing aggressively, likely to defend architecture leadership and capture AI/datacenter/mobile opportunities. Second, near-term earnings upside can be muted even when revenue prints are strong.

There were also unusual items affecting comparability in some quarters, especially 2025-03-31 and 2025-09-30. Normalized EBITDA was materially above reported EBITDA in those periods, so clean quarter-to-quarter comparisons should focus more on recurring revenue, gross profit, deferred revenue, and operating cash flow than on any single quarter's GAAP earnings figure.

**Balance Sheet Strength**

The balance sheet is a clear positive.

At 2025-12-31, ARM reported:

- Total assets: $10.176B
- Total equity: $7.798B
- Total liabilities: $2.378B
- Total debt: $461M
- Cash and cash equivalents: $2.807B
- Cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments: $3.542B
- Working capital: $4.679B

That is a strong liquidity position. Even after including debt, ARM appears to have net cash of roughly $3.081B when cash plus short-term investments are compared with total debt. Equity also grew from $6.419B at 2024-12-31 to $7.798B at 2025-12-31, while retained earnings rose from $3.333B to $4.134B. That is favorable capital accumulation.

Receivables rose meaningfully, which traders should monitor. Total receivables increased from $1.592B at 2024-12-31 to $2.011B at 2025-12-31. Some of that is naturally consistent with higher revenue, but if receivables growth continues to outpace revenue growth, it could become a working-capital risk signal. For now it does not look alarming, but it is worth watching alongside cash conversion.

Net PPE rose from $599M to $1.189B over the same one-year period. That is a large increase and aligns with much higher capital expenditure in the cash-flow statement. For a company known for IP intensity rather than fabrication intensity, this suggests ARM is scaling infrastructure, engineering capacity, or strategic investment programs. Traders should not automatically treat this as negative, but it does mean the business is becoming somewhat more investment-heavy than the pure royalty narrative suggests.

One notable data-quality issue should be flagged. The top-line fundamentals snapshot shows debt-to-equity of 5.912, but the balance-sheet figures imply something closer to 0.06x if total debt of $461M is divided by equity of $7.798B. That suggests either a vendor-definition mismatch or a formatting issue. Traders using leverage screens should verify that field before relying on it.

**Cash Flow And Earnings Quality**

Cash generation is still healthy, but the trajectory is more mixed than the headline valuation implies.

Quarterly operating cash flow was:

- 2024-12-31: $423M
- 2025-03-31: $258M
- 2025-06-30: $332M
- 2025-09-30: $567M
- 2025-12-31: $365M

Quarterly free cash flow was:

- 2024-12-31: $360M
- 2025-03-31: $180M
- 2025-06-30: $171M
- 2025-09-30: $427M
- 2025-12-31: $181M

The latest quarter's free cash flow of $181M was down sharply from $360M a year earlier, even though revenue was materially higher. The main driver was capex. Capital expenditure increased from $63M in 2024-12-31 to $184M in 2025-12-31. That is a large step-up and directly compresses free cash flow.

From a trader's perspective, this creates an important distinction:

- The operating business is growing.
- The free-cash-flow yield story is not improving nearly as fast, because ARM is spending more heavily.

Another quality-of-earnings issue is stock-based compensation. Quarterly SBC was $227M, $193M, $241M, $265M, and $285M across the five reported quarters. Over the latest four quarters, SBC totaled roughly $984M, which is higher than reported TTM net income of $801M and around the same order of magnitude as TTM free cash flow. That does not invalidate the business, but it does mean dilution and compensation structure should remain front and center in valuation debates.

Share count has also drifted up. Diluted average shares increased from 1.064B to 1.069B year over year in the latest comparable December quarter, and ordinary shares outstanding also rose across the reported periods. For a company trading at a premium multiple, even mild dilution matters.

**Macro Backdrop**

The macro setting is broadly constructive for growth equities, but not uniformly easy. Effective Fed Funds is 3.64% as of 2026-03-01 and has been stable. The 2Y-10Y spread is positive at 0.50%, so the yield curve is upward sloping rather than inverted. VIX at 18.36 suggests moderate rather than stressed market conditions.

That backdrop supports higher-quality growth stories like ARM. However, the 10-year Treasury at 4.26% is still high enough to matter for duration-sensitive valuation. For a name priced on future earnings expansion, any reacceleration in inflation or upward pressure on long yields could hit the multiple before it hits the business. ARM therefore carries both company-specific execution risk and macro multiple risk.

**Actionable Insights For Traders**

ARM's fundamentals are strong enough to support a premium, but probably not strong enough to support any valuation indefinitely. The bull case rests on three durable strengths: extremely high gross margins, visible liquidity and deferred revenue support, and a top line that is still compounding at a strong rate. The bear case rests on valuation, expense intensity, elevated SBC, and recent cash-flow compression from heavier capex.

The most important things to monitor in the next reporting cycle are:

- Whether revenue can sustain or exceed the recent $1.24B quarterly run rate.
- Whether operating margin starts to expand again despite rising R&D.
- Whether free cash flow rebounds toward the stronger $300M to $400M quarterly range.
- Whether receivables growth stays proportional to sales.
- Whether deferred revenue continues rising, which would strengthen visibility.
- Whether share-based compensation and share-count growth remain elevated.

The cleanest trader read-through is this: ARM is fundamentally a high-quality growth franchise, but right now it is priced more like a future compounding machine than a currently optimized earnings vehicle. If the next quarter brings revenue acceleration plus margin stabilization, the premium can persist. If revenue is merely in line and opex stays heavy, the stock is exposed to de-rating even if the business remains healthy.
