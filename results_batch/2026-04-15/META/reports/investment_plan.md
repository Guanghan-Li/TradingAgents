My decision for META is Buy, aligned more with the bull analyst, but with disciplined sizing rather than a full-position chase. The strongest evidence is the structured underwriting: the bull scenario is 30%, the base case is 50%, and the bear case is 20%, so the probability-weighted setup favors ownership. The position sizing work also explicitly supports action with a 2.0% initial weight, 4.0% target weight, medium-high conviction, and a 0.5% max portfolio loss. That is not a Sell setup, and it is stronger than a passive Hold.

The bull side’s best argument is that META’s core economics remain exceptional. Family of Apps is 98% of revenue, TTM revenue is about $201.0B, EBITDA is $101.9B, net income is $60.5B, operating margin is still 41.3%, ROE is 30.2%, and liquidity remains strong. The Broadcom AI chip deal is also meaningful because it supports the thesis that AI infrastructure is becoming a monetization enabler across META’s existing distribution base, not just a vague cost center.

The bear side’s best argument is timing and expectations. META has already rebounded 27.8% in less than three trading weeks, is still below the 200-day SMA near 680.72, has RSI at 67.11, and is pressing the upper Bollinger Band. On fundamentals, capex is very large, operating margin has compressed, buybacks paused in Q4 2025, and the late-April earnings catalyst requires real evidence that AI spending is translating into measurable ad efficiency, engagement, or revenue benefits. Those are valid risks, but they argue for controlled sizing, not avoidance.

The reason I am choosing Buy instead of Hold is that the underwriting already provides a risk-managed implementation plan: start at 2.0%, scale only on confirmation, and cap max loss at 0.5%. Holding off entirely would underweight the 80% combined bull/base probability and ignore that the business quality, sentiment, macro backdrop, and AI narrative are all constructive. Selling would be too aggressive because the bear thesis is mostly about valuation, timing, and proof risk, not franchise deterioration.

Strategic action for the trader: initiate or maintain a 2.0% starter position in META now, not the full 4.0%. Add toward the 4.0% target only if META closes decisively above the 200-day SMA area around 680 to 685 with supportive volume, or if earnings deliver a clean beat-and-raise with disciplined capex commentary and specific AI monetization evidence. Use the 625 to 630 zone as the key risk line, consistent with the 50-day SMA, 10-day EMA, and roughly 2 ATR stop logic. If META loses that area on a closing basis, reduce or exit the starter to keep the portfolio loss near the 0.5% cap.

The key lesson I would apply here is not to confuse a great company with an automatically great entry, but also not to let tactical resistance override a favorable underwriting setup. META deserves exposure, but the trade should be built in stages because the next move depends on confirmation at the 200-day SMA and late-April earnings proof.
