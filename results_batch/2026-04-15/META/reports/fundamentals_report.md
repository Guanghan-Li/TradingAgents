## META Fundamental Report as of April 15, 2026

META remains a fundamentally strong mega-cap platform business, but the core debate has shifted from whether the business is profitable to whether its very large AI and infrastructure spend will earn an adequate return quickly enough. The latest snapshot is still high quality: TTM revenue is about $201.0B, gross profit $164.8B, EBITDA $101.9B, net income $60.5B, operating margin 41.3%, profit margin 30.1%, ROE 30.2%, ROA 16.2%, and the current ratio is 2.6. For traders, the setup is fundamentally constructive, but not clean: revenue growth is strong, liquidity is strong, and forward earnings expectations are high, while capital intensity, margin compression, and some accounting noise are the main risks.

### Company Profile

META is a Communication Services company in Internet Content & Information. The business is still fundamentally a scaled digital advertising and engagement platform built around its family of social and messaging apps, with an increasing strategic emphasis on AI infrastructure and longer-duration platform investment. That shows up clearly in the financials: the company is still generating elite margins and cash from the core business, but its balance sheet and cash flow statement now also show a very large buildout in property, equipment, and construction in progress.

### Income Statement and Earnings Quality

The most recent reported quarter, 2025-12-31, was strong on revenue and still strong on profit in absolute dollars:

- Revenue: $59.9B
- Gross profit: $49.0B
- Operating income: $24.7B
- Net income: $22.8B
- Diluted EPS: $8.88

Against 2024-12-31, that implies:

- Revenue growth of about 23.8%
- Gross profit growth of about 23.9%
- Operating income growth of about 5.9%
- Net income growth of about 9.3%
- Diluted EPS growth of about 10.7%

The important nuance is margin shape. Q4 2025 gross margin stayed extremely high at about 81.8%, but operating margin was about 41.3%, down sharply from roughly 48.3% in Q4 2024. That says the business did not lose gross monetization power; instead, spending below gross profit ramped hard.

Expense growth confirms that reading:

- Q4 2025 operating expense was $24.2B versus $16.2B a year earlier
- R&D was $17.1B versus $12.2B a year earlier
- SG&A was $7.1B versus $4.0B a year earlier

For META, this is the key fundamental tradeoff: top-line growth remains excellent, but management is choosing to spend aggressively into AI, infrastructure, and platform capacity, which is compressing operating leverage.

One quarter needs special handling: 2025-09-30. That quarter showed $20.5B of operating income but only $2.7B of net income because tax provision surged to about $19.0B. That looks like a major tax-driven distortion rather than a collapse in the underlying business. Traders should not annualize the $1.05 diluted EPS from that quarter as a normal run rate.

### Cash Flow, Capital Allocation, and Investment Intensity

META is still producing very large operating cash flows:

- Q1 2025 OCF: $24.0B
- Q2 2025 OCF: $25.6B
- Q3 2025 OCF: $30.0B
- Q4 2025 OCF: $36.2B

The issue is not cash generation. The issue is how much of that cash is being reinvested.

Capital expenditures were extremely high and rising:

- Q1 2025 capex: $12.9B
- Q2 2025 capex: $16.5B
- Q3 2025 capex: $18.8B
- Q4 2025 capex: $21.4B

That is roughly $69.7B of capex across the four reported 2025 quarters, equal to about one-third of TTM revenue. Even so, free cash flow stayed positive each quarter:

- Q1 2025 FCF: $11.1B
- Q2 2025 FCF: $9.0B
- Q3 2025 FCF: $11.2B
- Q4 2025 FCF: $14.8B

That is a very important signal for traders: META is not a cash-stressed story. It is a cash-rich business choosing to run a heavy investment cycle.

Capital allocation also shifted:

- Dividends remained steady at about $1.3B per quarter
- Buybacks were heavy in Q1 through Q3 2025, then paused in Q4 2025
- META issued about $29.9B of long-term debt in Q4 2025

That combination suggests management wanted additional balance sheet flexibility during the infrastructure buildout. It is not a distress signal by itself, but it does tell traders that buybacks are no longer the only outlet for excess cash.

One data-quality caution matters: the prefetched headline free cash flow field is $23.4B, but the sum of the quarterly FCF figures shown in the cash flow statement is materially higher. Traders using FCF multiples should verify the vendor methodology before relying on a single FCF figure.

### Balance Sheet Strength

META's balance sheet is still strong despite the debt raise:

- Cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments: $81.6B at 2025-12-31
- Total debt: $83.9B
- Net debt: $22.9B
- Stockholders' equity: $217.2B
- Current assets: $108.7B
- Current liabilities: $41.8B
- Current ratio: 2.6

That means liquidity remains strong and net leverage is modest relative to EBITDA. Using the prefetched EBITDA figure of about $101.9B, net debt is only a small fraction of annual EBITDA.

The real balance-sheet story is fixed asset growth:

- Net PPE rose to $196.8B from $136.3B a year earlier
- Gross PPE rose to $254.1B from $179.6B a year earlier
- Construction in progress rose to $50.5B from $26.8B a year earlier

That is the clearest evidence that META is in a major infrastructure expansion phase. If traders are bullish, this is the asset base that could support future AI monetization and efficiency gains. If traders are cautious, this is the evidence that returns on capital need to stay high to justify the spend.

### Share Count, Valuation, and Market Expectations

META still benefits from a shrinking share base:

- Diluted average shares fell from 2.599B in Q4 2024 to 2.565B in Q4 2025

That modest reduction helped EPS growth. On valuation:

- TTM P/E: 28.6
- Forward P/E: 18.9
- TTM EPS: 23.52
- Forward EPS: 35.62
- Price to book: 7.82
- Dividend yield: 0.32%
- Beta: 1.31

The spread between trailing and forward P/E is important. The market is paying for meaningful forward earnings growth despite the capex surge. That implies expectations are already elevated. For traders, this cuts both ways: strong execution can support rerating, but any miss on monetization, margins, or capex discipline can compress the multiple.

### Macro Backdrop

The macro backdrop is acceptable, not exceptionally easy:

- Federal funds rate: 3.64%
- 10-year Treasury: 4.30%
- Unemployment rate: 4.3%
- VIX: 18.36

For META, that means the ad demand backdrop is still reasonably supported by a stable labor market, but the discount-rate backdrop is not especially loose. Higher long-end yields matter more for companies making large long-duration AI and infrastructure bets.

### Actionable Insights for Traders Following META

- The bullish fundamental case is that META still has elite gross margins, strong revenue growth, enormous operating cash flow, modest net leverage, and visible forward EPS expansion.
- The main bearish case is not weakness in the core franchise. It is that capex has become massive, margin expansion has paused, debt has risen, and buybacks slowed as cash is redirected into infrastructure.
- The most important next-quarter checkpoints are revenue growth versus expense growth, capex guidance, whether buybacks resume meaningfully, and whether tax-related noise disappears.
- If operating margin stabilizes while revenue keeps compounding, the current forward P/E can still look reasonable. If capex keeps climbing faster than monetization, traders should expect more valuation sensitivity.
- META currently looks stronger as a quality-growth compounder than as a near-term margin expansion story.
