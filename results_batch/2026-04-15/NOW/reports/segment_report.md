### ServiceNow (NOW) Segment View
ServiceNow does not report fully separate segment financials in the prefetched data, so the decomposition below is an inferred workflow-family view rather than a GAAP segment breakout. The mix still looks high quality: a recurring enterprise SaaS base, strong cross-sell across the Now Platform, and durable workflow entrenchment in IT, employee, and customer operations.

The core business remains IT-centric workflow automation, which likely anchors retention and expansion. Employee workflows and customer/industry workflows broaden wallet share, while Creator/Platform/AI products are strategically important because they increase platform stickiness and raise the odds of multi-product standardization. The main concentration risk is that all of these lines still depend on the same enterprise workflow automation budget and the same platform adoption narrative, so this is more diversified by use case than by economic engine.

Reported company-level economics imply healthy but mixed margin direction. Revenue increased from $2.96B in 2024-12-31 to $3.57B in 2025-12-31, while gross profit rose from $2.33B to $2.73B; however, cost of revenue grew faster than revenue, implying mild gross-margin pressure even as EBITDA stayed solid. News flow points to continued AI-led demand expansion, including Brazil AI Workflow Hub investment, but also a real competitive/valuation risk if the market leans into an AI disruption narrative before near-term results.
