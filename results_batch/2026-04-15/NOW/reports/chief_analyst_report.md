- Thesis: The final decision is Hold / Neutral because the structured evidence supports risk-controlled exposure, not a directional commitment. The scenario map assigns NOW a 50% Base case, 30% Bull case, and 20% Bear case. That distribution does not justify Sell, because the bear case is not dominant, but it also does not justify Buy, because the highest-probability outcome is range-bound execution rather than confirmed upside acceleration.

The bull case is credible. NOW still has strong business quality: Q4 revenue grew about 20.7% year over year, gross margin is about 77.5%, free-cash-flow margin is about 37.3%, and the company retains a liquid balance sheet. The segment mix is broad, with IT Workflows at roughly 35% of revenue, Employee Workflows around 20%, Customer & Industry Workflows around 20%, and Creator / Platform / AI around 25%. That mix gives NOW multiple paths for cross-sell, platform consolidation, and AI-led expansion. The AI Workflow Hub expansion in Brazil also supports the medium-term view that NOW is still investing behind international AI workflow demand.

The bear case is mainly about timing, valuation, and proof. NOW closed at 94.19, still below the 10 EMA at 94.30, the 50 SMA at 106.16, and the 200 SMA at 156.33. RSI recovering from 22.40 to 41.23 and a less negative MACD show stabilization, but not trend repair. The stock remains in a damaged technical structure, with elevated ATR around 5.72 and a near-term earnings catalyst that can move the thesis sharply in either direction.

Fundamental quality also has real questions beneath the headline strength. Cost of revenue rose from 631M to 834M year over year, faster than revenue, while Q4 net income grew only about 4.4%. That keeps margin durability in focus, especially because the highest-upside Creator / Platform / AI segment is also where infrastructure, delivery, and competitive costs may rise. The factor rules are similarly split: AI capex and workflow expansion support the upside case, while valuation stretch under slowing growth is the main downside risk.

Macro does not force a bearish conclusion, but it limits the case for aggressive buying. The backdrop is soft-landing rather than recessionary, with unemployment at 4.3%, fed funds at 3.64%, and a positively sloped 2Y-10Y curve. That supports enterprise software spending resilience. However, the 10Y at 4.26% and 30Y at 4.87% keep pressure on premium software multiples, meaning even solid but unspectacular Q1 results may not be enough for a sustained rerating.

The committee should therefore treat Hold as an active risk-management decision. NOW is too fundamentally strong for Sell without evidence of weaker cRPO, billings, guidance, AI attach, or competitive positioning. But NOW is also too technically damaged and catalyst-exposed for Buy before confirmation. The correct posture is Neutral: preserve limited participation through a small starter position, wait for Q1 validation, and be prepared to scale only if both the fundamentals and price action confirm the bull thesis.
