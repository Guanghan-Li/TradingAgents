**Segment View for AMZN**

AMZN remains a portfolio of one scale retail/logistics engine and several higher-margin service layers. The prefetched financials show TTM revenue of $716.9B, operating margin of 10.5%, and quarterly operating income that remained strong through 2025 despite seasonal cost pressure. Segment detail is not fully provided in the prefetched data, so revenue shares below are approximate reportable-segment estimates rather than exact disclosed mix.

AWS is the highest-quality business unit economically: structurally high-margin, recurring, and now reinforced by AI demand and Anthropic-related consumption growth. North America retail and marketplace remain the cash-flow and customer-frequency base, with margin leverage from fulfillment efficiency, ads, seller services, and Prime monetization. International is strategically important but more cyclical and lower-margin, with profitability dependent on regional scale and FX. Advertising and subscriptions are not separately reported as primary segments in the prefetched data, but they are key margin-accretive overlays across the retail ecosystem.

Recent segment catalysts are mixed. Alexa+ expansion in Italy supports devices, Prime engagement, and AI assistant monetization, though near-term profit contribution is likely modest. The reported Globalstar acquisition points to a longer-horizon connectivity/satellite push that could support logistics, devices, Kuiper-style broadband ambitions, and enterprise connectivity, but it raises capital-allocation and execution risk. Warehouse labor headlines are a reputational and operating-risk signal for the fulfillment-heavy retail base.
