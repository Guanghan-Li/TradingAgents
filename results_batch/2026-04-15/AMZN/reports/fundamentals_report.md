4. Valuation is more acceptable if forward EPS materializes. Trailing P/E near 35 is demanding, but forward P/E near 26 suggests the market expects strong earnings growth rather than pure multiple inflation.
5. After a 12.32% one-week move, AMZN is fundamentally strong but tactically less forgiving. Any future miss on operating margin, capex discipline, or free-cash-flow conversion could matter more because the stock has already outperformed.

Overall, the fundamental picture for AMZN is positive. The company has scale, margin improvement, strong absolute cash generation, balance-sheet flexibility, and leadership relative performance. For traders, that makes AMZN a high-quality fundamentally bullish name, but one where future upside probably depends on continued proof that capex-heavy growth converts into durable operating profit and better free-cash-flow conversion.
