Macro view for `VICI`: the regime looks like late-cycle but not recessionary. Labor is still stable (`4.3%` unemployment, `+178k` payrolls), the curve is positively sloped (`2Y-10Y +50 bp`), and market stress is contained (`VIX 18.36`). The growth backdrop is therefore still constructive for `VICI` tenant fundamentals and rent collection.

The problem for `VICI` is inflation and rates. March CPI and PPI accelerated on a sequential basis, while the effective fed funds rate has paused at `3.64%` after prior cuts. That points to a Fed likely on hold near term, with an easing path that is slower and more conditional than a pure disinflationary bull case. For `VICI`, that is mixed: stable macro growth helps gaming and leisure demand, but a `10Y` yield at `4.26%` and a steep long end raise the valuation hurdle for REITs and can limit multiple expansion.

Cross-asset implications for `VICI`: credit-sensitive and economically exposed cash flows look fine, but duration-sensitive equity valuation remains the main macro headwind. In practical terms, `VICI` screens more like a quality income name with resilient operating backdrop than a macro beta winner. The key macro risk is sticky inflation keeping long rates elevated or pushing them higher; the main upside catalyst is a renewed disinflation trend that reopens Fed cuts and lowers long-end yields.
