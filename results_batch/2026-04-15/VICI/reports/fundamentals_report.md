# VICI Fundamental Report

## Company profile and business quality

VICI is VICI Properties Inc., a diversified REIT in the Real Estate sector. The company’s financial profile is characteristic of a large-scale net-lease real estate platform: very high gross margins, high operating margins, recurring rental/lease-driven revenue, heavy long-term debt usage, and material dividend distributions.

As of the prefetched data retrieved on 2026-04-15, VICI has a market capitalization of about $30.1 billion, making it a large-cap REIT. Its beta of 0.728 indicates lower volatility than the broader equity market, which is consistent with the defensive income-oriented REIT profile. However, recent relative performance has been weak versus both real estate peers and the XLRE sector ETF.

## Valuation and market setup

VICI trades at a TTM P/E of 10.78x and a forward P/E of 9.54x, with TTM EPS of $2.61 and forward EPS of $2.95. That implies the market is pricing VICI at a relatively modest earnings multiple while expecting earnings growth. Price/book is 1.08x, only slightly above book value of $26.01 per share, suggesting valuation is not demanding relative to the company’s asset base.

The dividend yield is 6.29%, which is a major component of the investment case. Compared with the 10-year Treasury yield of 4.26% and 30-year yield of 4.87% as of 2026-04-14, VICI offers a positive income spread, but not an extremely wide one after accounting for REIT equity risk, tenant concentration risk, and leverage.

Recent trading levels appear below the 200-day average of $30.37 and near the 50-day average of $28.67, with a 52-week range of $26.55 to $34.01. This suggests the stock remains in a lagging technical and sentiment position despite a high dividend yield and low valuation multiples.

## Income statement trends

TTM revenue is $4.006 billion, with gross profit of $3.972 billion, EBITDA of $3.651 billion, and net income of $2.775 billion. The reported profit margin is very high at 69.28%, and operating margin is 80.31%, reflecting the asset-light accounting profile of a REIT with contractual rent streams and limited direct cost of revenue.

Quarterly revenue has been stable and gradually rising through 2025:

- Q1 2025 revenue: $984.2 million
- Q2 2025 revenue: $1.001 billion
- Q3 2025 revenue: $1.007 billion
- Q4 2025 revenue: $1.013 billion

This is a positive fundamental signal: top-line revenue is growing sequentially, albeit slowly. Q4 2025 revenue was also above Q4 2024 revenue of $976.1 million, representing roughly 3.8% year-over-year growth.

Net income was more volatile:

- Q1 2025 net income: $543.6 million
- Q2 2025 net income: $865.1 million
- Q3 2025 net income: $762.0 million
- Q4 2025 net income: $604.8 million

The most important issue in the quarterly statements is the swing in provision for doubtful accounts. Q4 2025 included a $153.1 million provision for doubtful accounts, compared with a negative provision of $142.0 million in Q2 2025 and negative $20.2 million in Q3 2025. That accounting item materially affected operating expenses and reported earnings. Traders should monitor whether this is isolated or an early signal of tenant credit stress.

Interest expense is also significant and stable at roughly $209 million to $214 million per quarter during 2025. Net interest expense was about $206 million in Q4 2025. Because VICI is levered and rate-sensitive, financing costs remain a central driver of earnings quality and valuation.

## Balance sheet strength and leverage

VICI reported total assets of $46.72 billion as of 2025-12-31, up from $45.37 billion at 2024-12-31. Stockholders’ equity increased to $27.80 billion from $26.54 billion over the same period, while retained earnings rose to $2.77 billion from $1.87 billion. This points to retained profitability and balance sheet growth.

Total debt was $17.69 billion at 2025-12-31, broadly stable versus $17.65 billion at 2024-12-31. Net debt was $16.21 billion, also essentially flat year over year. Debt/equity is listed at 62.679, which is meaningful but not unusual for a large REIT. The key question is not simply debt level, but whether cash flow, asset quality, and lease durability are sufficient to support dividends, refinancing, and growth investments.

Current ratio is very high at 17.233, supported by substantial current assets. Cash and equivalents were $563.5 million at year-end 2025, up from $524.6 million at year-end 2024. Current liabilities were $725.0 million, including dividends payable of $486.3 million.

The balance sheet appears liquid in the near term, but long-term debt remains the main structural risk. With long-duration Treasury yields still elevated, VICI’s future refinancing economics should be monitored closely.

## Cash flow and dividend coverage

VICI generated strong operating cash flow and free cash flow in every reported quarter of 2025:

- Q1 2025 free cash flow: $591.7 million
- Q2 2025 free cash flow: $639.2 million
- Q3 2025 free cash flow: $586.1 million
- Q4 2025 free cash flow: $691.6 million

Quarterly free cash flow exceeded quarterly common dividends paid in each reported 2025 quarter. Q4 2025 free cash flow of $691.6 million covered common dividends paid of $480.7 million, implying a cash payout ratio of roughly 69.5% for that quarter. Full-year 2025 quarterly free cash flow sums to approximately $2.509 billion, while quarterly common dividends paid sum to approximately $1.853 billion, implying full-year cash dividend coverage of about 1.35x.

This is one of the strongest supportive points for VICI. The dividend yield is high, but it appears covered by free cash flow based on the provided data. That said, REIT investors should also consider AFFO rather than only GAAP free cash flow; the prefetched dataset does not provide AFFO, so dividend quality should be cross-checked with management’s REIT-specific disclosure.

Capital expenditure is minimal, only $302,000 in Q4 2025 and below $1 million in each reported 2025 quarter, consistent with VICI’s lease structure and investment-property model.

- 1-week return: VICI +1.44% vs XLRE +2.29%, alpha -0.84%
- 1-month return: VICI -0.11% vs XLRE +2.63%, alpha -2.75%
- 3-month return: VICI +2.80% vs XLRE +6.77%, alpha -3.98%
- 6-month return: VICI -6.02% vs XLRE +7.99%, alpha -14.01%
- YTD return: VICI +1.56% vs XLRE +8.23%, alpha -6.67%

Peer ranking is also weak, with VICI ranked 6th out of 7 on a 3-month basis in the provided relative performance table. EQIX, DLR, SPG, PSA, and PLD have all outperformed VICI over the 3-month period.

This creates a split fundamental picture: valuation, dividend yield, and cash-flow coverage look attractive, but market sentiment and relative momentum are poor. Traders should avoid assuming that low valuation alone will drive near-term outperformance without a catalyst.

## Macro backdrop

The macro backdrop is mixed for VICI. The effective federal funds rate is 3.64% as of 2026-03-01 and has been stable for the last two monthly observations after prior declines. The Treasury curve is upward sloping, with the 2-year at 3.76% and 10-year at 4.26%, creating a 2Y-10Y spread of 0.50 percentage points. This is a more normal growth signal than an inverted curve.

Inflation remains relevant. CPI rose sequentially by 0.87% in the latest observation, and PPI rose 1.78%, which could limit how quickly rates decline. For a REIT, sticky inflation and long-term yields can pressure valuation multiples and refinancing assumptions, even if lease escalators provide some inflation protection.

The unemployment rate is 4.30%, down 0.10 percentage points sequentially, and nonfarm payrolls increased by 178,000. That points to a reasonably stable labor market, which is constructive for consumer-facing tenants. VIX at 18.36 is not extreme, suggesting market volatility is contained.

## Actionable trading insights

The bullish case for VICI is based on cheap valuation, strong margins, high dividend yield, and solid cash-flow coverage. At about 10.8x TTM earnings, 9.5x forward earnings, and 1.08x book value, the stock does not appear expensive. The 6.29% dividend yield is supported by quarterly free cash flow in the provided data, and revenue is growing steadily.

The bearish or cautious case is based on relative underperformance, leverage, rate sensitivity, and the Q4 2025 doubtful accounts provision. VICI has materially lagged XLRE over 1-month, 3-month, 6-month, and YTD periods. If investors remain concerned about tenant credit, long-term rates, or REIT refinancing costs, the stock may stay cheap for a reason.

For traders, the most important near-term monitor is whether VICI can reclaim relative strength versus XLRE while maintaining dividend coverage. A move back above the 200-day average near $30.37 would be a constructive technical confirmation that fundamentals are beginning to matter to the market. Conversely, a break toward the 52-week low of $26.55 despite stable earnings would signal persistent distribution or rate-driven pressure.

## Fundamental conclusion

VICI is fundamentally profitable, cash-generative, and attractively valued, with a covered high dividend based on the provided cash flow data. The company’s balance sheet is large and liquid, but leverage and interest expense remain central risks. The main negative is not current profitability; it is the combination of weak relative performance, rate sensitivity, and potential credit-quality questions suggested by the Q4 2025 provision for doubtful accounts.

For an investment committee, VICI should be viewed as an income-oriented REIT with appealing valuation support but limited recent momentum. The stock appears better suited for yield/value-oriented positioning than for momentum leadership unless relative performance improves.
