The relative-performance setup is therefore mixed: the intermediate trend is very strong, but the latest week suggests possible profit-taking, rotation, or a reset after a large three-month move.

## Valuation Snapshot

DELL trades at a trailing P/E of 20.45x and a forward P/E of 12.16x. The gap between trailing EPS of $8.67 and forward EPS of $14.58 implies the market is discounting a significant earnings acceleration. That makes the stock highly sensitive to whether forward earnings expectations are achieved.

Price-to-book is negative at -46.80x because reported book value is negative at -$3.79 per share. This makes conventional P/B valuation not useful for DELL. The negative equity position appears connected to leverage, accumulated capital returns, and treasury stock rather than a simple operating insolvency signal, but it still matters for balance-sheet risk assessment.

Using the prefetched market cap of $117.5B and TTM revenue of $113.5B, DELL trades near 1.0x sales. Adding net debt of approximately $20.0B gives an approximate enterprise value near $137.5B. Against reported EBITDA of approximately $11.46B, approximate EV/EBITDA is around 12.0x. The reported free cash flow figure of $6.61B implies an approximate FCF yield of 5.6%; summing the latest four quarterly cash-flow statements gives higher statement-derived FCF of approximately $8.55B, implying a stronger cash yield, but traders should treat the difference as a data-definition issue and monitor the next filing.

## Income Statement Analysis

The latest reported quarter, ending 2026-01-31, was strong. Revenue was $33.38B, up +23.6% sequentially from $27.01B and up +39.5% year over year from $23.93B. Net income was $2.26B, up +45.9% sequentially and +36.6% year over year. Diluted EPS was $3.37, up +47.8% sequentially and +56.7% year over year.

The key positive is that EPS grew faster than net income due to share-count reduction. Diluted average shares fell from approximately 713.0M in the year-ago quarter to approximately 670.3M in the latest quarter, a decline of about 6.0%. This confirms that buybacks are materially accretive to per-share earnings.

Margins are more nuanced. Latest-quarter gross profit was $6.73B, and gross margin was approximately 20.2%. That was down materially from approximately 24.3% in the year-ago quarter. Operating income was $3.15B, with an operating margin of approximately 9.4%, roughly similar to the year-ago quarter despite lower gross margin. This suggests scale and operating-expense control helped protect operating profitability, but the gross-margin decline is a major watch item.

Quarterly revenue progression has been volatile but upward into the latest quarter: $23.93B in 2025-01, $23.38B in 2025-04, $29.78B in 2025-07, $27.01B in 2025-10, and $33.38B in 2026-01. Net income progression was $1.65B, $0.97B, $1.16B, $1.55B, and $2.26B over the same reported quarters.

The latest quarter also had $3.98B of EBITDA, $3.21B of EBIT, $411M of interest expense, and $540M of tax provision. Net interest income was negative $315M, so financing cost remains a real earnings drag.

## Balance Sheet Analysis

DELL ended the latest quarter with $101.29B of total assets, $103.76B of total liabilities, and negative stockholders' equity of $2.47B. Negative equity is an important structural characteristic for DELL. It does not automatically mean weak operating performance, but it reduces balance-sheet flexibility and makes debt, liquidity, and cash generation more important for equity holders.

Cash and equivalents were $11.53B, up from $3.63B one year earlier. Total debt was $31.50B, up from $24.57B one year earlier. Net debt was $19.98B, slightly lower than $20.93B one year earlier because cash increased significantly. Approximate net debt to statement-derived latest-four-quarter EBITDA is about 1.7x, while total debt to EBITDA is about 2.7x. This is manageable for a large cash-generative company, but not low-risk, especially with short-term rates still near 3.6% and the 10-year Treasury at 4.26%.

Working capital remains negative at -$5.67B, though it improved from -$10.30B one year earlier. Current assets were $57.60B and current liabilities were $63.27B, consistent with the reported current ratio of 0.91. This means DELL relies on cash conversion, supplier terms, deferred revenue, and ongoing operations rather than a large current-asset surplus.

Accounts receivable rose to $17.59B, inventory rose to $10.44B, and accounts payable rose to $33.63B. All three increased sharply year over year. The combination of higher receivables, inventory, and payables is consistent with a much larger revenue base, but it also means working-capital quality should be monitored closely. A reversal in payables support or a slowdown in collections could pressure free cash flow.

Goodwill and other intangibles were approximately $24.08B, essentially stable year over year. Treasury stock rose to $14.53B from $8.50B, reflecting aggressive buybacks. Retained earnings improved to $3.33B from a deficit of -$1.16B, showing earnings accumulation despite capital returns.

## Cash Flow and Capital Allocation

Cash flow was a major strength in the latest quarter. Operating cash flow was $4.67B, capital expenditure was $721M, and free cash flow was $3.95B. This was a sharp improvement from the prior quarter's $503M of FCF and from the year-ago quarter's negative $150M of FCF.

For the latest four reported quarters, operating cash flow summed to approximately $11.19B, capex to approximately $2.63B, and free cash flow to approximately $8.55B. The summary fundamentals report a lower FCF figure of $6.61B, so traders should focus less on one exact number and more on the clear directional point: DELL is generating substantial cash, but reported FCF can swing materially by quarter.

The quality of latest-quarter cash flow needs scrutiny. Working capital contributed +$1.22B to operating cash flow, but this was driven heavily by a $9.75B increase in payables and accrued expenses, offset by a $6.79B increase in receivables and a $3.58B increase in inventory. The large payable contribution helped cash flow, but it may not be repeatable every quarter.

Capital returns are aggressive. In the latest quarter, DELL repurchased $1.86B of stock and paid $346M of dividends. Across the latest four quarters, buybacks totaled approximately $6.40B and dividends totaled approximately $1.46B, for total shareholder returns of about $7.86B. That is close to the statement-derived four-quarter FCF of $8.55B, meaning DELL is returning a very large share of cash generation to shareholders.

The dividend yield is 1.37%, and the primary capital-return lever is buybacks. The buyback program is clearly supporting EPS growth by shrinking the share count, but it also contributes to negative equity and can reduce flexibility if cash flow normalizes lower.

## Macro and Rate Context

The macro backdrop is moderately supportive but not risk-free. The latest effective Fed Funds rate is 3.64%, unchanged in the latest monthly reading, and the yield curve is upward sloping with the 2-year Treasury at 3.76% and 10-year at 4.26%. This suggests a more normal growth backdrop than an inverted-curve recession signal.

However, inflation indicators rose sequentially in the latest data: CPI increased +0.87% sequentially and PPI increased +1.78% sequentially. For DELL, producer-price pressure matters because hardware margins can be sensitive to component, logistics, and supply-chain costs. With latest-quarter gross margin already down year over year, cost inflation is a relevant risk.

Higher rates also matter because DELL has $31.5B of total debt and quarterly interest expense of $411M. The company is cash-generative enough to manage the debt load, but interest expense is large enough to affect earnings quality and valuation.

## Trading-Relevant Fundamental Insights

The bullish case is supported by accelerating revenue, strong latest-quarter earnings, strong cash generation, a shrinking share count, and major relative outperformance versus XLK over one, three, six-month, and year-to-date periods. If forward EPS of approximately $14.58 is credible, the forward P/E of 12.16x is not demanding for a large technology company with strong earnings momentum.

The caution case is that the stock has already moved substantially, with +48.80% three-month performance and +39.32% YTD performance. Gross margin declined meaningfully year over year in the latest quarter, working capital is volatile, book equity is negative, the current ratio is below 1.0, and capital returns are consuming most of free cash flow. The latest one-week underperformance versus XLK may indicate that the market is starting to test the sustainability of the move.

Actionable monitoring points for traders: watch whether gross margin can hold around or above 20%; watch whether receivables and inventory growth normalize; watch whether free cash flow remains strong without relying on large payable increases; watch net debt and interest expense; and watch whether forward EPS expectations remain stable after future guidance updates.

A fundamentally disciplined stance would treat DELL as a strong-momentum, cash-generative technology hardware name with improving earnings power, but not a low-risk balance-sheet story. The next major confirmation should come from sustained FCF, stable or improving gross margin, and evidence that growth is not being purchased through working-capital strain or margin sacrifice.
