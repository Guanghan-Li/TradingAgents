Macro view for DELL as of April 15, 2026: the US backdrop still looks like a soft landing, not a recession regime. Labor is holding up with unemployment at 4.3% and nonfarm payrolls up 178k in March 2026, while the Treasury curve has re-steepened to a positive 2Y-10Y spread of +50 bps. That combination argues for continued growth, but not a sharp downturn.

The inflation side is less clean. March 2026 CPI and PPI both moved up materially on a sequential basis, with PPI especially firm, which suggests pipeline price pressure is still present. The Fed has already eased from 4.09% in October 2025 to 3.64% by January 2026, then paused through March 2026. The likely policy message is: cuts already delivered, but additional near-term easing is less certain unless inflation cools again. In other words, policy is no longer highly restrictive, but it is not turning aggressively supportive either.

For markets, this is a mildly risk-on but higher-for-longer nominal-growth regime: supportive for cyclical earnings and credit, less supportive for duration-sensitive equity multiples because the 10Y Treasury at 4.26% and 30Y near 4.87% keep discount rates elevated. For DELL, that macro mix is broadly constructive for enterprise demand, infrastructure spending, and a stable IT budget environment, but it limits valuation expansion and raises sensitivity to any margin pressure from sticky input or financing costs. Macro therefore leans modestly supportive for DELL fundamentals, but not decisively bullish for DELL multiple rerating.

Key risks for DELL to incorporate: a renewed inflation surprise that delays Fed easing further, a rise in long-end yields that compresses tech valuations, weaker business capex if confidence rolls over, and any growth scare that steepens the curve for the wrong reason. Data gaps matter here: PMI, consumer confidence, and VIX were unavailable in the prefetched set, so the confidence/growth signal is less complete than usual.
