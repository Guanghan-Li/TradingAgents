### Segment view for SCCO

SCCO is a high-quality, copper-led miner with unusually strong consolidated profitability: TTM operating margin is 54.5%, profit margin is 32.3%, EBITDA is about $7.9B on $13.4B revenue, and free cash flow is about $3.34B. Quarterly results show broad operating leverage: revenue rose from $2.78B in 4Q24 to $3.87B in 4Q25, while operating income increased from $1.31B to $2.11B and normalized EBITDA rose from $1.55B to $2.36B. This implies segment economics are moving favorably, mainly from copper-price strength, scale, and cost absorption rather than visible diversification.

The business mix is concentrated in copper mining and related by-products, with Peru and Mexico as the core operating regions. Copper is the dominant value driver, while molybdenum, silver, zinc and other by-products support margins but do not meaningfully diversify the investment case. Recent news flow points to constructive demand and sentiment around copper, including growth-stock framing, analyst upgrades, and broader industry copper-strategy activity, but valuation risk is elevated after a large one-year share-price move and premium multiples.

| Exploration and development pipeline | Long-term positive, capital intensive | Near-term margin drag if capex accelerates | Supports duration of copper exposure, but valuation already prices in growth |
