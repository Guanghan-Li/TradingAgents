**Trend report**

Long-term trend remains bullish. `GLD` closed at `440.46` on April 15, 2026, versus a `close_200_sma` of `384.09`. That is roughly `14.7%` above the 200-day average, so the larger structure is still an uptrend, not a secular breakdown.

Medium-term trend is still impaired. Price is below the `close_50_sma` at `450.24`, about `2.2%` under that level. That means the March collapse broke the intermediate trend, and the recent rebound has not yet reclaimed the key trend-control area. Until `GLD` can close above the 50-day and hold it, the medium-term read remains recovery inside damage, not a confirmed new leg higher.

Short-term trend is constructive. Price is above the `close_10_ema` at `435.75`, and the 10-day EMA has been rising from `423.26` on March 27 to `435.75` on April 15. That shows short-horizon buyers are back in control, at least tactically.

Momentum has improved materially, but confirmation is incomplete. `macd` has recovered from `-14.48` on March 27 to `-2.37` on April 15. That is a major positive momentum inflection. However, MACD is still negative, which usually means upside is still a rebound unless and until momentum crosses into positive territory and price simultaneously clears resistance.

`rsi` at `51.54` is neutral and constructive. It has recovered from the high-30s at the end of March without reaching overbought conditions. That is healthy: it says the bounce has room to continue, but it does not yet signal a strong momentum thrust. Neutral RSI in this context is more consistent with consolidation-to-recovery than with immediate breakout acceleration.

Volatility is compressing. `atr` fell from `17.36` on March 27 to `12.03` on April 15, a decline of about `31%`. That suggests the violent liquidation phase is over and the market is stabilizing. Lower ATR is good for cleaner setups, but it also means the market is transitioning from shock rebound to a more selective directional phase.

Bollinger structure supports the same conclusion. On April 15, the upper band was `454.12` and the lower band was `398.52`, with price at `440.46`. `GLD` is trading in the upper half of the band structure, which is constructive, but not at the upper band itself, so there is no strong breakout signal yet. More importantly, the band width has narrowed sharply versus late March, confirming volatility compression. That usually precedes the next directional decision, but not necessarily an upside resolution.

Volume is the main technical caveat. The March selloff featured heavy participation, including `36.8M` shares on March 23 and `30.2M` on March 19. By contrast, the April rebound has mostly occurred on lighter volume, often in the `5.5M` to `9.7M` range. That weakens the conviction of the recovery. The rebound is real in price terms, but not yet strongly confirmed by participation.

**Macro-overlay interpretation**

This matters because `GLD` is not trading in a clean gold-bull macro regime. Sticky CPI and PPI support the inflation-hedge argument, and elevated long-end yields keep term-premium risk alive, both of which can support strategic gold demand. But the curve is positively sloped, labor remains stable, payroll growth is still positive, and the broader regime is classified as `RISK-ON` with medium confidence. That reduces the classic recessionary or panic-driven bid for gold.

So the technical recovery should be respected, but it should not be over-interpreted. In this regime, `GLD` can grind higher if inflation concern dominates and real yields stop rising, but a powerful breakout is less likely unless either real yields roll over or macro risk sentiment deteriorates.
