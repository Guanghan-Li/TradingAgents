# ASML Fundamental Research Report

## Company Profile and Investment Context

ASML is a technology company in the Semiconductor Equipment & Materials industry. Its fundamentals show a high-quality, high-margin semiconductor capital equipment franchise with strong profitability, substantial free cash flow, and exceptional returns on equity. The company is valued by the market as a scarce strategic supplier, reflected in a market capitalization of approximately $581.8B, a TTM P/E of 51.36x, a forward P/E of 32.44x, and a price-to-book ratio of 25.16x.

ASML’s financial profile is premium: TTM revenue is $32.67B, gross profit is $17.26B, EBITDA is $12.27B, and net income is $9.61B. Profit margin is 29.42%, operating margin is 35.30%, ROE is 50.46%, and ROA is 14.25%. These figures indicate a structurally profitable business with very strong pricing power and capital efficiency.

The key fundamental issue for traders is valuation sensitivity. ASML combines elite profitability and strong long-term semiconductor exposure, but the stock already discounts substantial future earnings growth. The forward EPS estimate of $45.68 versus TTM EPS of $28.85 implies the market expects a significant earnings step-up. Any disappointment in order growth, customer capex, export restrictions, gross margin, or 2026 guidance could pressure the multiple.

## Valuation and Market Position

ASML trades at a premium valuation on both earnings and book value. The TTM P/E of 51.36x is high in absolute terms, but the forward P/E of 32.44x suggests investors are pricing in strong earnings acceleration. The forward EPS estimate of $45.68 is materially above TTM EPS of $28.85, implying expected EPS growth of roughly 58% if realized.

The price-to-book ratio of 25.16x is also very elevated, but that is partly explained by the company’s asset-light economic model, high intellectual property value, and 50.46% ROE. Still, such a high multiple leaves limited room for operational disappointment.

The dividend yield is modest at 0.58%, meaning ASML is not primarily an income stock. Shareholder return is more dependent on earnings growth, free cash flow generation, and buybacks. Beta is 1.382, so ASML is meaningfully more volatile than the broad market and likely to be sensitive to semiconductor cycle expectations, AI infrastructure sentiment, interest rates, and geopolitical headlines.

Technically and relatively, ASML has performed strongly over the past 3 to 6 months. ASML returned +16.81% over 3 months versus XLK at +2.71%, producing +14.10% alpha. Over 6 months, ASML returned +50.95% versus XLK at +5.68%, producing +45.27% alpha. YTD, ASML is up +27.49% versus XLK at +4.27%. However, over the latest 1-week and 1-month periods, ASML slightly underperformed XLK: +4.27% versus +6.06% over 1 week and +7.72% versus +8.41% over 1 month. This suggests the longer-term relative trend remains very strong, but short-term momentum has become less dominant versus the sector ETF.

This was the strongest revenue quarter in the provided period and exceeded 2024-12-31 revenue of $9.263B. Diluted EPS was $7.34, up from $6.84 in 2024-12-31 and $5.48 in 2025-09-30.

The 2025-12-31 quarter showed a strong sequential recovery from 2025-09-30. Revenue rose from $7.516B to $9.718B, net income rose from $2.125B to $2.840B, and EBITDA rose from $2.743B to $3.909B. This points to strong operating leverage when revenue scales.

Margins remain a major strength. In 2025-12-31, gross margin was approximately 52.2%, operating margin was approximately 35.3%, and net margin was approximately 29.2%. These figures are consistent with the TTM profile: profit margin of 29.42% and operating margin of 35.30%.

R&D spending remains substantial. In 2025-12-31, research and development expense was $1.262B, up from $1.109B in 2025-09-30 and $1.116B in 2024-12-31. This is important because ASML’s competitive moat depends heavily on sustained technical leadership. The company is still investing aggressively while maintaining high profitability, which is a positive long-term signal.

Selling, general, and administrative expense was controlled relative to revenue. SG&A was $375.3M in 2025-12-31, compared with $303.2M in 2025-09-30 and $318.4M in 2024-12-31. R&D is the much larger operating expense category, reinforcing that ASML’s cost structure is innovation-heavy rather than distribution-heavy.

## Balance Sheet Analysis

ASML’s balance sheet is strong, but working capital and deferred revenue are important items to monitor.

As of 2025-12-31, total assets were $50.567B, total liabilities were $30.954B, and common stock equity was $19.612B. Cash and short-term investments were $13.322B, including $12.916B of cash and cash equivalents. Total debt was $4.391B. This implies ASML has a net cash position when comparing cash and short-term investments against total debt.

Liquidity is adequate but not excessive based on the current ratio of 1.262. Current assets were $30.616B and current liabilities were $24.264B, giving working capital of $6.352B. Working capital declined from $10.686B at 2024-12-31, but improved from $5.921B at 2025-09-30.

Inventory was $11.429B at 2025-12-31, compared with $10.892B at 2024-12-31 and $11.763B at 2025-09-30. Inventory remains large and should be watched closely. Elevated inventory can be normal for long-cycle semiconductor equipment manufacturing, but if revenue expectations soften, inventory may become a working-capital drag or signal customer timing risk.

Deferred revenue is very significant. Current deferred revenue was $16.006B at 2025-12-31, up from $12.571B at 2024-12-31. Non-current deferred revenue was $3.366B. The large deferred revenue balance supports future revenue visibility and suggests strong customer prepayments or order-related liabilities. This is a positive signal for backlog quality, but traders should verify whether future recognized revenue is already embedded in consensus expectations.

Share count declined. Ordinary shares were 385.418M at 2025-12-31 versus 393.284M at 2024-12-31. Treasury stock rose to $2.253B from $476M. This reflects material share repurchase activity and supports EPS growth.

Debt appears manageable. Total debt was $4.391B at 2025-12-31, and debt-to-equity was 23.916%. With $13.322B in cash and short-term investments and TTM free cash flow of $10.855B, ASML’s balance sheet provides flexibility for R&D, capex, dividends, buybacks, and cyclical volatility.

## Cash Flow Analysis

Cash flow is a major fundamental strength, although quarterly cash generation is uneven due to working capital swings.

TTM free cash flow was $10.855B. For 2025-12-31, ASML generated operating cash flow of $11.410B and free cash flow of $10.940B. This was exceptionally strong and was driven partly by a $7.641B positive working capital change. In contrast, 2025-03-31 free cash flow was negative $475.1M, and operating cash flow was negative $58.6M, primarily due to a $2.801B working capital outflow.

Quarterly free cash flow:

- 2025-12-31: $10.940B
- 2025-09-30: $243.7M
- 2025-06-30: $357.7M
- 2025-03-31: -$475.1M
- 2024-12-31: $8.839B

The important interpretation is that ASML is highly cash generative over a full cycle, but quarter-to-quarter free cash flow can be heavily influenced by customer prepayments, deferred revenue movement, receivables, inventories, and delivery timing.

Capital expenditure was $470.4M in 2025-12-31, $315.4M in 2025-09-30, $988.3M in 2025-06-30, and $416.5M in 2025-03-31. The business does require ongoing investment, but capex remains modest relative to EBITDA and operating cash flow.

ASML returned capital through both dividends and buybacks. Cash dividends paid were $619.1M in 2025-12-31, $619.6M in 2025-09-30, $714.4M in 2025-06-30, and $597.2M in 2025-03-31. Share repurchases were also significant: $1.700B in 2025-12-31, $172.2M in 2025-09-30, $1.485B in 2025-06-30, and $2.593B in 2025-03-31. This capital return program supports per-share value, but at a high valuation, repurchase efficiency depends on sustained earnings growth.

## Profitability and Quality

ASML’s profitability metrics are exceptional. ROE of 50.46% is very high and indicates strong capital efficiency. ROA of 14.25% is also strong for a complex manufacturing and technology business. Operating margin of 35.30% and profit margin of 29.42% indicate durable pricing power and operating discipline.

Gross profit of $17.258B on TTM revenue of $32.667B implies a TTM gross margin of roughly 52.8%. This is consistent with the quarterly gross margin range in the provided income statement. ASML’s gross margin stability supports the thesis that its products have differentiated value and limited direct substitution.

The company’s high R&D investment is not suppressing margins materially, which is a key quality marker. It suggests ASML can fund future technology leadership internally while still producing strong earnings and free cash flow.

## Growth and Earnings Expectations

The largest positive fundamental signal is the implied earnings growth embedded in forward EPS. TTM EPS is $28.85, while forward EPS is $45.68. That gap implies strong expected growth, likely reflecting semiconductor equipment demand, advanced node investment, AI-related compute demand, and customer capacity expansion.

However, this also creates a risk. With the stock priced at 51.36x TTM earnings and 32.44x forward earnings, traders should treat ASML as a high-expectation stock. The company does not merely need to remain excellent; it needs to deliver against elevated expectations.

Quarterly results show that revenue can fluctuate meaningfully. Revenue declined from $9.263B in 2024-12-31 to around $7.7B in the first three quarters of 2025, then rebounded to $9.718B in 2025-12-31. This cyclicality or delivery timing variability should be incorporated into risk management.

## Macro Backdrop

The macro environment is broadly supportive but not risk-free. The latest effective Fed Funds rate is 3.64% as of 2026-03-01, unchanged from February and down from 4.09% in October 2025. Lower policy rates generally support long-duration growth equities and high-multiple technology names like ASML.

Inflation remains relevant. CPI rose 0.87% sequentially to 330.29 in March 2026, and PPI rose 1.78% sequentially to 274.10. Rising producer prices could pressure supply chains or manufacturing costs, although ASML’s high gross margin suggests strong ability to absorb or pass through cost pressures.

The labor market appears stable, with unemployment at 4.30% and nonfarm payrolls increasing by 178,000 in March 2026. A stable labor backdrop supports demand, but for ASML, customer semiconductor capex and geopolitical export policy are more important than consumer labor data.

The Treasury curve shows short rates around 3.71%-3.76% and longer yields higher, with the 20-year at 4.84%. If long-duration yields rise further, valuation multiples for high-growth technology companies may face pressure. ASML’s premium valuation makes it rate-sensitive even though its fundamentals are strong.

## Key Risks

The first major risk is valuation. A 51.36x TTM P/E and 25.16x price-to-book ratio require sustained execution and strong future earnings growth. If forward EPS expectations fall, the stock could derate even if the company remains fundamentally strong.

The second risk is semiconductor capex cyclicality. ASML’s quarterly revenue and cash flow are uneven, and customer order timing can materially affect reported results.

The third risk is working capital volatility. Free cash flow was extremely strong in 2025-12-31 due to a large working capital inflow, but earlier quarters had much weaker free cash flow. Traders should avoid annualizing one strong cash flow quarter without checking backlog, deferred revenue, receivables, and inventory movement.

The fourth risk is geopolitical and export-control exposure. ASML operates in a strategic semiconductor equipment category, and restrictions on shipments to certain geographies could affect demand, order timing, or investor sentiment.

The fifth risk is momentum exhaustion. ASML has generated very strong 3-month, 6-month, and YTD relative returns. After a +50.95% 6-month move, the stock may be vulnerable to consolidation or profit-taking, especially if near-term semiconductor leadership rotates to peers.

## Actionable Trader Insights

ASML’s fundamentals support a high-quality long-term profile: strong margins, high ROE, strong FCF, net cash flexibility, and visible future revenue through deferred revenue. Fundamental investors can justify a premium multiple, but only if forward earnings growth materializes.

For momentum-oriented traders, ASML’s 3-month and 6-month alpha versus XLK remains compelling, but 1-week and 1-month underperformance versus XLK suggests near-term relative strength should be monitored closely. If ASML resumes outperforming XLK and semiconductor peers while maintaining earnings momentum, that would confirm continued leadership. If underperformance persists, the stock may be entering a consolidation phase after a large run.

For valuation-sensitive traders, the key trigger is whether forward EPS of $45.68 remains credible. The forward P/E of 32.44x is much more reasonable than the TTM P/E of 51.36x, but only if the earnings acceleration occurs. Any negative revision to forward EPS would materially weaken the valuation case.

For risk managers, important levels and metrics to track include the 50-day average of $1,395.41, the 200-day average of $1,074.57, the 52-week high of $1,547.22, and the 52-week low of $614.06. Given the strong 6-month advance, position sizing should account for elevated beta of 1.382 and high valuation sensitivity.
