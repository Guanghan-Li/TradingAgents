Macro view for `MU`: the backdrop is a soft-landing / mid-cycle regime, not a recession regime. Growth and labor are still holding up, inflation looks sticky rather than fully contained, and the Treasury curve has re-normalized with a positive 2Y-10Y spread of +0.50%. For `MU`, that is generally supportive for end-demand and capex-sensitive semiconductor spending, but less supportive for valuation expansion because long-end yields are elevated.

Policy path: the effective Fed Funds rate has fallen from 4.09% in October 2025 to 3.64% by March 2026, then paused. That reads as a completed easing phase moving into a wait-and-see stance. With unemployment at 4.3% and payrolls still adding 178k, the Fed has room to stay on hold. But CPI and especially PPI moved higher in the latest print, so the near-term bias is likely fewer cuts than the market might want, not an aggressive restart of easing.

Cross-asset implications for `MU`: the macro mix is modestly constructive for cyclical tech earnings because recession risk is lower and volatility is contained (`VIX` 18.36). The main macro offset is rates: a 10Y yield at 4.26% and 30Y at 4.87% raise discount-rate pressure, which can cap upside for growth equities even when fundamentals improve. Net-net, macro is better for `MU`'s revenue and demand setup than for its valuation multiple.

Key risks other analysts should incorporate for `MU`: sticky goods inflation and higher PPI can lift input and operating-cost pressure; a higher-for-longer Fed could keep real yields elevated and compress semiconductor multiples; and if the current bear-steepening continues, long-duration tech sentiment can weaken even without a recession. The positive curve means recession hedging should carry less weight than inflation/rates sensitivity in the near term.
