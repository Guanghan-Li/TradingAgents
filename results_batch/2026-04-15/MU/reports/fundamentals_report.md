## Fundamental Report for `MU`

`MU` remains in a very strong fundamental phase based on the prefetched live snapshot dated 2026-04-15. The company is a large-cap semiconductor name with a market cap of about $514.5B, trailing revenue of $58.1B, net income of $24.1B, EBITDA of $36.8B, and free cash flow of $2.89B on a TTM basis. Profitability metrics are unusually strong for a memory company: profit margin is 41.5%, operating margin is 67.6%, ROE is 39.8%, and ROA is 20.1%. This points to `MU` being deep in a highly favorable pricing and mix cycle rather than merely executing steady-state improvement.

The key fundamental story is acceleration. Quarterly revenue rose from $8.05B in 2025-02-28 to $9.30B, $11.32B, $13.64B, and then $23.86B in 2026-02-28. That is nearly 3x year over year in the latest quarter. Gross profit expanded from $2.96B to $17.76B over the same span, while operating income rose from $1.77B to $16.14B and net income from $1.58B to $13.79B. Diluted EPS moved from $1.41 to $12.07. This is not just revenue growth; it is powerful margin expansion layered on top of revenue growth.

Margin structure improved sharply across the cycle. Approximate gross margin moved from about 36.8% in 2025-02-28 to 37.7%, 44.7%, 56.0%, and then 74.4% in 2026-02-28. Operating margin similarly moved from about 22.0% to 23.3%, 32.7%, 45.0%, and then 67.6%. Net margin reached about 57.8% in the latest quarter. For traders, this matters because `MU` is no longer just benefiting from higher bit shipments; it is benefiting from very favorable pricing, mix, and operating leverage.

Cash generation also improved materially. Operating cash flow increased from $3.94B in 2025-02-28 to $11.90B in 2026-02-28. Free cash flow improved from negative $113M to positive $5.52B even though capital expenditures rose heavily to $6.39B in the latest quarter. That is an important quality signal: `MU` is funding aggressive expansion while still generating positive free cash flow. Capital intensity remains high, but the business is currently out-earning that burden.

Balance-sheet quality is strong. Cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments rose to $14.59B from $8.22B a year earlier. Total debt fell to $10.80B from $14.95B. Stockholders' equity increased to $72.46B from $48.63B. Working capital improved to $27.12B from $16.81B, and the current ratio is 2.897. The reported debt-to-equity figure of 14.902 appears consistent with roughly 14.9% rather than 14.9x when compared against the balance sheet, which is conservative for a semiconductor manufacturer in an expansion phase.

Asset growth shows where management is deploying capital. Net PPE rose from $43.17B to $52.09B over the last year, and construction in progress climbed from $4.43B to $7.49B. This strongly suggests ongoing capacity buildout. Traders should read that as a bullish medium-term supply and AI-memory positioning signal, but also as a reminder that `MU` remains exposed to execution risk if pricing weakens before new capacity is absorbed.

Working-capital details are important. Receivables surged from $6.50B to $17.31B year over year, and the latest quarter shows a $7.43B cash drag from receivables. Inventory moved down from $9.01B to $8.27B, which is constructive. The combination suggests demand and shipments are strong, but collections timing and customer concentration should be watched closely. If future quarters convert receivables into cash cleanly, that would further validate the current earnings quality. If receivables remain elevated while growth slows, the market could question sustainability.

Capital allocation is disciplined and growth-oriented. `MU` paid about $132M in dividends in the latest quarter and repurchased $350M of stock, while also repaying $1.68B of debt. Dividend yield is only 0.13%, so this is not an income story. It is a reinvestment-and-deleveraging story, with shareholder return secondary to capacity expansion and balance-sheet strengthening.

Valuation looks inexpensive on earnings, but traders should treat that carefully. TTM P/E is 21.53, while forward P/E is 4.65. Price-to-book is 7.10. The gap between trailing and forward P/E implies the market expects another major earnings step-up. That makes `MU` look cheap if the cycle persists, but it also means expectations are no longer low. The stock has already rerated substantially, so valuation support depends on continued execution.

From a market-confirmation standpoint, price action supports the fundamental narrative. `MU` is up 12.17% over 1 week, 34.98% over 3 months, 136.87% over 6 months, and 44.70% YTD. It ranks 1 of 9 peers over 3 months. Against XLK, alpha is +6.09% over 1 week, +32.25% over 3 months, and +131.16% over 6 months. The only mild caution is 1-month relative underperformance versus XLK, which may indicate consolidation after a large move rather than a broken thesis.

The macro backdrop is broadly supportive. Fed funds are 3.64% and stable, the yield curve is upward sloping, unemployment is 4.3%, payrolls are still growing, and VIX is subdued at 18.36. CPI and PPI both increased sequentially, which is not ideal for cost pressure, but for `MU` the more important issue is end-demand and pricing power. Right now the company appears strong enough to absorb a still-firm rate environment.

### Actionable trader takeaways for `MU`

1. The bull case is straightforward: `MU` is showing explosive revenue growth, extreme margin expansion, positive free cash flow despite heavy capex, rising cash, falling debt, and clear relative-strength leadership. That combination usually supports further upside if the cycle remains intact.
2. The main risk is not balance-sheet stress; it is cyclicality and expectations. The latest quarter may represent a very strong point in the memory cycle, so any sign of ASP normalization, weaker bookings, or margin compression could hit the stock hard after such a large rerating.
3. Receivables are the cleanest near-term line item to monitor. Revenue is surging, but the cash conversion drag from receivables was large. If the next report shows collections catching up while inventory remains controlled, that would be a strong confirmation signal.
4. Capex remains elevated, but that is currently a strength rather than a weakness because `MU` is self-funding expansion. If free cash flow stays positive through heavy investment, the market can justify a premium multiple on book value.
5. The very low forward P/E suggests the market still sees upside in earnings power. If consensus is right, `MU` is not expensive. If consensus proves too high, the stock has room to de-rate even with good absolute results.

### Bottom line on `MU`

Fundamentally, `MU` looks like a high-momentum cyclical leader with unusually strong earnings quality for this stage of expansion. The balance sheet is healthy, cash generation is strong, leverage is falling, and capacity investment is scaling. The report supports a **BUY** stance for `MU`, with the caveat that traders should actively monitor receivables, capex efficiency, and any early signs that memory pricing or margins are rolling over.
