## MU Segment View

MU remains a memory-cycle story with business-mix quality improving as AI-oriented DRAM, especially HBM/server memory, appears to be driving the sharp revenue and margin inflection. Prefetched financials show quarterly revenue rising from $8.1B in 2025-02-28 to $23.9B in 2026-02-28, while gross profit expanded from $3.0B to $17.8B and operating income from $1.8B to $16.1B. That scale of operating leverage strongly implies the highest-value DRAM franchises are outgrowing the rest of the portfolio and lifting mix.

By segment, MU’s highest-quality exposure is likely Compute and Networking, where AI server DRAM/HBM demand and pricing are the clearest positive catalysts in the supplied news flow. Mobile is more mature and more pricing-sensitive, but should benefit secondarily from tighter industry supply and richer content. Embedded is typically the most durable franchise because of longer product cycles and stickier customer programs, though it usually lags at the start of memory upcycles. Storage has upside from enterprise SSD demand, but it is also exposed to NAND volatility and usually carries less structural pricing power than leading-edge DRAM/HBM.

The main concentration risk is that MU’s current earnings power appears heavily tied to the memory pricing upcycle and to AI-linked DRAM mix, rather than broad-based strength across all end markets. Recent headlines repeatedly emphasize memory pricing, AI demand, and debate over whether margin expansion can continue, which supports a view that MU is benefitting from a favorable but cyclical setup. If HBM/server DRAM tightness eases, or if hyperscaler demand normalizes, segment mix could deteriorate quickly even if headline revenue stays elevated.

| Segment | Growth outlook | Margin trend | Trading implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| Compute & Networking | Strongest; AI server DRAM/HBM likely leading growth | Expanding fastest on richer mix and pricing | Core bull driver for MU |
| Mobile | Moderate; benefits from industry pricing recovery more than unique demand surge | Improving, but below AI/server mix quality | Supportive, not primary thesis |
| Embedded | Steadier, lower-beta growth | Relatively resilient, less explosive | Adds durability to MU story |
| Storage | Recovering, but more NAND-cycle exposed | Improving with pricing, still more volatile | Upside with higher risk to revisions |
