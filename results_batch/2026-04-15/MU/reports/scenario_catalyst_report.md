## MU Scenario Framing

MU remains a high-beta memory-cycle equity with strong operating leverage to DRAM/NAND pricing and AI-linked HBM demand. Prefetched fundamentals point to a market that is underwriting substantial forward earnings acceleration: trailing P/E is 21.5x, but forward P/E is 4.6x, while the stock is trading well above both its 50-day and 200-day averages after a sharp rally. That setup supports a constructive medium-term view, but near-term event risk is elevated because expectations are now high and recent news flow already shows profit-taking after a strong run.

### Scenario Summary
- **Bull case (35%)**: Memory pricing upcycle persists, AI/HBM demand remains supply-constrained, and margin expansion continues. In this case, current forward valuation still looks inexpensive versus earnings power, supporting further upside despite the rally.
- **Base case (45%)**: Fundamentals stay strong, but the stock consolidates after its sharp move as investors wait for confirmation that pricing and margins can keep improving. Earnings revisions remain positive, but multiple expansion slows.
- **Bear case (20%)**: The stock has moved too far ahead of the cycle; pricing momentum moderates, AI enthusiasm cools, or management commentary signals a less favorable supply/demand balance. Given MU's cyclicality and high beta, downside could be sharp if expectations reset.

### Key Signposts
- DRAM/NAND pricing trend and commentary on sustainability of the memory upcycle.
- HBM/AI revenue mix, customer qualification progress, and any signs of supply bottlenecks easing.
- Gross margin and operating margin follow-through from already elevated profitability.
- Whether pullbacks remain orderly consolidation or turn into estimate-cut-driven derating.

### Thesis Invalidation Logic
- Bull/base framing weakens if pricing commentary rolls over, gross margin expansion stalls, or management signals weaker second-half supply discipline.
- Bear framing weakens if MU keeps posting strong margin expansion and upward estimate revisions despite the recent rally and profit-taking.
