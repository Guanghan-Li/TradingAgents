VALE fundamental report as of 2026-04-15

VALE is a large-cap diversified mining company in the Basic Materials sector, focused on industrial metals and mining. The market is currently valuing VALE at about $76.9B. On headline valuation, VALE screens as a mixed but potentially attractive cyclical name: trailing P/E is 32.0x, but forward P/E drops sharply to 8.0x, implying the market expects a material earnings rebound from depressed recent results. Price-to-book is 2.12x, dividend yield is high at 7.12%, beta is moderate at 0.889, and the stock has been trading with strong momentum, near the upper end of its 52-week range ($17.83 high vs $8.93 low).

From a business quality standpoint, VALE still shows meaningful scale and operating power. TTM revenue is 213.6B, gross profit is 74.7B, EBITDA is 76.9B, and free cash flow is 15.45B. Operating margin is 27.64%, which is strong for a miner, but net margin is only 6.47%, showing that below-the-line items, impairments, and other non-operating effects are materially reducing earnings conversion. Return on assets is 7.79%, while return on equity is only 5.87%, which is modest relative to the capital intensity and cyclicality of the business.

The key issue in the recent financial history is earnings volatility driven by unusual items rather than a collapse in revenue. In 2025-12-31 quarter, VALE posted total revenue of 11.06B, up from 10.42B in 2025-09-30 and 8.80B in 2025-06-30. Gross profit also improved to 4.28B in 2025-12-31 from 3.79B in 2025-09-30 and 2.72B in 2025-06-30. Operating income remained positive at 3.50B in 2025-12-31, actually above 3.37B in 2025-09-30. That matters because the core business appears to have held up operationally.

However, reported earnings were severely hit in 2025-12-31. Net income attributable to common shareholders was negative 3.84B, versus positive 2.69B in 2025-09-30, positive 2.12B in 2025-06-30, and positive 1.39B in 2025-03-31. The main reason was large unusual charges: total unusual items were negative 4.26B in 2025-12-31, including 3.84B of capital asset impairment. EBITDA collapsed to negative 97M on a reported basis, but normalized EBITDA was still positive 4.17B. Normalized income was negative 1.03B, which is much better than reported net loss and suggests the quarter was distorted by one-off items rather than a full operating breakdown.

This distinction is critical for traders. If the market treats the 2025-12-31 quarter as a new earnings base, VALE may look expensive on trailing numbers. If instead the market focuses on normalized earnings power and forward EPS of 2.1961, then the stock looks inexpensive. The wide gap between trailing P/E and forward P/E is one of the clearest signals in this dataset.

Quarterly revenue trend is constructive. Total revenue moved from 8.12B in 2025-03-31 to 8.80B in 2025-06-30, 10.42B in 2025-09-30, and 11.06B in 2025-12-31. Gross profit followed a similar improvement path. This suggests better pricing, improved shipment mix, stronger end-market demand, or some combination of these. For a cyclical miner, sequential revenue and gross profit expansion into year-end is a positive sign, especially when paired with strong stock performance.

Cash generation remains intact, although quarterly free cash flow is not especially large relative to the company’s size. Operating cash flow was 2.70B in 2025-12-31, 2.58B in 2025-09-30, 1.86B in 2025-06-30, and 1.67B in 2025-03-31. Free cash flow was 510M in 2025-12-31, 1.18B in 2025-09-30, 688M in 2025-06-30, and 414M in 2025-03-31. The step-down from operating cash flow to free cash flow is driven by heavy capital expenditure, which is typical for mining. Capital expenditure was 2.19B in 2025-12-31 and 1.39B in 2025-09-30. This shows VALE is still a capital-intensive business where gross cash generation can look stronger than true residual shareholder cash.

On the balance sheet, VALE is reasonably liquid but not conservatively positioned. Current ratio is 1.153, so near-term obligations appear covered, but not with a wide buffer. Working capital improved through 2025, reaching 2.42B at 2025-12-31 from 391M at 2024-12-31. Cash and short-term investments rose to 7.57B at 2025-12-31 from 5.01B at 2024-12-31. That improvement is helpful.

Debt increased during 2025. Total debt rose to 21.80B at 2025-12-31 from 17.72B at 2024-12-31. Net debt also increased to 13.76B from 12.06B. Debt-to-equity is listed at 61.326, which is elevated and worth monitoring closely in a cyclical commodity business. Long-term debt rose to 20.61B by 2025-12-31. In isolation, the debt load is not catastrophic relative to EBITDA and asset base, but it reduces flexibility if commodity prices weaken or further impairments occur.

Equity also became more volatile. Common stock equity was 33.51B at 2025-12-31, up from 33.41B at 2024-12-31, but it had been as high as 41.04B in 2025-09-30. That drop reflects earnings volatility and substantial accumulated translation and other equity adjustments. Foreign currency translation adjustments remain deeply negative at roughly negative 42.13B. For a global miner with non-USD exposures, this is a real balance-sheet sensitivity and can create noise or pressure in reported equity.

Asset quality deserves attention. Net PPE increased to 43.63B at 2025-12-31 from 39.98B at 2024-12-31, and construction in progress remained high at 10.46B, showing sustained investment in productive assets and future capacity. But goodwill and other intangibles were still 8.95B, while the quarter included major impairments. Repeated asset impairments are usually a warning sign that prior capital allocation assumptions or commodity outlooks have been too optimistic. Traders should watch whether 2025-12-31 was a clearing event or the start of further write-downs.

Capital allocation looks shareholder-friendly on yield, but not aggressively on buybacks. Cash dividends paid were 97M in 2025-12-31, 1.49B in 2025-09-30, and 1.98B in 2025-03-31. Repurchases were zero in the reported periods. That means VALE is returning capital primarily via dividends, not buybacks. The 7.12% dividend yield is attractive, but because this is a miner, yield durability depends on commodity prices, cash flow, and management’s willingness to sustain payouts through volatility. With FCF positive but not huge at the quarterly level, the dividend is appealing but should not be treated as bond-like.

Operationally, the company still looks stronger than GAAP earnings suggest. Quarterly operating income stayed positive and generally improved through 2025. The problem is that other income/expense and special charges overwhelmed the operating recovery in the latest quarter. Interest burden also rose sharply in 2025-12-31: interest expense was 1.15B versus 303M in 2025-09-30 and 347M in 2025-06-30. Net interest income was negative 970M in 2025-12-31, a significant deterioration. Even if some of that is timing or classification noise, it is a short-term red flag.

Market behavior is strongly supportive. Over the last week, VALE returned +5.26%, materially ahead of XLB at -0.71%. Over one month, VALE gained +17.08% versus XLB +4.47%. Over three months, VALE gained +24.88% versus XLB +5.91%. Over six months, VALE gained +68.51% versus XLB +17.59%. YTD, VALE is up +32.88% versus XLB +11.90%. VALE ranks 1 out of 7 peers over the last three months in the provided peer set. That indicates the market is already leaning into a recovery thesis.

Relative performance versus peers also supports this interpretation. VALE’s one-week return was close to FCX and better than most other materials peers, while its three-month and six-month performance substantially exceeded much of the group. This suggests traders are rewarding VALE for improving fundamentals, valuation normalization potential, or iron ore and metals exposure. It also raises the risk that a lot of near-term optimism is already reflected in price.

Macro backdrop is moderately constructive for a cyclical materials name. As of 2026-04-15, effective Fed funds are 3.64%, with a normal upward-sloping yield curve and a 2Y-10Y spread of +0.50%. Unemployment is 4.3%, payrolls are still growing, and VIX at 18.36 implies risk appetite is not stressed. Inflation remains positive, with CPI up sequentially and PPI up 1.78% sequentially, which can support commodity-sensitive revenue if industrial demand remains healthy. This macro backdrop is not restrictive enough on its face to undermine the bull case for a major miner like VALE.

The core bull case for VALE is straightforward. First, reported earnings likely understate normalized earnings power because the latest quarter was heavily distorted by impairment charges. Second, revenue and gross profit trends improved sequentially through 2025. Third, the forward valuation is low relative to the current stock price, suggesting upside if earnings normalize. Fourth, free cash flow remains positive and dividend yield remains high. Fifth, the stock’s relative strength indicates institutional capital is already responding to better conditions.

The core bear case is also clear. First, the latest quarter still produced a large actual net loss, and impairments can sometimes signal structural rather than temporary issues. Second, leverage increased during 2025, reducing resilience. Third, interest expense spiked in the latest quarter. Fourth, current ratio is only modestly above 1.0, so liquidity is adequate rather than strong. Fifth, after a 68.5% six-month rally, valuation upside may depend on continued flawless execution and supportive commodity prices.

Actionable trader read-through for VALE:
- Bullish traders should focus on normalized EBITDA, forward EPS, sequential revenue growth, and sector-leading relative strength. The main thesis is that reported Q4 2025 earnings were unusually depressed and that the market is discounting recovery correctly.
- Neutral traders should recognize that the quality of the latest quarter is mixed: operating trend improved, but the income statement was damaged by major charges and higher interest burden. This supports a “good business trend, messy accounting quarter” interpretation.
- Risk-aware traders should monitor future impairment disclosures, net debt trajectory, interest expense normalization, and dividend sustainability. These are the biggest variables that could break the recovery narrative.
- Momentum traders should note that VALE has already massively outperformed XLB and most peers. That confirms strength, but also means entries after this run are less forgiving if the next earnings update disappoints.

Bottom line: VALE fundamentally looks like a cyclical recovery story with strong operating momentum, depressed trailing earnings, low forward valuation, and meaningful shareholder yield. The main debate is whether the 2025-12-31 impairments were a one-time reset or evidence of deeper asset-quality problems. Right now, the balance of evidence favors improving core operations with non-trivial balance-sheet and earnings-quality risk.
