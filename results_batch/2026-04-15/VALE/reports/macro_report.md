Macro regime for `VALE`: soft-landing / mid-cycle expansion, not recession. The US macro backdrop shows steady labor conditions, still-positive payroll growth, an upward-sloping 2Y-10Y curve at +50 bps, and a Fed that has already eased from late 2025 levels but is now on hold near 3.64%. That combination is generally supportive for cyclical commodity demand, which is a macro positive for `VALE`.

The complication is inflation. CPI and PPI both accelerated sequentially into March 2026, especially PPI, while unemployment dipped to 4.3%. That argues the Fed is unlikely to rush into more cuts. With the long end elevated and the 10Y at 4.26%, the market is pricing firmer nominal growth and/or term premium, not imminent easing. For `VALE`, that is a mixed setup: better global growth expectations help iron ore and base-material demand, but higher real rates and a firmer USD regime can pressure EM equities, commodities, and valuation multiples.

Policy path: base case is Fed hold, with cuts delayed unless inflation cools materially or labor softens. A renewed hiking cycle is not the base case, but sticky inflation is the main upside-rate risk. Cross-asset implication for `VALE`: macro is better for earnings sensitivity than for multiple expansion. In other words, the current regime favors operationally leveraged cyclicals if global industrial demand holds up, but it does not obviously favor a broad re-rating.

Trading use for `VALE`: macro read is `neutral to modestly constructive`. The curve says recession risk is low near term, which helps bulk materials demand. The main risks other analysts should incorporate are: persistent inflation keeping rates high, USD strength tightening global financial conditions, and the absence of confirming soft-data signals here because PMI and consumer confidence were unavailable.
