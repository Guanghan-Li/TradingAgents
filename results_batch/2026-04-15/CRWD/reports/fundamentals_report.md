CRWD is CrowdStrike Holdings, Inc., a Technology sector company in Software - Infrastructure. The fundamental profile remains that of a high-growth cybersecurity software platform with very strong liquidity and free-cash-flow generation, but with a premium valuation and still-weak GAAP trailing profitability. The latest quarterly data show a meaningful improvement in GAAP operating performance, with CRWD moving from several quarters of operating losses to positive operating income in the quarter ended 2026-01-31.

Company profile and valuation

CRWD has a market capitalization of about $104.3B. The valuation is demanding: forward P/E is 66.6x, price/book is 23.5x, and TTM EPS is still negative at -$0.64. Forward EPS is listed at $6.17, implying the market is already discounting substantial earnings normalization. With TTM revenue of $4.812B, CRWD trades at roughly 21.7x TTM sales using the provided market cap. Using cash and debt from the latest balance sheet, estimated enterprise value is about $99.9B, or roughly 20.8x TTM revenue.

This valuation leaves little room for disappointment. The stock can be fundamentally attractive only if CRWD sustains strong revenue growth, converts recent operating leverage into durable GAAP profitability, and keeps free cash flow margins high. The basic financials list free cash flow at $1.605B, implying an approximate FCF yield of only 1.5% on the current market cap, so valuation support depends more on future growth than current cash yield.

Revenue and margin trend

CRWD reported latest quarterly revenue of $1.305B for 2026-01-31. Revenue increased from $1.059B in the comparable 2025-01-31 quarter, a gain of about 23.3% year over year. Sequential growth also remained solid: revenue rose from $1.234B in 2025-10-31 to $1.305B in 2026-01-31, up about 5.8% quarter over quarter.

Gross profit was $993.7M in the latest quarter, producing a gross margin of about 76.1%. This is an improvement from roughly 74.1% in the 2025-01-31 quarter. The gross-margin profile remains high quality for a software infrastructure company and supports the possibility of operating leverage if sales, marketing, R&D, and G&A scale more slowly than revenue.

Operating income improved sharply to $13.8M in the latest quarter, compared with operating losses of $69.4M, $113.0M, $124.7M, and $85.3M in the four prior reported quarters. Latest quarterly operating margin was about 1.1%. Net income also turned positive at $59.4M, or about 4.5% of revenue, helped by positive net interest income of $40.3M. EBITDA improved to $142.9M from $55.5M in the prior quarter and from $17.6M in the year-ago quarter.

The key fundamental question is whether this latest positive operating income is the beginning of a sustained margin inflection or a one-quarter improvement. If CRWD can remain GAAP operating profitable while sustaining 20%+ revenue growth, the high valuation becomes more defensible. If operating losses resume, the stock remains exposed to multiple compression.

Expense structure and operating leverage

Latest quarterly operating expense was $979.9M, or about 75.1% of revenue. This is high, but improved materially from the year-ago quarter, when operating expense was $869.8M on $1.059B of revenue, or about 82.2% of revenue. That decline of roughly 7 percentage points is the clearest evidence of operating leverage in the income statement.

R&D was $356.4M in the latest quarter, or about 27.3% of revenue. SG&A was $623.5M, or about 47.8% of revenue. Within SG&A, selling and marketing expense was $463.6M, or about 35.5% of revenue, while G&A was $159.9M, or about 12.3% of revenue. CRWD is still spending heavily to acquire and retain customers, so traders should monitor whether sales efficiency improves without slowing billings and revenue.

Stock-based compensation remains a major consideration. Latest quarterly SBC was $274.0M, equal to about 21.0% of revenue. Diluted average shares increased from 246.9M in the 2025-01-31 quarter to 258.1M in the 2026-01-31 quarter, about 4.5% dilution year over year. SBC supports non-GAAP and cash-flow optics but creates real shareholder dilution. Continued dilution at this level weakens per-share value creation unless growth and margin expansion remain strong.

Balance sheet quality

Current assets were $7.419B versus current liabilities of $4.184B, producing working capital of $3.235B and a current ratio of 1.773. Debt to equity is listed at 18.336%, which is manageable given the cash balance.

Total assets were $11.087B, total liabilities were $6.614B, and stockholders' equity was $4.428B. Tangible book value was $2.928B. The balance sheet gives CRWD flexibility for acquisitions, product investment, and operating volatility. It also reduces solvency risk, which is important for a premium-growth software name in a higher-rate environment.

Deferred revenue is a key indicator for CRWD because it reflects contracted demand and future revenue recognition. Current deferred revenue was $3.421B and noncurrent deferred revenue was $1.332B, for total deferred revenue of about $4.753B. Total deferred revenue increased from about $3.729B at 2025-01-31 to $4.753B at 2026-01-31, up roughly 27.5% year over year. Current deferred revenue also rose sharply from $2.851B in 2025-10-31 to $3.421B in 2026-01-31, up about 20.0% sequentially. This supports the view that demand and billings remain healthy.

Goodwill and intangible assets increased to $1.500B, with goodwill at $1.363B. Cash flow data show business purchase outflows of $380.9M in the 2025-10-31 quarter and $213.9M in the 2025-01-31 quarter. This indicates acquisition activity is part of the recent financial story. Traders should watch integration execution, acquired revenue contribution, and whether acquisitions mask or enhance organic growth.

Cash flow and quality of earnings

CRWD's cash generation is a major strength. Latest quarterly operating cash flow was $497.9M, and latest quarterly free cash flow was $378.1M. Free cash flow improved from $240.8M in the 2025-01-31 quarter, up about 57.0% year over year. Latest quarterly FCF margin was about 29.0% of revenue.

Across the latest four reported quarters, operating cash flow sums to about $1.612B and free cash flow sums to about $1.241B. The prefetched basic financials list free cash flow at $1.605B, which may reflect a different TTM or provider definition. Either way, CRWD is clearly cash-flow positive despite negative TTM GAAP EPS.

The quality caveat is that cash flow is significantly supported by non-cash addbacks, especially SBC. In the latest quarter, SBC was $274.0M, depreciation and amortization was $77.5M, and other non-cash items were $130.5M. Receivables also consumed cash, with change in receivables at -$348.7M in the latest quarter. This can be consistent with strong billings, but if receivables keep rising faster than revenue, it could pressure cash conversion.

The macro backdrop is mixed for CRWD. The latest effective Fed Funds rate is 3.64%, unchanged sequentially, and the 10-year Treasury yield is 4.30%. The yield curve is upward sloping, with the 2Y-10Y spread at 0.52 percentage points, consistent with normal growth expectations. VIX is 18.36, suggesting moderate market volatility.

For CRWD, the key macro sensitivity is valuation duration. A 66.6x forward P/E and roughly 20x+ sales multiple are more vulnerable when long rates are elevated. CPI and PPI increased sequentially in the macro dataset, which could limit rate-cut expectations and weigh on premium-growth multiples. The offset is that cybersecurity demand is generally mission-critical, and CRWD's net cash balance reduces balance-sheet sensitivity to rates.

Actionable fundamental insights for traders

CRWD's strongest fundamental positives are 23%+ year-over-year revenue growth, 76% gross margin, improving operating leverage, a recent return to GAAP net profitability, very strong cash generation, $4.4B of approximate net cash, and deferred revenue growth of roughly 27.5% year over year. These factors support a constructive long-term fundamental view if execution continues.

The main risks are valuation, dilution, and durability of GAAP profitability. At a roughly $104B market cap, CRWD needs sustained growth and margin expansion. SBC at about 21% of revenue and share dilution of about 4.5% year over year are significant. A single quarter of positive operating income is encouraging, but traders should require confirmation that the margin inflection persists.

For bullish confirmation, watch for the next report to show revenue growth near or above the recent 5% to 6% sequential pace, continued positive operating income, FCF margin above 25%, stable or rising deferred revenue, and SBC falling as a percentage of revenue. For bearish confirmation, watch for revenue growth slipping materially, operating losses returning, receivables growth continuing to pressure cash flow, acquisition-related goodwill rising without clear growth benefit, or share count dilution staying elevated.

Overall, CRWD's fundamentals are improving, but the stock is priced for excellence. The balance sheet and cash flow provide support, while the valuation and relative underperformance argue for discipline. In an investment committee context, CRWD should be treated as a high-quality growth compounder candidate with high multiple risk, not as a low-risk value opportunity.
