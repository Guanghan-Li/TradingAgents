NET is in a tactical rebound, not a confirmed trend repair. The macro backdrop is supportive enough for risk-taking, but not supportive enough to ignore valuation pressure from a 4.26% 10Y Treasury and re-accelerating inflation. That matters for NET because it behaves like a long-duration growth equity. The price action supports a cautious stance: the stock has bounced sharply off a capitulation-style selloff, but it still has not reclaimed the key moving averages that would signal a durable reversal.

**Technical Read**

The dominant fact is that NET remains below all three key trend references. On 2026-04-15, NET closed at 190.13 versus the `close_10_ema` at 193.05, `close_50_sma` at 193.83, and `close_200_sma` at 202.01. That means short-term, medium-term, and long-term trend confirmation are all still missing. The bounce is real, but it has not yet repaired the damage from the breakdown.

The recent sequence matters. NET rallied strongly from 160.19 on 2026-02-23 to 225.48 on 2026-03-18, then rolled over hard to 166.99 on 2026-04-10. That was a drawdown of about 26% from the March high. Since that low, NET has rebounded about 13.9% into 190.13 by 2026-04-15. That is a meaningful rebound, but the stock is still about 15.7% below the March peak, so the larger pattern is still lower high risk until resistance breaks.

Momentum confirms caution. `macd` has deteriorated from +4.53 on 2026-04-07 to -3.70 on 2026-04-15. That is not what a fully repaired breakout looks like. It says the recent bounce came after a sharp momentum breakdown and has not yet rebuilt enough thrust to negate it. `rsi` tells a similar story. The jump from 32.97 on 2026-04-10 to 46.28 on 2026-04-15 shows the panic condition has eased, but RSI still sits in neutral territory. Buyers stopped the immediate bleeding, but they have not established strong upside momentum.

Volatility context is important. On 2026-04-10, NET closed materially below the `boll_lb` of 183.35. That kind of lower-band violation usually marks an exhaustion move, and the subsequent bounce fits that pattern. But by 2026-04-15, NET at 190.13 is still much closer to the lower band at 173.70 than the upper band at 236.05, and still below the implied Bollinger mid-area. That means price has reverted from panic, but it has not transitioned into strength.

`atr` reinforces that this is still a difficult tape. ATR at 13.86 is elevated versus the prior range and large relative to the share price. In practice, that means NET can move several percentage points in either direction without changing the broader setup. Traders treating this like a low-volatility trend name will likely get shaken out.

Volume adds an important non-indicator read. The selloff on 2026-04-09 and 2026-04-10 came with 11.43M and 13.74M shares, far above the typical 2M to 5M range seen earlier in the period. That looks like forced de-risking, institutional distribution, or at minimum broad liquidation. The rebound on 2026-04-13 through 2026-04-15 happened on lower volume than the breakdown days. That does not invalidate the bounce, but it does reduce confidence that the move already represents full institutional re-accumulation.

**Macro Overlay**

The macro regime is best read as moderately supportive for tactical upside but not ideal for valuation-driven upside. The risk-on classification, falling VIX, resilient payrolls, and positive 2Y-10Y curve all support the idea that enterprise demand and software spending can remain intact. That is helpful for NET fundamentally.

The problem is rates. With the 10Y at 4.26%, the long end still imposes valuation pressure on high-growth equities. Inflation re-acceleration in CPI and PPI also reduces the odds of fast Fed easing. So even if macro is not recessionary, it is still a regime where NET likely needs execution and strong price confirmation to extend higher. Multiple expansion alone is less likely to carry it.

That mix explains the chart well: macro is good enough to support a bounce, but not good enough to erase technical damage automatically.

**Actionable Trading Read**

For now, NET is a `HOLD`, not a fresh aggressive `BUY`.

The most important resistance zone is roughly 193 to 194 first, where the `close_10_ema` and `close_50_sma` cluster. If NET cannot reclaim and hold that zone, the bounce remains fragile. Above that, the more important structural test is near 202, the `close_200_sma`. A close back above 202 with improving momentum would materially strengthen the case that the April washout was a bear trap rather than the start of a larger leg lower.

On the downside, 184 to 179 is the first support band based on the last two closes and recent rebound structure. Below that, the 173.70 `boll_lb` becomes important, and below that the 166.99 panic low is the obvious retest level. If NET loses 178 to 179 decisively, the probability of a full retest rises meaningfully.

For swing traders, the cleanest long setup is not chasing 190 after a straight rebound. It is either:
- a confirmed reclaim of 194 and then 202 on improving momentum and solid volume, or
- a pullback that holds above the upper-170s and then turns back up.

For existing holders, the chart does not justify an outright risk-off exit if the position is sized appropriately, because the stock has already had a capitulation event and is attempting stabilization. But it also does not justify adding aggressively before NET proves it can reclaim the moving-average cluster.
