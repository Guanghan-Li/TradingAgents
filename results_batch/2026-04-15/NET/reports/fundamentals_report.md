NET fundamental report as of 2026-04-15

Cloudflare, Inc. (NET) remains a high-growth infrastructure software company with strong gross margins, improving cash generation, and a very liquid balance sheet, but it is still not consistently GAAP profitable and trades at an expensive valuation. The latest supplied quarter is 2025-12-31, and the market is currently pricing NET for continued growth plus a clean path to materially higher earnings.

Key takeaways for traders:
- NET is fundamentally strong on revenue growth and cash generation, but weak on GAAP earnings quality.
- NET’s balance sheet is liquid, though leverage rose sharply during 2025 and debt-to-equity is elevated.
- Valuation is the main risk: forward P/E of 131.9 and price-to-book of 45.8 leave little room for execution misses.
- Recent stock performance is weak versus peers and XLK, which suggests either fading momentum or a de-rating despite solid operating scale.

Valuation analysis
- Forward P/E: 131.9
- Price/book: 45.8
- EPS (TTM): -0.29

NET is not being valued on present earnings. The market is valuing NET on expected future earnings inflection and premium durable growth. That creates asymmetric downside if growth slows, sales efficiency weakens, or margin expansion takes longer than expected.

A simple reality check:
- If forward EPS estimates are met, the stock is still expensive.
- If forward EPS is missed, the multiple can compress quickly.
- If NET delivers sustained revenue growth above 25% with operating leverage, the valuation can remain elevated longer than bears expect.

Actionable insights for traders
- Bull case on NET: revenue is still compounding at a premium rate, gross margins remain strong, and free cash flow is scaling. If traders believe NET can convert this into durable operating profit over the next several quarters, the business quality supports continued institutional interest.
- Bear case on NET: valuation is extremely demanding relative to current earnings, dilution remains ongoing, and debt rose sharply in 2025. Weak relative price action suggests the market is already questioning how much future perfection is priced in.
- What matters most next for NET: sustained revenue growth above 25%, continued FCF expansion, lower SBC as a percent of revenue, and clear improvement in operating margin. Without those, the stock likely remains vulnerable to further de-rating.
- Best fundamental read: NET is a quality growth company, not yet a quality value stock. Traders should treat it as an execution-and-multiple story, not a balance-sheet rescue or deep-value setup.

Bottom line
NET’s fundamentals are good enough to justify serious attention, but not clean enough to make the current valuation obviously safe. The business is scaling, cash generation is real, and the liquidity position is strong. The main tension is that GAAP profitability, dilution control, and leverage optics still lag the premium multiple. Fundamentally, NET looks like a strong company with a fragile valuation setup.
