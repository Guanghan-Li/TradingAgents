The recent rebound from the April washout looks more like mean reversion than confirmed accumulation. The 193-194 zone remains immediate resistance, and the 202 area is the more important structural reclaim level.

Macro conditions are mixed rather than decisively supportive. Risk appetite and enterprise demand remain constructive, but a 4.26% 10-year Treasury and renewed inflation pressure are material headwinds for a high-multiple, long-duration equity like NET. With forward P/E around 131.9, negative trailing EPS, elevated debt, ongoing dilution, and weak relative performance versus XLK, the margin for error is too narrow.

Sentiment is mildly positive due to the Piper Sandler upgrade, but that is not enough to offset the broader risk/reward imbalance. The news base is thin, social confirmation is unavailable, and the bounce has not yet been validated by strong technical follow-through.
