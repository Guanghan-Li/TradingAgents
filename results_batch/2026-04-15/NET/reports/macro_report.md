Macro view for NET as of 2026-04-15:

The macro regime is best characterized as late-cycle but not recessionary: growth and labor remain resilient, inflation pressure has re-accelerated at the margin, and the Treasury curve has normalized into a positive slope. This is not the classic hard-landing setup. It is closer to a nominal-growth expansion with sticky inflation risk, where the Fed has already eased from late-2025 levels but is now likely constrained from cutting aggressively.

For NET, the key macro read-through is mixed. A normal 2Y-10Y curve and still-expanding payrolls support enterprise demand and risk appetite, which is constructive for high-growth software and infrastructure names. However, the recent CPI and PPI increases argue against a rapid decline in discount rates. NET is a long-duration growth equity, so valuation sensitivity to real yields and the 10Y Treasury remains a central risk.

Policy backdrop: the effective fed funds rate is 3.64%, unchanged in February and March after declines from 4.09% in October 2025. That suggests the Fed has moved toward neutral and is now pausing to evaluate inflation persistence. With CPI up 0.87% sequentially and PPI up 1.78%, the next policy impulse is more likely to be cautious/hold than an acceleration of cuts unless labor weakens materially.

Yield curve: the 2Y-10Y spread is +50 bps, with the 10Y at 4.26% and 30Y at 4.87%. This upward-sloping curve is consistent with normal growth expectations, but the long end is high enough to pressure equity multiples. For NET, this means the macro setup can support revenue durability, but the stock may need earnings execution rather than multiple expansion to outperform.

Cross-asset implications for NET:

- Equities: resilient labor and normalized curve support risk appetite, but sticky inflation caps broad multiple expansion.
- Rates: 10Y at 4.26% is a valuation headwind for high-duration growth equities like NET.
- Credit: no acute recession signal from the curve; this supports enterprise spending and cloud/security budgets.
- Volatility: VIX at 18.36 is moderate and declining, which supports tactical risk-taking, though not a complacent regime.
- USD/liquidity: Fed pause near neutral suggests liquidity conditions are no longer tightening aggressively, but they are not strongly easing either.

Primary macro risks for NET:

1. Inflation persistence: March CPI and PPI acceleration could delay Fed cuts and keep discount rates elevated.
2. Long-end yield pressure: 10Y/30Y yields above 4% can compress growth multiples even if fundamentals remain solid.
3. Labor reversal: current payrolls and unemployment are supportive, but deterioration would hurt enterprise demand assumptions.
4. Policy disappointment: markets expecting faster easing could reprice if the Fed remains on hold.
5. Valuation sensitivity: NET likely remains more exposed to rates than defensive or value equities because cash flows are growth-weighted.
