FINAL TRANSACTION PROPOSAL: **HOLD**

Macro view for `VWO`: the backdrop is modestly constructive but not strong enough for a high-conviction BUY. The U.S. macro regime looks closest to a soft-landing / late-cycle reflation mix: labor remains firm, the Treasury curve is normally upward sloping, and Fed policy appears stable near neutral. That reduces recession risk and supports global risk appetite, which is generally favorable for emerging-market equities such as `VWO`.

The main constraint is inflation and rates. CPI rose +0.87% sequentially in March 2026 and PPI rose +1.78%, suggesting renewed price pressure at both consumer and producer levels. With the effective Fed Funds rate unchanged at 3.64% and the 10Y Treasury at 4.26%, the Fed likely has limited urgency to cut further. High U.S. long-end yields can pressure EM equity multiples, keep the dollar supported, and tighten external financing conditions for parts of the `VWO` universe.

Policy path: the Fed has eased from 4.09% in October 2025 to 3.64% by March 2026, but the last two readings are unchanged. That suggests the easier-policy impulse has slowed. For `VWO`, this is better than renewed tightening, but less supportive than an active cutting cycle.

Rates curve: the 2Y-10Y spread is +50 bps, with 2Y at 3.76% and 10Y at 4.26%. A positive curve argues against an imminent U.S. recession signal and supports cyclicals and risk assets. However, the 20Y and 30Y yields near 4.84%-4.87% show term premium or long-duration supply/inflation concerns remain material.

Cross-asset implications for `VWO`: steady Fed policy, a normal curve, and lower VIX are supportive for EM equities, but sticky inflation and elevated long rates argue against aggressive positioning. A HOLD stance is appropriate pending evidence that inflation cools without weakening labor or that the Fed resumes a clearer easing path.
