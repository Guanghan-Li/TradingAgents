## AVGO Fundamental Report

AVGO (Broadcom Inc.) remains one of the strongest large-cap fundamental stories in semiconductors as of April 15, 2026, but the stock is no longer cheap on trailing numbers. The core setup is clear: revenue is still accelerating, operating leverage is strong, free cash flow is exceptional, and leverage is trending down. The main counterweights are a very rich trailing valuation, heavy goodwill/intangible exposure, and a recent quarter in which buybacks plus dividends exceeded free cash flow.

### Company Profile and Current Fundamental Position
AVGO is classified in Technology / Semiconductors with a market cap of about $1.88 trillion. On the prefetched snapshot, AVGO shows TTM revenue of $68.28 billion, EBITDA of $37.22 billion, net income of $24.97 billion, free cash flow of $25.50 billion, profit margin of 36.6%, operating margin of 44.9%, ROE of 33.4%, ROA of 10.7%, and a current ratio of 1.90. Those are elite profitability and cash-generation metrics for a company of this size.

Valuation is mixed depending on which lens is used. AVGO trades at 77.2x trailing earnings but 22.0x forward earnings, with forward EPS of 18.00 versus trailing EPS of 5.14. That gap implies the market is pricing in a large earnings ramp rather than paying for current earnings alone. Price/book at 23.5x is not very useful here because the balance sheet is dominated by acquisition-related intangibles.

### Income Statement Trends
The latest reported quarter in the supplied data is 2026-01-31. AVGO posted:
- Revenue of $19.31 billion
- Gross profit of $13.16 billion
- Operating income of $8.67 billion
- EBITDA of $11.15 billion
- Net income of $7.35 billion
- Diluted EPS of $1.50

The quality of the quarter was strong.
- Revenue grew 7.2% quarter over quarter from $18.02 billion and about 29.5% year over year from $14.92 billion.
- Gross profit grew 7.4% sequentially.
- Operating income grew 13.2% sequentially.
- EBITDA grew 13.0% sequentially.
- Net income grew about 33.5% year over year, although it fell sequentially from $8.52 billion.

The sequential net income decline needs context. The prior quarter had a negative tax provision of $1.65 billion, which unusually boosted earnings. Pretax income actually improved to $8.20 billion from $6.87 billion, so the business itself strengthened even though reported net income fell quarter to quarter.

Margin structure remains excellent.
- Gross margin in the latest quarter was about 68.1%.
- Operating margin was about 44.9%.
- EBITDA margin was about 57.7%.
- Net margin was about 38.1%.

R&D was $2.97 billion in the latest quarter, roughly 15.4% of revenue, versus $2.25 billion a year earlier. That suggests AVGO is still funding product development aggressively while maintaining very high margins.

### Balance Sheet and Financial Position
AVGO’s balance sheet is strong on liquidity but structurally acquisition-heavy.
- Cash and equivalents: $14.17 billion
- Current assets: $32.06 billion
- Current liabilities: $16.86 billion
- Working capital: $15.20 billion
- Total assets: $169.90 billion
- Total debt: $66.06 billion
- Net debt: $51.88 billion
- Equity: $79.87 billion

The positive read:
- Net debt has fallen from $57.25 billion a year earlier to $51.88 billion.
- Working capital improved sharply from essentially breakeven a year earlier to $15.20 billion.
- Current ratio near 1.9 gives AVGO solid near-term balance sheet flexibility.

The main structural risk is asset quality. Goodwill and other intangibles are $128.10 billion, about 75% of total assets. Tangible book value is negative at -$48.23 billion. That does not mean the business is weak, but it does mean AVGO depends heavily on acquired intangible value and future cash flows to justify book equity. If growth slowed materially or an acquired asset underperformed, impairment risk would matter.

Working-capital line items deserve monitoring because they are growing quickly.
- Receivables rose to $14.00 billion from $7.68 billion a year earlier.
- Inventory rose to $2.96 billion from $1.91 billion a year earlier.

That is consistent with a scaling business, but traders should watch whether receivables keep rising faster than revenue. If they do, cash conversion could soften.

### Cash Flow and Capital Allocation
This is where AVGO is strongest. In the latest quarter:
- Operating cash flow was $8.26 billion
- Capital expenditures were only $250 million
- Free cash flow was $8.01 billion

That implies a free cash flow margin of roughly 41.5%, which is outstanding.

Cash flow has also been climbing steadily:
- 2025-01-31 FCF: $6.01 billion
- 2025-04-30 FCF: $6.41 billion
- 2025-07-31 FCF: $7.02 billion
- 2025-10-31 FCF: $7.47 billion
- 2026-01-31 FCF: $8.01 billion

This is a strong trend and supports the forward earnings story.

Capital allocation is shareholder-friendly but aggressive.
- Dividends paid in the latest quarter: $3.09 billion
- Share repurchases: $7.85 billion
- Total direct capital return: about $10.94 billion

That exceeded quarterly free cash flow, so AVGO effectively used both balance-sheet capacity and cash on hand to fund capital returns. Cash fell by $2.00 billion in the quarter, and net debt issuance was positive at $824 million. This is not a red flag by itself because the company still has large cash generation and falling year-over-year net debt, but it is a signal that recent shareholder return activity was more aggressive than internally funded cash generation alone.

### Trader-Focused Fundamental Takeaways
AVGO’s core fundamental case is strong. Revenue growth is accelerating, margins are extremely high, FCF is compounding quarter after quarter, and net debt is slowly improving. On fundamentals alone, AVGO still screens as an institutional-quality compounder.

The main reason traders should stay selective is valuation and expectations. A 77x trailing P/E means the market is discounting a lot of future earnings power already. The forward P/E near 22x is much more reasonable, but only if AVGO continues to deliver on the implied EPS ramp. That makes the next few quarters especially important.

High-conviction bullish signals to monitor:
- Revenue holding above the $19 billion quarterly run rate
- FCF staying near or above $8 billion per quarter
- Operating margin holding in the mid-40% range
- Net debt continuing to trend lower

Main risk signals to monitor:
- Receivables or inventory rising materially faster than revenue
- Buybacks and dividends continuing to exceed FCF for multiple quarters
- Any sign of margin compression after the recent rally
- Any impairment or integration concerns tied to the very large intangible asset base

Bottom line: AVGO’s fundamentals remain excellent, and the latest quarter supports the bullish growth and cash-flow narrative. The stock’s recent outperformance is fundamentally understandable. The debate for traders is no longer business quality; it is whether future execution can keep up with a premium market narrative.
