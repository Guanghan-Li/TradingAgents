# ALB Fundamental Research Report

## Company Overview

ALB is Albemarle Corporation, a Basic Materials company in the Specialty Chemicals industry. The company is heavily exposed to lithium and specialty chemical markets, making its earnings highly cyclical and sensitive to commodity pricing, EV battery demand, customer inventory cycles, project execution, and global industrial conditions.

As of the prefetched data retrieved on 2026-04-15, ALB has a market capitalization of approximately $21.85 billion. The stock has rebounded sharply from its 52-week low of $50.85 and is trading much closer to its 52-week high of $206.00, implying that the market is already discounting a meaningful recovery in fundamentals. Based on book value of $61.999 and price-to-book of 2.99x, the implied share price is roughly $185, around 10% below the 52-week high and more than 250% above the 52-week low.

## Valuation and Market Position

ALB’s valuation is recovery-oriented rather than trailing-earnings-supported. The company reports negative trailing EPS of -$5.75 and negative trailing net income of approximately -$677 million, yet trades at a forward P/E of 21.0x on forward EPS of $8.81. This means the current valuation depends heavily on a rebound in profitability.

The price-to-book ratio of 2.99x is elevated for a cyclical materials company that currently has negative ROE of -4.66% and a trailing profit margin of -9.93%. Traders should view this as a sentiment and forward-cycle valuation, not a cheap asset-value setup.

Relative performance confirms that the market has been rotating back into ALB. ALB gained +4.47% over the last week versus XLB at -0.71%, producing +5.19 percentage points of short-term sector alpha. Over one month, ALB returned +14.12% versus XLB at +4.47%. Over six months, ALB returned +93.48% versus XLB at +17.59%, a very large +75.89 percentage points of alpha. However, over three months ALB slightly lagged XLB, returning +5.07% versus +5.91%, suggesting recent momentum has been uneven after the large six-month recovery.

## Revenue, Margins, and Earnings Quality

ALB’s latest quarterly income statement shows improving revenue but still pressured profitability. Q4 2025 revenue was $1.428 billion, up from $1.308 billion in Q3 2025 and $1.232 billion in Q4 2024. Sequential revenue growth from Q3 to Q4 was approximately 9.2%, while year-over-year revenue growth versus Q4 2024 was approximately 15.9%.

Gross profit improved to $197.9 million in Q4 2025 from $117.6 million in Q3 2025. Q4 gross margin was approximately 13.9%, up from roughly 9.0% in Q3. This is a constructive sign, but margins remain modest for a company valued on a strong forward earnings recovery.

The main negative is earnings quality. Q4 2025 net income was -$414.2 million, and diluted EPS was -$3.87. This loss was heavily affected by unusual items of -$247.6 million, including a $245.6 million capital asset impairment. Q3 2025 also included large unusual items of -$183.3 million and net income of -$160.7 million. Normalized income was still negative in Q4 2025 at approximately -$218.5 million, but the impairment amplified the reported loss.

Operating income improved to $30.3 million in Q4 2025 from -$33.6 million in Q3, but total operating income as reported was -$217.4 million due to special charges. This distinction matters: core operating performance appears to be stabilizing, while GAAP profitability remains burdened by impairments and restructuring-related costs.

## Balance Sheet and Liquidity

ALB’s balance sheet remains liquid but not risk-free. The company had $1.618 billion of cash and cash equivalents at 2025-12-31, down from $1.932 billion at 2025-09-30 but up from $1.192 billion at 2024-12-31. Current assets were $4.008 billion versus current liabilities of $1.798 billion, producing working capital of $2.210 billion. The current ratio of 2.229 indicates adequate short-term liquidity.

Total debt was $3.297 billion at 2025-12-31, down meaningfully from $3.735 billion in Q3 2025 and $3.616 billion at year-end 2024. Net debt declined to $1.576 billion from $1.695 billion in Q3 and $2.324 billion at year-end 2024. This debt reduction is a positive fundamental development, especially for a cyclical company emerging from a downturn.

Stockholders’ equity was $9.533 billion, with common stock equity of $7.298 billion and preferred stock equity of $2.235 billion. Tangible book value was $5.584 billion. The reported debt-to-equity ratio of 33.954 is manageable, but traders should account for cyclicality, capital intensity, and the preferred dividend burden.

Net PPE was $8.729 billion, down from $9.222 billion in Q3 and $9.450 billion at 2024 year-end. The decline partly reflects impairments and depreciation. Construction in progress was $642.9 million at Q4 2025, down from $1.010 billion at Q4 2024, suggesting capital project intensity may be moderating.

## Cash Flow and Capital Allocation

Cash flow is one of the stronger areas in the current data. Q4 2025 operating cash flow was $388.5 million, and free cash flow was $233.1 million. Q3 2025 free cash flow was also positive at $223.4 million, while Q2 was negative at -$126.8 million and Q1 was positive at $362.8 million. For full-year 2025 quarterly aggregation, ALB generated positive free cash flow across the year, helped by lower capital expenditures and working capital benefits.

Capital expenditure was -$155.4 million in Q4 2025, much lower than -$355.7 million in Q4 2024. This capex moderation is important because ALB’s prior investment cycle was capital-intensive. Lower capex improves near-term free cash flow, but traders should monitor whether lower investment affects future growth capacity or simply reflects disciplined spending after a downturn.

ALB also used cash to reduce debt. Q4 2025 long-term debt payments were $459.8 million, while long-term debt issuance was only $18.4 million. Net debt issuance/payments were -$441.4 million in Q4. This is a positive signal that management is prioritizing balance sheet repair.

Dividend payments remain a cash obligation. Q4 2025 cash dividends paid totaled $89.3 million, including $47.6 million of common dividends and $41.7 million of preferred dividends. The dividend yield is modest at 0.85%, so the equity income case is not the primary reason to own ALB. The preferred dividend burden is notable because preferred stock dividends reduced income available to common shareholders.

## Profitability and Return Metrics

Trailing profitability remains weak. ALB’s trailing revenue is $5.143 billion, gross profit is $673.5 million, EBITDA is $737.8 million, and net income is -$677.4 million. Profit margin is -9.93%, operating margin is only 2.263%, ROE is -4.656%, and ROA is 0.300%.

The gap between positive EBITDA and negative net income highlights the importance of depreciation, impairments, interest expense, taxes, and preferred dividends. Q4 2025 depreciation and amortization was $163.7 million, interest expense was $57.8 million, and preferred stock dividends were $41.7 million. These fixed or semi-fixed burdens mean ALB needs a meaningful improvement in gross profit and operating income for common shareholders to see durable EPS recovery.

## Macro and Sector Context

The macro backdrop is mixed but not hostile. The effective Federal Funds Rate was 3.64% as of 2026-03-01 and unchanged sequentially. The yield curve is upward sloping, with the 2-year Treasury at 3.76% and the 10-year Treasury at 4.26%, producing a 50 basis point 2Y-10Y spread. This is consistent with normal growth expectations rather than imminent recession stress.

Inflation remains relevant for ALB’s cost base. CPI rose 0.87% sequentially in the latest data, while PPI rose 1.78%, indicating producer-level cost pressure. For ALB, higher producer inflation can pressure input, labor, logistics, and construction costs, although commodity-linked selling prices may offset some of that if lithium and specialty chemical markets strengthen.

Unemployment was 4.30%, down 0.10 percentage points sequentially, and nonfarm payrolls increased by 178,000. This supports continued industrial and consumer demand. VIX was 18.36 as of 2026-04-14, down 3.97% sequentially, indicating moderate but not extreme market volatility.

## Trading-Relevant Fundamental Interpretation

The bull case is that ALB is moving through the trough of the lithium cycle. Revenue is improving, gross margin recovered sequentially in Q4, operating cash flow and free cash flow are positive, capex is moderating, and net debt is declining. The market is already rewarding this setup, as shown by strong one-month, six-month, and YTD alpha versus the Materials ETF.

The bear case is valuation and earnings risk. Trailing EPS is negative, trailing net income is deeply negative, ROE is negative, and recent quarters included large impairments. The forward P/E of 21.0x assumes a major earnings rebound to forward EPS of $8.81. If lithium pricing, volumes, or margins disappoint, ALB could de-rate quickly because the current stock price already reflects substantial recovery expectations.

For traders, the key question is not whether Q4 GAAP earnings were weak; they clearly were. The key question is whether Q4 cash flow, revenue improvement, gross margin recovery, debt reduction, and capex discipline mark a sustainable turn. ALB’s large rally from the 52-week low increases the need for confirmation from upcoming quarters.

## Actionable Monitoring Points

Traders should monitor whether quarterly gross margin can remain above Q4’s 13.9% level and move toward a structurally higher range. A relapse toward Q3’s 9.0% gross margin would weaken the recovery narrative.

Free cash flow is a key confirmation metric. Consecutive positive FCF quarters in Q3 and Q4 are constructive. If ALB can continue generating positive FCF while reducing debt and maintaining capex discipline, the balance sheet repair thesis strengthens.

Watch unusual items and impairments. Q4’s $245.6 million impairment and Q3’s $181.1 million impairment suggest management is still cleaning up prior investments or asset values. Continued large charges would undermine confidence in book value and earnings quality.

Interest expense and preferred dividends are meaningful claims ahead of common shareholders. Q4 interest expense was $57.8 million, and preferred dividends were $41.7 million. Common EPS recovery requires operating profit to comfortably exceed these recurring burdens.

Relative strength is supportive but extended. ALB’s six-month gain of +93.48% is far above XLB’s +17.59%, and the stock is much closer to its 52-week high than low. Positive fundamentals may already be partially priced in.
