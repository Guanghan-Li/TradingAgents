Macro view for `DDOG`: the backdrop is a soft-landing / mid-cycle expansion, not recession. Labor is still resilient (`4.3%` unemployment, positive payroll growth), the Fed has already eased from late 2025 levels and is now on hold around `3.64%`, and the Treasury curve has re-steepened to a normal positive slope (`2Y-10Y = +50 bps`). That combination is generally supportive for `DDOG` because it favors steady enterprise software demand and keeps broad risk appetite intact.

The main offset is inflation/rates. CPI and PPI both accelerated sequentially in the latest print, and the long end is still elevated (`10Y 4.26%`, `30Y 4.87%`). For a long-duration growth name like `DDOG`, that means the macro regime is better for fundamentals than for valuation expansion. Base case: macro supports revenue resilience for `DDOG`, but upside in the stock is more likely to come from execution and AI/observability demand than from a big multiple rerating unless long yields fall.

Likely policy path: the Fed looks biased to stay patient near current levels unless labor rolls over materially. The recent easing cycle has already happened; the current signal is pause, not urgent further cuts. For `DDOG`, that implies a constructive but rate-sensitive setup: better than a recessionary macro tape, but still vulnerable to hawkish repricing if inflation firms again.

Cross-asset implications for `DDOG`:
- Equities: positive for quality growth, especially profitable software, as long as macro stays in soft landing mode.
- Rates: elevated long yields are the clearest macro headwind for `DDOG` valuation.
- Credit/risk sentiment: subdued VIX (`18.36`) points to orderly conditions, supportive for growth-stock participation.
- USD/financial conditions: if inflation surprises up and financial conditions tighten, `DDOG` would likely underperform shorter-duration sectors.

Concrete risks other analysts should incorporate for `DDOG`:
- A bear-steepening move in Treasuries could compress `DDOG`'s multiple even if fundamentals hold.
- Sticky inflation could delay any further Fed easing and keep discount rates high.
- If labor cracks and payroll momentum fades, enterprise IT and cloud spend could weaken with a lag.
- If soft landing persists without lower yields, `DDOG` may trade more on earnings quality than on macro beta.

Overall macro stance on `DDOG`: mildly constructive on demand, neutral-to-cautious on valuation.
