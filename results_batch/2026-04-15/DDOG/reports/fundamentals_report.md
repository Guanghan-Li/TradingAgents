DDOG fundamental report as of 2026-04-15

Datadog, Inc. is a large-cap application software company in cloud observability and monitoring, with a market cap of about $42.84B. The business still screens as a premium growth asset rather than a mature software name: trailing P/E is 390.5, forward P/E is 45.9, price/book is 11.43, beta is 1.29, and the 52-week range is $87.70 to $201.69. That valuation setup matters because DDOG is no longer being priced on current earnings power; it is being priced on expected margin expansion.

The core operating story is still strong on growth and gross margin. TTM revenue is $3.427B and TTM gross profit is $2.741B, implying roughly 80% gross margin. The latest reported quarter, 2025-12-31, showed revenue of $953.2M, up about 29.2% YoY from $737.7M, and up about 7.6% sequentially from $885.7M. Gross profit in the quarter was $766.3M, for a gross margin of about 80.4%, which shows the model remains highly scalable at the gross-profit line. Total deferred revenue was about $1.262B at 2025-12-31 versus about $0.985B a year earlier, up roughly 28%, which broadly supports the view that demand and contracted revenue visibility remain healthy.

Profitability is the mixed part of the DDOG fundamental picture. TTM net income is $107.7M, EPS is $0.31, profit margin is 3.14%, operating margin is about 1.0%, ROE is 3.34%, and ROA is slightly negative in the snapshot. In the latest quarter, net income was $46.6M and diluted EPS was $0.13. However, GAAP operating performance through 2025 was uneven: operating income was negative in 2025-03-31, 2025-06-30, and 2025-09-30 before turning positive again in 2025-12-31. Q4 pretax income also benefited from $47.3M of interest income versus $7.2M of interest expense, so DDOG's large cash/investment balances are helping reported earnings. Traders should treat DDOG as a company with strong business economics but only modest GAAP earnings conversion so far.

Cash generation is much stronger than GAAP earnings. Quarterly operating cash flow was $327.1M in Q4 2025, and quarterly free cash flow was $291.0M, a very strong 30.5% FCF margin for the quarter. Summing the four 2025 quarters gives about $1.05B of operating cash flow and about $914.7M of free cash flow, broadly consistent with the snapshot's $879.6M TTM FCF. Capex remains manageable, with $36.0M in Q4. This means DDOG remains a high-quality cash compounder even though GAAP operating margins still look thin.

The main reason cash flow so materially exceeds net income is stock-based compensation and balance-sheet float. Stock-based compensation was $205.4M in Q4 alone, and about $750.7M across 2025, which is roughly 21.9% of annual revenue. That is still very high. Share count is rising alongside it: ordinary shares outstanding increased to 352.5M from 342.1M a year earlier, about 3.0% dilution. For traders, this is one of the most important DDOG fundamental watchpoints. If SBC remains above 20% of revenue, the market may continue to discount the quality of the free-cash-flow profile.

The balance sheet is a clear strength. DDOG ended 2025 with about $4.475B of cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments against total debt of about $1.279B, plus a current ratio of 3.38 and working capital of $3.79B. Equity rose to $3.732B. Current debt and capital lease obligations fell to just $39.4M from roughly $666.0M at 2024-12-31/2025-03-31 levels, largely because DDOG repaid $635.7M of debt in 2025-06-30. That sharply reduced near-term balance-sheet risk. Even though the snapshot shows debt/equity of 34.27, the more important practical point is that DDOG has liquidity well in excess of borrowings.

DDOG's recent financial history shows the business is still scaling. Revenue over the last five reported quarters moved from $737.7M to $761.6M to $826.8M to $885.7M to $953.2M. Gross profit followed the same path, from $593.5M to $603.9M to $660.8M to $709.2M to $766.3M. Net income was volatile but improved into year-end: $45.6M in 2024-12-31, $24.6M in 2025-03-31, $2.6M in 2025-06-30, $33.9M in 2025-09-30, and $46.6M in 2025-12-31. Goodwill and intangible assets also increased meaningfully during 2025, reflecting acquisition activity, including a $115.3M business purchase in 2025-06-30.

Market behavior suggests fundamentals have not yet translated into price leadership. DDOG was up 3.91% over the last week, but it lagged XLK's 6.08% gain. The underperformance is much more pronounced over longer windows: 1-month alpha vs XLK is -12.79%, 3-month alpha is -6.27%, 6-month alpha is -31.97%, and YTD alpha is -13.79%. Among listed peers in the provided set, DDOG ranks just 7th of 9 on 3-month performance. This tells traders that the market is still de-rating the multiple, likely because it wants clearer evidence of durable operating leverage and lower dilution.

The macro backdrop is mixed-to-slightly supportive for a long-duration software name like DDOG. Effective Fed funds are 3.64%, down from late-2025 levels, and the yield curve is upward sloping with the 10-year Treasury at 4.26%. Lower short rates help premium software valuations at the margin, but long rates are still high enough to keep pressure on expensive growth equities. Inflation also ticked higher in the latest CPI and PPI readings. In that setting, DDOG likely needs strong execution, not just an easier macro backdrop, to re-rate materially.

Actionable trader read-through for DDOG:

1. The bull case is strong revenue growth, about 80% gross margin, rising deferred revenue, excellent liquidity, and robust free cash flow. Those are the attributes of a durable software compounder.
2. The bear case is that valuation is still rich, GAAP operating margins remain weak, interest income is helping earnings, and SBC-driven dilution is still heavy.
3. The most important next-print checkpoints are revenue growth staying above 25%, GAAP operating margin remaining positive and improving, SBC falling as a percentage of revenue, and deferred revenue growth continuing to track top-line growth.
4. Fundamentally, DDOG does not look broken or balance-sheet constrained. It looks like a quality business whose stock still needs a cleaner margin story to regain relative strength.
5. If traders are positioning around fundamentals, DDOG is more attractive when the setup is 'growth stable, margins inflecting, dilution easing.' It is less attractive when the setup is 'growth merely good, margins flat, multiple still premium.'
