Macro view for `NVDA`:

The prefetched macro regime for `NVDA` is a mixed but not recessionary setup. The Treasury curve is positively sloped with the 2Y-10Y spread at `+0.52%`, which argues against an imminent recession and supports continued capex and enterprise spending, a macro positive for `NVDA` demand. But the inflation data are the more important near-term signal for `NVDA` valuation: March 2026 CPI rose `+0.87%` m/m and PPI rose `+1.78%` m/m, which points to renewed price pressure and raises the risk that the Fed stays restrictive for longer than equity bulls want.

For `NVDA`, that creates a push-pull backdrop. Growth-sensitive fundamentals are helped by a normal curve, a still-contained `VIX` at `18.36`, and a Fed that has already cut from late-2025 levels to an effective funds rate of `3.64%`. But higher long-end yields matter directly for `NVDA` because long-duration growth equities are sensitive to discount-rate pressure; the `10Y` at `4.30%` and `30Y` at `4.90%` are not especially friendly to multiple expansion. The most likely policy path for `NVDA` is a Fed on hold near term, with any further easing delayed unless inflation cools materially.

Base case for `NVDA`: macro is still supportive enough for demand, but less supportive for valuation. That means the macro backdrop favors earnings resilience over P/E expansion. If inflation stays hot and long yields keep rising, `NVDA` faces multiple compression risk even without a recession. If inflation rolls back down while the curve stays positively sloped, `NVDA` would regain a cleaner macro tailwind.

Key macro risks other analysts should incorporate for `NVDA`:
- Re-acceleration in inflation delaying or reducing Fed cuts.
- Rising long-bond yields pressuring `NVDA`'s duration-heavy valuation.
- Missing labor, payroll, GDP, and PMI data leave the growth signal incomplete; if those later show weakening, `NVDA` cyclical demand assumptions need to be marked down.
- A still-calm `VIX` suggests markets are not pricing acute stress, so any inflation or rates surprise could hit crowded growth leadership like `NVDA` disproportionately.
