## NVDA Fundamental Report

**NVDA** is one of the highest-quality large-cap growth companies in the market on current fundamentals. Based on the prefetched live snapshot dated **2026-04-15**, NVIDIA Corporation operates in **Technology / Semiconductors**, carries a market cap of about **$4.83T**, and is producing unusually strong scale, margin, and cash generation at the same time. The core fundamental picture remains dominated by AI infrastructure demand, with the latest quarterly statements showing continued acceleration rather than normalization.

### Company profile and baseline financial quality

NVDA’s headline metrics are exceptional even by mega-cap standards. Trailing twelve-month revenue is **$215.9B**, gross profit is **$153.5B**, EBITDA is **$133.2B**, and net income is **$120.1B**. Reported profitability ratios are extremely high: **55.6% profit margin**, **65.0% operating margin**, **101.5% ROE**, and **51.2% ROA**. The business also carries a strong liquidity profile with a **3.9 current ratio** and **$58.1B** of headline free cash flow in the summary snapshot.

Valuation is still expensive in absolute terms, but not as stretched as headline sentiment alone might imply. **TTM P/E is 40.7x**, while **forward P/E is 17.7x**. That large drop from trailing to forward earnings multiple tells traders the market still expects substantial earnings expansion. **Price/book is 30.7x**, which is rich, but for NVDA that reflects extraordinary profitability and asset-light economics more than balance-sheet weakness.

### Income statement trend: growth is still accelerating

The most important datapoint is the quarter ended **2026-01-31**.

- Revenue: **$68.13B**, up from **$57.01B** in the prior quarter and **$39.33B** a year earlier.
- That is about **+19.5% QoQ** and **+73.2% YoY**.
- Gross profit: **$51.09B**, about **+22.1% QoQ** and **+77.9% YoY**.
- Operating income: **$44.30B**, about **+23.0% QoQ** and **+84.3% YoY**.
- Net income: **$42.96B**, about **+34.6% QoQ** and **+94.5% YoY**.
- Diluted EPS: **$1.76**, up from **$1.30** prior quarter and **$0.89** a year earlier.

Margins also improved:

- Gross margin in the latest quarter was about **75.0%**.
- Operating margin was about **65.0%**.
- Net margin was about **63.1%**.

That is important because NVDA is not just growing quickly; it is gaining operating leverage while growing. R&D rose to **$5.51B** from **$3.71B** a year earlier, but revenue growth far outpaced expense growth. Operating expense increased from **$4.69B** to **$6.79B** over the same period, which is meaningful but still much slower than top-line growth. This is exactly the pattern traders want to see in a dominant platform company: high reinvestment, but even faster monetization.

One nuance: latest-quarter net margin was boosted by **$5.60B** of other income/expense, including investment-related gains. Core operating performance is still very strong without that benefit, but traders should not assume every quarter will repeat that magnitude of non-operating lift.

### Balance sheet: extremely strong, with one notable accounting/watch item

NVDA’s balance sheet remains a major strength.

- Total assets: **$206.8B**.
- Stockholders’ equity: **$157.3B**.
- Total debt: **$11.04B**.
- Cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments: **$62.56B**.
- Net cash position: roughly **$51.5B**.
- Working capital: **$93.44B**.

This is a fortress balance sheet. The company has ample liquidity, modest absolute debt, and substantial financial flexibility for capex, acquisitions, buybacks, and strategic inventory builds.

Two balance-sheet trends matter for traders:

1. **Receivables and inventory are both rising fast.**
- Receivables increased to **$38.47B** from **$23.07B** a year earlier.
- Inventory increased to **$21.40B** from **$10.08B** a year earlier.

This can be read positively as preparation for continued demand and large-scale shipment commitments. It can also become a risk signal if customer digestion or hyperscaler spending slows. For now, the growth in working capital looks consistent with business expansion, not distress.

2. **Goodwill and intangibles jumped sharply.**
- Goodwill and other intangibles reached **$24.14B** versus **$6.00B** a year earlier.
- Cash flow statement shows **$13.17B** of business purchases in the latest quarter.

That suggests material M&A activity. Traders should watch whether acquired assets contribute to revenue growth, software/platform depth, or systems integration. If not, the goodwill build could become a medium-term quality concern.

A data-quality caveat is worth flagging: the headline summary shows **debt-to-equity of 7.255**, but the quarterly balance sheet shows debt of **$11.0B** versus equity of **$157.3B**, which implies leverage closer to **0.07x**. That discrepancy likely comes from vendor methodology or stale field definitions. Traders using factor models should reconcile this before relying on the headline leverage ratio.

### Cash flow and capital allocation: elite

NVDA’s cash generation is still one of the strongest features of the story.

Latest quarter cash flow:

- Operating cash flow: **$36.19B**.
- Capex: **$1.28B**.
- Free cash flow: **$34.90B**.

That implies very high cash conversion relative to earnings. Even with working-capital investment, the business is throwing off enormous excess cash.

Capital allocation has been aggressive:

- Share repurchases were **$3.82B** in the latest quarter.
- Buybacks over the last five reported quarters total roughly **$47.9B**.
- Dividends remain minimal at about **$242M-$245M** per quarter.

This tells traders management still prefers buybacks over cash dividends, which fits a high-growth, high-return business. Diluted average shares fell from **24.71B** a year ago to **24.43B** in the latest quarter, so buybacks are large enough to matter at scale.

Another data caveat: the headline fundamentals section lists **free cash flow of $58.13B**, but summing the most recent four quarterly free cash flow figures gives a much higher number. That likely reflects different trailing windows or vendor definitions. It does not change the direction of the story, but modelers should normalize the dataset before using it mechanically.

### Valuation and market behavior

The market is paying up for NVDA, but fundamentals still support the premium better than most peers.

- **TTM P/E: 40.7x** is high.
- **Forward P/E: 17.7x** is much more moderate if earnings forecasts hold.
- **Beta: 2.335** means traders should still expect large swings.
- **52-week range: $95.04 to $212.19**.
- Current price trend is roughly flat-to-up versus long averages: **50-day average $182.12**, **200-day average $181.12**.

Relative performance shows solid but not dominant recent leadership:

- **1-week: +9.22%**.
- **1-month: +8.54%**.
- **3-month: +7.03%**.
- **6-month: +5.61%**.
- **YTD: +5.31%**.

Against **XLK**, NVDA has outperformed over **1 week**, **3 months**, and **YTD**, but was roughly inline over **1 month** and slightly behind over **6 months**. It ranked **3rd of 8 peers** on 3-month performance. That says the stock still has positive relative momentum, but leadership has broadened to peers like **AMD** and **AVGO**, which have recently outperformed on shorter windows.

### Macro context and what it means for NVDA

Macro backdrop is currently supportive enough for high-quality growth:

- Effective Fed funds proxy sits around **3.64%**.
- Treasury curve is positively sloped, with **2Y at 3.78%** and **10Y at 4.30%**.
- **VIX at 18.36** suggests a relatively calm risk environment.
- CPI and PPI both moved higher in the latest reading.

For NVDA, this means:

- A neutral-to-supportive rate backdrop helps justify higher growth multiples.
- Rising inflation and PPI are not ideal, but NVDA’s gross margins are so high that the company has more cushion than most hardware-linked names.
- If inflation re-accelerates enough to push long yields higher, valuation multiple compression becomes the bigger risk than operating weakness.

### Actionable trader takeaways

The fundamental case for **NVDA** remains strong. Revenue growth, margin expansion, cash flow generation, and balance-sheet strength all point in the same direction. This is not a turnaround or hope-driven story; it is a currently dominant earnings machine.

The main bullish evidence is:

- Revenue and EPS are still compounding at very high rates.
- Margins are expanding, not compressing.
- Free cash flow is massive.
- Net cash is large and debt is manageable.
- Buybacks are significant and reduce share count.

The main things traders should watch closely are:

- Inventory and receivables growth for early signs of demand normalization or customer digestion.
- Whether recent acquisition-related goodwill creates integration risk.
- Whether valuation can hold if macro yields rise or AI capex expectations soften.
- Dataset inconsistencies in debt-to-equity and free cash flow fields when building screens or models.

Bottom line: **NVDA fundamentals remain decisively strong, with the stock best described as premium-quality but valuation-sensitive.** The business itself is still executing at an elite level. The bigger debate for traders is no longer operational strength; it is how much of that strength is already priced in, and whether future quarters can keep clearing an unusually high bar.
