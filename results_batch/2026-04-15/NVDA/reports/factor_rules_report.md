The strongest bearish signal is `Valuation stretch under slowing growth`: it becomes the dominant risk if forward growth guidance is revised lower while the stock’s multiple stays rich or expands faster than earnings expectations. The `Balance sheet resilience` rule is supportive but secondary; it improves downside durability through net cash and free cash flow, but it does not by itself resolve the core growth-versus-valuation debate.

Use the balance-sheet rule mainly as a risk-buffer modifier, not as the primary directional driver.
