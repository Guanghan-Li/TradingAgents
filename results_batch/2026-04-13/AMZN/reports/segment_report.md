## Amazon Segment Analysis – Business Unit Decomposition & Value Drivers

### Executive Summary
Amazon operates three primary revenue engines: **North America retail** (~60% of revenue), **International retail** (~20%), and **AWS** (~15-17%), with **Advertising** emerging as a high-margin fourth pillar (~5-7%). Q4 2025 results show total revenue of $213.4B (+13.6% YoY), with operating income of $25.0B and operating margin expanding to 11.7% (vs 11.3% in Q4 2024). The company is doubling down on AI infrastructure with a $200B capex commitment, primarily benefiting AWS and advertising targeting capabilities.

### Segment-by-Segment View

**AWS (Amazon Web Services)**: The crown jewel. While exact segment revenue isn't broken out in the provided data, AWS typically contributes 60-70% of operating profit despite being 15-17% of revenue. Recent news highlights partnerships (Pearson AI training) and Anthropic backing, signaling continued cloud/AI dominance. Margin profile remains best-in-class (30%+ operating margin). **Concentration risk**: AWS growth deceleration would disproportionately impact profitability.

**North America Retail**: Largest revenue contributor but lower margin (~5-7% operating margin). Q4 2025 showed strong holiday performance. Expansion into automotive sales (Kia, Chevrolet, Jeep listings) diversifies GMV but remains low-margin. Prime subscription stickiness provides recurring revenue base.

**International Retail**: Structurally lower margin due to emerging market mix and logistics complexity. Recent quarters show path to profitability as scale improves. Currency headwinds remain a risk.

**Advertising**: Fastest-growing, highest-incremental-margin segment. Sponsored products and video ads benefit from first-party data moat. AI investments enhance targeting ROI, driving advertiser demand.

### Margin Trajectory & Capital Intensity
Operating margin expanded 40bps YoY (Q4: 11.7% vs 11.3%), driven by AWS mix shift and advertising scale. However, the $200B AI capex cycle (2025-2030E) will pressure near-term FCF. Free cash flow of $23.8B (TTM) reflects heavy infrastructure investment. **Trade-off**: Short-term capital intensity for long-term AI/cloud leadership.

### Key Catalysts & Risks
- **Upside**: AWS AI workload acceleration, advertising share gains, international margin inflection
- **Downside**: Regulatory scrutiny (Anthropic review mentioned in news), AWS competition from Microsoft/Google, retail margin compression from wage/logistics inflation
