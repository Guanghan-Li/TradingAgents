## Macro Regime Assessment

**Growth & Labor**: The economy is expanding at a healthy pace. Nonfarm payrolls added 178k jobs in March, unemployment fell to 4.30%, and Q4 2025 GDP stood at $31.4T. This is a solid consumption environment—critical for Amazon's retail and AWS segments.

**Inflation**: Headline CPI rose 0.87% month-over-month (March), while PPI jumped 2.08% (February). Producer inflation is running hotter than consumer inflation, suggesting margin pressure for goods-heavy businesses, though Amazon's scale and pricing power typically allow pass-through.

**Fed Policy**: The Fed cut 45 bps between October 2025 and January 2026 (from 4.09% to 3.64%), then paused for three months. The effective rate of 3.64% sits near neutral. With inflation still above target and labor tight, the Fed is likely done cutting unless growth deteriorates.

**Yield Curve**: The 2Y-10Y spread is +51 bps—a normal, upward-sloping curve. No recession signal. The curve steepens significantly at the long end (30Y at 4.90%), reflecting term premium and growth expectations. This is constructive for equities.

**Volatility**: VIX at 19.23 is below the 20 threshold, indicating calm markets and risk-on sentiment.

## Cross-Asset Implications

- **Equities**: Soft-landing regime favors growth stocks. Stable rates and no recession risk support higher multiples.
- **Rates**: Front-end anchored by Fed pause; long-end reflects growth optimism. No tailwind from further cuts.
- **Credit**: Tight spreads likely; default risk low in this environment.
- **FX**: Dollar strength if Fed holds while others cut; neutral to slight headwind for AMZN international revenue.

## Risks

1. **Inflation re-acceleration**: PPI at +2.08% MoM is hot. If it feeds into CPI, Fed may hike, compressing equity multiples.
2. **Policy mistake**: Fed on hold too long → overheating; or cuts too soon → inflation expectations unanchor.
3. **Curve steepening**: If driven by term premium (fiscal concerns), long-duration growth stocks underperform.
4. **Exogenous shock**: Geopolitical, supply chain, or financial stress could flip regime quickly (VIX is calm but not complacent).
