## Macroeconomic Analysis – April 13, 2026

**Regime: Reflation / No-Landing**

The U.S. economy is exhibiting resilient growth alongside reaccelerating inflation, forcing the Federal Reserve into a prolonged pause after its 2025 cutting cycle.

### Growth & Labor
- **Nonfarm payrolls** added 178k in March, keeping unemployment at a low 4.30% (down 0.10% MoM).
- Labor market remains tight, supporting consumer spending but adding wage pressure.

### Inflation
- **CPI** surged 0.87% MoM in March (annualized ~11%), signaling a sharp reacceleration.
- **PPI** jumped 2.08% MoM in February, indicating upstream cost pressures are intensifying.
- This inflation pulse likely explains why the Fed has held rates steady at 3.64% since January after cutting 45bps from October–December 2025.

### Policy Path
- The Fed appears **on hold indefinitely**. Further cuts are off the table until inflation cools materially.
- Risk of a hawkish pivot (rate hike) if inflation remains elevated through Q2 2026.

### Yield Curve & Rates
- **2Y-10Y spread: +0.51%** – normal upward slope, no recession signal.
- Curve is steep (1M: 3.66% → 30Y: 4.90%), reflecting expectations of higher long-term neutral rates and term premium.
- **Implication**: Rates are likely to stay elevated or drift higher if inflation persists.

### Cross-Asset Implications
| Asset Class | Outlook | Rationale |
|-------------|---------|-----------|
| **Equities** | Neutral/Cautious | Growth supports earnings, but higher-for-longer rates compress valuations. Defensives outperform. |
| **Fixed Income** | Bearish | Reaccelerating inflation + Fed on hold = upward pressure on yields. Duration risk elevated. |
| **Credit** | Neutral | Solid growth supports fundamentals, but rising rates could widen spreads. Watch for cracks in lower-quality issuers. |
| **Commodities** | Bullish | Inflation backdrop and tight labor market support demand. Energy and industrials benefit. |
| **USD** | Bullish | Higher real rates and Fed hawkishness relative to other central banks support the dollar. |

### Risks
1. **Stagflation tail risk**: If growth slows while inflation stays hot, the Fed faces an impossible trade-off.
2. **Policy error**: Holding rates too high for too long could invert the curve and trigger recession in H2 2026.
3. **Fiscal/political uncertainty**: No GDP data beyond Q4 2025; fiscal policy shifts could alter the growth/inflation mix.
