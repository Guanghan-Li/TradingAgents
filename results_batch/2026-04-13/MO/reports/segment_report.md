# Altria Group (MO) – Segment Analysis

## Business Unit Decomposition

Altria operates a concentrated portfolio anchored by **Smokeable Products** (primarily Marlboro cigarettes), which historically drives ~85–90% of operating company revenue. The remaining mix includes **Oral Tobacco** (Copenhagen, Skoal moist snuff), a small **Wine** segment (Ste. Michelle), and equity stakes in **AB InBev** and formerly **JUUL** (largely impaired). Q4 2025 and Q1 2025 saw combined **$2.13B in unusual charges**—predominantly asset write-offs and impairments—signaling strategic repositioning or valuation pressure on non-core or next-gen assets.

**Revenue Durability:** Smokeable remains a cash fortress despite volume declines (~2–4% annually), sustained by aggressive pricing power (net revenue per unit up mid-single digits). Oral Tobacco is stable but mature. The equity method income from AB InBev ($107–148M/quarter) provides diversification but no operational control.

**Concentration Risk:** Over-reliance on combustible tobacco exposes Altria to regulatory tightening, litigation tail risk, and secular volume erosion. The JUUL write-down and ongoing impairments underscore failed diversification bets into reduced-risk products.

## Segment Economics

| Dimension | Profile |
|-----------|---------|
| **Margin Profile** | Gross margin ~71–73% (smokeable-driven); operating margin 116.7% (TTM) reflects equity income and cost discipline but is distorted by one-time charges |
| **Capital Intensity** | Low capex (~$200–300M/year); asset-light manufacturing with high fixed-cost leverage |
| **Cyclicality** | Defensive; tobacco demand inelastic in downturns, though premium mix can compress |

**Margin Trend:** Normalized EBITDA stable at $3.2–3.5B/quarter, but reported EBIT volatile due to impairments. Underlying smokeable margins expanding via price/mix despite volume pressure.
