# TSLA Technical Analysis Report
**Date: 2026-04-13 | Price: $353.83**

---

## Executive Summary

TSLA is in a confirmed downtrend with price trading below all major moving averages and negative momentum persisting. The stock has declined ~15% from mid-February highs near $417, breaking through critical support levels. While RSI shows the stock is not yet oversold, the combination of bearish technicals and a hostile macro environment (elevated rates, inflation pressure on consumer discretionary) suggests further downside risk. The risk-on regime has failed to lift TSLA, indicating sector-specific weakness beyond broad market sentiment.

## Trend Analysis

### Moving Average Structure: Bearish Alignment
- **10 EMA: $356.24** — Price closed below the 10 EMA, confirming short-term bearish momentum. The 10 EMA has been declining sharply since early April, reflecting accelerating selling pressure.
- **50 SMA: $393.05** — Price is ~10% below the 50 SMA, which continues to slope downward. This medium-term average has been in decline since late March, signaling a structural breakdown in the trend.
- **200 SMA: $397.49** — Price is ~11% below the 200 SMA, confirming a long-term bearish posture. The 200 SMA is flattening, suggesting the long-term uptrend is exhausted.

**Death Cross Watch:** The 50 SMA ($393.05) is converging toward the 200 SMA ($397.49). If the 50 crosses below the 200, it will trigger a classic "death cross" — a strong bearish signal that typically precedes extended downtrends. This cross appears imminent within the next 1-2 weeks if current momentum persists.

**Actionable Insight:** All three moving averages are acting as resistance. Any rally attempts will face selling pressure at $356 (10 EMA), $393 (50 SMA), and $397 (200 SMA). Traders should fade rallies into these levels rather than chase breakouts.

## Momentum Indicators: Weak but Not Capitulation

### RSI: 40.05 — Neutral Zone, No Oversold Signal Yet
The RSI is at 40.05, which is below the neutral 50 level but well above the oversold threshold of 30. This indicates:
- **Bearish momentum is intact**, but selling pressure has not reached panic levels.
- **No reversal signal yet.** RSI needs to either break below 30 (oversold, potential bounce) or reclaim 50 (bullish momentum shift) to provide a tradable signal.
- **Historical context:** RSI dipped to 31.75 on March 30 during the prior selloff, and TSLA bounced briefly. However, that bounce failed, and RSI is now declining again. This suggests weak buying interest even at depressed levels.

**Actionable Insight:** Wait for RSI to reach 30 or below before considering a counter-trend long. Until then, the path of least resistance is lower. If RSI breaks below 30 and forms a bullish divergence (price makes a lower low, RSI makes a higher low), that would be a high-probability reversal setup.

### MACD: -13.84 — Deeply Negative, No Crossover in Sight
The MACD is at -13.84, indicating strong bearish momentum. Key observations:
- **No bullish crossover:** The MACD line remains well below the signal line (not shown but implied by the negative reading). A bullish crossover would require the MACD to turn positive, which is not imminent.
- **Momentum is stabilizing but not reversing:** The MACD was at -14.57 on April 9 and has improved slightly to -13.84 on April 13. This marginal improvement suggests selling pressure is easing, but it's not enough to signal a trend reversal.
- **Divergence watch:** If price makes a new low but MACD makes a higher low, that would be a bullish divergence — a leading indicator of a potential bottom. No divergence is present yet.

**Actionable Insight:** The MACD confirms the downtrend is intact. Do not anticipate a reversal until the MACD crosses above zero or forms a clear bullish divergence. For now, this indicator supports a bearish or neutral stance.

## Volatility and Range Analysis

### Bollinger Bands: Price Near Lower Band, But No Squeeze
- **Upper Band: $404.59** — Resistance is ~14% above current price.
- **Lower Band: $333.38** — Price is ~6% above the lower band, suggesting the stock is approaching oversold territory but has not yet touched the band.
- **Band Width:** The bands are wide (~$71 range), indicating elevated volatility. This is consistent with the ATR reading of 14.26, which shows daily swings of ~4% on average.

**Interpretation:**
- **No Bollinger Band squeeze:** The bands are not contracting, so there's no imminent breakout setup. Instead, the wide bands reflect ongoing volatility and uncertainty.
- **Lower band as support:** If TSLA drops to $333, it would touch the lower Bollinger Band, which often acts as a short-term support level. However, in strong downtrends, price can "walk the band" lower, so this is not a reliable reversal signal on its own.

**Actionable Insight:** If TSLA drops to $333-$335 (lower Bollinger Band), watch for a bounce. However, any rally will likely be capped by the 10 EMA ($356) or the middle Bollinger Band (implied ~$369). Use the lower band as a tactical entry for short-term trades, but do not expect a sustained reversal without confirmation from RSI or MACD.

### ATR: 14.26 — Elevated Volatility Persists
The ATR is at 14.26, which translates to ~4% daily swings. This is elevated relative to TSLA's historical norms and reflects the ongoing uncertainty around the stock. Key implications:
- **Wide stop-losses required:** Traders should set stop-losses at least 1.5x ATR (~$21) away from entry to avoid being stopped out by normal volatility.
- **Position sizing:** Reduce position size to account for the elevated volatility. A 4% daily swing means a $10,000 position could fluctuate by $400/day.

**Actionable Insight:** The elevated ATR suggests the market is still pricing in significant uncertainty. This is not a low-volatility, high-conviction setup. Trade smaller size and use wider stops.

## Macro Integration: Why the Technicals Matter More in This Environment

The macro backdrop is **neutral-to-negative** for TSLA:
- **Higher-for-longer rates (10Y at 4.29%):** TSLA is a long-duration growth stock. Elevated rates compress its valuation multiple, making it structurally unattractive relative to bonds or value stocks.
- **Inflation reaccelerating (CPI +0.87% MoM, PPI +2.08% MoM):** This squeezes consumer purchasing power, which is critical for TSLA's vehicle demand. Higher financing costs (driven by elevated rates) also reduce affordability.
- **Risk-on regime not helping:** Despite a risk-on classification (score +3/6), TSLA has continued to decline. This suggests **sector-specific weakness** (consumer discretionary under pressure) is overwhelming the broader risk-on tailwind.

**Actionable Insight:** The macro environment provides no catalyst for a TSLA reversal. Even if the stock bounces on technical oversold conditions, the macro headwinds will cap any rally. This is a "sell the rip" environment, not a "buy the dip" setup.
