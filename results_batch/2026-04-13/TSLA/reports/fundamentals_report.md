# Tesla, Inc. (TSLA) Fundamental Analysis Report
**Report Date:** April 13, 2026

## Executive Summary

Tesla demonstrates significant fundamental challenges with deteriorating profitability, weakening margins, and substantial underperformance versus sector peers. The company trades at an extreme valuation (PE 327.6x) despite declining earnings trajectory and operational headwinds.

## Company Profile

- **Sector:** Consumer Cyclical
- **Industry:** Auto Manufacturers  
- **Market Cap:** $1.33 trillion
- **Beta:** 1.915 (high volatility)

## Valuation Analysis

Tesla's valuation metrics signal extreme overvaluation relative to current earnings power:

- **PE Ratio (TTM):** 327.62x vs Forward PE 129.48x
- **Price to Book:** 16.16x
- **EPS (TTM):** $1.08 (down significantly)
- **Forward EPS:** $2.73 (implies 153% growth needed to justify forward PE)

The 327x trailing PE is unsustainable for a mature auto manufacturer. Even the forward PE of 129x assumes aggressive earnings recovery that recent trends don't support.

- **Net Margin:** 3.4% (Q4 2025) vs 9.0% (Q4 2024) - **collapsed 62%**

## Conclusion

Tesla exhibits severe fundamental deterioration masked by brand strength and past momentum. The 64% YoY collapse in net income, combined with a 327x PE ratio, creates an untenable risk/reward profile. Persistent negative alpha versus sector peers, ongoing restructuring charges, and margin compression indicate structural competitive challenges. The company's 4.93% ROE fails to justify equity risk premium over 4.29% risk-free 10-year Treasuries.

**Recommendation Context:** This analysis provides evidence for significant fundamental weakness. Traders should weigh this against technical factors, but the fundamental case suggests elevated downside risk at current valuation levels.
