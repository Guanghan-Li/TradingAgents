## Segment Analysis: Tesla, Inc. (TSLA)

### Business Unit Overview

Tesla operates a concentrated business model with **~95% revenue from Automotive** (vehicle sales + leasing), supplemented by Energy Storage and Services. This concentration creates both scale advantages and cyclical vulnerability.

**Margin Trajectory:** Gross margins improved sequentially from 16.3% (Q1 2025) to 20.1% (Q4 2025), driven by manufacturing efficiency gains, pricing discipline, and favorable mix. However, TTM profit margin of 4.0% remains compressed versus historical peaks, reflecting aggressive R&D investment ($1.78B in Q4 2025, +39% YoY) for FSD, battery tech, and new platforms.

**Strategic Catalysts:**
- **FSD Monetization:** Dutch regulatory approval signals European expansion pathway for supervised autonomous driving—a high-margin software revenue stream currently underpenetrated
- **Volume Expansion:** Rumored "Model Q" sub-$30K vehicle could unlock mass-market TAM, though margin dilution and marketing execution risks noted by analysts
- **Energy Storage:** Battery/Megapack demand remains strong but lacks granular disclosure; likely <5% of revenue but growing faster than automotive

**Concentration Risk:** Single-product-line dependency (Model 3/Y dominate) exposes Tesla to EV demand cycles, competitive pressure from BYD/legacy OEMs, and geographic concentration (China manufacturing/sales critical).
