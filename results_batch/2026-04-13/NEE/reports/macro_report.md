## Macro Regime Assessment (as of 2026-04-13)

**Regime: Late-cycle normalization with inflation watch**

The Fed has paused its easing cycle at 3.64% after cutting ~45 bps from October 2025 through January 2026. The 2Y-10Y curve is positively sloped (+51 bps), signaling no imminent recession risk. However, **producer inflation is accelerating** (PPI +2.08% sequential), while consumer inflation remains elevated (CPI +0.87%). This divergence suggests input-cost pressures may not yet be fully passed through.

Labor markets remain tight (4.3% unemployment, +178k payrolls), supporting consumption but limiting the Fed's room to ease further if inflation re-accelerates. VIX at 19 reflects complacency—markets are pricing a soft landing.

**Policy path:** The Fed is likely on hold through mid-2026 unless growth weakens materially or inflation convincingly decelerates. The risk is **premature easing optimism** if PPI inflation feeds into core CPI.

**Cross-asset implications:**
- **Rates:** Front-end anchored near 3.65-3.70%; long-end vulnerable to term premium repricing if inflation persists
- **Equities:** Growth/duration-sensitive sectors (utilities, tech) benefit from stable rates but face headwinds if the Fed signals "higher for longer"
- **Credit:** Benign default environment, but refinancing risk rises for issuers with 2026-27 maturities
- **FX:** Dollar supported by relatively high real rates vs. peers

**Key risks:**
1. **Inflation re-acceleration** from PPI → CPI pass-through forces Fed hawkish pivot
2. **Curve inversion** if front-end reprices higher (recession signal)
3. **Exogenous shock** (geopolitical, fiscal) given low VIX/complacency
