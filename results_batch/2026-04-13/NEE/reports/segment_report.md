# NextEra Energy (NEE) Segment Analysis

## Business Unit Overview

NextEra Energy operates through two primary segments that drive distinct value propositions:

**Florida Power & Light (FPL)** – The regulated utility segment serving ~12 million people across Florida. This is NEE's stability anchor, generating predictable rate-base growth (~10% annually) through infrastructure investment and regulatory cost recovery. FPL benefits from Florida's population growth (+1.5% annually) and rising electricity demand from data centers and EV adoption.

**NextEra Energy Resources (NEER)** – The competitive renewables platform and the largest wind/solar generator in North America. NEER's backlog (~15 GW contracted) provides multi-year revenue visibility, though merchant exposure and equity method investments (wind/solar partnerships) introduce earnings volatility. Recent news highlights AI data center demand as a tailwind for contracted capacity.

## Segment Economics

**Margin Profile**: FPL delivers stable ~60% gross margins under cost-of-service regulation. NEER margins are higher (~70%+) but cyclical, tied to PPA pricing, renewable energy credit (REC) values, and merchant power prices.

**Capital Intensity**: Both segments are capital-heavy. FPL deploys ~$8B annually in T&D and generation hardening; NEER invests ~$6-8B in wind/solar/storage projects. The negative FCF (-$15.2B TTM) reflects this growth capex, funded by debt (146% D/E) and equity.

**Cyclicality**: FPL is non-cyclical (weather-adjusted demand is stable). NEER faces commodity exposure (natural gas prices impact merchant power) and policy risk (IRA tax credit extensions, interconnection delays).

## Key Value Drivers

1. **Regulated Rate Base Growth (FPL)** – Drives 6-8% annual EPS contribution. Florida PSC has approved grid modernization and storm hardening investments, supporting through 2028.

2. **Renewables Backlog Conversion (NEER)** – 15 GW pipeline at ~$20B capital value. Execution risk is low (80% of backlog is with investment-grade counterparties), but interconnection delays could push COD dates.

3. **AI/Data Center Load Growth** – News cites utilities leveraging managed EV charging; NEE is positioning for hyperscale data center contracts in Florida (FPL) and PJM/ERCOT (NEER). This could add 2-3% to long-term demand growth.

4. **IRA Tax Credit Monetization** – NEER benefits from production tax credits (PTCs) and investment tax credits (ITCs). Transferability provisions improve cash realization, but policy risk remains if administration changes.

## Concentration Risks

- **Geographic**: FPL is 100% Florida-exposed (hurricane risk, regulatory capture risk).
- **Counterparty**: NEER's top 10 offtakers represent ~60% of contracted capacity. Utility bankruptcies (low probability) would impair cash flows.
- **Policy**: 40% of NEER's value derives from federal tax incentives. Repeal or modification of IRA credits would compress returns.

## Investment Thesis

NEE trades at 27.9x TTM P/E (vs. utility sector avg ~18x), pricing in NEER's growth optionality. The 2.65% yield is below sector average, reflecting capital reinvestment priority. **Segment-level view**: FPL justifies a 20x multiple (regulated comps); NEER warrants 35x on backlog visibility and decarbonization tailwinds. Blended fair value implies **modest upside** (+5-10%) if NEER executes backlog and AI load materializes. Key risk: rising rates pressure both segments' NPV (duration ~15 years).

**Recommendation**: **HOLD** with bias to upgrade if Q1 2026 earnings (announced soon per news) show accelerated data center contracting or Florida load growth >3%.
