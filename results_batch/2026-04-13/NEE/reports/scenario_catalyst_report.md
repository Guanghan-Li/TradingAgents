## NextEra Energy (NEE) – Scenario & Catalyst Analysis

**Current Price Context:** NEE is trading near its 52-week high ($96.21), well above its 200-day average ($81.69), reflecting strong momentum in the regulated utility + renewables thesis.

---

### Bull Case (40% probability)
**Thesis:** NEE captures outsized AI data-center load growth, accelerates rate-base expansion at Florida Power & Light (FPL), and NextEra Energy Resources (NEER) benefits from renewed federal clean-energy incentives post-2026 election cycle. Regulated earnings visibility + renewables optionality command a premium multiple.

**Valuation Implication:** Forward P/E expands toward 23–24×, implying $100–105 per share by year-end 2026.

**Signposts:**
- Q1 2026 earnings (late April) show >10% YoY EPS growth and raise full-year guidance
- FPL files for incremental rate case citing AI/data-center infrastructure investment
- Federal policy clarity on IRA tax-credit extensions or new grid-modernization funding
- NEER signs multi-GW power-purchase agreements with hyperscalers (Microsoft, Google, Amazon)

---

### Base Case (45% probability)
**Thesis:** NEE delivers steady 6–8% earnings growth driven by regulated rate-base expansion, but NEER faces margin pressure from higher interest rates and commodity-price volatility. Valuation holds near current forward P/E of ~21×.

**Valuation Implication:** Price range $90–95, tracking in-line earnings and modest multiple compression if rates stay elevated.

**Signposts:**
- Q1 results meet consensus; guidance reaffirmed but not raised
- Fed holds rates steady through mid-2026 (per macro context: 3.64% effective rate)
- Renewables backlog grows modestly but no transformational deals announced
- Dividend growth continues at 10% annually, supporting income-investor demand

---

### Bear Case (15% probability)
**Thesis:** Regulatory headwinds in Florida (rate-case delays, storm-cost recovery disputes), rising debt-service costs (Debt/Equity 146×), and NEER project delays erode earnings visibility. Climate-transition skepticism post-election reduces renewables appetite.

**Valuation Implication:** Forward P/E compresses to 18–19×, implying $80–85 per share.

**Signposts:**
- Q1 miss on EPS or guidance cut
- Florida PSC denies or scales back FPL rate request
- NEER project cancellations or write-downs due to supply-chain or permitting issues
- Federal rollback of IRA incentives or grid-investment programs
- Free cash flow remains deeply negative (currently -$15.2B TTM), raising equity-dilution risk
