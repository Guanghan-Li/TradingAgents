## NEE Technical Analysis Report
**Date:** 2026-04-13 | **Ticker:** NEE | **Close:** $92.14

---

### Executive Summary

NEE is exhibiting **short-term momentum loss within a structurally bullish long-term trend**. Price has pulled back 2.1% from the April 9 high of $94.48 and now trades below the 10 EMA ($93.08), signaling near-term weakness. However, the stock remains firmly above both the 50 SMA ($91.63) and 200 SMA ($80.95), confirming the primary uptrend is intact. The RSI at 49.50 indicates neutral momentum with no extreme positioning, while the MACD at +0.68 (down from +0.82 on April 10) shows decelerating bullish momentum. Bollinger Bands are contracting (width: $5.28), suggesting reduced volatility and potential for a directional move. ATR at 1.88 reflects moderate volatility suitable for position sizing.

**Macro overlay:** In a risk-on regime with Fed on hold and PPI inflation accelerating, NEE faces a mixed backdrop. Stable long-term rates (10Y at 4.29%) support utility valuations, but rising input costs threaten margins. The defensive characteristics are attractive in late-cycle, but the stock's recent consolidation suggests the market is pricing in these cross-currents.

**Actionable insight:** NEE is at a tactical inflection point. A break below $91.63 (50 SMA) would signal deeper correction risk toward $89.66 (lower Bollinger Band). Conversely, a reclaim of $93.08 (10 EMA) with expanding volume would confirm resumption of the uptrend toward $94.94 (upper Bollinger Band). Current positioning favors **HOLD** with defined risk parameters.

### Detailed Technical Analysis

#### **Trend Structure: Bullish Long-Term, Consolidating Short-Term**

- **200 SMA ($80.95):** NEE trades 13.8% above the 200 SMA, confirming a robust long-term uptrend. This distance provides a substantial cushion and indicates the bull market remains structurally intact. No death cross risk.
  
- **50 SMA ($91.63):** Price is 0.6% above the 50 SMA, which has been rising steadily (from $89.50 on March 25 to $91.63 today). This medium-term average is acting as dynamic support. A break below would be the first warning sign of trend deterioration.
  
- **10 EMA ($93.08):** Price closed below the 10 EMA for the first time since April 2, signaling a loss of short-term momentum. The 10 EMA has flattened (from $93.29 on April 10 to $93.08 today), reflecting the recent consolidation. This is a tactical sell signal for momentum traders but not yet a structural concern.

**Key observation:** The moving averages are properly stacked (10 EMA > 50 SMA > 200 SMA), which is textbook bullish alignment. However, the 10 EMA is now above price, creating the first crack in the bullish structure. Watch for a potential "death cross" of the 10 EMA below the 50 SMA if weakness persists—this would occur around $91.50 and would be a stronger sell signal.

#### **Momentum: Decelerating but Not Broken**

- **MACD (+0.68):** The MACD remains positive, confirming the trend is still bullish. However, it has declined from +0.82 on April 10, indicating momentum is decelerating. The MACD peaked at +0.82 and has now rolled over, which often precedes a deeper pullback. Critically, the MACD has not yet crossed below its signal line (macds), so no formal sell signal has triggered. If the MACD crosses below zero, it would confirm a momentum shift to bearish.
  
- **RSI (49.50):** The RSI has dropped sharply from 63.14 on April 9 to 49.50 today, reflecting the recent selling pressure. However, it remains in neutral territory (30-70 range), meaning NEE is neither overbought nor oversold. This is actually constructive—it suggests the pullback is healthy profit-taking rather than panic selling. An RSI drop below 30 would signal oversold conditions and a potential bounce, while a move above 70 would indicate overbought risk.

**Key observation:** The momentum indicators are flashing yellow, not red. The MACD deceleration and RSI decline confirm short-term weakness, but neither has reached levels that would trigger a formal sell signal. This is a consolidation, not a reversal—yet.

#### **Volatility: Contracting Bands Signal Pending Breakout**

- **Bollinger Bands ($89.66 - $94.94):** NEE is trading in the middle of the Bollinger Bands, equidistant from both the upper and lower boundaries. The band width has contracted from $4.04 on March 25 to $5.28 today, indicating declining volatility. In technical analysis, Bollinger Band contractions ("squeezes") often precede sharp directional moves. The question is: which way?
  
  - **Upper band ($94.94):** Price tested this level on April 9 ($94.48 high) but failed to break out. A sustained move above $94.94 would signal a breakout and likely target the $96-97 zone.
  
  - **Lower band ($89.66):** This represents the first major support level. A break below would likely accelerate selling toward the 50 SMA ($91.63) and potentially the March 20 low of $89.50.

- **ATR (1.88):** The ATR has declined from 2.18 on March 25 to 1.88 today, confirming the contraction in volatility. This is consistent with the Bollinger Band squeeze. For risk management, a 2x ATR stop-loss would be $3.76 below entry, while a 1.5x ATR target would be $2.82 above entry.

**Key observation:** The volatility contraction is the most important technical development. NEE is coiling for a breakout, and the direction will likely be determined by whether it holds the 50 SMA ($91.63) or breaks the 10 EMA ($93.08). Traders should wait for the breakout rather than trying to anticipate it.

#### **Volume Analysis: Declining Participation**

Volume on April 13 was 3.90 million shares, well below the recent average of ~8 million. This low volume during the pullback suggests a lack of conviction from sellers, which is mildly bullish. However, it also means the consolidation could extend as the market waits for a catalyst. A breakout above $93.08 on volume >7 million would be a strong buy signal, while a breakdown below $91.63 on heavy volume would be a sell signal.

### Macro-Technical Integration

**Late-cycle dynamics favor defensives, but inflation is a headwind:**

- **Positive:** NEE's defensive characteristics (regulated utility, stable cash flows, 2.7% dividend yield) are attractive in a late-cycle environment where growth is slowing but recession risk remains low. The risk-on regime (+3/6 score) supports continued equity exposure, and NEE's 13.8% gain above the 200 SMA reflects this.
  
- **Negative:** PPI inflation at +2.08% sequential is a direct threat to NEE's margins. As a capital-intensive utility with significant renewable energy projects, NEE faces rising costs for equipment, construction, and materials. The Fed's pause at 3.64% means no near-term relief from financing costs. This macro headwind likely explains why NEE failed to break out above $94.94 despite the risk-on regime.

**Sector rotation:** The macro regime report notes "cyclicals leading defensives," which is a headwind for utilities. In a true risk-on environment, capital typically flows out of defensives like NEE and into cyclicals (industrials, financials, discretionary). This rotation pressure may be capping NEE's upside.

**Rate sensitivity:** The 10-year yield at 4.29% is stable but elevated. Utilities are negatively correlated with long-term rates (higher rates = lower utility valuations). If the 10Y breaks above 4.50%, NEE could face additional selling pressure. Conversely, if rates decline toward 4.00%, NEE would likely rally.
