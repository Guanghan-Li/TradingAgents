**Macro Regime: Mid-Cycle Expansion with Inflation Resurgence**

The Fed paused its easing cycle at 3.64% after cutting 45bps from October through December 2025. Since January 2026, the policy rate has been unchanged—a clear signal that the inflation rebound has halted further accommodation.

**Inflation is re-accelerating sharply**: CPI rose 0.87% month-over-month in March (10%+ annualized), and PPI surged 2.08% in February. This is not a soft landing—it's a reflation episode. The labor market remains tight (unemployment fell to 4.30%, payrolls +178k), supporting wage pressure and demand-side inflation.

**The yield curve is normalizing**: the 2Y-10Y spread is +51bps, upward sloping, with no inversion or recession signal. However, the 10Y at 4.29% reflects market concern that the Fed may need to tighten again if inflation persists. The front end (3.66-3.71%) is anchored near the current policy rate, but the long end is pricing in either higher terminal rates or a risk premium.

**Policy path**: The Fed is likely done cutting. If CPI and PPI remain elevated through Q2, the next move could be a hike, not a cut. The market is not pricing this yet (VIX at 19.23 suggests complacency). Cross-asset implications: rates higher, equities face multiple compression, credit spreads may widen, and commodities benefit from the inflation backdrop.

**Key risks for other analysts**:
1. **Policy error**: Fed may need to hike into a slowing economy if inflation doesn't cool.
2. **Curve bear-steepening**: Long-end yields rise faster than short-end, tightening financial conditions.
3. **Stagflation lite**: Growth decelerates but inflation stays sticky—worst case for risk assets.
4. **Labor market inflection**: If payrolls weaken while inflation is high, the Fed loses its dual-mandate cushion.
