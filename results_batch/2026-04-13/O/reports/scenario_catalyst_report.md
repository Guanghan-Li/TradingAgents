# Scenario & Catalyst Analysis: Realty Income (O)
**Analysis Date:** 2026-04-13

## Executive Summary

Realty Income trades at a crossroads: elevated rates pressure REIT valuations while a documented "Great Rotation" into dividend stocks provides technical support. The 5.07% yield offers a 78bp premium over the 10Y Treasury, but the 73.5 debt-to-equity ratio and 54x TTM PE signal stretched fundamentals. Q1 earnings (late April) will test whether occupancy and same-store NOI can justify the premium multiple.

---

## Scenario Framework

### **Bull Case (30% probability)**
**Thesis:** Fed pivots dovish by mid-2026; 10Y yields compress to 3.75-3.90%. Retail occupancy remains above 98%, same-store rent growth accelerates to 2.5%+, and the dividend rotation sustains premium multiples. O re-rates to 40x forward PE on scarcity value.

**Valuation Implication:** $72-76/share (15-20% upside)

**Signposts:**
- Q1 occupancy ≥98.5%, same-store NOI growth ≥2%
- Fed signals rate cuts at June FOMC
- 10Y Treasury falls below 4.0%
- Acquisition pipeline >$2B at sub-7% cap rates

---

### **Base Case (50% probability)**
**Thesis:** Rates remain range-bound (10Y: 4.1-4.5%) through 2026. O maintains 98% occupancy and 1.5-2.0% rent growth but faces margin compression from higher refinancing costs. Dividend coverage stays adequate (80-85% AFFO payout). Stock trades sideways in $60-66 range.

**Valuation Implication:** $62-65/share (flat to +3%)

**Signposts:**
- Q1 AFFO/share in-line with consensus ($0.44-0.45)
- Debt refinancing at 5.5-6.0% (vs. legacy 4.2%)
- Occupancy 97.5-98.5%
- Dividend maintained but no increase

---

### **Bear Case (20% probability)**
**Thesis:** Retail tenant stress emerges (bankruptcies, lease restructurings). 10Y yields spike above 4.75% on inflation resurgence or fiscal concerns. O's high leverage forces asset sales or dividend cut. Forward PE compresses to 28-30x on REIT sector de-rating.

**Valuation Implication:** $48-52/share (-20 to -25%)

**Signposts:**
- Q1 occupancy drops below 97%
- Tenant watch-list >5% of ABR
- 10Y Treasury >4.75%
- Dividend coverage falls below 75% AFFO
- Credit rating downgrade (currently BBB+)
