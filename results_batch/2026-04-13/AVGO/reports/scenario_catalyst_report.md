# Broadcom (AVGO) Scenario & Catalyst Analysis
**As of 2026-04-13**

---

## Executive Summary

Broadcom sits at the intersection of AI infrastructure buildout and semiconductor cycle recovery. The company's custom AI chip business (hyperscaler ASICs) and networking portfolio position it as a direct beneficiary of accelerating AI capex, while its diversified exposure (enterprise software, broadband, wireless) provides downside cushion. Recent momentum is strong—analyst upgrades, sector leadership, and forward PE compression to 21× (vs. TTM 73×) signal earnings inflection. Key risks: macro slowdown crimping enterprise IT budgets, geopolitical supply-chain disruption, and hyperscaler concentration risk.

---

## Scenario Framework

### **Bull Case (35% probability)**
**Thesis:** AI infrastructure spending accelerates through 2026–27; Broadcom captures outsized share via custom silicon wins (Google TPU, Meta MTIA successors) and 800G/1.6T networking ramps. VMware integration delivers $3B+ synergies ahead of schedule. Semiconductor cycle turns decisively positive in H2 2026.

**Valuation Implication:** 25–28× forward PE on $20+ FY27 EPS → $500–560 price target.

**Signposts to watch:**
- Hyperscaler capex guidance raises (GOOGL, META, MSFT earnings)
- AI revenue run-rate exceeds $15B annually (next earnings call)
- Gross margin expansion above 77% (VMware software mix)
- 800G Ethernet design wins at 3+ hyperscalers

---

### **Base Case (50% probability)**
**Thesis:** AI spending remains robust but growth moderates from 2025 peaks. Broadcom sustains $12–14B AI revenue with steady but unspectacular growth. Enterprise IT budgets stabilize (no recession, no boom). Semiconductor inventory digestion extends into Q3 2026. VMware delivers in-line synergies.

**Valuation Implication:** 21–23× forward PE on $18 FY27 EPS → $380–415 range.

**Signposts to watch:**
- AI revenue growth decelerates to 15–20% Y/Y
- Enterprise networking/broadband revenue flat to +5%
- Inventory days remain elevated (>90 days)
- Peer multiples (NVDA, AVGO, MRVL) compress in tandem

---

### **Bear Case (15% probability)**
**Thesis:** Macro downturn (recession, Fed policy error, geopolitical shock) triggers enterprise IT freeze and hyperscaler capex cuts. Custom AI chip commoditization accelerates (in-house alternatives, TSMC direct engagement). VMware integration stumbles (customer churn, execution delays). Semiconductor downcycle deepens.

**Valuation Implication:** 15–18× forward PE on $15 FY27 EPS → $225–270 downside.

**Signposts to watch:**
- Hyperscaler capex guidance cuts >10%
- AI revenue growth turns negative Q/Q
- VMware revenue retention <90%
- Gross margin compression below 75%
- Debt/Equity above 90 (integration stress)

## Thesis Summary

Broadcom's **base case** reflects a "Goldilocks" AI infrastructure environment: strong but decelerating growth, stable enterprise demand, and gradual semiconductor recovery. The **bull case** hinges on AI capex re-acceleration and flawless VMware execution—plausible but not yet fully de-risked. The **bear case** requires a macro shock or structural AI spending reversal—low probability near-term but tail risk remains given geopolitical uncertainty and elevated valuations across semis.

**Next 90 days:** Watch hyperscaler earnings (late April), AVGO's June earnings, and FOMC June meeting for directional clarity. Current forward PE of 21× prices in the base case; bull case requires multiple expansion or EPS beats.
