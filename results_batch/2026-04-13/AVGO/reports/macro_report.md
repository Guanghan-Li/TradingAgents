## Macroeconomic Analysis for AVGO (as of 2026-04-13)

### Regime Identification
**Mid-cycle expansion with inflation watch.** The economy is growing modestly (payrolls +178k, unemployment 4.3%), the Fed has paused after 6 months of cuts (now at 3.64%), and the yield curve is positively sloped (+51bp 2s10s). No recession signal. However, producer prices accelerated sharply (+2.08% MoM), while consumer inflation remains elevated (+0.87% MoM) — this creates a risk that the Fed's easing cycle is over.

### Policy Path
The Fed cut 45bp from October 2025 to January 2026, then held steady for three months. With PPI spiking and CPI sticky, the next move is more likely a hike than a cut if inflation does not cool. The market is pricing a neutral-to-slightly-restrictive stance (3.64% funds rate vs. ~4.3% 10Y yield).

### Cross-Asset Implications for AVGO
- **Rates:** The 10Y at 4.29% is not prohibitive for tech capex, but any further steepening (inflation-driven) would pressure long-duration equity multiples. Broadcom's infrastructure software and AI exposure benefits from stable-to-lower rates; a reversal would be a headwind.
- **Growth:** Solid labor market and no yield-curve inversion support enterprise IT spending and data-center buildouts — both core to AVGO's revenue.
- **Inflation:** PPI acceleration is a margin risk for hardware (semiconductors), though AVGO's software mix (post-VMware) offers some insulation. Input cost pressure is real.
- **Volatility:** VIX at 19 suggests complacency. Any Fed hawkish pivot or inflation surprise could spike vol and hit high-beta tech names.

### Concrete Risks
1. **Inflation re-acceleration forces Fed hike** → rate-sensitive tech multiples compress.
2. **PPI pass-through squeezes chip margins** → hardware segment under pressure.
3. **Curve bear-steepening** (long-end sells off) → duration risk for AVGO's valuation.
4. **Macro stability supports AI capex** → positive for data-center and networking revenue.
