# VICI Properties Inc. – Segment Analysis

## Business Model & Segment Overview

VICI Properties operates as an **experiential real estate REIT** with near-total concentration in **gaming and entertainment properties** under long-term triple-net leases. The company's portfolio is anchored by marquee casino assets leased to investment-grade operators, primarily Caesars Entertainment and MGM Resorts. Recent news highlights ongoing **tenant diversification efforts** (Alberta casino transaction) and an **eighth consecutive annual dividend increase**, signaling management confidence in cash flow durability.

## Segment Economics

**Revenue Composition:** ~98% from gaming property leases, with minimal exposure to non-gaming experiential real estate. The triple-net lease structure pushes operating expenses to tenants, yielding an **80%+ operating margin** and **69% net margin**—exceptional even for REITs.

**Margin Profile:** Quarterly revenue has been remarkably stable ($976M–$1,013M across recent quarters), with EBITDA margins consistently above 80%. The income statement shows minimal cost of revenue ($6–7M/quarter) and controlled G&A ($14–20M/quarter). Provision for doubtful accounts spiked in Q4 2025 ($153M) but reversed in Q2 2025 (-$142M), likely reflecting lease modification accounting rather than credit deterioration.

**Capital Intensity & Cyclicality:** Low maintenance capex (landlord responsibilities are minimal under triple-net leases). Free cash flow of $1.47B (TTM) supports the 6.34% dividend yield. Gaming exposure introduces **moderate cyclicality** tied to consumer discretionary spending, but long-term lease contracts (10–15 year initial terms with escalators) provide downside protection.

## Concentration Risks

- **Tenant Concentration:** Caesars and MGM likely represent >70% of revenue (typical for gaming REITs). Credit quality is strong (both investment-grade), but a single tenant distress event would materially impact cash flows.
- **Sector Concentration:** Near-total gaming exposure means VICI's performance is tied to regional gaming trends, regulatory shifts, and consumer leisure spending.
- **Geographic Concentration:** U.S.-centric portfolio with recent expansion into Canada (Alberta transaction). Limited international diversification.
