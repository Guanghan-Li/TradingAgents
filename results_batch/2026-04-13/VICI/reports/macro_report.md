**Yield Curve**: The 2Y-10Y spread is +51bp, a normal upward slope consistent with growth expectations and no imminent recession signal. However, the 10Y yield at 4.29% and 30Y at 4.90% reflect elevated term premia and inflation risk premia.

## Cross-Asset Implications for VICI (REIT)

- **Interest Rate Sensitivity**: REITs are negatively correlated with long-term rates. The 10Y at 4.29% and a Fed on hold create headwinds for REIT valuations. If inflation forces the Fed to hike, REITs face further pressure.
- **Growth Tailwind**: Solid employment and GDP support commercial real estate fundamentals (occupancy, rent growth), a modest positive for VICI's triple-net lease model.
- **Inflation Pass-Through**: VICI's leases often have CPI escalators. Sticky inflation may support rent growth, but higher rates offset this benefit.
- **Credit Spreads**: With the VIX data unavailable, we lack a direct volatility read, but a stable labor market and no yield curve inversion suggest credit conditions remain supportive.

## Key Risks

1. **Inflation Re-Acceleration**: PPI at +2.08% MoM is a red flag. If this feeds into CPI, the Fed may resume tightening, pressuring rate-sensitive REITs.
2. **Fed Policy Error**: If the Fed holds too long and inflation entrenches, a sharp hike cycle could follow, compressing REIT multiples.
3. **Term Premium Expansion**: Long-end yields (30Y at 4.90%) reflect inflation uncertainty. Further steepening would hurt REIT valuations.
4. **Data Gaps**: Missing ISM PMI and Consumer Confidence limit our view of forward momentum and sentiment.
