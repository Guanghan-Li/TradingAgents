# VICI Technical Analysis Report
**Date:** April 13, 2026 | **Current Price:** $28.15

## Executive Summary

VICI is exhibiting early signs of a technical recovery after a sharp March selloff, but the rebound lacks conviction. Price has crossed above the 10 EMA and RSI has normalized from oversold levels, yet the stock remains below both the 50 and 200 SMAs. The macro backdrop is mixed: a RISK-ON regime supports risk assets, but elevated long-term rates (10Y at 4.29%) and sticky inflation create persistent headwinds for rate-sensitive REITs. The technical setup suggests a consolidation phase with resistance at the 50 SMA ($28.35).

---

## Price Action & Trend Analysis

**Recent Performance:**
- VICI peaked at $29.87 on March 2, then sold off sharply to $26.61 by March 27 (-10.9% decline)
- Current price of $28.15 represents a +5.8% recovery from the March low
- The stock paid a $0.45 dividend on March 19, which accounts for part of the price gap

**Trend Structure:**
- **Short-term (10 EMA: $27.81):** Price just crossed above the 10 EMA on April 9-10, signaling a potential shift in near-term momentum. This is the first bullish crossover since the March decline.
- **Medium-term (50 SMA: $28.35):** Price is approaching this key resistance level from below. The 50 SMA has been declining steadily (from $28.43 on March 25 to $28.35 today), indicating weakening medium-term trend strength.
- **Long-term (200 SMA: $29.51):** Price remains 4.6% below this benchmark, confirming the longer-term downtrend that began in late February. The 200 SMA is also declining, a bearish structural signal.

**Key Observation:** The price is sandwiched between the 10 EMA (support) and 50 SMA (resistance), defining a narrow consolidation zone. A breakout above $28.35 with volume would confirm bullish continuation; failure to reclaim this level risks a retest of the March lows.

## Macro-Technical Integration

**Macro Headwinds:**
- The 10Y Treasury yield at 4.29% remains elevated, compressing REIT valuations via higher cap rates
- The Fed is on hold at 3.64%, with no near-term easing expected given sticky inflation (CPI +0.87% MoM, PPI +2.08% MoM)
- If PPI inflation feeds through to CPI, the Fed may pivot hawkish, further pressuring rate-sensitive sectors

**Macro Tailwinds:**
- The RISK-ON regime (score +3/6) supports cyclicals and growth assets, which could lift VICI if broader equity momentum continues
- Solid employment (payrolls +178k, unemployment 4.30%) supports commercial real estate fundamentals, benefiting VICI's triple-net lease model
- VICI's leases often include CPI escalators, so sticky inflation may support rent growth over time

**Net Assessment:** The macro environment is a tug-of-war. RISK-ON sentiment and strong fundamentals are positives, but elevated rates and Fed policy uncertainty are negatives. The technical setup reflects this ambiguity—price is recovering but lacks conviction.

## Recommendation: **HOLD**

**Rationale:**
VICI is in a transitional phase. The technical indicators (MACD turning up, RSI normalized, price above 10 EMA) suggest the worst of the March selloff is over, but the recovery is not yet confirmed. Key concerns include:
1. **Weak volume** on the rebound, indicating lack of buyer conviction
2. **Resistance at the 50 SMA** ($28.35), which has capped prior rallies
3. **Macro headwinds** from elevated long-term rates and Fed policy uncertainty

**For existing holders:** Maintain positions but set a stop-loss at $27.80 (below 10 EMA) to protect against a failed recovery. If price clears $28.35 with volume, consider adding exposure.

**For new buyers:** Wait for confirmation. A close above $28.35 on volume >7M shares would be a clearer entry signal. Alternatively, a pullback to $27.55 (Bollinger middle) could offer a better risk/reward entry.

**Timeframe:** This is a 2-4 week tactical view. Longer-term investors should monitor the Fed's next move and whether VICI can reclaim the 200 SMA ($29.51).
