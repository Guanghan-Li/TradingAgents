**Macro Regime Assessment:**

The data reveals a **late-cycle, moderating inflation** environment with the Fed in a cautious easing phase:

1. **Inflation backdrop**: CPI at 330.29 (+0.87% MoM) shows sticky but decelerating consumer inflation. However, PPI surged +2.08% MoM to 267.85, signaling potential pipeline pressures that could reignite headline inflation or squeeze margins for companies unable to pass through costs.

2. **Fed policy path**: The effective Fed Funds rate has declined from 4.09% (Oct 2025) to 3.64% (Mar 2026), a cumulative 45bp easing. The Fed appears to be normalizing policy toward neutral, but the pace has slowed (unchanged Feb-Mar), suggesting data-dependency and concern about re-accelerating inflation (note the PPI spike).

3. **Yield curve**: The 2Y-10Y spread is +51bp, a normal upward slope consistent with growth expectations and no imminent recession signal. However, the curve is relatively flat by historical standards, and the 10Y at 4.29% reflects elevated term premium—likely due to fiscal concerns or inflation risk premium.

4. **Growth and labor**: Critical data (unemployment, payrolls, ISM PMI, VIX) failed to load, limiting visibility. GDP at $31.4T (Q4 2025) provides a baseline but no sequential growth rate. The absence of labor and sentiment data is a significant blind spot.

**Cross-Asset Implications:**

- **Equities**: Defensive sectors (healthcare, staples) benefit from stable-to-lower rates and late-cycle positioning. JNJ, as a healthcare mega-cap, should see valuation support from the 10Y yield stabilizing near 4.3%. However, PPI pressure is a margin risk for all corporates.
  
- **Rates**: The Fed's pause (Feb-Mar) and upward-sloping curve suggest the easing cycle may be nearing its end. If PPI feeds into CPI, the next move could be a hold or even a hike, which would pressure duration assets.

- **Credit**: Stable Fed policy and no recession signal support investment-grade spreads. JNJ's AAA-equivalent credit profile is a safe haven in this environment.

- **FX/Commodities**: Without VIX or broader risk sentiment, hard to assess risk-on/risk-off. The PPI spike could reflect commodity input costs, a headwind for margin-sensitive sectors.

**Key Risks:**

- **Inflation re-acceleration**: The PPI surge is a red flag. If it passes through to CPI, the Fed may need to tighten, inverting the curve and pressuring equities.
- **Data gaps**: Missing unemployment, payrolls, and PMI data obscure the labor market and manufacturing health—critical for assessing recession risk.
- **Fiscal/term premium**: The 10Y at 4.29% with a flat curve suggests elevated term premium, possibly from fiscal deficits or geopolitical risk. This could cap equity multiples.

**JNJ-Specific Macro View:**

JNJ is a **defensive, rate-sensitive mega-cap**. The current macro regime is **moderately supportive**:
- Lower rates (3.64% Fed Funds, down from 4.09%) reduce discount rates, supporting valuation.
- Stable growth expectations (upward curve) favor steady earnings.
- Healthcare demand is non-cyclical, insulating JNJ from growth slowdowns.
- **Risk**: PPI pressure could squeeze margins if input costs rise faster than pricing power allows. Monitor pharmaceutical and medical device input costs.
