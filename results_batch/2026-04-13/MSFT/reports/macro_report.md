**Macro Regime: Mid-Cycle Expansion with Inflation Watch**

The current macro backdrop supports risk assets, including large-cap tech like MSFT, but with caveats around inflation persistence.

**Growth & Labor:** The labor market remains healthy—unemployment fell to 4.30% in March with payrolls adding 178k jobs. This is consistent with above-trend growth and sustained consumer spending power, which underpins enterprise IT budgets and cloud adoption (core MSFT revenue drivers).

**Inflation & Policy:** The Fed has paused at 3.64% after cutting 45bps from October through January. CPI rose 0.87% sequentially, but PPI surged 2.08%, signaling potential pipeline pressure. If producer inflation flows through to services and wages, the Fed may hold longer than markets expect. For MSFT, this matters: higher-for-longer rates compress valuation multiples for long-duration growth stocks, though strong earnings can offset.

**Yield Curve & Recession Risk:** The 2Y-10Y spread is +0.51%, a normal upward slope. No inversion, no imminent recession signal. The 10Y at 4.29% is elevated but stable. This environment favors equities over bonds and supports corporate capex (AI infrastructure spend benefits MSFT Azure).

**Cross-Asset Implications:** VIX at 19.23 reflects contained volatility and risk-on sentiment. A normal curve + low vol + Fed pause = constructive for equities, especially mega-cap tech with pricing power.

**Concrete Risks:**
1. **PPI-to-CPI pass-through:** If producer inflation accelerates into consumer prices, the Fed may hike, not cut. Watch March/April CPI prints.
2. **Duration risk:** At 4.29%, the 10Y yield is a headwind for high-multiple stocks. Any backup toward 4.5%+ would pressure MSFT's valuation.
3. **Policy error:** If the Fed holds too long and growth slows, tech spending could decelerate faster than expected.

**Bottom Line for MSFT:** The macro setup is favorable—growth is solid, the Fed is accommodative, and there's no recession on the horizon. However, inflation persistence and elevated long-end yields are risks to monitor. MSFT's pricing power and secular AI tailwinds should insulate it, but valuation is rate-sensitive.
