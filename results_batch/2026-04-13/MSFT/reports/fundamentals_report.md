# Microsoft Corporation (MSFT) Fundamental Analysis Report
**Report Date:** April 13, 2026

## Executive Summary

Microsoft Corporation demonstrates robust fundamental strength despite recent underperformance relative to sector peers. The company maintains exceptional profitability metrics with a 39% profit margin and strong cash generation, though capital expenditure has surged dramatically in recent quarters, reflecting aggressive AI infrastructure investment.

## Company Profile

- **Sector:** Technology
- **Industry:** Software - Infrastructure
- **Market Capitalization:** $2.84 trillion
- **Current Valuation:** PE Ratio 23.89x (TTM), Forward PE 20.19x

## Financial Performance Analysis

### Revenue & Profitability Trends

Microsoft's quarterly revenue shows consistent growth trajectory:
- Q4 FY2025 (Dec 2025): $81.3B
- Q3 FY2025 (Sep 2025): $77.7B
- Q2 FY2025 (Jun 2025): $76.4B
- Q1 FY2025 (Mar 2025): $70.1B

**Key Insight:** Sequential revenue growth of 4.6% Q3→Q4 demonstrates accelerating momentum. TTM revenue of $305.5B with gross profit of $209.5B yields a healthy 68.6% gross margin.

Operating income remains strong at $38.3B (Q4), representing a 47.1% operating margin - best-in-class for enterprise software.

### Earnings Quality

- **Net Income (Q4 2025):** $38.5B, up 59.5% YoY from $24.1B
- **Diluted EPS:** $5.16 (Q4), showing strong progression from $3.23 (Q4 2024)
- **Normalized EBITDA:** $57.9B (Q4), indicating robust operational cash generation

The company's tax rate has remained stable around 19-20%, with effective tax management supporting bottom-line growth.

### Balance Sheet Strength

**Assets & Equity (Dec 31, 2025):**
- Total Assets: $665.3B
- Stockholders' Equity: $390.9B
- Book Value per Share: $52.62

**Liquidity Position:**
- Cash & Equivalents: $24.3B
- Short-term Investments: $65.2B
- Total Liquid Assets: $89.5B
- Current Ratio: 1.39x (healthy but declining from 1.43x in Q2)

**Debt Profile:**
- Total Debt: $57.6B
- Net Debt: $16.0B (minimal given cash position)
- Debt-to-Equity: 31.5% (conservative leverage)

**Critical Observation:** Working capital decreased to $50.2B from $54.1B sequentially, driven by increased current liabilities related to deferred revenue ($51.4B), reflecting strong advance bookings.

### Cash Flow Dynamics

**Operating Cash Flow:** $35.8B (Q4 2025), down from $45.1B (Q3) due to working capital timing
**Free Cash Flow:** $5.9B (Q4), dramatically compressed from $25.7B (Q3)

**Major Red Flag:** Capital expenditure exploded to $29.9B in Q4 2025, nearly double the $15.8B spent in Q4 2024. This represents the highest quarterly CapEx in company history and reflects massive AI datacenter buildout.

**Cash Deployment:**
- Dividends: $6.8B (Q4)
- Share Buybacks: $7.4B (Q4)
- Total shareholder returns: $14.2B despite compressed FCF

### Asset Composition & Investment Strategy

**Property, Plant & Equipment:** Net PPE surged to $286.2B (from $189.7B YoY), with gross PPE of $391.2B. The $104.9B accumulated depreciation suggests relatively young asset base being aggressively expanded.

**Intangibles:** Goodwill ($119.6B) and other intangibles ($20.3B) remain stable, indicating organic growth focus rather than M&A.

## Relative Performance Context

Microsoft significantly underperformed its peer group and sector benchmark:

**3-Month Performance:**
- MSFT: -20.17%
- Sector ETF (XLK): -0.93%
- Alpha: -19.24 percentage points

**Peer Comparison (3M):**
- AMD: +21.13%
- AVGO: +9.51%
- NVDA: +1.80%
- MSFT ranks 7th out of 8 peers

**Interpretation:** The market is pricing in concern over Microsoft's aggressive CapEx cycle and uncertain AI monetization timeline, despite strong fundamental execution.

## Macro Environment Assessment

**Supportive Factors:**
- Fed Funds Rate: 3.64% (stable, accommodative stance)
- Unemployment: 4.3% (healthy labor market)
- VIX: 19.23 (moderate volatility)
- Yield Curve: Normal upward slope (2Y-10Y spread +0.51pp)

**Headwinds:**
- PPI up 2.08% (input cost pressure)
- CPI at 330.29, up 0.87% (persistent inflation)

The macro backdrop remains constructive for technology investment, though inflation persistence may limit multiple expansion.

## Investment Considerations

**Strengths:**
1. Exceptional profitability (39% net margin, 47% operating margin)
2. Strong balance sheet with minimal net leverage
3. Consistent revenue growth across cloud, productivity, and gaming segments
4. $140B+ intangible asset base provides competitive moat
5. Return on Equity of 34.4% demonstrates capital efficiency

**Concerns:**
1. CapEx surge to $29.9B/quarter raises questions about capital discipline and ROI timeline
2. Free cash flow compression from $25.7B to $5.9B quarter-over-quarter
3. Significant underperformance vs. peers suggests market skepticism
4. Forward PE of 20.19x implies modest growth expectations despite AI investments

**Actionable Insight:** Microsoft is executing a massive infrastructure bet on AI leadership. The fundamental business remains exceptionally strong, but near-term FCF will be constrained by unprecedented CapEx. Investors must weigh the company's execution track record against the risk that AI monetization lags investment by 12-24 months.

**FINAL TRANSACTION PROPOSAL: HOLD**

**Rationale:** Microsoft's fundamentals remain exceptionally strong with 47% operating margins, 34% ROE, and accelerating revenue growth. However, the unprecedented $30B quarterly CapEx run-rate and resulting FCF compression create near-term uncertainty around AI investment returns. The 20% underperformance vs. sector suggests the market is already pricing in execution risk. Current valuation at 20x forward earnings appears reasonable for a business of this quality. Recommend HOLD pending evidence of AI monetization inflection or CapEx moderation in coming quarters.
