## Macroeconomic Analysis – April 13, 2026

**Regime: Late-Cycle Easing with Sticky Inflation**

The Fed has delivered 45 bps of cuts since October 2025, pausing at 3.64% for the past three months. This signals a cautious easing cycle—policy is moving toward neutral but not aggressively. The 2Y-10Y spread of 50 bps is flat but not inverted, suggesting growth expectations are muted but not recessionary. VIX at 19 confirms low stress.

**Inflation remains the binding constraint.** CPI rose 0.87% month-over-month in March, and PPI surged 2.08%—producer-level pressures are building and will likely pass through to consumers. The Fed's pause reflects this: they've stopped cutting because inflation is not cooperating. Unemployment at 4.3% and payrolls adding 178k show the labor market is solid, giving the Fed no urgency to ease further.

**Policy path:** The Fed is on hold. No cuts are priced in until inflation cools materially. If PPI continues to run hot, the next move could be a hike, not a cut. The curve's flatness reflects this uncertainty—markets are not pricing a dovish pivot.

**Cross-asset implications:**
- **Bonds (BND):** Duration risk is elevated. If the Fed hikes or holds longer than expected, the long end will sell off. The 10Y at 4.31% is vulnerable to repricing higher if inflation persists. BND, as a broad investment-grade fund, will underperform in a rising-rate environment.
- **Equities:** Sticky inflation + no Fed support = multiple compression risk. Growth stocks are most vulnerable.
- **Credit:** Spreads are tight (VIX 19), but if the Fed tightens into slowing growth, credit could widen.

**Key risks:**
1. PPI acceleration forces Fed to hike → curve bear-steepens → BND sells off.
2. Growth slows faster than inflation → stagflation lite → bad for both stocks and bonds.
3. Labor market cracks (unemployment spikes) → Fed cuts aggressively → curve bull-steepens → BND rallies.
