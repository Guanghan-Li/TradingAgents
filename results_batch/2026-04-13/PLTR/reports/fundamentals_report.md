# Comprehensive Fundamental Analysis Report: Palantir Technologies Inc. (PLTR)
**Report Date:** April 13, 2026  
**Sector:** Technology - Software Infrastructure  
**Analysis Period:** Q4 2024 through Q4 2025

## Executive Summary

Palantir demonstrates exceptional fundamental transformation with revenue growth accelerating to 70% YoY and profitability surging 670% YoY in Q4 2025. However, the stock faces severe valuation compression, declining 27.96% over six months while significantly underperforming the Technology sector ETF (XLK) by 27.81%. The disconnect between strong fundamentals and weak price action suggests market repricing of growth expectations.

## Financial Performance Analysis

### Revenue Growth Trajectory
Palantir's revenue acceleration is remarkable across all quarters of 2025:
- **Q4 2025:** $1,406.8M (+70.0% YoY from $827.5M)
- **Q3 2025:** $1,181.1M (+42.7% YoY from $827.5M baseline)
- **Q2 2025:** $1,003.7M 
- **Q1 2025:** $883.9M

Sequential quarterly growth remained strong: Q4 grew 19.1% from Q3, indicating sustained momentum rather than one-time spikes. TTM revenue of $4.48B represents a fundamental inflection in the business model.

### Profitability Breakthrough
The company achieved a dramatic profitability inflection in 2025:

**Operating Metrics (Q4 2025 vs Q4 2024):**
- Operating Income: $575.4M vs $11.0M (+5,123%)
- Operating Margin: 40.9% vs 1.3% (expansion of 3,960 bps)
- Net Income: $608.7M vs $79.0M (+670%)
- Profit Margin: 36.3% vs 9.5%

**Gross Margin Expansion:**
- Q4 2025: 84.6% ($1,190.8M gross profit on $1,406.8M revenue)
- Q4 2024: 78.9% ($653.0M gross profit on $827.5M revenue)
- Improvement of 570 bps demonstrates operating leverage and pricing power

The company's EBITDA of $1.44B (TTM) with EBITDA margin approaching 32% signals sustainable profitability at scale.

### Cash Flow Generation
Free cash flow generation validates earnings quality:
- **Q4 2025 FCF:** $764.0M (54% FCF margin)
- **Q4 2024 FCF:** $457.2M (55% FCF margin)
- **TTM FCF:** $1.26B

Operating cash flow of $777.3M in Q4 2025 (+68.8% YoY) exceeded net income, indicating high-quality earnings with minimal working capital drag. The company generated $2.13B in operating cash flow over the trailing four quarters.

## Balance Sheet Strength

### Liquidity Position
Palantir maintains fortress-like liquidity:
- **Cash & Short-term Investments:** $7.18B (as of Q4 2025)
- **Current Ratio:** 7.11x (exceptional)
- **Working Capital:** $7.18B
- **No meaningful debt:** Only $229M in capital lease obligations

The company holds $5.75B in short-term investments beyond $1.42B in cash, providing strategic flexibility for M&A, buybacks, or organic investment.

### Capital Structure
- **Total Equity:** $7.39B (up 47.6% from $5.00B in Q4 2024)
- **Debt-to-Equity:** 3.063 (misleading—primarily capital leases, not traditional debt)
- **Book Value per Share:** $3.09
- **Tangible Book Value:** $7.39B (no goodwill impairment risk)

The equity base expanded $2.38B in one year, driven by retained earnings ($608.7M in Q4 alone) and stock-based compensation ($196.4M in Q4).

## Operational Efficiency Analysis

### Expense Management
Despite 70% revenue growth, operating expenses grew at a controlled rate:

**Q4 2025 Operating Expenses:** $615.4M
- Selling & Marketing: $302.1M (21.5% of revenue)
- R&D: $144.2M (10.2% of revenue)
- G&A: $169.8M (12.1% of revenue)

**Q4 2024 Operating Expenses:** $641.9M
- Operating expenses actually *declined* 4.1% YoY while revenue grew 70%
- This demonstrates exceptional operating leverage

### Stock-Based Compensation
SBC remains elevated but declining as % of revenue:
- Q4 2025: $196.4M (14.0% of revenue)
- Q4 2024: $281.8M (34.0% of revenue)
- Improvement of 2,000 bps as % of revenue

While SBC dilutes shareholders, the absolute dollar decline while revenue surges indicates improving unit economics.

## Valuation Analysis

### Current Valuation Metrics
- **Market Cap:** $319.6B
- **P/E Ratio (TTM):** 212.08x
- **Forward P/E:** 71.78x
- **Price-to-Sales:** 71.4x ($319.6B / $4.48B)
- **Price-to-Book:** 43.25x
- **EV/FCF:** ~253x ($319.6B / $1.26B FCF)

### Valuation Concerns
The stock trades at extreme multiples even after the 28% six-month decline:
- Forward P/E of 71.78x implies $1.86 EPS expectation for next year
- At 212x TTM earnings, the stock prices in decades of perfect execution
- P/S ratio of 71x is unsustainable even for high-growth SaaS companies
- Typical SaaS companies trade at 5-15x sales; even best-in-class rarely exceed 25x

### Implied Growth Expectations
To justify current valuation at a 25x forward P/E (still premium):
- Stock would need to fall to ~$52 (65% downside from current levels)
- Alternatively, EPS must grow 187% to grow into current valuation
- At 71.78x forward P/E, market expects sustained 40%+ growth for 5+ years

## Risk Factors

### Valuation Risk (Critical)
At 212x P/E and 71x sales, any growth deceleration triggers severe multiple compression. If growth slows to 30% (still strong), fair value P/S might be 15-20x, implying 65-72% downside.

### Concentration Risk
Government contracts represent significant revenue. Policy changes, budget cuts, or contract losses create binary outcomes. Commercial revenue growth must accelerate to diversify.

### Competition Intensification
Microsoft, Snowflake, Databricks, and others compete in data analytics. Palantir's moat depends on proprietary ontology and switching costs, but commoditization risk exists.

### Stock-Based Compensation
While improving, $196M quarterly SBC (14% of revenue) dilutes shareholders. At current share count of 2.39B, SBC adds ~3-4% annual dilution.

### Market Sentiment
Severe underperformance vs peers suggests institutional selling. Technical breakdown below key support levels could trigger further liquidation.
