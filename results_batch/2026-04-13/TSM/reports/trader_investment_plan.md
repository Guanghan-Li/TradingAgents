## Decision Framework

The key question: Is the risk/reward favorable at $370 with earnings 3 days away?

**Yes, with proper position sizing.** Here's why:

1. **The forward multiple is reasonable**: 20x P/E for a business with 35% ROE, 48% net margins, and dominant market position in AI chip manufacturing is not expensive. The market has already discounted a significant earnings beat.

2. **Technical setup shows accumulation, not distribution**: The 16% YTD gain with massive alpha vs sector on declining volume during consolidation is classic institutional positioning, not retail FOMO.

3. **The earnings "risk" is asymmetric**: Multiple credible sources (Zacks, Motley Fool, IBD) expect a beat. If they're right, we're positioned. If they're wrong, our stop at $351 (50 SMA) caps downside at ~5%.

4. **The alternative is worse**: Waiting for "certainty" means chasing at $390-400 post-earnings if the thesis plays out. The structured analysis shows 85% probability of base case or better outcomes.

## Why Not Wait?

The bear case argues for waiting until after earnings. But consider:

1. **Information asymmetry works both ways**: If we wait and TSM beats/guides up, we're chasing at higher prices with worse risk/reward
2. **The market has already priced in a beat**: That forward P/E compression from 35x to 20x isn't hope - it's institutional positioning
3. **Position sizing manages uncertainty**: We're not going all-in; we're taking a measured position with defined risk

The real mistake would be paralysis. We have a high-quality business with exceptional fundamentals, trading at a reasonable forward multiple, with clear technical support levels. The earnings event is a catalyst, not a coin flip.
