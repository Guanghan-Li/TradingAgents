**Macro Regime Assessment**

The current macro backdrop shows a **late-cycle normalization** with the Fed in pause mode after a cutting cycle:

**Growth & Labor**: Nonfarm payrolls rose 178k (+0.11%) in March with unemployment at 4.30% (down from 4.40%), signaling a stable but cooling labor market. GDP data is stale (Q4 2025 at $31.4T), but the employment trend suggests modest expansion.

**Inflation**: PPI at 267.85 (Feb) rose 2.08% sequentially—a concerning acceleration. CPI data unavailable due to FRED errors, but producer-level inflation pressure suggests sticky inflation remains a risk.

**Policy Path**: Fed Funds effective rate at 3.64% (stable since Jan 2026) after 45bp of cuts from Oct-Dec 2025. The Fed appears to be holding at a restrictive-neutral level, likely data-dependent given the PPI uptick.

**Yield Curve**: The curve is **positively sloped** with no inversion:
- Front end: 3.66-3.71% (1M-6M)
- Belly: 3.78% (2Y) to 3.91% (5Y)
- Long end: 4.90% (30Y)

The 2Y-10Y spread (estimated ~30-40bp positive) signals no imminent recession risk. The steep 10Y-30Y segment (estimated 80bp+) reflects term premium and long-run inflation expectations.

**Cross-Asset Implications for JPM**:
- **Net Interest Margin**: Stable short rates (3.64%) and steep curve favor bank profitability
- **Credit Risk**: Soft landing scenario (no recession signal) supports loan book quality
- **Trading Revenue**: Volatility data unavailable (VIX error), but stable macro reduces trading volatility
- **Capital Markets**: Positive curve supports underwriting and M&A activity

**Key Risks**:
1. **Inflation re-acceleration**: PPI +2.08% could force Fed hawkish pivot
2. **Policy error**: Fed on hold too long if inflation persists
3. **Data gaps**: Missing CPI, VIX, ISM limit confidence in regime call
