# JPMorgan Chase (JPM) – Scenario & Catalyst Analysis
**As of April 13, 2026**

---

## Executive Summary

JPMorgan Chase enters Q1 earnings week (April 14, 2026) with solid fundamentals but facing a complex macro backdrop. The stock trades at a forward P/E of 13.4×, below its historical premium, reflecting investor caution around net interest income (NII) trajectory, credit normalization, and geopolitical tail risks (Iran/Hormuz tensions). The Fed's pause at 3.64% and a steepening yield curve offer modest tailwinds, but private credit competition and potential loan-loss reserve builds loom. We assign a **40% base case** (modest beat, stable outlook), **35% bull case** (strong beat, NII resilience, M&A fees surge), and **25% bear case** (miss on credit or NII guidance cut).

---

## Scenario Map

### 🟢 Bull Case (35% probability)
**Thesis:** JPM delivers a Q1 beat driven by resilient NII (deposit repricing lags help), robust investment banking fees (Leo Pharma IPO and M&A pipeline), and stable credit metrics. Management reaffirms full-year NII guidance and signals confidence in a soft landing. The stock re-rates toward 15× forward P/E (~$350).

**Valuation Implication:** Target $340–$360 (15–16× forward EPS of $23.37).

**Signposts:**
- Q1 NII >$23B (vs. consensus ~$22.5B)
- Investment banking fees +15% Y/Y
- Net charge-off ratio <0.30%
- CEO Dimon commentary on "manageable" geopolitical risks

---

### 🟡 Base Case (40% probability)
**Thesis:** JPM posts in-line Q1 results with modest NII pressure offset by fee income. Credit quality remains benign but management flags "normalization" in consumer segments. Full-year NII guidance trimmed 2–3%. Stock trades sideways in $300–$320 range as investors await clarity on Fed path and loan growth.

**Valuation Implication:** Fair value $310–$320 (13–14× forward EPS).

**Signposts:**
- Q1 NII $22–$23B
- Loan growth +3–4% Y/Y
- Provision for credit losses in line with consensus
- Neutral tone on 2026 outlook

---

### 🔴 Bear Case (25% probability)
**Thesis:** JPM disappoints on NII (deposit costs spike faster than expected) and/or signals credit stress in commercial real estate or consumer unsecured. Geopolitical escalation (Hormuz blockade) triggers market volatility, compressing trading revenue. Management cuts full-year NII guidance by 5%+. Stock tests $280 support.

**Valuation Implication:** Downside to $270–$290 (11–12× forward EPS).

**Signposts:**
- Q1 NII <$22B
- Net charge-offs >0.35% or reserve build >$2B
- CRE criticized loans spike
- VIX >25 sustained

## Key Risks & Monitoring Points

1. **NII Sensitivity:** Every 25bp move in Fed funds impacts annual NII by ~$1.5B. Watch deposit beta (currently ~40%) for acceleration.
2. **Credit Normalization:** Consumer card net charge-offs normalizing toward 3.5–4.0% (from pandemic lows). CRE office exposure (~$40B) under scrutiny.
3. **Regulatory Overhang:** Basel III Endgame rules could require $20B+ capital raise if finalized as proposed (low probability near-term).
4. **Geopolitical Tail Risk:** Hormuz blockade or Iran escalation could disrupt energy markets, spike volatility, and compress trading margins.

---

**Next Decision Point:** April 14, 2026, pre-market earnings release. Await Q1 NII print, credit metrics, and full-year guidance revision.
