**Key Themes to Monitor:**

3. **Red Flags** - Market commentary specifically warns that certain indicators in bank earnings could serve as "red flags" for Wall Street, though specifics remain vague. Traders should watch for:

   - Guidance revisions

## Macroeconomic & Geopolitical Context

### Geopolitical Risk Premium

**Iran-US Tensions:** President Trump ordered a Hormuz blockade against Iran, though subsequent reports indicate Iran "wants to talk." This creates a volatile backdrop for energy markets and global trade. The Strait of Hormuz is a critical chokepoint for global oil supply, and any sustained disruption would have cascading effects on:
- Energy prices (bullish for oil, bearish for energy-intensive sectors)
- Inflation expectations
- Central bank policy trajectories
- Risk appetite across asset classes

**Market Reaction:** Despite these tensions, major indices (Dow, S&P 500, Nasdaq) edged higher, suggesting either:
1. Markets are pricing in diplomatic resolution
2. Geopolitical risk premium is being offset by other factors
3. Complacency regarding tail risks

### Sector Rotation: Technology Dominance

The most striking feature of the current market is the continued outperformance of semiconductors and technology:

**Semiconductor Surge:**
- Marvell Technology hitting record highs, driven by Amazon demand
- Sandisk and Marvell breaking out to new highs
- Broadcom leading a broader semiconductor rally
- Price target increases: B. Riley raised Marvell to $156 from $135

**Strategic Moves:**
- Nvidia executed a $2 billion "game-changing" acquisition (details not specified in headlines but characterized as significant)
- Amazon's infrastructure spending driving chip demand

**Implications for JPM:**
This tech strength creates a challenging backdrop for traditional financials. Capital is flowing toward high-growth technology names, potentially limiting upside for banks even if earnings are solid. JPM's trading desks may benefit from volatility, but the wealth management and asset management divisions face headwinds if clients continue rotating toward tech.

### Energy Sector Strength

Marathon Petroleum received a price target increase from Jefferies ($263 to $279, Buy rating maintained), indicating analyst confidence in energy names. This aligns with geopolitical tensions that could support oil prices.
