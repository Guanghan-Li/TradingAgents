# Technical Analysis Report: SNOW (Snowflake Inc.)
**Analysis Date:** 2026-04-13  
**Macro Regime:** RISK-ON (Score +3/6, Medium Confidence)

---

## Executive Summary

SNOW is in a **severe technical downtrend** despite a constructive macro backdrop. The stock has declined ~28% from its mid-February peak of $182.29 to the current level of $131.64, with a particularly violent selloff in early April (Apr 9-10) that saw intraday lows near $118. Price is trading below all key moving averages, MACD momentum is deeply negative, and RSI is approaching oversold territory. The disconnect between the RISK-ON macro regime (which should favor cloud/growth equities) and SNOW's technical deterioration suggests company-specific headwinds or sector rotation away from high-valuation SaaS names.

**Recommendation Context:** While oversold conditions and Bollinger Band compression hint at potential mean reversion, the broken technical structure and bearish moving average alignment argue for caution. Any long positions require tight risk management; short-term traders may watch for stabilization signals, but trend followers should remain sidelined or short until the 50 SMA is reclaimed.

## Detailed Technical Analysis

### 1. Trend Structure: Broken and Bearish

**Moving Average Alignment:**
- **10 EMA (142.57) < 50 SMA (166.99) < 200 SMA (212.31):** Classic death cross configuration. All shorter-term averages are below longer-term averages, signaling entrenched bearish sentiment.
- **Distance from 50 SMA:** Price is ~21% below the 50 SMA, a level that historically acts as resistance in downtrends. Any rally toward $167 will likely face heavy selling.
- **Distance from 200 SMA:** At ~38% below the 200 SMA, SNOW is in deep bear market territory. The 200 SMA is declining (from $215.74 on Mar 27 to $212.31 on Apr 13), confirming the long-term trend has turned negative.

**Price Action:**
- **Feb 13 Peak ($182.29) → Apr 10 Low ($118.30):** A 35% decline in less than 2 months. The selloff accelerated in late March, with a climactic flush on Apr 9-10 (volume spiked to 15.5M and 23.3M shares, respectively—well above the 20-day average of ~6M).
- **Recent Bounce:** Apr 10 low of $121.11 → Apr 13 close of $131.64 represents an 8.7% bounce. However, this is occurring within a broader downtrend and lacks confirmation from momentum indicators.

**Actionable Insight:** The trend is unambiguously down. Traders should not fight this structure. For longs, wait for the 10 EMA to cross above the 50 SMA (currently ~$25 away) before considering re-entry. For shorts, any rally toward the 50 SMA ($167) or the declining 10 EMA ($142.57) offers high-probability fade opportunities.

### 2. Momentum: Deeply Negative, No Divergence Yet

**MACD (-11.15):**
- The MACD line is at its most negative reading in the dataset, having deteriorated from -4.04 on Mar 25 to -11.15 on Apr 13. This reflects accelerating bearish momentum.
- **No Bullish Divergence:** Despite the Apr 10 price low, MACD continued to make new lows on Apr 13, indicating sellers remain in control. A bullish divergence (price making lower lows while MACD makes higher lows) would be the first sign of momentum exhaustion—this has not occurred.
- **Signal Line:** While not provided in the data, the MACD histogram's trajectory suggests the MACD line is still below its signal line, confirming the sell signal remains active.

**RSI (32.16):**
- RSI has declined from 40.21 on Mar 26 to 21.17 on Apr 10 (the lowest reading in the dataset), before recovering slightly to 32.16 on Apr 13.
- **Oversold but Not Extreme:** RSI below 30 is traditionally oversold, but in strong downtrends, RSI can remain <30 for extended periods. The current reading of 32.16 suggests the stock is oversold but not yet at capitulation levels (typically <20).
- **Divergence Watch:** If price makes a new low below $121.11 but RSI holds above 21.17, that would constitute a bullish divergence—a potential early reversal signal. This has not yet materialized.

**Actionable Insight:** Momentum is firmly bearish. The lack of bullish divergence in either MACD or RSI means the downtrend has not yet exhausted itself. Contrarian longs should wait for RSI to dip below 20 (indicating panic) or for MACD to show positive divergence. Trend followers can remain short with confidence.

### 3. Volatility: Elevated and Compressing

**Bollinger Bands:**
- **Upper Band (186.76) / Lower Band (125.25):** The bands are wide (~$61 range), reflecting the recent volatility spike. Price is currently near the lower band, having briefly pierced it on Apr 10 ($118.30 low vs. $128.26 lower band on that date).
- **Band Walk:** In strong trends, price can "walk" the upper or lower band. SNOW has been walking the lower band since late March, a bearish signal. However, the Apr 10 pierce and subsequent bounce suggest potential exhaustion.
- **Squeeze Potential:** If volatility contracts (ATR declines, bands narrow), a breakout in either direction becomes more likely. Currently, the bands are still wide, so no squeeze is imminent.

**ATR (9.64):**
- ATR has spiked from ~8.35 on Apr 8 to 9.64 on Apr 13, reflecting the Apr 9-10 panic selling. This is the highest ATR reading in the recent dataset.
- **Risk Management Implication:** With ATR near $10, a 2x ATR stop-loss would be ~$20 wide. Traders must size positions accordingly to avoid being stopped out by normal volatility.

**Actionable Insight:** The lower Bollinger Band pierce on Apr 10 is a classic oversold signal, often followed by a short-term bounce (which we've seen). However, in strong downtrends, these bounces are typically sold. Use the middle Bollinger Band (~$156, not provided but calculable as the 20 SMA) as the first resistance level. If price reclaims the middle band, the downtrend may be weakening. Until then, treat rallies as selling opportunities.

### 4. Volume Analysis: Capitulation or Distribution?

**Recent Volume Spikes:**
- **Apr 9:** 15.5M shares (vs. 20-day avg of ~6M) as price dropped from $150.35 to $132.24.
- **Apr 10:** 23.3M shares (nearly 4x average) as price plunged to $121.11 intraday before closing at $121.11.

- **Distribution Concern:** If the stock rallies back toward $140-145 on declining volume, that would signal distribution (smart money exiting into strength), increasing the likelihood of another leg down.

**Actionable Insight:** Monitor volume closely. A sustained rally above $140 on volume exceeding 10M shares would suggest accumulation and potential trend reversal. Conversely, a rally on declining volume is a red flag for shorts to re-enter.

## Macro-Technical Disconnect: Why Is SNOW Falling in a RISK-ON Environment?

The macro regime is RISK-ON (VIX falling, yield curve normal, cyclicals leading), which should favor growth equities like SNOW. Yet SNOW is down ~28% from recent highs. Possible explanations:

1. **Company-Specific Issues:** Earnings miss, guidance cut, or competitive pressures (e.g., Databricks, Palantir gaining share). Check recent news/earnings.
2. **Sector Rotation:** Investors may be rotating from high-valuation SaaS (~10x sales) into cheaper cyclicals or AI infrastructure plays (e.g., NVDA, AMD).
3. **Rate Sensitivity:** Despite the Fed's pause, the 10Y yield at 4.29% remains elevated. High-duration growth stocks (SNOW has no earnings, so it's valued on future cash flows) are sensitive to rate levels, not just rate direction.
4. **Technical Breakdown:** Once the 50 SMA was lost in late March, algorithmic selling and stop-loss cascades likely accelerated the decline, creating a self-fulfilling downtrend.

**Actionable Insight:** The macro backdrop is supportive, but it's not enough to override broken technicals. If SNOW-specific headwinds ease (e.g., strong earnings, new product launch), the stock could snap back violently given the oversold conditions. However, without a fundamental catalyst, the technical damage will take time to repair.
