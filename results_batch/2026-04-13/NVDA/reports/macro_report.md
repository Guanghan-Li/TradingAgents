**Macro Regime: Mid-Cycle Normalization with Sticky Inflation**

The macro backdrop as of April 13, 2026 shows a resilient economy with persistent inflation pressures that have forced the Fed into a holding pattern. After cutting 45bps from October 2025 (4.09%) to January 2026 (3.64%), the FOMC has paused for three consecutive months. The labor market remains healthy—unemployment fell to 4.30% and payrolls added 178k—while the 2Y-10Y curve sits at +0.51%, signaling no imminent recession.

However, **producer inflation is running hot at +2.08% sequential**, and headline CPI rose +0.87%. This suggests the Fed's cutting cycle may be over; further easing is unlikely unless growth deteriorates. The 10Y Treasury yield at 4.29% and real rates in positive territory create a headwind for long-duration, high-multiple growth equities.

**Implications for NVDA:**
- **Supportive**: Solid growth supports AI capex; no recession signal; VIX at 19.23 shows risk appetite.
- **Headwind**: Sticky inflation limits Fed easing; 10Y at 4.29% pressures valuation multiples; higher-for-longer rates scenario.
- **Key risk**: If PPI inflation passes through to CPI, the Fed may need to hold or even hike, which would be a material negative for high-beta growth names.

**Concrete risks for equity/technical analysts:**
1. **Duration sensitivity**: Any 10Y move above 4.5% would compress NVDA's multiple.
2. **Policy reversal risk**: Fed may have cut too much; reacceleration in inflation could force a hawkish pivot.
3. **Real rate headwind**: Positive real rates favor value over growth.
4. **Volatility regime shift**: VIX is calm now, but inflation surprises could spike volatility and hit high-beta stocks hardest.
