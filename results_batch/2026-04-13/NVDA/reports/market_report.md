## Macro Overlay: Duration Risk Caps Upside

The technical picture is constructive, but the macro backdrop introduces meaningful headwinds:

1. **10Y Yield at 4.29%:** High-multiple growth stocks like NVDA (trading at ~35x forward earnings) are sensitive to rising real rates. A move above 4.5% would compress valuation multiples and pressure the stock.

2. **Sticky Inflation (PPI +2.08%):** Producer inflation running hot limits the Fed's ability to cut rates further. The market has priced in a "higher-for-longer" scenario, which is a headwind for duration-sensitive equities.

3. **Fed on Hold:** After cutting 45bps from October 2025 to January 2026, the Fed has paused for three months. No further easing is expected unless growth deteriorates, removing a key tailwind for risk assets.

4. **RISK-ON Regime (Score +3/6):** The medium-term regime is RISK-ON, which supports growth and cyclicals. However, the "medium confidence" rating and sticky inflation suggest this regime is fragile. A CPI surprise or Fed hawkish pivot could flip sentiment quickly.

**Actionable Insight:** The macro setup is neutral-to-slight-headwind. NVDA's AI demand story must be strong enough to overcome a 4.29% 10Y yield. Stress-test your thesis against a 4.5-4.75% 10Y scenario. If yields spike, expect NVDA to underperform.
