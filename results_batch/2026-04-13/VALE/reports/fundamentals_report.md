# Vale S.A. (VALE) - Fundamental Analysis Report
**Report Date:** April 13, 2026  
**Sector:** Basic Materials | **Industry:** Other Industrial Metals & Mining

---

## Executive Summary

Vale S.A. demonstrates **strong momentum** with exceptional 6-month returns (+66.58%) significantly outperforming the Materials sector ETF (+18.85%). However, Q4 2025 results reveal **severe operational challenges** with a massive net loss of $3.84B driven by $3.84B in asset impairments. The company faces a critical inflection point: robust commodity pricing and market positioning versus deteriorating asset quality and profitability metrics.

**Current Valuation:** Market cap $75.3B trading at 31.7x TTM P/E (elevated for a materials company), but forward P/E of 7.9x suggests market expects strong earnings recovery.

## Financial Performance Analysis

### Profitability Crisis in Q4 2025

**Q4 2025 marked a catastrophic quarter:**
- Net loss: **-$3.84B** (vs. +$2.69B in Q3 2025)
- Asset impairments: **$3.84B** - largest single-quarter writedown in recent history
- Special charges: **$4.15B** including impairments and other one-time items
- Operating income collapsed to **-$697M** from +$2.79B in Q3

**Sequential quarterly trend (Net Income):**
- Q4 2024: -$694M
- Q1 2025: +$1.39B
- Q2 2025: +$2.12B  
- Q3 2025: +$2.69B
- Q4 2025: **-$3.84B** ← sharp reversal

The Q4 impairment suggests Vale wrote down underperforming assets, potentially mines or facilities no longer economically viable at current cost structures.

### Revenue & Margin Dynamics

**Revenue trajectory (quarterly):**
- Q4 2024: $10.12B
- Q1 2025: $8.12B (-19.8%)
- Q2 2025: $8.80B (+8.4%)
- Q3 2025: $10.42B (+18.4%)
- Q4 2025: $11.06B (+6.1%)

Revenue recovered strongly through 2025, up 9.3% Q4/Q4. However, **operating margins deteriorated sharply:**

- Q3 2025 operating margin: 32.4% (3,367M / 10,420M)
- Q4 2025 operating margin: **31.6%** (3,497M / 11,060M) - before special items
- Reported operating income: **-6.3%** after impairments

**Gross profit margins remain healthy at 38.7%** in Q4 2025, indicating core mining operations are profitable, but asset writedowns and special charges devastated bottom-line results.

## Balance Sheet Health

### Debt & Liquidity Position

**Total debt increased significantly:**
- Q4 2024: $17.72B
- Q1 2025: $18.55B
- Q2 2025: $20.30B
- Q3 2025: $21.21B
- Q4 2025: **$21.80B** (+23.0% YoY)

**Net debt:** $13.76B (total debt $21.80B - cash $7.37B)

**Debt-to-equity ratio: 61.3%** - elevated but manageable for capital-intensive mining operations. However, the rapid debt accumulation (up $4.08B in one year) while simultaneously reporting losses raises concerns about capital allocation efficiency.

**Liquidity remains adequate:**
- Cash & equivalents: $7.37B (up from $4.95B in Q4 2024)
- Current ratio: **1.15x** - tight but acceptable
- Working capital: $2.42B

The company raised $1.16B in long-term debt in Q4 2025 while paying only $92M in debt repayments, suggesting aggressive refinancing or new project financing.

### Equity Deterioration

**Stockholders' equity:**
- Q4 2024: $33.41B
- Q4 2025: $33.51B (+0.3%)

Despite massive losses, equity remained relatively stable due to:
1. Foreign currency translation adjustments improving by $1.25B
2. Retained earnings reset to $0 (from $4.75B in Q3) - likely dividend distribution
3. Other equity adjustments

**Book value per share: $8.31** vs. current price ~$17.45 = **2.1x price-to-book**, reasonable for a mining company with growth prospects.

**Quarterly OCF:**
- Q4 2024: $2.91B
- Q1 2025: $1.67B
- Q2 2025: $1.86B
- Q3 2025: $2.58B

**Key OCF drivers in Q4:**
- Add-back of $3.84B impairment charge (non-cash)
- Depreciation: $860M
- Working capital contributed +$179M (inventory reduction -$349M offset by payables +$48M)

### Capital Expenditure & Free Cash Flow

**CapEx intensity:**
- Q4 2025: $2.19B (19.8% of revenue)
- Q3 2025: $1.39B (13.4% of revenue)
- Q2 2025: $1.17B (13.3% of revenue)

**Free cash flow:**

- Q3 2025: $1.18B
- Q2 2025: $688M

FCF turned positive but remains constrained by elevated CapEx. The $2.19B Q4 CapEx spike suggests major project investments or mine development.

### Dividend Policy Under Pressure

**Cash dividends paid:**

- Q1 2025: $1.98B
- Q4 2025: $97M - dramatically reduced

**Dividends payable on balance sheet:** $2.65B as of Q4 2025 (vs. $0 in Q3), indicating a declared but unpaid dividend, likely pending board approval given the Q4 losses.

**Current dividend yield: 7.42%** - attractive but sustainability questionable given negative earnings and elevated payout ratio.
