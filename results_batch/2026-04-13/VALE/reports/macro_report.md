**Macroeconomic Regime Assessment – April 13, 2026**

**Regime: Late-Cycle Normalization with Sticky Inflation**

The Fed has delivered ~45 bps of cumulative cuts since October 2025, pausing at 3.64% for the past three months. This signals the committee believes it has reached a neutral-to-slightly-restrictive stance. However, the March CPI print rose 0.87% month-over-month—an annualized pace near 11%—indicating inflation remains far from the 2% target. The unemployment rate of 4.3% is historically low, and the labor market shows no signs of distress.

**Yield Curve & Recession Signal**

The 2Y-10Y spread sits at +50 bps, a flat but positively sloped curve. This configuration typically reflects:
- Growth expectations are modest but not recessionary
- The market does not price imminent Fed cuts
- Term premium remains compressed, likely due to persistent inflation uncertainty

A flat curve with sticky inflation is not a classic recession signal; instead, it suggests the economy is grinding through a high-rate environment without breaking.

**Policy Path & Cross-Asset Implications**

Given the inflation backdrop, the Fed is likely on hold through mid-2026. Any resumption of cuts would require either:
1. A sharp labor market deterioration (unemployment rising above 4.5%), or
2. A sustained disinflation trend (CPI sequential prints below 0.2% for multiple months)

Neither condition is met. The VIX at 19.23 reflects complacency—markets are not pricing macro stress.

**Cross-Asset Implications:**
- **Equities:** Vulnerable to multiple compression if the Fed holds longer than expected. Cyclicals and rate-sensitive sectors (REITs, utilities) face headwinds.
- **Fixed Income:** The curve is unlikely to steepen materially without a growth shock. Long-duration bonds offer limited upside.
- **Commodities:** Sticky inflation and a resilient economy support industrial metals and energy. However, a strong dollar (implied by higher-for-longer rates) caps upside.
- **Credit:** Spreads remain tight (VIX low), but a prolonged high-rate environment increases refinancing risk for leveraged issuers.

**Risks for Other Analysts to Incorporate:**
1. **Inflation Re-acceleration:** If CPI remains elevated, the Fed may hike again, shocking equity multiples.
2. **Labor Market Surprise:** A sudden uptick in unemployment could force rapid cuts, steepening the curve and benefiting long-duration assets.
3. **Geopolitical/Supply Shock:** Energy or food price spikes would entrench inflation expectations, forcing the Fed to stay restrictive despite growth weakness.
