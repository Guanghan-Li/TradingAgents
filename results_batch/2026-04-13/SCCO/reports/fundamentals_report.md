# Southern Copper Corporation (SCCO) - Fundamental Analysis Report
**Report Date:** April 13, 2026

## Executive Summary

Southern Copper Corporation demonstrates exceptional operational performance with strong momentum across all key metrics. The company is capitalizing on elevated copper prices and robust demand, delivering record profitability while maintaining a fortress balance sheet. Recent quarterly results show accelerating revenue growth and margin expansion, positioning SCCO as a top-tier performer in the materials sector.

## Company Profile

**Name:** Southern Copper Corporation  
**Sector:** Basic Materials  
**Industry:** Copper  
**Market Capitalization:** $162.2 billion

SCCO operates as one of the world's largest integrated copper producers, with significant operations in Peru and Mexico. The company benefits from vertically integrated operations spanning mining, smelting, and refining.

Q4 2025 revenue reached **$3.87 billion**, representing a **39% year-over-year increase** from Q4 2024's $2.78 billion. Sequential quarterly progression shows consistent growth:

- Q1 2025: $3.12 billion
- Q2 2025: $3.05 billion  
- Q3 2025: $3.38 billion
- Q4 2025: $3.87 billion (+14.5% QoQ)

Trailing twelve-month revenue stands at $13.42 billion with a gross profit of $8.17 billion, yielding an impressive **60.9% gross margin**.

### Profitability - Industry-Leading Margins

Net income for Q4 2025 reached **$1.31 billion**, up 65% from Q4 2024's $794 million. The company's profitability metrics are exceptional:

- **Operating Margin:** 54.5% (among the highest in the sector)
- **Profit Margin:** 32.3%
- **EBITDA:** $7.90 billion TTM
- **EBITDA Margin:** 58.9%

Quarterly EPS progression demonstrates consistent earnings power:
- Q4 2025: $1.55

TTM EPS of $5.20 supports the current valuation framework.

### Balance Sheet - Fortress Strength

- Short-term investments: $605 million
- Total liquid assets: $4.91 billion

### Cash Flow - Exceptional Generation

Q4 2025 operating cash flow reached **$1.49 billion**, with free cash flow of **$1.07 billion** after capex of $423 million.

**Trailing Free Cash Flow:** $3.34 billion

The company maintains disciplined capital allocation:

Cash flow from operations has remained consistently strong across quarters, averaging $1.27 billion per quarter in 2025.

## Risk Factors & Considerations

**Valuation Risk:** At 37.8x TTM earnings, SCCO trades at a premium to historical norms. Any softening in copper prices or margin compression could lead to multiple contraction.

**Commodity Price Exposure:** The company's fortunes are directly tied to copper prices. Current elevated prices may not be sustainable if global growth slows.

**Debt Levels:** While manageable, the $7.4 billion debt load requires monitoring, particularly if cash flows weaken.

**Geopolitical Risk:** Operations concentrated in Peru and Mexico expose the company to political and regulatory risks in these jurisdictions.

**Tax Rate:** Effective tax rate of 36-37% is relatively high, limiting net income conversion.
