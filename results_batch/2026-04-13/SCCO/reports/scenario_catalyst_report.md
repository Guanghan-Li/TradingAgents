# Scenario & Catalyst Analysis: SCCO (Southern Copper Corporation)
*Analysis Date: 2026-04-13*

## Executive Summary

Southern Copper has surged **136% over the past year**, driven by the copper supercycle narrative (electrification, AI infrastructure, grid buildout) and exceptional operational performance. The company trades at **$196+ (near 52-week high of $223.89)** with a **37.8x TTM P/E** and **14.7x book value**—rich multiples for a materials producer. Goldman Sachs recently upgraded from Sell to Neutral but set a **$178 price target**, implying ~9% downside and signaling valuation concerns despite improved sentiment.

**Key tension:** Best-in-class fundamentals (42.7% ROE, 54% operating margin, 32% profit margin) versus stretched valuation after a parabolic run. The bull case hinges on sustained copper demand exceeding supply, while the bear case centers on multiple compression and China demand disappointment.

## Bull Case (30% probability)

**Thesis:**  
Copper enters a structural deficit as electrification (EVs, renewables, grid upgrades) and AI data center buildout collide with underinvestment in new mines. SCCO, as a low-cost producer with tier-1 assets in Peru and Mexico, captures outsized margin expansion. China announces infrastructure stimulus, and geopolitical stability (US-Iran ceasefire) supports risk assets.

**Valuation Implication:**  
Copper rallies to $5.00–$5.50/lb (vs. ~$4.30 recent). SCCO's earnings power jumps to $7–$8 EPS, justifying a 30–35x forward multiple on scarcity premium. Target: **$240–$260**.

**Signposts to Watch:**
- Copper inventories (LME + COMEX) fall below 100k tonnes
- China PMI manufacturing rebounds above 51 for 2+ months
- SCCO Q1 2026 earnings beat with margin expansion guidance
- Major miner (Freeport, BHP) announces mine closure or delay
- US/EU infrastructure bills accelerate copper-intensive projects

---

## Base Case (50% probability)

**Thesis:**  
Copper trades $4.00–$4.50/lb in a range as China demand stabilizes (not surges) and supply slowly responds. SCCO maintains industry-leading margins but faces modest cost inflation (labor, energy). The stock consolidates after its 136% run, with P/E compressing toward 28–32x forward as growth expectations normalize. Goldman's $178 target reflects this mean-reversion risk.

**Valuation Implication:**  
Forward EPS ~$6.00–$6.50, 28x multiple = **$170–$180 fair value**. Current price ($196+) implies 10–15% downside, but strong FCF ($3.3B) and 2% dividend provide a floor. Range: **$170–$200**.

**Signposts to Watch:**
- Copper holds $4.00/lb support, fails to break $4.80 resistance
- SCCO reports Q1 earnings in-line; no major guidance change
- Fed holds rates at 3.50–3.75% through mid-2026 (neutral for commodities)
- China GDP growth prints 4.5–5.0% (steady, not accelerating)
- Peer valuations (FCX, TECK) remain at 25–30x forward

---

## Bear Case (20% probability)

**Thesis:**  
China's property sector deterioration deepens, crushing copper demand. Copper falls to $3.50–$3.80/lb as recession fears mount. SCCO's valuation—already stretched at 37.8x TTM—collapses toward historical norms (20–25x) as investors rotate out of cyclicals. The 136% run reverses sharply; technical support at $140–$150 (200-day MA at $139) is tested.

**Valuation Implication:**  
EPS falls to $4.50–$5.00 on lower copper prices and volume. 25x multiple = **$120–$125**. Downside to $120–$150 range (35–40% from peak).

**Signposts to Watch:**
- Copper breaks below $4.00/lb and tests $3.50
- China PMI falls below 48; property starts decline >15% YoY
- SCCO misses Q1 earnings; cites cost pressures or volume weakness
- Fed signals prolonged restrictive policy (funds above 4%)
- Technical breakdown: 50-day MA ($188) crosses below 200-day MA ($139)

## Risk-Reward Assessment

- **Current Price:** ~$196 (near 52-week high $223.89)
- **Base Case Fair Value:** $170–$180 → **10–15% downside**
- **Bull Case Upside:** $240–$260 → **25–35% upside**
- **Bear Case Downside:** $120–$150 → **35–40% downside**

**Asymmetry:** Risk-reward is **unfavorable** at current levels. The base case implies near-term downside, and the bull case requires multiple positive catalysts (China stimulus + copper breakout) to justify the valuation. The 136% run has front-loaded much of the bull thesis.

**Recommendation Bias:** **HOLD** or **TRIM** for existing holders. New buyers should wait for a pullback toward $170–$180 (base case fair value) or a confirmed copper breakout above $4.80/lb with China PMI >51.
