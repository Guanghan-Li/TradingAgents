# Southern Copper (SCCO) Segment Analysis

## Business Unit Decomposition

Southern Copper operates as an integrated copper producer with a concentrated business model focused on copper extraction and processing, supplemented by valuable byproduct metals. The company's revenue stream is heavily weighted toward copper (~80-85% of revenue), with molybdenum, silver, and zinc contributing the remainder.

**Concentration Risk:** SCCO exhibits high commodity concentration risk. Copper price volatility directly drives 80%+ of revenue, creating significant earnings sensitivity to global copper demand cycles. Geographic concentration in Peru and Mexico adds regulatory and political risk.

**Revenue Durability:** Strong. SCCO operates low-cost, long-life mines with decades of reserves. Operating margins of 54.5% and EBITDA margins near 60% provide substantial cushion during commodity downturns. Recent quarterly performance shows 39% YoY revenue growth (Q4 2025 vs Q4 2024), driven by both volume and copper price strength.

**Strategic Positioning:** SCCO benefits from structural copper demand tailwinds (electrification, EVs, renewable energy infrastructure). The company's low-cost position (among lowest quartile globally) ensures profitability across commodity cycles. Recent Goldman Sachs upgrade to Neutral reflects improving sentiment on copper fundamentals.

## Segment Economics

- **Margin Profile:** Exceptional. Gross margins ~56%, operating margins ~54.5%, net margins ~32%. Byproduct credits (molybdenum, silver, zinc) enhance copper segment economics.
- **Capital Intensity:** Moderate to high. Mining requires ongoing capex for sustaining production and expansion, but SCCO generates strong free cash flow ($3.34B TTM) to self-fund growth.
- **Cyclicality:** High. Copper is a pro-cyclical industrial metal. However, structural demand drivers (energy transition) may dampen traditional cyclicality.

## Value Drivers & Trading Implications

**Key Catalysts:**
1. **Copper Price Momentum:** Spot copper prices remain elevated on supply constraints and energy transition demand. Each 10% move in copper prices impacts SCCO revenue by ~8-10%.
2. **Production Volume Growth:** Recent quarters show strong volume execution. Q4 2025 revenue of $3.87B (vs $2.78B Q4 2024) reflects both price and volume gains.
3. **Byproduct Leverage:** Molybdenum and silver prices have strengthened, providing margin upside beyond copper.
4. **Geopolitical Easing:** Recent US-Iran ceasefire reduces risk premium in commodities, but also signals stable demand environment.

**Valuation Concern:** Stock up 136% over one year. PE ratio of 37.8x is elevated for a cyclical commodity producer, suggesting much of the copper bull case is priced in. Forward PE of 32.8x implies modest earnings growth expectations.
