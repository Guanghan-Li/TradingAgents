## Macroeconomic Analysis – CRWD Context

**Regime: Mid-to-Late Cycle, Inflation Watch**

The macro backdrop as of April 13, 2026 shows a resilient economy with a **healthy labor market** (unemployment 4.3%, payrolls +178k) and a **normal upward-sloping yield curve** (2Y-10Y +51 bps), signaling no imminent recession. However, **producer inflation is running hot** (PPI +2.08% MoM), while headline CPI rose a more modest +0.87%. This divergence suggests pipeline price pressures that may feed through to consumers in coming months.

The **Fed has paused at 3.64%** after cutting ~45 bps from October 2025 through January 2026, and has held steady for three months. With the curve steepening and PPI accelerating, the risk is that the Fed's easing cycle is over—or could even reverse if inflation re-accelerates. The **VIX at 19.23** reflects moderate uncertainty, not panic.

**Cross-Asset Implications:**
- **Equities (including CRWD):** Mid-cycle pause typically supports risk assets, but sticky inflation and potential Fed hawkishness are headwinds. Growth/tech names sensitive to rates (like CRWD) face duration risk if the long end continues to rise.
- **Rates:** The 10Y at 4.29% and 30Y at 4.90% suggest the market is pricing in higher-for-longer. Any inflation surprise could push yields higher, pressuring valuations.
- **Credit:** Spreads likely stable given solid growth, but watch for Fed rhetoric shift.
- **Dollar:** Pause + inflation = potential USD strength if Fed turns hawkish relative to other central banks.

**Key Risks:**
1. **PPI-to-CPI pass-through** forces Fed to hike or extend the pause, tightening financial conditions.
2. **Curve steepening** continues, raising long-term borrowing costs and compressing equity multiples.
3. **Labor market cracks** (not evident yet) would flip the regime to stagflation risk.

**Actionable View for CRWD:**
CrowdStrike operates in a secular growth sector (cybersecurity) but is a high-multiple, rate-sensitive name. The current macro supports **HOLD** bias: growth is solid (supportive of IT spending), but rising long-end yields and inflation uncertainty cap upside. A Fed pivot to hawkish or a 10Y break above 4.5% would be a **SELL** signal for duration-sensitive growth. Conversely, if PPI cools and the Fed signals more cuts, that's a **BUY** setup.
