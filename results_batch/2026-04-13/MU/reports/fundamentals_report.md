## Financial Performance Analysis

### Revenue Trajectory: Explosive Growth

Micron's quarterly revenue demonstrates a dramatic recovery and acceleration:

- **Q1 FY2026 (Feb 2026):** $23.86B
- **Q4 FY2025 (Nov 2025):** $13.64B (+74.9% QoQ)
- **Q3 FY2025 (Aug 2025):** $11.32B
- **Q2 FY2025 (May 2025):** $9.30B
- **Q1 FY2025 (Feb 2025):** $8.05B

**Key Insight:** Revenue nearly tripled from $8.05B (Feb 2025) to $23.86B (Feb 2026), representing a 196% year-over-year increase. The most recent quarter shows a massive 74.9% sequential acceleration, indicating surging demand for memory products.

### Profitability: Margin Expansion at Scale

**Gross Margin Evolution:**
- Q1 FY2026: 74.4% ($17.76B gross profit on $23.86B revenue)
- Q4 FY2025: 56.1%
- Q3 FY2025: 44.7%
- Q2 FY2025: 37.7%
- Q1 FY2025: 36.8%

**Operating Margin:** 67.6% (TTM), with Q1 FY2026 at 67.6% ($16.14B operating income)

**Net Margin:** 41.5% (TTM), with Q1 FY2026 at 57.8% ($13.79B net income)

**Critical Observation:** Gross margins have doubled in one year, from 36.8% to 74.4%. This is extraordinary in the semiconductor industry and suggests:
1. Pricing power from tight supply/strong demand
2. Operational leverage from fixed-cost absorption
3. Product mix shift toward higher-margin solutions (likely HBM, data center products)

### Earnings Performance

- **Q1 FY2026 EPS:** $12.07 (diluted)
- **Q4 FY2025 EPS:** $4.60
- **Q3 FY2025 EPS:** $2.83
- **Q2 FY2025 EPS:** $1.68
- **Q1 FY2025 EPS:** $1.41

**TTM EPS:** $21.20  
**Forward EPS (analyst estimates):** $98.16

**Actionable Insight:** The forward PE of 4.25x (based on $98.16 forward EPS) is exceptionally low for a company growing this rapidly. If analysts are correct, MU is trading at a significant discount to normalized earnings power.

## Key Risks

### Cyclicality Risk (HIGH)

Memory semiconductors are notoriously cyclical. Current margins (74% gross, 68% operating) are unsustainably high and will compress when:
- Supply increases (industry capacity additions)
- Demand normalizes (AI/data center buildout slows)
- Pricing power erodes

**Historical Pattern:** Memory cycles typically last 2-3 years from trough to peak. MU appears to be in mid-to-late upcycle.

### Inventory Risk (MODERATE)

- **Inventory:** $8.27B (Feb 2026), relatively flat vs $9.01B (Feb 2025)
- **Days Inventory:** ~50 days (estimated)

**Assessment:** Inventory is well-controlled, but any demand slowdown could lead to rapid margin compression.

### Geopolitical Risk (MODERATE-HIGH)

- **China exposure:** Significant revenue from Chinese customers
- **Taiwan risk:** Supply chain dependencies
- **Trade policy:** Potential for export restrictions on advanced memory

### Capital Intensity Risk (MODERATE)

- **Capex as % of revenue:** 27% (Q1 FY2026)
- **Depreciation:** $2.3B/quarter

**Concern:** High capex requirements mean FCF is sensitive to revenue fluctuations. A 20% revenue decline could turn FCF negative.

## Key Opportunities

### AI/HBM Demand (HIGH CONVICTION)

High-bandwidth memory (HBM) for AI accelerators is a structural growth driver. MU is a leading supplier of HBM3E, with:
- Limited competition (Samsung, SK Hynix)
- Tight supply
- Premium pricing (3-5x DRAM ASPs)

**Estimate:** HBM could represent 20-30% of DRAM revenue by 2027, up from ~10% currently.

### Data Center Expansion (HIGH)

Cloud providers (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud) are expanding capacity for AI workloads, driving demand for:
- DDR5 server DRAM
- Enterprise SSDs
- CXL memory

### Automotive/Industrial (MODERATE)

Secular growth in automotive electronics (ADAS, EVs) provides diversification from PC/mobile cyclicality.

### Market Share Gains (MODERATE)

Micron has historically been #3 in DRAM (behind Samsung, SK Hynix) and #5-6 in NAND. Technology leadership in HBM and advanced nodes could drive share gains.
