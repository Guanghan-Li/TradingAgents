## Macro Regime: Reflationary Pause with Reaccelerating Inflation

**Growth & Labor**: The economy remains on solid footing. Nonfarm payrolls added 178k in March, unemployment ticked down to 4.30%, and Q4 GDP stood at $31.4T. This is a healthy, mid-cycle growth environment with no immediate recession risk.

**Inflation**: The critical development is **reaccelerating inflation**. CPI rose 0.87% month-over-month in March, and PPI surged 2.08% MoM in February. These are annualized rates well above the Fed's 2% target and suggest the disinflationary trend has stalled or reversed. This is the dominant macro risk.

**Fed Policy Path**: The Fed cut 45 bps from October (4.09%) to January (3.64%) and has now paused for three consecutive months. The prefetched data shows the effective rate at 3.64%, near what the Fed likely views as neutral. Given the inflation reacceleration, the Fed is almost certainly **done cutting** and may need to **hike** if the next 1-2 CPI/PPI prints remain hot. The market has not fully priced this risk.

**Yield Curve**: The 2Y-10Y spread is +51 bps, a normal upward slope consistent with growth expectations and no imminent recession. However, the curve is steep (30Y at 4.90% vs 1M at 3.66%), suggesting the market expects the Fed to stay restrictive. If the Fed signals hikes, expect the front end to rise and the curve to flatten or invert.

**Cross-Asset Implications**:
- **Rates**: Higher for longer. Front-end yields (2Y-5Y) are vulnerable to upside if the Fed turns hawkish. Long-end may be anchored by slower long-term growth expectations.
- **Equities**: Growth/tech stocks face valuation pressure from higher discount rates. Cyclicals and value may outperform given solid growth, but margin compression from inflation is a risk.
- **Credit**: Investment-grade spreads may widen modestly; high-yield is vulnerable if the Fed overtightens.
- **Commodities**: Inflation backdrop is supportive, but a hawkish Fed could strengthen the dollar and cap gains.
- **Volatility**: VIX at 19.23 is moderate but could spike if the Fed surprises hawkish or if inflation prints remain hot.

**Key Risks for Other Analysts**:
1. **Fed policy error**: If inflation stays hot, the Fed may hike into a slowing economy in H2 2026, risking a hard landing.
2. **Margin compression**: PPI up 2.08% MoM suggests input cost pressures that companies may struggle to pass through.
3. **Duration risk**: Long-duration assets (growth stocks, long bonds) are vulnerable to rising real rates.
4. **Curve inversion risk**: If the Fed hikes aggressively, the 2Y-10Y could invert, signaling recession risk.

**For MU (Micron Technology)**:
- **Positive**: Solid growth and tight labor support semiconductor demand (data centers, AI, consumer electronics).
- **Negative**: MU is a rate-sensitive cyclical. Higher-for-longer rates pressure valuations. Inflation could squeeze margins if DRAM/NAND pricing power weakens.
- **Verdict**: Macro backdrop is **mixed**. Growth is supportive, but the Fed's next move and inflation trajectory are critical. If the Fed hikes, expect multiple compression. If inflation cools, MU benefits from the growth tailwind.
