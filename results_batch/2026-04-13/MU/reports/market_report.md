**Interpretation:**

- However, MU is now approaching the **$420-440 resistance zone** (prior support from mid-March). This is a critical test. A break above $440 would confirm the recovery; a rejection would suggest the rally is a bear-market bounce.

**Volume Analysis:**

**Actionable Insight:** The V-shaped recovery from $321.80 is impressive, but MU is now at a critical juncture. Watch for a break above $440 on strong volume to confirm the recovery. A failure here would suggest the rally is exhausted.

### 5. Macro Overlay: RISK-ON Regime Supports Cyclicals, But Fed Risk Looms

**Macro Context:**
- **Regime:** RISK-ON (score +3/6, medium confidence). VIX is falling (19.23), market breadth is strong (S&P 500 above 200-day), and cyclicals are outperforming defensives.
- **Fed Policy:** The Fed has paused at 3.64% for 3 months after cutting 45 bps from October to January. However, CPI (+0.87% MoM) and PPI (+2.08% MoM) are reaccelerating, suggesting the Fed may need to hike if inflation persists.
- **Yield Curve:** 2Y-10Y spread is +51 bps (normal upward slope), but the 30Y at 4.90% suggests the market expects the Fed to stay restrictive.

**Implications for MU:**
- **Positive:** The RISK-ON regime and solid growth (payrolls +178k, unemployment 4.30%) support semiconductor demand (data centers, AI, consumer electronics). MU is a cyclical growth stock that benefits from this backdrop.
- **Negative:** MU is **rate-sensitive**. If the Fed hikes in response to hot inflation, long-duration growth stocks like MU will face valuation pressure (higher discount rates compress multiples). Additionally, reaccelerating PPI (+2.08% MoM) suggests input cost pressures that could squeeze MU's margins if DRAM/NAND pricing power weakens.

**Actionable Insight:** The macro backdrop is **mixed**. The RISK-ON regime supports MU in the near term, but the Fed's next move is a wildcard. If the April CPI/PPI prints come in hot, expect a hawkish Fed pivot that could pressure MU. Monitor the 2Y yield (currently 3.78%)—a spike above 4.00% would signal Fed hike expectations and be a headwind for MU.
