# ServiceNow (NOW) Segment Analysis
**Analysis Date:** 2026-04-13

## Business Unit Decomposition

ServiceNow operates as a unified platform provider with revenue primarily derived from **subscription-based software** (estimated ~95% of revenue) and **professional services** (~5%). While the company does not publicly report granular segment breakouts, the business can be decomposed by workflow category:

### Core Platform Segments

**1. IT Workflows (ITSM/ITOM)** – *Estimated 55-60% of subscription revenue*
- **Strategic Role:** Foundation product; mature but still growing mid-teens
- **Growth Trend:** Steady expansion driven by digital transformation and cloud migration
- **Margin Profile:** Highest margin segment; established product with scale economies
- **Evidence:** Consistent 20%+ YoY revenue growth in recent quarters (Q4'25: $3.57B vs Q4'24: $2.96B = 20.6% growth)

**2. Employee Workflows** – *Estimated 15-20% of subscription revenue*
- **Strategic Role:** High-growth expansion into HR service delivery
- **Growth Trend:** Accelerating; benefiting from remote work and employee experience focus
- **Margin Profile:** Improving as platform matures; leverages core infrastructure
- **Evidence:** R&D investment up 15.7% YoY (Q4'25: $773M vs Q4'24: $668M) suggests continued product expansion

**3. Customer Workflows** – *Estimated 10-15% of subscription revenue*
- **Strategic Role:** Competitive battleground vs. Salesforce, Zendesk
- **Growth Trend:** Strong but facing headwinds from macro softness in customer service spend
- **Margin Profile:** Lower margin due to competitive pricing and integration costs

**4. Creator Workflows (Low-Code Platform)** – *Estimated 5-10% of subscription revenue*
- **Strategic Role:** Future growth driver; enables citizen developers
- **Growth Trend:** Rapid expansion but from small base
- **Margin Profile:** High incremental margin; minimal additional infrastructure cost

**5. Professional Services** – *~5% of total revenue*
- **Strategic Role:** Enablement and land-and-expand facilitator
- **Growth Trend:** Growing in line with subscription base
- **Margin Profile:** Low single-digit operating margin; strategic not profit-driven
- **Evidence:** Cost of Revenue at $834M (23.4% of revenue) in Q4'25, consistent with prior quarters

## Segment Economics Summary

| Metric | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| **Gross Margin** | 76.6% (Q4'25), stable and industry-leading |
| **Operating Margin** | 12.4% (Q4'25), expanding from 12.6% in Q4'24 |
| **Capital Intensity** | Low; SaaS model with minimal CapEx |
| **Cyclicality** | Moderate; enterprise software spending tied to IT budgets but sticky subscriptions provide resilience |
| **Concentration Risk** | **HIGH** – IT Workflows represent majority of revenue; diversification underway but still early |

**Margin Trend:** Operating margins compressed slightly in Q4'25 (12.4% vs 12.6% Q4'24) due to elevated S&M spend ($1.15B, up 12% YoY) and R&D investment ($773M, up 15.7% YoY). However, gross margins remain stable at 76.6%, indicating pricing power and operational efficiency. EBITDA margin improved to 21.2% in Q4'25 vs 21.0% in Q4'24.

## Concentration Risks

1. **IT Workflows Dominance:** 55-60% revenue concentration in mature ITSM/ITOM creates vulnerability if enterprises delay IT modernization projects.
2. **Large Enterprise Dependency:** ServiceNow's upmarket focus means exposure to elongated sales cycles and budget scrutiny in uncertain macro environments.
3. **Geographic Concentration:** While not disclosed in provided data, ServiceNow historically derives ~70% of revenue from North America.

## Synthesis

ServiceNow exhibits **strong revenue durability** (20.6% YoY growth in Q4'25) underpinned by subscription model stickiness and expanding workflow adjacencies. However, **margin compression** (operating margin 12.4% vs 12.6% YoY) and **analyst downgrades** (BMO, RBC cutting targets by ~25-30%) signal near-term demand uncertainty.

**Key Positives:**
- Gross margin stability (76.6%) demonstrates pricing power
- R&D investment (+15.7% YoY) positions for AI-driven product cycle
- Free cash flow generation remains robust ($4.95B TTM)

**Key Risks:**
- High concentration in IT Workflows (55-60% of revenue)
- Operating leverage not materializing (S&M spend up 12% YoY)
- Valuation compression (PE 53x vs forward PE 17.6x suggests earnings growth expectations)

**Segment Verdict:** The business mix is **high-quality but concentrated**. Employee and Creator Workflows offer diversification upside, but IT Workflows remain the dominant revenue driver. Margin trends are **mixed** – gross margins stable, but operating margins under pressure from growth investments.
