**MACRO REGIME: Late-Cycle Reflation Risk**

The Fed has delivered 45bp of cuts since October 2025 (4.09% → 3.64%), but inflation is reaccelerating sharply. CPI rose 0.87% month-over-month in March, and PPI surged 2.08% in February—both well above the pace consistent with 2% annual inflation. Meanwhile, the labor market remains tight: unemployment fell to 4.3% and payrolls added 178k jobs.

**Policy Path:** The Fed is now trapped. It eased into a reacceleration, and the data argue for a pause or even hikes, but the curve (2Y-10Y +51bp) and stable VIX (19.23) suggest markets are pricing continued accommodation. The next move is likely *on hold* until inflation data clarifies, but the risk is skewed toward hawkish surprise if CPI prints remain hot.

**Cross-Asset Implications:**
- **Rates:** 10Y at 4.29% is vulnerable to bear steepening if the Fed signals it's done cutting or considers hikes. Front-end (1Y-2Y ~3.7%) is mispriced for reflation.
- **Equities:** Growth/duration-sensitive names face compression risk. Value and commodities may outperform if stagflation fears build.
- **Credit:** Spreads are complacent (VIX 19). A hawkish Fed pivot would widen spreads, especially in leveraged/cyclical sectors.
- **FX/Commodities:** Dollar strength likely if Fed turns hawkish. Commodity inflation (PPI spike) supports energy/materials but threatens margins elsewhere.

**Concrete Risks:**
1. **Policy error:** Fed cut too much; next CPI print >0.5% MoM forces hawkish pivot.
2. **Curve inversion:** If Fed hikes, 2Y-10Y could flatten or invert, signaling recession.
3. **Stagflation:** Sticky inflation + slowing growth (GDP data is stale, Q4 2025) = worst case for risk assets.
