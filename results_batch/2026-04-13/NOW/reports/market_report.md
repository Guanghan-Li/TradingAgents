# Technical Analysis Report: NOW (ServiceNow)
**Date: 2026-04-13**

---

## Executive Summary

ServiceNow (NOW) has experienced a **catastrophic breakdown**, plunging 29% from its March 6 peak of $124.34 to the current price of $88.48. The stock is now trading below all major moving averages (10 EMA, 50 SMA, 200 SMA), signaling a confirmed downtrend across all timeframes. While short-term oversold conditions suggest a potential tactical bounce, the macro backdrop of rising rates and reflation risk creates a hostile environment for high-duration growth stocks like NOW.

## Price Action & Trend Analysis

**Current Price:** $88.48  
**Recent Peak (March 6):** $124.34  
**Decline from Peak:** -28.9%

The selloff accelerated dramatically in the past week:
- April 7-10: -17.5% decline in just 4 trading days
- April 9: Single-day capitulation drop of -7.9% on 42M volume (nearly 2x average)
- April 10: Follow-through selling with -7.6% on 58M volume (highest in dataset)

**Key Technical Levels:**
- **10 EMA (95.67):** Price is 7.5% below, indicating short-term downtrend
- **50 SMA (107.21):** Price is 17.5% below, confirming intermediate-term breakdown
- **200 SMA (157.45):** Price is 43.8% below, signaling long-term bear market territory

The stock has violated every major support level. The 50 SMA, which held as support through February-March, was decisively broken on March 20 and has since acted as resistance. The distance from the 200 SMA is extreme and suggests either a structural repricing or an oversold extreme.

## Momentum & Oscillators

**RSI (31.94):** Oversold but not capitulation territory. RSI dipped to 22.4 on April 10 (extreme oversold), but has since recovered modestly to 31.94. This suggests selling pressure has eased slightly, but momentum remains firmly bearish. Historical context: RSI has not been this low since the dataset began in February, indicating unprecedented weakness.

**MACD (-6.07):** Deeply negative and accelerating lower. The MACD has been negative since late March and is now at its most bearish reading in the dataset. The histogram (not shown but implied by the trend) is expanding negatively, confirming that bearish momentum is intensifying, not stabilizing. No bullish crossover is imminent.

**Interpretation:** Momentum indicators show a stock in free fall with no signs of stabilization. While RSI suggests short-term oversold conditions, the MACD confirms the trend is still firmly down. In strong downtrends, RSI can remain oversold for extended periods—this is not yet a reversal signal.

## Volatility & Risk Assessment

**ATR (5.64):** Volatility has spiked to elevated levels, reflecting panic selling. The ATR has increased from ~4.8 in late March to 5.64 currently, a 17.5% jump. This expansion in volatility is typical of capitulation phases but also increases risk for any position.

**Bollinger Bands:**
- **Upper Band (121.23):** 37% above current price
- **Lower Band (85.57):** Just 3.3% below current price
- **Current Position:** Price is hugging the lower band, indicating extreme downside momentum

The Bollinger Band width has contracted from March highs, but the recent selloff has pushed price to the lower band. Historically, when price touches the lower band in a downtrend, it often "walks the band" lower rather than bouncing immediately. The proximity to the lower band suggests limited downside in the very short term (support near $85), but not a reversal signal.

**Risk Implication:** Any long position faces elevated volatility risk. A 5.64 ATR implies daily swings of ~6.4% (1 ATR = ~6.4% of current price). Stop-loss placement must account for this noise.

## Volume Analysis

Volume patterns confirm distribution:
- **April 9-10:** Combined 100M shares traded (vs. ~15-20M average), indicating institutional capitulation
- **April 13 (today):** Volume has normalized to 21.7M, suggesting the panic phase may be exhausting

The volume spike on April 9-10 is a classic capitulation signature, but follow-through volume today is not confirming a reversal—it's simply less panicked. True bottoms typically require a volume spike followed by price stabilization or reversal on declining volume. We have the spike but not the stabilization.

## Macro Context Integration

The macro regime is **hostile to NOW**:

1. **Reflation Risk:** CPI reaccelerating (+0.87% MoM) and PPI surging (+2.08% MoM) argue for higher-for-longer rates. The 10Y yield at 4.29% is a headwind for high-duration growth stocks. NOW trades at a premium valuation (historically 10-15x sales), making it highly sensitive to discount rate changes.

2. **Fed Policy Trap:** The Fed has paused after 45bp of cuts, but the next move is likely hawkish if inflation persists. Front-end rates (2Y at 3.78%) are mispriced for reflation. If the Fed signals a pause or hike, growth stocks will face further multiple compression.

3. **Sector Rotation:** The macro regime favors cyclicals and value over growth. The "RISK-ON" classification is misleading—it's risk-on for commodities and value, not for high-multiple SaaS names like NOW. The VIX at 19.23 is complacent, suggesting the market hasn't fully priced reflation risk.

4. **Credit Spreads & Liquidity:** While credit spreads are neutral, the yield curve steepening (2Y-10Y +51bp) suggests growth expectations are normalizing. This is bearish for growth stocks that priced in perpetual low rates.

**Bottom Line:** NOW is a "long-duration growth" asset in a "rising real rate" environment. The macro setup argues for continued underperformance until either: (a) inflation data softens, allowing the Fed to resume cuts, or (b) NOW's valuation compresses enough to reflect the new rate regime.

## Trade Setup & Recommendation

**Bear Case (Base Case):**
- Price remains below all moving averages with no signs of reclaiming the 50 SMA (107.21)
- MACD is deeply negative with no bullish divergence
- Macro headwinds (reflation, Fed pause) are structural, not transient
- Institutional distribution (volume spike) suggests smart money is exiting
- Next support: $85 (lower Bollinger Band), then $80 psychological level

**Bull Case (Contrarian):**
- RSI at 31.94 is oversold; April 10 low (RSI 22.4) may mark a short-term capitulation
- Price is at the lower Bollinger Band, historically a tactical bounce zone
- Volume exhaustion on April 13 suggests selling pressure is waning
- If macro data surprises dovish (softer CPI), growth stocks could snap back violently

**Actionable Insight:**

**For Existing Longs:** Exit on any bounce toward $95-100 (10 EMA resistance). The risk/reward is unfavorable. A move back to $107 (50 SMA) would require a macro catalyst (Fed pivot, soft CPI) that is not currently priced in.

**For New Positions:** 
- **Avoid initiating longs** until price reclaims the 50 SMA ($107) and MACD turns positive. The trend is your enemy.
- **Tactical short-term trade (high risk):** A bounce to $92-95 is possible if RSI mean-reverts, but this is a scalp, not an investment. Use tight stops below $85.
- **Short opportunity:** If price fails to reclaim $95 on a bounce, consider shorting with a target of $80. The macro setup supports further downside.

**Stop-Loss Guidance:** Given ATR of 5.64, any position requires a stop at least 1.5x ATR away (~$8-9 range) to avoid noise. For longs, stop below $85 (lower band). For shorts, stop above $95 (10 EMA).
