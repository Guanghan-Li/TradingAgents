## Macro Regime Assessment

**Growth & Labor**: The economy shows resilient momentum. Nonfarm payrolls added 178k in March (+0.11% sequential), while unemployment fell to 4.30% (-0.10pp), signaling a tight labor market. GDP data is stale (Q4 2025), but the employment trend suggests continued expansion into Q2 2026.

**Inflation**: Headline CPI rose 0.87% month-over-month to 330.29 in March—an annualized pace near 11%, well above target. Producer prices surged 2.08% in February, indicating persistent pipeline pressures. This is a **reflationary regime** with sticky inflation.

**Fed Policy**: The effective Fed Funds rate has been stable at 3.64% since January after a 108bp easing cycle from October 2025. The Fed appears to be **on hold**, likely pausing to assess whether inflation will re-accelerate. With CPI running hot and labor tight, the next move is more likely a hike than a cut if inflation doesn't cool.

**Yield Curve**: The 2Y-10Y spread is +51bp (upward sloping), ruling out imminent recession fears. However, the curve is **bear-steepening**—10Y at 4.29% and 30Y at 4.90% reflect term premium and inflation risk. Front-end rates (3.66-3.78%) are anchored near the policy rate, but long-end yields price in higher-for-longer inflation or fiscal concerns.

**Market Sentiment**: VIX at 19.23 is modestly elevated but not distressed, suggesting cautious optimism rather than panic.

## Cross-Asset Implications for ANET

- **Equities (Growth/Tech)**: Rising long-end yields (4.29% 10Y) compress valuation multiples for duration-sensitive growth stocks. ANET, a high-growth networking play, faces headwinds from higher discount rates. However, strong nominal growth supports enterprise IT spending.
- **Credit**: Stable Fed policy and low recession risk support credit spreads, but rising yields increase refinancing costs for leveraged firms.
- **Dollar**: Hawkish Fed pause + higher real yields = dollar strength, which can pressure multinational revenue for US exporters (less relevant for ANET's domestic-heavy revenue).

## Key Risks

1. **Inflation Re-acceleration**: If CPI stays hot, the Fed may hike, shocking equities and steepening the curve further.
2. **Growth Slowdown**: Tight labor + high rates could trigger a delayed slowdown in H2 2026, hurting cyclical demand for networking capex.
3. **Fiscal/Term Premium Shock**: Long-end yields could spike if fiscal concerns or supply overwhelm demand, pressuring all risk assets.
