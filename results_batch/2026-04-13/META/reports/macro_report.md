## Macroeconomic Analysis for META (as of 2026-04-13)

### Regime Identification
**Mid-cycle expansion with inflation persistence.** The economy is growing (payrolls +178k, unemployment 4.3% and falling), but inflation is reaccelerating. The 2.08% sequential jump in PPI and 0.87% rise in CPI signal pricing pressure is building in the pipeline. The Fed has paused at 3.64% after cutting ~45bp from October 2025 through January 2026, suggesting they've reached a wait-and-see stance.

### Policy Direction
**On hold, skewed toward higher-for-longer.** The Fed's pause since January, combined with rising inflation prints, means the next move is more likely a hold or hike than a cut. If PPI/CPI continue this trajectory, the market should price out any remaining cut expectations for 2026. Policy uncertainty is elevated.

### Yield Curve & Recession Signal
**No recession signal.** The 2Y-10Y spread is +51bp (upward sloping), consistent with normal growth expectations. However, the 10Y at 4.29% is elevated and reflects term premium + inflation risk premium. The curve steepness is not bullish for duration-sensitive assets—it signals the market expects rates to stay higher.

### Cross-Asset Implications for META
1. **Rates pressure on multiples:** 10Y at 4.29% compresses the present value of distant cash flows. META, as a mega-cap growth/tech name, is sensitive to the risk-free rate. Higher-for-longer rates are a headwind to valuation.
2. **Inflation pass-through:** Rising PPI may squeeze margins if META cannot pass costs through (cloud infrastructure, data center energy, labor). However, META's pricing power in digital ads is strong.
3. **Consumer resilience:** Solid labor market (4.3% unemployment, rising payrolls) supports ad spending, which is META's core revenue driver.
4. **Volatility regime:** VIX at 19.23 is moderate—not complacent, not panicked. This suggests the market is pricing some uncertainty but not stress.

### Concrete Risks for Other Analysts
- **Inflation reacceleration risk:** If next CPI/PPI prints confirm the trend, the Fed may signal a hawkish tilt, spiking rates and compressing tech multiples.
- **Duration risk:** META's valuation is long-duration. A 10Y move from 4.29% to 4.50%+ would be a material headwind.
- **Policy error risk:** If the Fed holds too long and tips the economy into slowdown, ad spending (META's lifeblood) would contract.
- **Curve bear-steepening:** If the long end sells off further (30Y at 4.90% already), it signals inflation expectations are unanchored—bad for all risk assets.
