The following 8 indicators were selected to capture **trend structure, momentum recovery, volatility regime, and reversal signals** in a post-selloff environment:

**Why these?** META just experienced a high-volatility selloff. We need to assess: (1) Is the bounce sustainable? (2) Where are the key resistance levels? (3) Is volatility contracting (stabilization) or expanding (more pain)? This suite answers all three.

### 2. Momentum: Recovering, But Not Confirmed

**MACD:** -4.30 (improving from -26.99 on March 30)  
**RSI:** 57.51 (neutral, up from 22.66 oversold on March 27)

**Interpretation:**

This suggests **selling pressure is exhausted** and buyers are stepping in. The RSI confirms this: it was deeply oversold at 22.66 (extreme fear) and has now normalized to 57.51 (neutral). This is a **classic V-shaped reversal pattern** in momentum.

**Actionable Insight:**  
- **Bullish Trigger:** MACD crosses above zero (not there yet, but close). RSI holds above 50 (currently 57.51, so confirmed).
- **Bearish Risk:** If RSI fails to hold 50 and rolls over, it signals the bounce is a dead-cat bounce. Watch for divergence: if price makes a higher high but RSI makes a lower high, that's a sell signal.

**Current Read:** Momentum is **constructive but not confirmed**. The bounce is real, but it's not yet a new uptrend.

**Bollinger Bands:** Upper $654.87, Lower $529.34 (width: $125.53)

However, the ATR is declining, which is a **stabilization signal**. The Bollinger Bands are wide, reflecting the recent volatility spike, but price is now in the middle of the bands ($629.82 vs. midpoint ~$592). This suggests the panic is over, but the market hasn't decided on direction yet.

**Actionable Insight:**  
- **Stop-Loss Guidance:** Use 1.5x ATR = $31.25. For a long entry at $630, place a stop at $599 (just below the 10 EMA and 1.5 ATR).
- **Breakout Watch:** If price breaks above the upper Bollinger Band ($654.87), it's a volatility breakout—often a strong continuation signal. Conversely, a break below the lower band ($529.34) would signal renewed selling.

**Current Read:** Volatility is **contracting but still elevated**. This is a stabilization phase, not a low-volatility grind.

### 4. Price Action: Bounce Off Lower Bollinger Band

**Key Observation:**  
On March 27, META closed at $525.72, just above the lower Bollinger Band ($529.34 at that time). This was a **capitulation event**—price hit the lower band, RSI hit 22.66 (extreme oversold), and volume spiked to 30M+ shares. Since then, price has rallied +20% in 12 trading days. This is a **textbook mean-reversion setup**.

**Actionable Insight:**  
- **Mean Reversion Logic:** Price tends to revert to the Bollinger midpoint (20 SMA) after touching the bands. The midpoint is currently around $592, and price has already exceeded it ($629.82). This suggests the mean reversion is **overdone** and price is now testing the upper half of the range.
- **Next Move:** If price breaks above the upper band ($654.87), it's a breakout. If it fails at the 50 SMA ($631.10) and falls back toward the midpoint, it's a failed breakout and a short opportunity.
