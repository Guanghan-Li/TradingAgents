## Indicator Selection Rationale

For MAIN's current market environment, I've selected 8 complementary indicators that provide a multi-dimensional view of trend, momentum, volatility, and potential reversal zones:

1. **10 EMA** - Short-term momentum and immediate trend direction
2. **50 SMA** - Medium-term trend and dynamic support/resistance
3. **200 SMA** - Long-term trend benchmark and structural health
4. **MACD** - Momentum divergence and trend change signals
5. **RSI** - Overbought/oversold conditions and momentum strength
6. **Bollinger Upper/Lower Bands** - Volatility envelope and mean-reversion zones
7. **ATR** - Volatility measurement for risk management and position sizing

These indicators avoid redundancy while covering trend (moving averages), momentum (MACD, RSI), volatility (Bollinger Bands, ATR), and provide both lagging confirmation and leading reversal signals.

## Technical Picture: Downtrend with Early Stabilization Signals

### Price Structure & Trend Analysis

MAIN has experienced a **-10.4% decline** from the February 13th high of $60.12 to the current level of $53.85. The stock is trading:
- **1.4% above** the 10 EMA ($53.45) - first close above this average in recent sessions
- **4.4% below** the 50 SMA ($56.30) - significant resistance zone
- **8.3% below** the 200 SMA ($58.70) - confirming intermediate-term downtrend

**Critical observation**: The 50 SMA has crossed below the 200 SMA (death cross pattern), with the 50 SMA at $56.30 vs 200 SMA at $58.70. This is a classic bearish configuration that typically persists for weeks to months. The gap between these averages is widening, confirming downtrend momentum.

### Momentum Indicators: Divergence Emerging

**MACD Analysis**: The MACD line has improved from -1.51 (March 31st) to -0.79 (April 13th), a **+47% reduction in bearish momentum**. This is significant - while still negative, the rate of improvement suggests selling pressure is exhausting. The MACD has been making higher lows since March 27th (-1.38) even as price made lower lows, creating a **bullish divergence** - a potential early reversal signal.

**RSI Analysis**: The last available RSI reading (April 10th) was 43.11, up from the oversold territory of 26.49 on March 27th. The RSI has climbed **+62% from its low**, moving from deeply oversold toward neutral. Notably, RSI never broke below 26, suggesting strong underlying support and limited panic selling. The current reading below 50 indicates bears still control momentum, but the trajectory is constructive.

### Volatility & Mean Reversion Setup

**Bollinger Bands**: Current price ($53.85) sits at the **58th percentile** of the Bollinger Band range (lower band $51.15, upper band $56.08). The bands have contracted significantly from their March widths:
- March 31st band width: $7.19
- April 13th band width: $4.93
- **-31% contraction** in volatility envelope

This compression typically precedes a volatility expansion and directional move. The price is no longer hugging the lower band (as it was March 27-30), suggesting the downside momentum has paused.

**ATR Analysis**: ATR has declined from 1.55 (April 6th) to 1.44 (April 10th), confirming the volatility compression. For position sizing, a 1.44 ATR suggests stop-losses should be placed at least 1.5-2x ATR ($2.16-$2.88) away from entry to avoid normal noise.

### Volume & Conviction

Recent volume has normalized to 450k-860k shares after the March 12th spike (1.7M shares) that marked the breakdown below $55. The lack of follow-through volume on subsequent down days suggests **exhaustion of sellers** rather than capitulation. April's volume profile shows no panic, which is constructive for a potential base formation.

## Macro Regime Integration

The **RISK-ON** regime (+3/6 score) with falling VIX and cyclicals outperforming creates a favorable backdrop for risk assets. However, MAIN's -10% decline occurred *during* this risk-on period, indicating **idiosyncratic weakness** rather than broad market pressure.

**Key macro considerations**:
- **Fed on hold at 3.64%**: Higher-for-longer rates pressure BDCs (Business Development Companies like MAIN) through compressed net interest margins and credit risk
- **Sticky inflation (CPI +0.87% MoM)**: Reduces real yields on MAIN's dividend, which is a key attraction for income investors
- **Upward-sloping yield curve (2Y-10Y +51bp)**: Soft landing priced in, but long-end rates at 4.90% create competition for MAIN's yield

MAIN's sector (BDCs) is rate-sensitive and credit-sensitive. The late-cycle environment with inflation persistence is **structurally challenging** for this name, explaining the underperformance despite the risk-on regime.
