**Macroeconomic Analysis for AMD Trading Decision**

**Regime Identification: Mid-Cycle Pause with Inflation Watch**

The Federal Reserve has delivered 45 basis points of cuts from October 2025 through January 2026, bringing the Fed Funds rate to 3.64%, where it has remained stable for three consecutive months (Jan-Mar 2026). This pause signals the Fed is monitoring inflation persistence before committing to further easing.

**Key Macro Signals:**

**Inflation Backdrop:** CPI rose 0.87% month-over-month in March, while PPI surged 2.08% sequentially in February. Producer-level inflation is running hot and will likely feed through to consumer prices, constraining the Fed's ability to ease further in the near term. This is a yellow flag for rate-sensitive sectors.

**Labor Market:** Unemployment fell to 4.30% with payrolls adding 178k jobs in March. The labor market remains healthy, supporting consumer spending and economic resilience. No signs of deterioration that would force the Fed to accelerate cuts.

**Yield Curve:** The 2Y-10Y spread is +0.51 percentage points with a normal upward slope across the entire curve. This indicates the bond market expects continued growth and no imminent recession. The 10Y yield at 4.29% reflects term premium and growth expectations, not distress.

**Policy Path:** The Fed has paused after a modest easing cycle. With inflation still elevated and labor strong, the next move is more likely to be data-dependent patience rather than aggressive cuts. Markets should not price in a return to zero rates.

**Volatility:** VIX at 19.23 is in the "normal" range—above complacency but below panic. Risk appetite is present but measured.

**Cross-Asset Implications for AMD:**

AMD is a cyclical semiconductor stock highly sensitive to:
1. **Growth expectations** (positive: normal yield curve, healthy labor)
2. **Rate policy** (mixed: easing cycle paused, but rates still restrictive by historical standards)
3. **Risk appetite** (neutral: VIX moderate, no euphoria or fear)
4. **Inflation** (negative: persistent inflation limits Fed flexibility, raises input costs)

**Concrete Risks:**
- **Upside risk:** If inflation cools faster than expected, the Fed could resume cuts, boosting cyclical tech.
- **Downside risk:** If PPI inflation persists or accelerates, the Fed may hold rates higher for longer, pressuring valuations for long-duration growth stocks like AMD.
- **Geopolitical/supply chain:** Not captured in this macro data but critical for semiconductors.

**Macro Verdict:** The environment is **cautiously supportive** for cyclical tech. Growth is intact, the curve is normal, and the Fed has eased. However, inflation persistence is a binding constraint. AMD can perform well if earnings and AI demand narratives dominate, but macro is not a strong tailwind—it's neutral to slightly positive.
