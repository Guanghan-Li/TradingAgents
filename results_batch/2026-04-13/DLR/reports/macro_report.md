**MACRO REGIME: Reflation / No-Landing**

The Fed has delivered ~45bp of cuts from October 2025 through January 2026, then paused at 3.64%. The labor market remains firm (unemployment fell to 4.30%, payrolls +178k), but inflation is reaccelerating: CPI rose 0.87% MoM in March (~11% annualized), and PPI spiked 2.08% MoM in February. This combination—solid growth, sticky inflation, and a Fed on hold—defines a reflationary regime.

**Yield Curve & Rates:**  
The 2Y-10Y spread is +51bp, a normal upward slope. Front-end yields (3.68%) are anchored near the policy rate, but the 10Y (4.29%) and 30Y (4.90%) reflect term premium and inflation expectations. The curve steepness signals growth optimism but also warns that the Fed's cutting cycle may be over. Real rates remain positive and restrictive for rate-sensitive assets.

**Policy Path:**  
The Fed is likely on hold through mid-2026. With unemployment falling and inflation reaccelerating, the next move is more likely a hike than a cut if PPI/CPI trends persist. The market is pricing modest easing expectations (front-end yields below policy rate), which could reprice violently if the Fed signals a hawkish pause or hike.

**Cross-Asset Implications:**  
- **Equities:** Growth stocks and long-duration assets face headwinds from higher real rates. Cyclicals and value may outperform if growth holds.  
- **Fixed Income:** Duration is risky; the curve may bear-steepen if inflation stays hot. Front-end bills offer carry with less risk.  
- **REITs (DLR context):** Higher-for-longer rates pressure valuations via discount rates. However, data center demand is structurally strong (AI, cloud), and inflation can support rent growth. Net effect: modest headwind from rates, offset by demand tailwinds.  
- **Credit:** Spreads are tight (VIX 19.23 signals complacency). A Fed pivot to hawkish or inflation surprise could widen spreads.

**Key Risks:**  
1. **Inflation persistence:** PPI +2.08% MoM is a red flag. If it feeds into CPI, the Fed may hike.  
2. **Curve bear-steepening:** If long-end yields rise faster than short-end, duration assets (including REITs) suffer.  
3. **Policy error:** The Fed may have cut too much too fast. A reversal would shock markets.  
4. **Complacency:** VIX at 19 suggests low fear. A volatility spike could trigger deleveraging.
