# Digital Realty Trust (DLR) – Scenario & Catalyst Analysis

## Executive Summary

Digital Realty Trust is a data center REIT trading at **$191.05** (near 52-week high) with a **53.2× P/E** and **2.58% yield**. The company is executing a **S$7B Asia-Pacific AI expansion**, opening innovation labs in Japan, and benefiting from structural AI/cloud demand. Recent analyst upgrades (Morgan Stanley to $210, Cantor Fitzgerald OW $211) reflect optimism, but elevated valuation and **81.7× debt-to-equity** create downside risk if leasing velocity disappoints or rates stay higher for longer.

---

## Scenario Map

### 🐂 **Bull Case** (35% probability)
**Thesis:** AI infrastructure buildout accelerates globally; DLR's Singapore/Japan expansion captures outsized share of hyperscaler demand. Fed cuts 50–75 bps by year-end, compressing cap rates and driving REIT multiple expansion. Leasing spreads remain strong (mid-single digits), and the company pre-leases 60%+ of new capacity before delivery.

**Valuation Implication:** Price target $210–220 (15–20% upside). Forward P/E compresses to 45× on 2027E earnings as growth re-rates the multiple.

**Signposts:**
- Pre-leasing announcements for Singapore AI campus (>50% committed)
- Fed pivot: 2 cuts by Q3 2026
- Hyperscaler capex guidance raises (MSFT, GOOGL, META earnings)
- DLR guides FY26 FFO growth >8%

---

### ⚖️ **Base Case** (50% probability)
**Thesis:** AI demand is real but lumpy; DLR fills new capacity over 18–24 months vs. 12. Fed holds rates at 3.5–3.75% through mid-2026, keeping financing costs elevated. The company maintains investment-grade credit but FCF is pressured by S$7B capex cycle. Stock trades sideways in $180–200 range as market awaits leasing proof points.

**Valuation Implication:** Fair value $190–195 (flat to +3%). Multiple stays at 55–60× forward P/E until visibility improves.

**Signposts:**
- Quarterly leasing updates show steady but unspectacular absorption (15–20 MW/quarter)
- Fed holds rates through June FOMC
- Debt-to-EBITDA stays below 6.5×
- Dividend coverage remains >80% of FFO

---

### 🐻 **Bear Case** (15% probability)
**Thesis:** AI hype cycle peaks; hyperscalers slow data center commitments as model training efficiency improves faster than expected. DLR's levered balance sheet (81.7× D/E) becomes a liability if EBITDA growth stalls. Higher-for-longer rates (Fed funds >4% through 2026) pressure REIT valuations. Stock de-rates to 40× P/E on growth concerns.

**Valuation Implication:** Downside to $155–165 (15–20% decline). Dividend cut risk emerges if FCF/FFO coverage falls below 70%.

**Signposts:**
- Hyperscaler capex guidance cuts (MSFT, GOOGL reduce data center spend)
- DLR reports sub-10 MW net absorption in any quarter
- Fed hikes or holds above 4% at June/September FOMC
- Debt refinancing at >6% weighted average cost
