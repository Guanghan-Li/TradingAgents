## Macroeconomic Regime Assessment (as of 2026-04-13)

**Regime: Reflationary Pause with Reaccelerating Inflation Risk**

The Federal Reserve executed a 45 bp easing cycle from October 2025 (4.09%) to January 2026 (3.64%), then paused. That pause now looks prescient: **inflation is reaccelerating sharply**. CPI rose 0.87% month-over-month in March, and PPI surged 2.08% in February—both well above the pace consistent with the Fed's 2% target. Meanwhile, the labor market remains tight: unemployment fell to 4.30% and payrolls added 178k jobs in March.

The Treasury curve is flat (+50 bp 2s10s), signaling that the market does not expect robust growth but also sees no imminent recession. The VIX at 19.23 suggests complacency, not stress.

**Policy Path:** The Fed is likely on hold for the foreseeable future. If inflation continues to run hot, the next move could be a hike, not a cut. The market has not priced this risk.

**Cross-Asset Implications:**
- **Rates:** Front-end yields are anchored near 3.70%, but if the Fed signals hawkishness, short rates could rise. The curve may steepen bearishly (long end sells off more than front).
- **Equities:** Reflationary growth with sticky inflation is a headwind for valuations. Sectors sensitive to rates (tech, growth) face pressure. Value and commodities may outperform.
- **Credit:** Spreads are tight (VIX low), but if the Fed tightens, credit could widen. High-yield is vulnerable.
- **FX & EM:** A hawkish Fed pivot would strengthen the dollar and pressure emerging markets. EM equities and currencies are at risk if U.S. real rates rise.
- **Commodities:** Hot PPI and solid payrolls suggest demand-driven inflation. Energy and industrial metals may benefit, but a Fed hike would cap upside.

**Key Risks:**
1. **Inflation persistence:** If CPI and PPI remain elevated, the Fed may need to hike, shocking markets.
2. **Dollar strength:** A hawkish Fed would lift the dollar, pressuring EM and commodities.
3. **Growth slowdown:** The flat curve and moderate PMI (data unavailable) suggest growth is not accelerating. If payrolls weaken, the Fed is stuck between inflation and recession.
4. **Geopolitical/supply shocks:** PPI surge could reflect supply-side pressures, not just demand. Watch energy and goods prices.

**For VWO (Emerging Markets):** This macro backdrop is **cautiously negative**. EM assets typically underperform when the Fed is on hold or tightening, especially if the dollar strengthens. The reflationary impulse (solid payrolls, hot inflation) is not strong enough to offset the risk of higher U.S. real rates. EM equities face headwinds unless global growth accelerates or the Fed pivots dovish—neither is likely near-term.
