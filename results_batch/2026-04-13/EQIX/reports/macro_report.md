## Macroeconomic Analysis for EQIX (as of 2026-04-13)

### Regime Identification: Late-Cycle Pause with Inflation Persistence

The Fed has delivered 45bp of cuts since October 2025, bringing the effective funds rate to 3.64%, but has now paused for three consecutive months (Jan-Mar 2026). This pause coincides with sticky inflation: CPI rose 0.87% month-over-month in March, an annualized pace near 11% if sustained—well above the Fed's 2% target.

The Treasury curve is upward-sloping (+51bp 2Y-10Y), signaling no imminent recession fears, but the curve is relatively flat by historical standards. The 10Y at 4.29% and 30Y at 4.90% suggest the market is pricing a higher-for-longer terminal rate, not aggressive easing.

**Key regime characteristics:**
- **Growth**: GDP at $31.4T (Q4 2025) suggests expansion continues, but we lack recent payrolls and PMI data to confirm momentum.
- **Inflation**: Reaccelerating. The March CPI jump is a red flag; the Fed cannot resume cuts until this cools.
- **Policy**: On hold. The Fed is data-dependent and likely concerned about reigniting inflation if it cuts further.
- **Volatility**: VIX at 19.23 is elevated vs. the long-term average (~15), indicating moderate uncertainty but not panic.

### Policy Path: Higher-for-Longer, Cuts Delayed

The Fed's recent pause after a modest easing cycle suggests:
1. **No near-term cuts**: With CPI running hot, the next move is more likely a hold or even a hike if inflation persists.
2. **Terminal rate uncertainty**: The market is pricing ~3.6% as neutral, but if inflation stays elevated, the Fed may need to hold above 4%.
3. **Data dependency**: Missing unemployment and payrolls data is critical. If labor remains tight, wage-driven inflation could force the Fed's hand.

### Cross-Asset Implications

**For EQIX (Data Center REIT):**
- **Rates sensitivity**: REITs are negatively correlated with rates. The 10Y at 4.29% and a flat-to-higher policy path is a headwind for valuation multiples.
- **Growth sensitivity**: Data center demand is tied to cloud/AI capex, which is resilient in expansion but vulnerable if rates choke off tech spending.
- **Inflation pass-through**: EQIX has pricing power in long-term contracts, but rising costs (energy, construction) may compress margins if inflation persists.
- **Curve steepness**: A steeper curve (+51bp) is modestly positive for REITs, as it signals growth expectations, but the absolute level of rates matters more.

**Broader cross-asset view:**
- **Equities**: Higher-for-longer rates pressure growth/tech multiples. Defensive sectors and value may outperform.
- **Credit**: Spreads are tight (VIX 19 suggests complacency), but if the Fed holds longer, refinancing risk rises for leveraged issuers.
- **Commodities**: Sticky inflation supports energy/materials, but a strong dollar (implied by higher US rates) is a headwind.

### Concrete Risks

1. **Inflation reacceleration**: If the March CPI spike is not transitory, the Fed may hike, shocking risk assets.
2. **Labor market surprise**: Missing payrolls/unemployment data is a blind spot. A hot labor market would confirm inflation persistence; a weak print could trigger recession fears.
3. **Curve inversion risk**: If the Fed holds too long and growth slows, the curve could invert again, signaling recession.
4. **Geopolitical/supply shocks**: Energy or supply chain disruptions could reignite inflation, forcing the Fed to tighten further.
5. **Tech capex slowdown**: If higher rates crimp AI/cloud spending, EQIX's demand outlook weakens.

### Bottom Line for EQIX

**Macro backdrop is mixed-to-negative for data center REITs:**
- **Bearish**: Higher-for-longer rates (10Y at 4.29%) compress REIT multiples. Sticky inflation limits Fed flexibility.
- **Neutral**: Growth is still positive (normal curve, GDP expansion), supporting demand for data center capacity.
- **Risk**: If inflation persists or labor data surprises hot, the Fed may hike, triggering a sharp repricing in rate-sensitive equities.

**Recommendation for investment committee**: Incorporate a **higher-for-longer rates scenario** into EQIX valuation. Monitor April CPI and payrolls closely; a second hot inflation print would be a strong sell signal for REITs. If inflation cools, the Fed could resume cuts in H2 2026, providing a tailwind. Current macro regime favors caution on rate-sensitive names like EQIX.
