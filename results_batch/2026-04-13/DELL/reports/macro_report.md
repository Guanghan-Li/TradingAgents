## Macroeconomic Analysis (as of 2026-04-13)

**Regime Identification: Late-Cycle Expansion with Disinflation in Progress**

The macro backdrop shows a resilient economy navigating the final stages of a tightening cycle:

**Growth & Labor:** Nonfarm payrolls rose 178k (+0.11% MoM) in March with unemployment ticking down to 4.30%, signaling continued labor market tightness. This is a Goldilocks setup—strong enough to support corporate earnings but not overheating.

**Inflation:** Headline CPI rose 0.87% MoM (March), while PPI surged 2.08% (February). The PPI spike is concerning and may flow through to corporate input costs, particularly for hardware manufacturers like DELL. Disinflation is underway but uneven.

**Yield Curve & Policy Path:** The Treasury curve shows a shallow inversion at the front (6M: 3.71% > 1Y: 3.68%) but steepens aggressively beyond 2Y (30Y: 4.90%). This shape suggests:
- Markets expect the Fed to hold or cut modestly in 2026
- Recession fears have faded (steepening = growth optimism)
- Term premium is returning to longer maturities

The absence of Fed Funds data limits precision, but the 2Y yield at 3.78% implies the policy rate is near terminal and cuts are priced for late 2026.

**Risk Sentiment:** VIX at 19.23 (down 1.33%) reflects moderate, stable risk appetite—supportive for cyclical equities.

**Cross-Asset Implications:**
- **Equities:** Late-cycle steepening + falling VIX = risk-on. Cyclicals and tech should outperform defensives.
- **Credit:** Tight labor + disinflation = benign default environment.
- **FX:** Dollar likely range-bound; Fed on hold while growth holds.
- **Commodities:** PPI strength suggests input cost pressures persist.

**Key Risks:**
1. **PPI pass-through:** If producer inflation (2.08% MoM) flows to consumer prices, the Fed may delay cuts, tightening financial conditions.
2. **Curve re-inversion:** A growth shock could flatten/invert the curve again, signaling recession.
3. **Labor market crack:** Unemployment at 4.30% is low but rising sequentially—watch for acceleration.

**DELL-Specific Macro View:**
- **Positive:** Steepening curve + stable growth supports capex spending (enterprise IT demand).
- **Negative:** Elevated PPI threatens hardware margins; component costs may stay sticky.
- **Neutral:** Fed on hold = no immediate rate relief, but no further tightening either.
