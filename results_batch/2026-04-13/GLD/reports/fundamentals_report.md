# Fundamental Analysis Report: GLD (SPDR Gold Shares)
**Report Date:** April 13, 2026

## Executive Summary

GLD (SPDR Gold Shares) is the world's largest physically-backed gold exchange-traded fund, designed to track the price of gold bullion. As an ETF rather than an operating company, GLD does not generate revenue, earnings, or cash flows in the traditional sense. Instead, its value derives directly from its holdings of physical gold stored in vaults.

## Company Profile

**Name:** SPDR Gold Shares  
**Ticker:** GLD  
**Asset Class:** Commodity ETF (Gold)  
**Market Capitalization:** $113.3 billion  
**Structure:** Grantor trust holding physical gold bars

GLD provides investors with exposure to gold prices without the complexities of physical ownership, storage, or insurance. Each share represents a fractional ownership of the trust's gold holdings.

## Current Valuation Metrics

**Price to Book Ratio:** 2.56  
This elevated P/B ratio indicates GLD is trading at a significant premium to its book value of $170.02 per share. For a gold ETF, this premium typically reflects:

- Convenience premium over physical gold ownership

**Dividend Yield:** 0.0%  
As expected for a commodity ETF, GLD does not pay dividends. Gold itself generates no income, and the trust structure passes through no distributions beyond potential capital gains.
