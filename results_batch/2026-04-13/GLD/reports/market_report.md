# GLD Technical Analysis Report
**Date:** 2026-04-13 | **Price:** $435.36 | **Regime:** RISK-ON

## Executive Summary

GLD is in a **corrective consolidation phase** following a sharp 18% decline from the March 2 peak ($490.00) to the March 26 low ($400.64). Price has stabilized in the $427-$438 range over the past week, but remains structurally weak with price trading **3.2% below the 50-day SMA** ($449.97) while the 10-day EMA ($432.40) has just crossed above price—a sign of fading short-term momentum. The RISK-ON macro backdrop (falling VIX, cyclicals leading, Fed on pause) is fundamentally bearish for gold as a defensive asset.

## Trend Structure: Bearish Intermediate, Neutral Short-Term

### Multi-Timeframe Alignment
- **200 SMA ($382.70):** Price is +13.8% above, confirming the long-term uptrend remains intact.
- **50 SMA ($449.97):** Price is -3.2% below. The 50 SMA has been declining since early April, signaling deteriorating intermediate-term momentum.
- **10 EMA ($432.40):** Price closed at $435.36, just +0.7% above the 10 EMA. The 10 EMA has flattened after a sharp recovery from $421 on March 30, indicating short-term momentum is stalling.

**Key Observation:** The 50 SMA is declining toward the 10 EMA. If the 10 EMA fails to reclaim the 50 SMA decisively, a "death cross" setup will form, typically a bearish continuation signal. The last time price was this far below the 50 SMA was mid-February, which preceded the rally to $490—but that rally occurred in a different macro context (pre-tariff shock, higher geopolitical risk).

- **Actionable Insight:** Watch for a MACD crossover above zero. If MACD crosses above its signal line (not provided, but implied by the improving histogram), it would confirm short-term bullish momentum. However, in a RISK-ON regime, gold rallies tend to be short-lived unless accompanied by a VIX spike or dollar weakness.

- **Current RSI:** 48.99 (as of 4/13)

## Macro-Technical Conflict: Why Gold Is Struggling

The RISK-ON regime is fundamentally at odds with gold's safe-haven profile:
- **Falling VIX:** Risk appetite is rising, reducing demand for defensive assets.
- **Cyclicals Leading Defensives:** Investors are rotating into growth, not hedges.
- **Fed on Pause:** With the Fed Funds rate stable at 3.64% and no further cuts priced, the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding gold is rising.
- **Steep Yield Curve:** The +124 bps curve slope signals no recession fear, removing a key tailwind for gold.

**Critical Missing Data:** Without CPI, we cannot calculate real rates—the primary driver of gold. If inflation has cooled to ~2%, real rates are positive (~1.5-2.0%), which is bearish for gold. If inflation remains sticky above 3%, real rates are near zero, which is neutral-to-bullish.
