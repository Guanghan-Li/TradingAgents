# ASML Segment Analysis: Monopolistic EUV Franchise Drives Durable Economics

## Business Unit Decomposition

ASML operates a **highly concentrated** business model centered on lithography systems for semiconductor manufacturing. While the company does not break out detailed segment revenue in public filings, industry analysis and management commentary reveal three core revenue streams:

**1. New Systems Sales (~65-70% of revenue)**
- **EUV Systems**: Leading-edge extreme ultraviolet lithography machines ($150-200M per unit) with **zero direct competition**. Critical for sub-7nm chip production. Customers: TSMC, Samsung, Intel.
- **DUV Systems**: Deep ultraviolet immersion and dry lithography for mature nodes ($30-80M per unit). Facing gradual commoditization but still essential for 28nm+ production.

**2. Installed Base Management (~25-30% of revenue)**
- Service contracts, spare parts, upgrades, and field operations. **Highest margin segment** (~70%+ gross margin) with recurring revenue characteristics. Growing as installed base expands (1,000+ systems globally).

**3. Applications & Development (~5% of revenue)**
- Customer co-development, process optimization, and training. Strategic positioning tool rather than profit center.

## Segment Economics & Concentration Risk

**Margin Profile**: Gross margin expanded to **52.8%** (Q4'25) from **51.7%** (Q4'24), driven by:
- EUV mix shift (EUV carries 55-60% gross margin vs. 45-50% for DUV)
- Installed base growth (service revenue at 70%+ margin)
- Pricing power in monopolistic EUV franchise

**Capital Intensity**: Moderate. R&D-intensive (13-15% of revenue) but asset-light manufacturing model (outsourced component production). Free cash flow of **$10.9B** demonstrates strong cash conversion.

**Cyclicality**: **High**. Semiconductor capex cycles drive lumpiness. Q3'25 revenue of $7.5B vs. Q4'25 $9.7B shows quarterly volatility. However, EUV backlog provides 12-18 month visibility.

**Concentration Risks**:
- **Customer**: Top 3 customers (TSMC, Samsung, Intel) represent ~80% of EUV demand
- **Geography**: China exposure (~30-40% of revenue historically) faces export control headwinds
- **Technology**: EUV monopoly creates regulatory/geopolitical target risk

## Value Driver Map: AI Capex Cycle Meets Export Constraints

**Near-term catalysts (Q2-Q4 2026)**:
1. **AI-driven logic capex**: TSMC, Intel ramping 2nm/18A capacity → EUV system orders accelerating. Evidence: News mentions "high-flying AI stock" positioning.
2. **Memory recovery**: HBM (high-bandwidth memory) production requires advanced EUV for logic die → Samsung/SK Hynix orders resuming.
3. **Q1 earnings (April 15)**: Consensus expects guidance raise based on AI demand. News: "Mark your calendars for April 15."

**Medium-term headwinds (2026-2027)**:
1. **China export controls**: Tightening restrictions on DUV sales to China → 10-15% revenue headwind. Partially offset by non-China demand.
2. **Customer inventory digestion**: Post-2023 overbuild → some DUV demand deferred to 2027.

**Long-term structural drivers (2027+)**:
1. **High-NA EUV transition**: Next-generation $350M+ systems for sub-2nm nodes → ASP expansion and installed base growth.
2. **Geopolitical diversification**: US/EU fab buildouts (CHIPS Act, EU Chips Act) → geographic revenue rebalancing.
