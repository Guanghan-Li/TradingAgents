# ASML Scenario & Catalyst Analysis
**Instrument:** ASML | **Analysis Date:** 2026-04-13

## Executive Summary

ASML sits at a critical inflection point heading into Q1 earnings (April 15). The stock trades at 51.5× TTM earnings after recovering from $614 lows to near $1,485 current levels. Three scenarios dominate the forward outlook, anchored by AI-driven lithography demand, geopolitical export controls, and customer capex cycle timing.

---

## Scenario Framework

### 🐂 Bull Case (35% probability)
**Thesis:** AI infrastructure buildout drives sustained EUV tool demand through 2027. ASML beats Q1 on backlog conversion and raises full-year guidance. China headwinds prove transient as Taiwan/Korea customers accelerate orders.

**Valuation Implication:** 60× forward PE justified by 25%+ EPS CAGR → $1,800–2,000 target.

**Key Signposts:**
- Q1 bookings >€6B (vs. €5.2B consensus)
- 2026 revenue guidance raised to €35B+ (from €32–33B prior)
- TSMC announces incremental N2/N3 capacity expansion
- China DUV sales stabilize at €3–4B/year floor

---

### ⚖️ Base Case (45% probability)
**Thesis:** ASML delivers in-line Q1 results. Full-year outlook remains €32–33B revenue with 2H weighting. EUV demand is solid but not explosive; China DUV sales decline 20–30% YoY due to export restrictions. Margins compress 100–150bps on mix.

**Valuation Implication:** 45–50× forward PE → $1,400–1,550 range-bound through mid-2026.

**Key Signposts:**
- Q1 revenue €5.8–6.2B, in-line with Street
- Gross margin 51–52% (down from 53% in 2025)
- Management flags 2H backend loading due to tool delivery timing
- No major customer capex cuts announced

---

### 🐻 Bear Case (20% probability)
**Thesis:** Semiconductor capex cycle rolls over as AI infrastructure spending pauses. ASML misses Q1 and guides down for 2026. Geopolitical tensions escalate, triggering broader China tool ban. Customer inventory digestion extends into 2027.

**Valuation Implication:** 35–40× forward PE → $1,000–1,200 retest of 2025 lows.

**Key Signposts:**
- Q1 revenue miss (<€5.5B) with bookings <€4B
- 2026 guidance cut to €28–30B
- Intel or Samsung announces capex reduction >20%
- New U.S./EU export restrictions on DUV tools to China

## Invalidation Triggers

### Bull Case Invalidated If:
- **Q1 bookings <€4.5B** → Demand inflection thesis breaks
- **TSMC cuts 2026 capex by >15%** → Leading-edge cycle weakening
- **Gross margin <50%** → Mix deterioration worse than expected
- **Evidence:** Watch for customer earnings commentary on tool delivery delays or order pushouts

### Base Case Invalidated If:
- **Q1 revenue variance >10% vs. guidance** → Execution risk or demand shock
- **New geopolitical restrictions announced** → Shifts to Bear scenario
- **EUV bookings >€8B in a single quarter** → Shifts to Bull scenario
- **Evidence:** Monitor U.S. Commerce Dept announcements and ASML's weekly buyback disclosures (proxy for management confidence)

### Bear Case Invalidated If:
- **Q1 bookings exceed €6B** → Demand resilience stronger than feared
- **China DUV sales stabilize above €4B/year** → Geopolitical risk overstated
- **AI capex commentary from hyperscalers remains robust** → Downstream demand intact
- **Evidence:** Track Microsoft, Google, Meta capex guidance; Samsung/Intel foundry utilization rates

---

## Macro Overlay

- **Fed Policy:** Neutral at 3.64%, curve normal (2Y-10Y +51bps) → supportive for growth equities but no tailwind from easing
- **Inflation:** CPI +0.87% MoM (March) → still elevated, limits multiple expansion
- **VIX:** 19.23 → moderate volatility, not crisis mode
- **Implication:** ASML's 51× PE is vulnerable to risk-off rotation if earnings disappoint, but macro is not a primary driver

---

## Recommendation for Investment Committee

**Lean HOLD into April 15 earnings.** Risk/reward is balanced:
- **Upside:** Bull case delivers +20–25% if AI demand thesis confirmed
- **Downside:** Bear case risks -25–30% if cycle concerns resurface

**Tactical Playbook:**
- If Q1 beats with raised guidance → upgrade to BUY, target $1,750
- If Q1 in-line but 2H guidance soft → maintain HOLD, reassess in July
- If Q1 misses or bookings <€4B → downgrade to SELL, exit on bounce

**Position Sizing:** Limit to 3–5% of portfolio given binary event risk and geopolitical overhang.
