## Volatility & Risk Management

**Bollinger Bands (Upper: $1,487.24 | Lower: $1,242.11):**  
Price is trading just **$2.40 below the upper band**, suggesting the stock is approaching short-term overbought conditions. The band width is **$245.13**, indicating elevated volatility—typical after a sharp V-shaped recovery. 

**Tactical Implication:** Prices often "walk the band" in strong trends, so a touch of the upper band is not an automatic sell signal. However, given the rapid 18% rally, a pullback to the middle band (20 SMA, approximately $1,365) or the lower band ($1,242) would be a normal retracement. The middle band is rising, which is bullish.

**ATR (55.56):**  
The Average True Range has declined from a peak of $59.79 on April 8 to $55.56 today, suggesting volatility is compressing as the rally matures. This is typical behavior after a panic low—initial volatility spikes, then contracts as the trend stabilizes.

**Stop-Loss Guidance:** Use ATR to set risk parameters. A 1x ATR stop from current price would be $1,484.84 - $55.56 = **$1,429.28**. A 1.5x ATR stop (more conservative) would be **$1,401.50**, just below the 10 EMA. For swing traders, the 50 SMA at $1,392.94 is a logical stop-loss level.
