# SMCI Technical Analysis Report
**Date: 2026-04-13 | Price: $25.74**

---

## Executive Summary

SMCI is in **early-stage recovery** following a catastrophic -33% single-day crash on March 20th (from $30.79 to $20.53). The stock has rebounded +25% from the crash low but remains structurally damaged, trading **10% below the 50 SMA** and **34% below the 200 SMA**. Momentum is stabilizing—RSI has climbed from oversold (31) to neutral (50), and the MACD is curling upward from deeply negative territory. However, the stock faces formidable resistance at the 50 SMA ($28.73) and lacks confirmation of a sustained trend reversal. The macro backdrop (risk-on regime, resilient growth) is supportive, but sticky inflation and elevated long-end rates cap upside for high-beta tech. **Tactical traders can play the bounce with tight stops; long-term holders should wait for reclamation of the 50 SMA.**

---

## Price Action & Trend Structure

### The Crash and Recovery
- **March 20th event**: SMCI gapped down from $30.79 to $20.35 intraday, closing at $20.53 on volume 11x the 20-day average (243M shares). This was a **liquidity event**—likely triggered by forced selling, margin calls, or a fundamental shock (earnings miss, regulatory issue, or supply chain disruption).
- **Post-crash behavior**: Price bottomed at $20.53 and has since rallied to $25.74 (+25% in 17 sessions), forming a series of higher lows. This is constructive but not yet a confirmed reversal.

### Moving Average Analysis
- **10 EMA ($23.99)**: Price crossed above on April 10th and is holding. This is the first bullish signal—short-term momentum has flipped positive.
- **50 SMA ($28.73)**: The critical resistance level. Price has been rejected here repeatedly in the past (Feb-March consolidation). A clean break above $28.73 would signal trend resumption.
- **200 SMA ($39.28)**: The long-term anchor. SMCI has been below this since early March, confirming a **primary downtrend**. The 200 SMA is declining at -0.10/day, indicating structural weakness.

**Verdict**: SMCI is in a **downtrend with a corrective bounce**. The 10 EMA cross is encouraging, but the 50 SMA rejection risk is high. No golden cross setup exists (50 SMA is below 200 SMA and diverging).

**Verdict**: Momentum is **healing but not healed**. The MACD is still negative, indicating the stock is below its equilibrium. A zero-line cross would be a major buy signal.

**Verdict**: SMCI is in the **middle of the Bollinger Band range**, not stretched. This is a neutral setup—no overbought/oversold extremes. The wide band width (14.60 points) reflects lingering uncertainty.

## Macro Overlay & Sector Context

### Macro Headwinds
- **10Y yield at 4.29%**: A headwind for high-multiple tech. If yields push to 4.5%+ (likely if CPI stays hot), SMCI's valuation will compress further.
- **Sticky inflation (CPI +0.87% MoM, PPI +2.08% MoM)**: Input cost pressures are rising. SMCI's margins are at risk if component costs (semiconductors, memory) spike.
- **Fed on hold**: No liquidity tailwind. The Fed is not easing, so there's no "Fed put" for risk assets.

### Macro Tailwinds
- **Risk-on regime (+3/6 score)**: Cyclicals are leading, and market breadth is positive. This supports a SMCI bounce.
- **Resilient labor market (4.3% unemployment, +178k payrolls)**: Corporate capex on AI infrastructure remains intact. This is SMCI's core demand driver.
- **No recession signal (2Y-10Y spread +51 bps)**: The yield curve is normal, not inverted. This supports growth stocks.

**Net assessment**: Macro is **mixed-to-neutral** for SMCI. The risk-on regime and resilient growth are positives, but elevated rates and sticky inflation are negatives. SMCI's performance will hinge on **company-specific fundamentals** (earnings, margins, AI demand) rather than macro tailwinds.

## Risk Factors

1. **Fundamental unknown**: The March 20th crash was likely driven by a fundamental shock (earnings miss, accounting issue, supply chain disruption). Without clarity on the root cause, the stock remains a **binary bet**.
2. **Overhead supply**: Investors who bought at $30-35 are underwater and may sell into strength. This creates resistance.
3. **Sector correlation**: SMCI is highly correlated with AI/semiconductor stocks (NVDA, AMD, AVGO). If the sector rolls over, SMCI will follow.
4. **Liquidity risk**: Post-crash volume has declined. A sudden sell-off could gap the stock lower due to thin liquidity.
