## Macro Regime Assessment

**Current Regime: Mid-Cycle Expansion with Sticky Inflation**

The Fed has paused its easing cycle at 3.64% after cutting ~45 bps from October 2025 through December 2025. The pause reflects persistent inflation pressure:

- **CPI** rose 0.87% MoM in March (330.29 index)
- **PPI** surged 2.08% MoM in February (267.85 index) — significant producer-side pressure likely to pass through to consumers
- **Labor market** remains tight: unemployment fell to 4.30% with solid +178k payroll gains

**Yield Curve**: Positively sloped (1M: 3.67% → 30Y: 4.91%), signaling no recession risk. The 10Y-2Y spread is positive, indicating growth expectations. However, the 30Y at 4.91% reflects elevated term premium and inflation compensation.

**VIX** at 19.23 suggests moderate, not stressed, volatility — consistent with mid-cycle conditions.

## Policy Path & Cross-Asset Implications

The Fed is **on hold** and likely to remain so through mid-2026 given:
1. Sticky core inflation (especially PPI spike)
2. Resilient labor market
3. No recession signal from the curve

**For VYM (High Dividend Yield ETF)**:
- **Positive**: No recession, stable growth supports dividend sustainability and equity risk appetite
- **Negative**: 10Y at 4.31% and 30Y at 4.91% create yield competition; dividend stocks must clear a higher hurdle rate
- **Sector risk**: Utilities, REITs (VYM holdings) are rate-sensitive; higher-for-longer rates compress valuations
- **Value tilt**: Mid-cycle + higher rates historically favor value over growth, but absolute rate level matters

## Key Risks

1. **Inflation re-acceleration**: PPI +2.08% MoM is a red flag; if it persists, Fed may hike, not cut
2. **Duration risk**: Long-end rates (30Y: 4.91%) vulnerable if term premium rises further
3. **Growth slowdown**: Payrolls +178k is solid but decelerating; watch for labor market cracks
4. **Policy error**: Fed may be behind the curve if inflation doesn't cool
