## Macro-Technical Integration

### Rate Environment Impact

The **10-Year Treasury at 4.31%** creates a **3.0% yield spread** vs. VYM's ~3.3% dividend yield (estimated). This is a narrow spread by historical standards, reducing the relative attractiveness of dividend equities. The **30-Year at 4.91%** is particularly problematic for long-duration dividend stocks (utilities, REITs in VYM's portfolio).

**Technical Implication**: The rally from $145 to $153 (+5.5%) has likely priced in the "no recession" scenario (positive yield curve, stable growth). Further upside requires either:
1. **Rate relief**: 10Y yield declining below 4.0% (not likely given PPI spike)
2. **Earnings growth**: Q1 earnings season confirming dividend sustainability (upcoming catalyst)

### RISK-ON Regime Confirmation

The **RISK-ON macro regime (+3/6 score)** supports the technical breakout. Key supporting factors:
- **VIX at 19.23**: Declining from March highs, confirming risk appetite
- **Sector Rotation**: Cyclicals leading defensives (bullish for value/dividend stocks)
- **Market Breadth**: S&P 500 above 200-day average (rising tide lifts all boats)

**Technical Implication**: In RISK-ON regimes, breakouts have higher follow-through probability, and pullbacks are shallower (10-20% retracements vs. 38-50% in RISK-OFF). This supports a **buy-the-dip strategy** rather than chasing breakouts.
