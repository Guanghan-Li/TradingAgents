## Macroeconomic Analysis for FCX (Freeport-McMoRan)

**Regime: Late-Cycle Pause with Sticky Inflation**

The Fed has delivered ~45bp of cuts since October 2025 and is now on hold at 3.64%. The pause is driven by **persistent inflation pressure**: CPI rose 0.87% MoM in March (~10.4% annualized) and PPI surged 2.08% MoM in February (~25% annualized). Labor remains tight—unemployment fell to 4.30% with +178k payrolls—limiting the Fed's ability to ease further despite a flat yield curve signaling growth concerns.

**Yield Curve & Recession Signal**: The 2Y-10Y spread is +0.50%, normalized from inversion but still flat. This reflects market skepticism about growth durability. The 10Y at 4.31% and 30Y at 4.91% suggest real rates remain restrictive. No imminent recession signal, but the curve is not pricing robust expansion.

**Policy Path**: The Fed is trapped. Hot PPI and sticky CPI prevent further cuts, but the flat curve and modest payroll growth suggest the economy is not accelerating. Expect the Fed to remain on hold through Q2 2026 unless inflation cools materially or labor cracks. Any hawkish pivot would strengthen the dollar and pressure commodities.

**Cross-Asset Implications for FCX**:
- **Copper exposure**: Industrial metals are growth-sensitive. The flat curve and restrictive real rates are headwinds. China stimulus and global capex cycles matter more than domestic Fed policy here.
- **Inflation backdrop**: Elevated PPI supports commodity price floors but raises input costs for miners.
- **Dollar risk**: A paused Fed with sticky inflation typically supports dollar strength, which is bearish for dollar-denominated commodities.
- **VIX at 19.23**: Low volatility suggests complacency. A growth or inflation surprise could spike volatility and pressure cyclical equities like FCX.

**Key Risks**:
1. **Stagflation scenario**: If inflation stays hot and growth slows, FCX faces margin compression and demand weakness.
2. **Fed hawkish pivot**: Any signal of rate hikes would strengthen the dollar and crush industrial metals.
3. **China slowdown**: FCX's copper revenue is highly sensitive to Chinese demand; watch for property/infrastructure data.
4. **Curve steepening**: If long rates rise faster than short rates (bear steepening), it signals inflation fears and is bearish for real assets.
