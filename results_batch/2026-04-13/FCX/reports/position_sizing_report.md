# Position Sizing Analysis for FCX (Freeport-McMoRan Inc.)

## Executive Summary

FCX is a high-beta copper producer showing recovery momentum after a significant March drawdown. Current price of $68.03 sits near recent highs but remains volatile with ATR of 2.78. The combination of cyclical commodity exposure, elevated leverage (34.1 D/E), and 1.47 beta warrants a disciplined, scaled entry approach.

## Sizing Recommendation

**Conviction Level:** Moderate

**Target Position Weight:** 4.0% of portfolio

**Initial Entry Weight:** 2.5% of portfolio

**Maximum Loss Budget:** 1.2% of portfolio

## Rationale

**Volatility Context:**
- Current ATR of 2.78 represents ~4.1% of stock price
- Recent 60-day range shows 27% peak-to-trough drawdown (Feb 25 high of $69.75 to Mar 19 low of $50.79)
- Beta of 1.473 indicates amplified market sensitivity

**Stop-Loss Framework:**
Using a 2.5x ATR stop provides reasonable breathing room while limiting downside:
- Stop level: $68.03 - (2.5 × 2.78) = $61.08
- Risk per share: $6.95
- For 2.5% initial position, this represents ~1.0% portfolio risk

**Scaling Strategy:**
1. Enter 2.5% position at current levels ($68.03)
2. Add 1.5% on pullback to $64-65 range OR on breakout above $70 with volume confirmation
3. Total target: 4.0% position

**Risk Factors Limiting Size:**
- High debt-to-equity (34.1) increases financial risk in commodity downturn
- Copper price sensitivity creates macro exposure
- Recent volatility suggests position could move ±10% quickly

**Supporting Factors:**
- Forward PE of 17.2 vs TTM PE of 44.8 suggests earnings inflection
- Positive free cash flow of $1.56B supports dividend (0.88% yield)
- Strong recovery from March lows shows buyer support

## Position Management

- Trail stop to breakeven once position moves 1.5x ATR in your favor
- Consider trimming 25-30% of position if stock reaches $72-74 (prior resistance zone)
- Re-evaluate if ATR expands above 3.5 (increased volatility regime)

This sizing balances the attractive setup with the inherent volatility and leverage risks in the name. The scaled approach allows for tactical flexibility while maintaining disciplined risk management.
