## Macro Regime Assessment

**Current Regime: Reflation / No-Landing**

The data reveals a Fed on pause after a modest easing cycle (Oct 2025: 4.09% → Mar 2026: 3.64%), now confronting reaccelerating inflation:

- **CPI**: +0.87% MoM (Mar) → ~10.4% annualized run-rate
- **PPI**: +2.08% MoM (Feb) → pipeline pressures building
- **Labor**: 4.3% unemployment, +178k payrolls → still-tight labor market
- **Yield Curve**: 2Y-10Y +51bps → normal slope, no recession signal
- **VIX**: 19.23 → complacent risk appetite

**Policy Path**: The Fed cut 45bps from Oct-Jan, then paused. With inflation reaccelerating and unemployment low, the next move is more likely a hold or hike than further cuts. The market is pricing a terminal rate near 3.64%, but upside risk exists if inflation persists.

**Cross-Asset Implications**:
- **Rates**: Curve steepness favors long-duration risk, but inflation could force repricing higher across the curve
- **Equities**: Growth stocks (like NET) face headwinds from higher-for-longer rates; value/cyclicals better positioned
- **Credit**: Spreads likely stable given no recession signal, but rate volatility is a risk
- **FX**: Dollar strength likely if Fed holds while others cut

**Key Risks**:
1. Inflation stays elevated → Fed forced to hike → equity multiple compression
2. Labor market cracks suddenly → rapid policy pivot, but late
3. Curve inversion if short rates rise faster than long → recession signal
