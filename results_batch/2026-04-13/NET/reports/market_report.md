## Macro Regime Implications for NET

### Reflation/No-Landing Regime Analysis
- **Fed Policy**: With the Fed on hold at 3.64% after a modest easing cycle, and CPI reaccelerating at a 10.4% annualized pace, the next move is more likely a hold or hike than further cuts. This is **toxic for high-multiple growth stocks** like NET, which are valued on discounted future cash flows.
- **Rate Sensitivity**: NET's valuation is highly sensitive to the discount rate. A 50bp increase in the 10-year yield (currently 4.29%) could compress NET's forward P/E by 10-15%, implying further downside to $160-$170 even without earnings deterioration.
- **Risk-On Regime Caveat**: While the macro regime is classified as "risk-on" (score +3/6), this is a **low-conviction risk-on** environment. VIX at 19.23 is elevated relative to true risk-on periods (VIX <15), and the falling VIX trend is more about complacency than genuine risk appetite.

### Sector Rotation Dynamics
- **Cyclicals Leading Defensives**: The risk-on signal is driven by cyclicals outperforming, but NET is a **secular growth story**, not a cyclical. In a higher-for-longer rate environment, the market favors value/cyclicals over growth/tech.
- **Actionable Insight**: Even if the S&P 500 continues higher, NET may underperform as capital rotates away from rate-sensitive growth names. This is a **stock-specific headwind** that overrides the broader risk-on backdrop.
