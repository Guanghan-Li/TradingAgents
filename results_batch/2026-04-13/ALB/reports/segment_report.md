## Albemarle Corporation (ALB) - Segment Analysis

### Business Unit Decomposition

Albemarle operates primarily in **lithium** for energy storage (EV batteries), with supporting segments in **bromine specialties** and **catalysts**. The company has faced severe margin compression through 2025 as lithium prices collapsed from 2022-23 peaks, driving massive asset impairments ($246M in Q4 2025, $181M in Q3 2025) and restructuring charges.

**Revenue trajectory** shows sequential volatility: Q1 2025 $1.08B → Q2 $1.33B → Q3 $1.31B → Q4 $1.43B. The Q4 uptick suggests stabilization, but operating margins remain razor-thin (Q4 operating income only $30M on $1.43B revenue = 2.1% margin). Normalized EBITDA has compressed from $262M (Q4 2024) to $184M (Q4 2025), a 30% decline.

**Concentration risk** is extreme: lithium likely represents 60-70% of revenue but has swung from highly profitable to breakeven/loss-making. The $678M TTM net loss reflects both operational deterioration and non-cash impairments as management writes down lithium asset values to reflect lower long-term price assumptions.

### Segment Economics & Strategic Positioning

- **Energy Storage (Lithium)**: Cyclical trough. Oversupply from Chinese producers crushed pricing. However, news flow suggests potential catalysts: (1) US$1 trillion minerals consortium positioning ALB for domestic supply chain premiums, (2) Iran geopolitical tensions supporting lithium as strategic material, (3) 203% stock rally suggests market pricing recovery. Capital intensity remains high (negative FCF periods), but $453M TTM FCF shows discipline.

- **Specialties (Bromine/Flame Retardants)**: Stable but small. Likely 20-25% of revenue. Higher margins (historically 25-30% EBITDA) provide ballast but insufficient to offset lithium drag.

- **Catalysts (Ketjen)**: Refining catalysts and battery materials. ~10-15% of revenue. Defensive, tied to refining utilization and battery production ramps.

**Margin profile**: Gross margin collapsed to 13.1% (Q4 2025: $198M gross profit / $1.43B revenue) vs. healthier 20-25% in prior cycles. Operating leverage is negative—SG&A at $156M/quarter (~11% of revenue) is sticky, crushing profitability at current volumes.

4. **Cost restructuring** (impacted: All segments, upward, 3-6mo) - Evidence: $4M restructuring charges, SG&A discipline needed.

### Concentration Risk Assessment

**Critical**: 65% revenue exposure to lithium creates binary outcome risk. If lithium prices remain depressed (<$12k/ton), ALB faces continued losses and potential covenant pressure (Debt/Equity 34x is elevated). However, current valuation ($186/share, 21x forward P/E on $8.64 EPS estimate) suggests market expects recovery. The 203% one-year rally front-runs fundamentals—downside risk if recovery delays.

**Mitigant**: Specialties and Catalysts provide ~$400-500M annual EBITDA floor, supporting debt service. $453M TTM FCF and 2.2x current ratio offer liquidity cushion.
