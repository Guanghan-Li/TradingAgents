## VICI Scenario Summary

VICI screens as a high-margin, income-oriented REIT with a 6.34% dividend yield, low beta, and valuation that looks inexpensive versus broad equities on earnings multiples. The central debate is less about near-term operating stress and more about duration sensitivity: with the 10-year Treasury at 4.29%, VICI's multiple and relative yield appeal will likely be driven by whether long rates stabilize or re-accelerate higher. Recent news flow is constructive on dividend durability and diversification, which supports the base-to-bull setup as long as tenant rent coverage and access to capital remain intact.

### Bull Case (30%)
Rates remain range-bound or ease, income demand stays firm, and VICI's eighth straight dividend hike reinforces the market's view that its lease cash flows are durable. In that setup, investors may re-rate the stock toward the upper end of its recent range as spread compression versus Treasuries becomes more attractive.

### Base Case (50%)
Operations remain steady, rent collection stays solid, and VICI continues to be treated as a bond-like equity with modest internal growth. The stock likely trades sideways to modestly higher, with total return driven mostly by the dividend rather than multiple expansion.

### Bear Case (20%)
Long-end yields rise further, refinancing concerns across net-lease REITs intensify, or company-specific tenant/concentration concerns re-enter the narrative. In that case, VICI could remain de-rated despite stable reported earnings because the market discounts REIT cash flows at a higher required yield.

### Key Signposts
- 10-year Treasury direction versus the current 4.29% level.
- Evidence that dividend growth remains supported by AFFO/free cash flow and not balance-sheet stretch.
- Any update showing tenant diversification is materially improving concentration risk.
- Relative performance of high-yield and net-lease REIT peers as a read-through on sector risk appetite.

### Invalidation Logic
The constructive thesis weakens if higher-for-longer rates push REIT yield spreads out further, if financing/refinancing commentary deteriorates, or if tenant health/concentration becomes a larger issue than the current income narrative suggests.
