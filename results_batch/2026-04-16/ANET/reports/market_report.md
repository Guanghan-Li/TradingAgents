Since then, price has held the breakout zone and extended to new short-term highs.

The moving-average configuration is constructive. Price is about 9.7% above the 10 EMA, about 18.9% above the 50 SMA, and about 20.2% above the 200 SMA. That confirms powerful upside momentum but also warns that ANET is extended versus its trend base. This supports a bullish intermediate-to-longer-term trend read rather than a purely short-covering bounce. The short-term tactical issue is not whether the trend is up; it is whether new entries at 161 have enough reward-to-risk after such a fast move.

MACD strongly confirms the upside momentum shift. MACD moved from deeply negative readings near -3.28 on March 31 and -3.04 on April 2 to +5.83 on April 16. In a risk-on macro overlay, this favors trend-following interpretation: pullbacks that hold rising short-term support are more likely to be buyable than immediate reversal signals.

RSI adds nuance. The latest RSI is 70.35, just above the classic overbought threshold. That does not automatically mean ANET should be faded, especially because the broader macro overlay is risk-on and the stock is above all major trend averages. In strong institutional trends, RSI can stay elevated while price continues higher. However, RSI near 70 after a move from 116.13 on March 30 to 161.01 on April 16 shows that short-term upside is crowded and vulnerable to consolidation. Traders should distinguish between trend strength and entry quality: the trend is bullish, but chasing after a vertical advance carries a higher pullback risk.

Bollinger Bands show ANET is pressing the upper volatility envelope. The April 16 close of 161.01 is just below the upper band at 161.63, while the lower band is far below at 110.46. The band width has expanded meaningfully, reflecting a volatility breakout rather than a quiet drift higher. Price riding the upper band can be bullish in a breakout phase, but it also means the stock is stretched. A close above the upper band with follow-through would support upside continuation; a rejection from the upper band followed by a move back below 154-155 would suggest near-term exhaustion.

ATR is elevated and stable around 6.16, which is important for risk management. At a 161.01 close, ATR is roughly 3.8% of price. That means normal daily noise in ANET is wide enough that tight stops are likely to get hit. A tactical stop set only 2-3 points below entry would not respect the current volatility regime. A volatility-aware trader would consider using ATR-based sizing and wider invalidation levels, especially because the stock recently produced several large-range sessions: April 8 had a high-low range of 5.04 points, April 13 had 7.34 points, and April 16 had 7.63 points.

Key support levels are now layered. The first tactical support area is 154-155, near the April 14-15 closing zone and the April 16 low-side consolidation area. If ANET holds above that region, the breakout remains very healthy. The next important support is the rising 10 EMA at 146.76, which also aligns with the April 8-10 breakout shelf around 145-147. A pullback into that zone could offer a better risk-reward continuation entry if momentum stabilizes. Below that, the 50 SMA at 135.46 and 200 SMA at 133.91 define the medium-term trend support cluster. A decisive failure back below 134-135 would materially weaken the bullish thesis and imply the April breakout has failed.

Resistance is less defined because ANET closed at the session high and near a short-term breakout high. The immediate reference is the Bollinger upper band at 161.63. A sustained move above 162 would confirm upside continuation, but traders should require follow-through volume or at least a close above the band rather than relying on an intraday spike. If ANET clears 162 cleanly, the next upside targets would likely need to be derived from measured-move or prior higher-timeframe levels not available in the prefetched data. Based only on the provided short-window context, the move is in price-discovery mode above recent resistance.

The macro backdrop supports the bullish technical read but does not remove valuation risk. The medium-term macro regime is classified as risk-on with a composite score of +5 / 6 and high confidence. Falling VIX trend, improving credit-spread proxy, positive breadth, and cyclicals leading defensives all support growth and momentum exposure. That aligns well with ANET’s April breakout. In practical trading terms, ANET can continue to work while risk appetite remains strong, but the setup is vulnerable to any inflation or rate shock that causes long-duration growth multiples to compress.

Actionable read: ANET is technically bullish, but extended. Momentum traders can treat a confirmed close above 162 as a continuation signal, ideally with volume expansion and RSI holding strong rather than diverging. Swing traders may get better reward-to-risk by waiting for a controlled pullback toward 154-155 or, more attractively, the 146-147 breakout/10 EMA zone. Risk managers should size positions around ATR near 6.16 and avoid tight stops. A failure below 154 would warn of short-term exhaustion; a failure below 146-147 would indicate the breakout is losing structure; a failure below 134-135 would materially damage the intermediate bullish trend.
