ANET fundamental report, based on the prefetched live snapshot dated April 16, 2026.

ANET remains a very high-quality large-cap technology name from a pure fundamentals standpoint. The business is producing rare combinations of fast growth, very high margins, heavy free cash flow generation, and a fortress balance sheet. The main counterweight is valuation: the stock is priced for sustained excellence, so traders should treat ANET as a strong-business/high-expectations setup rather than a cheap value opportunity.

ANET’s current profile is unusually strong for a hardware-classified company. Market cap is about $202.8B, TTM revenue is $9.01B, TTM net income is $3.51B, TTM EBITDA is $3.93B, and TTM free cash flow is $3.39B. Profitability is elite: gross margin is about 64.1%, operating margin is 41.5%, net margin is 39.0%, return on equity is 31.4%, and return on assets is 14.4%. Current ratio is 3.05, which supports the view that liquidity is not a constraint. Beta is 1.475, so ANET can still trade with higher-than-market volatility even though the underlying business quality is strong.

The income statement shows clear operating momentum through 2025. Quarterly revenue rose from $1.93B in Q4 2024 to $2.00B in Q1 2025, $2.20B in Q2, $2.31B in Q3, and $2.49B in Q4 2025. Q4 2025 revenue growth versus Q4 2024 was about 28.9%, and each quarter in 2025 set a new revenue high. Operating income also scaled cleanly, from $799.7M in Q4 2024 to $1.03B in Q4 2025. Net income reached $955.8M in Q4 2025 versus $801.0M a year earlier, roughly 19.3% YoY growth.

Margins remain excellent, although Q4 suggests slight mix or cost pressure relative to the immediately prior quarters. Q4 2025 gross profit was $1.564B on $2.488B of revenue, or about 62.9% gross margin. That is lower than Q3 2025 gross margin of about 64.6% and Q2 2025 gross margin of about 65.2%, but still very strong in absolute terms. Operating margin in Q4 2025 stayed robust at about 41.5%, showing ANET is still converting growth into substantial operating profit.

Expense discipline looks healthy. In Q4 2025, R&D was $348.4M, about 14.0% of revenue, while SG&A was $182.5M, about 7.3% of revenue. That matters because ANET is still investing materially in product and go-to-market while preserving high operating leverage. This is one of the most important signals in the report: ANET is not protecting margins by starving investment.

Earnings quality also looks solid. In Q4 2025, pretax income was $1.137B and net income was $955.8M, with no meaningful unusual-item noise in the quarter. Interest income contributed $103.0M, which is meaningful and reflects the value of its very large cash and investment balances. One nuance for traders: if policy rates continue easing from the current 3.64% fed funds level, this interest-income tailwind could soften somewhat over time.

The balance sheet is a major strength. As of Q4 2025, ANET had $10.743B in cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments, up from $8.304B at Q4 2024. Total current assets were $16.387B against current liabilities of $5.377B, and working capital was $11.01B. Stockholders’ equity reached $12.37B. The provided debt-to-equity figure is 0.732, but the detailed balance sheet does not show obvious large conventional financial debt; liabilities are dominated by deferred revenue, payables, and other operating items. That is an important distinction, because operating liabilities are much less concerning than leverage used to fund operations.

Deferred revenue is one of the most bullish balance-sheet signals in the packet. Current deferred revenue rose to $4.003B from $1.727B a year earlier, and non-current deferred revenue rose to $1.370B from $1.064B. Combined deferred revenue increased to roughly $5.37B versus about $2.79B a year ago, a very large jump. That strongly suggests rising backlog, customer prepayments, or multi-period contractual visibility. For traders, this supports the idea that ANET’s revenue base has real forward support rather than being purely spot-driven.

There are still a few working-capital items worth monitoring. Inventory increased to $2.247B from $1.835B year over year, and receivables increased to $1.887B from $1.141B. Those are large increases. They are not automatically bearish because revenue growth has also been strong and cash flow remains excellent, but if inventory and receivables continue to outgrow revenue in upcoming quarters, traders should treat that as an early warning sign for demand normalization or collections pressure.

Cash flow is another clear strength. Operating cash flow was $1.262B in Q4 2025, above net income of $955.8M, which implies strong cash conversion. Free cash flow was $1.225B in Q4 2025 after only $37M of capex. The last four quarterly free cash flow prints were $613.3M, $1.176B, $1.238B, and $1.225B, so the business is throwing off cash at a very high rate. That low-capex, high-FCF profile gives ANET substantial resilience and supports continued buybacks.

Capital allocation is supportive. ANET repurchased $620.1M of stock in Q4 2025, after $787.1M in Q1 2025 and $195.9M in Q3 2025. Share count has edged down modestly: diluted average shares were 1.2758B in Q4 2025 versus 1.2834B in Q4 2024. The buyback is not dramatically shrinking the share base yet, but it does help offset dilution and signals management confidence.

Valuation is the main tension in the ANET story. TTM P/E is 58.55, forward P/E is 37.56, price-to-book is 16.35, and the implied price-to-sales ratio is roughly 22.5x using market cap versus TTM revenue. Those are demanding multiples for any large-cap company, including one with ANET’s margins. The forward P/E dropping meaningfully below TTM P/E implies the market expects a large next-step earnings increase, so the stock likely needs continued high execution to hold its multiple.

Price action confirms that fundamentals are being rewarded. ANET was the top-ranked name in the provided 3-month peer set at +28.72%, ahead of XLK’s +5.19%, for 23.53 points of alpha. It also outperformed XLK over 1 week, 1 month, 6 months, and YTD. That strength is bullish in trend terms, but it also means the stock is more exposed to sharp reactions if the next print is merely good instead of excellent.

Macro backdrop is mixed but manageable for ANET. The fed funds rate has drifted down to 3.64%, VIX is a contained 18.17, and the yield curve is upward sloping from 3.7% bills to about 4.9% at 30 years. Lower policy rates are generally supportive for growth-equity valuation, but higher long yields can still pressure expensive multiples. ANET’s strong net-cash position and operating cash generation reduce financing risk materially.

Actionable trader takeaways for ANET:

1. The core bull case is still intact. ANET has fast growth, software-like margins, rising deferred revenue, strong cash conversion, and a large liquidity cushion.
2. The balance sheet is better than the headline debt-to-equity ratio suggests because liabilities appear heavily operating-based rather than funded-debt-based.
3. The biggest near-term confirmation signal is whether deferred revenue remains elevated and converts into continued 2026 revenue growth without margin damage.
4. The main risk is not business fragility but valuation compression. At roughly 58.5x trailing earnings, the stock has less room for execution misses.
5. Inventory and receivables should be watched closely in the next report. If they keep rising faster than revenue, the market may start questioning demand quality.
6. Interest income is currently a meaningful earnings helper because ANET holds over $10.7B in cash and short-term investments; lower short rates could reduce that support later.

Bottom line: ANET’s fundamental profile is excellent and supports why the stock has been a leadership name. For traders, this is fundamentally a high-quality momentum compounder, but the setup now depends on ANET continuing to deliver premium growth and premium margins because the valuation already reflects a large amount of optimism.
