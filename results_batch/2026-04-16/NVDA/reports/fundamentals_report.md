**NVDA Fundamental Report Update**

Using the supplied live fundamentals context, NVDA still screens as one of the highest-quality large-cap growth companies in the market: very large scale, extreme profitability, strong liquidity, modest balance-sheet leverage, and heavy cash generation. The main debate is no longer whether the business is strong; it is whether growth, margins, and capital deployment can stay strong enough to justify a still-premium valuation and a high-beta stock profile.

**Company profile and base fundamentals**

NVDA is a Technology / Semiconductors company with a market cap of about $4.82T. TTM revenue is $215.9B, gross profit is $153.5B, EBITDA is $133.2B, and net income is $120.1B. That implies exceptionally high profitability for a hardware-linked company: profit margin of 55.6%, operating margin of 65.0%, ROE of 101.5%, and ROA of 51.2%. Reported dividend yield is only 0.02%, so the equity case is overwhelmingly growth and earnings-power driven rather than income-driven.

Valuation remains rich on backward-looking numbers but much less extreme on forward estimates. NVDA trades at 40.6x TTM EPS and 17.7x forward EPS, with forward EPS of 11.21 versus TTM EPS of 4.89. That gap implies the market is still underwriting a major step-up in earnings. Price/book is 30.6x, which is expensive in isolation, but balance-sheet book value understates the value of a business with this level of margin structure and cash generation.

**Quarterly financial history**

The last five reported quarters show a business that is still scaling rapidly:

- Revenue: $39.3B, $44.1B, $46.7B, $57.0B, $68.1B
- Net income: $22.1B, $18.8B, $26.4B, $31.9B, $43.0B
- Gross profit: $28.7B, $26.7B, $33.9B, $41.8B, $51.1B
- Operating income: $24.0B, $21.6B, $28.4B, $36.0B, $44.3B

For the quarter ended 2026-01-31, revenue was $68.1B, up about 19.5% sequentially from $57.0B and about 73.2% from $39.3B a year earlier. Net income rose to $43.0B, up about 34.6% sequentially and about 94.5% year over year. Diluted EPS reached 1.76 versus 1.30 in the prior quarter and 0.89 a year earlier.

Margin quality is the core bullish feature. Gross margin in the last five quarters was approximately 73.0%, 60.5%, 72.4%, 73.4%, and 75.0%. Operating margin was about 61.1%, 49.1%, 60.8%, 63.2%, and 65.0%. Net margin was about 56.2%, 42.6%, 56.5%, 56.0%, and 63.1%. That progression shows that the temporary cost pressure in the 2025-04-30 quarter was not structural; profitability has since re-expanded to even stronger levels.

Operating expense discipline is also notable. In the latest quarter, R&D was $5.5B and SG&A was $1.3B. R&D is growing, but at roughly 8.1% of revenue it is being absorbed easily by top-line scale. SG&A is only about 1.9% of revenue. This is a rare combination: rapid innovation spend without any visible operating-leverage breakdown.

**Balance sheet analysis**

NVDA’s balance sheet remains very strong. As of 2026-01-31:

- Total assets: $206.8B
- Cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments: $62.6B
- Total debt: $11.0B
- Stockholders’ equity: $157.3B
- Working capital: $93.4B
- Current ratio: 3.91

That leaves NVDA with net cash of roughly $51.5B. Despite the prefetched debt/equity figure of 7.255, the balance-sheet values imply debt is only about 7% of equity, so leverage is low, not aggressive. Liquidity is not a concern.

The main balance-sheet change to monitor is asset mix. Goodwill rose to $20.8B from $5.2B a year earlier, and total goodwill plus intangibles reached $24.1B. Cash flow data shows $13.2B of business purchases in the latest quarter. That signals meaningful M&A activity. The company can easily afford it, but this introduces new integration and capital-allocation risk that was less relevant when growth was driven almost entirely organically.

Working-capital trends deserve monitoring but are not yet alarming. Inventory rose to $21.4B from $19.8B sequentially and $10.1B a year earlier. Receivables rose to $38.5B from $33.4B sequentially. Those balances are growing fast because revenue is growing fast. On a rough days basis, receivables actually look manageable rather than stretched, and inventory days do not currently suggest obvious channel stress.

**Cash flow and capital allocation**

Cash generation remains elite. In the latest quarter:

- Operating cash flow: $36.2B
- Capex: $1.28B
- Free cash flow: $34.9B

That means FCF was about 51.2% of quarterly revenue, which is extraordinary. OCF/Net Income conversion was about 84%, and FCF/Net Income conversion was about 81%. The business is not just reporting accounting earnings; it is converting a large share of them into cash.

Over the last four reported quarters, quarterly FCF sums to about $96.7B, although the prefetched summary lists TTM FCF at $58.1B. That discrepancy likely reflects provider timing or methodology differences, so traders should treat the exact TTM cash-flow figure with caution. The broader conclusion is unchanged: NVDA is producing very large absolute cash flows.

Capital returns remain aggressive. Share repurchases were $3.8B in the latest quarter, after $12.5B, $9.7B, and $14.1B in the three prior quarters. Diluted average shares fell from 24.71B a year ago to 24.43B in the latest quarter, so buybacks are contributing to EPS growth. Dividends are immaterial at roughly $242M per quarter.

One subtle point: financing cash flow is deeply negative because NVDA is returning capital while still investing heavily. That is healthy as long as operations stay this strong. If growth slows, buyback pace becomes a discretionary lever.

**Market and valuation context**

In the supplied relative-performance window, NVDA was up 7.85% over one week, beating XLK by 0.85%. Over one month, it was roughly in line with XLK. Over three and six months, it outperformed the sector ETF, but it has not been the strongest peer recently; AMD and AVGO outperformed over one week and one month. That suggests NVDA fundamentals are still excellent, but leadership is no longer uncontested.

The macro backdrop is moderately supportive. The effective Fed Funds rate is 3.64% as of 2026-03-01 and has been stable, while the front end of the Treasury curve sits around 3.7% to 3.8%. Stable policy rates help long-duration growth equities maintain elevated multiples. Inflation is still rising sequentially in the supplied CPI/PPI data, which could limit further multiple expansion even if fundamentals remain strong.

**Actionable trader takeaways**

1. NVDA’s core bull case is still fundamental, not narrative. Revenue growth, margin expansion, and cash conversion all remain exceptional.
2. The forward PE of 17.7x is far less demanding than the trailing PE of 40.6x, so the stock is effectively priced for continued earnings ramp. If that ramp holds, valuation can compress without the stock necessarily falling.
3. The biggest operating watch items are inventory, receivables, and the new goodwill/intangibles base after acquisitions. None look broken yet, but these are the first places deterioration would likely show.
4. Buybacks are helping EPS, but the main driver is still operating performance. Traders should not mistake financial engineering for the primary source of earnings growth.
5. Beta of 2.335 means even a fundamentally strong setup can produce sharp downside on any guidance miss, margin compression, or macro multiple reset.
6. Relative performance versus peers is good but not dominant. For momentum traders, that matters: NVDA is strong, but it is no longer automatically the top semiconductor leader in every time frame.

**Bottom line**

NVDA’s supplied fundamentals support a view that the business remains structurally dominant and financially outstanding. The balance sheet is liquid, leverage is low, margins are still widening, and free cash flow is enormous. The main risks are not solvency or weak profitability; they are expectation risk, valuation sensitivity, and execution risk around fast growth, working-capital scaling, and recent acquisition activity. For traders, that means NVDA still deserves a quality premium, but the stock likely needs continued near-flawless execution to materially outperform from here.
