### NVDA Segment View

NVDA is now economically a Data Center company with smaller adjacency businesses around it. Using FY2026 segment revenue implied by NVIDIA's February 25, 2026 earnings release and quarterly revenue trend disclosure, Data Center represented about 89.7% of FY2026 revenue, Gaming 7.4%, Professional Visualization 1.5%, Automotive 1.1%, and OEM & Other 0.3%. That concentration explains why consolidated profitability is so extreme: quarterly revenue rose from $57.0B in Q3 FY2026 to $68.1B in Q4 FY2026 while gross margin expanded from 73.4% to 75.0% and operating income rose 23% QoQ, which strongly suggests the AI systems mix remains margin-accretive.

For NVDA, Data Center is both the growth engine and the main risk bucket. Q4 FY2026 Data Center revenue reached $62.3B, up 22% QoQ and 75% YoY, supported by Blackwell demand, accelerated computing, and agentic AI infrastructure. Gaming is no longer the earnings driver, but it still matters as a consumer ecosystem anchor; Q4 revenue of $3.7B was up 47% YoY but down 13% sequentially as holiday channel inventory normalized, so durability is lower than Data Center. Professional Visualization is small but strategically useful because workstation AI and Omniverse-linked workflows can extend NVDA's enterprise stack; its Q4 revenue of $1.3B was up 74% QoQ and 159% YoY, implying strong incremental mix quality.

Automotive remains too small to move near-term numbers, but it is becoming a credible long-duration option on AV, robotics, and edge AI. Full-year Automotive revenue rose 39% to $2.3B, and March 16, 2026 announcements around robotics, industrial digital twins, Jetson, Isaac, and autonomous systems support a multi-year pipeline. The main concentration risk is straightforward: if hyperscaler or sovereign AI capex slows, export policy tightens, or competitive price/performance narrows, almost all of NVDA's current earnings power is exposed at once. Segment operating profit is not separately disclosed in the provided context, so margin direction below is inferred from business mix and consolidated margin behavior.
