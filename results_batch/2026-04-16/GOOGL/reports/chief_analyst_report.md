The stock is also extended after a roughly 23% rally in less than three weeks, with RSI above 71. So the bear is right on asymmetry and timing, but not strong enough on the full thesis to justify stepping away from GOOGL entirely.

The reason I still land on Buy is that the company is executing too well to ignore. Revenue rose from about $90.2B to $113.8B through 2025, operating income rose from about $30.6B to $35.9B, gross margin improved, and the balance sheet remains fortress-like with about $126.8B in cash and short-term investments against manageable net debt. I do not want to repeat a common mistake in these situations: treating overbought leadership as automatically bearish. The better lesson is to separate a good company from a bad entry price and solve that with sizing, not indecision.

So the investment plan for GOOGL is straightforward. Initiate at 3.0% weight, not the full 6.0%, and build only if one of two things happens: either GOOGL pulls back into the roughly 317 to 321 area and holds that breakout zone, or the late-April to early-May earnings update confirms that AI monetization is becoming tangible while margins remain stable and capex commentary stays credible. Do not chase a full-size position near current extension. Keep total portfolio risk capped at 0.6%. If GOOGL decisively loses the low-320s and momentum rolls over, or if earnings show AI spend rising without clear revenue contribution, stop adding and cut the position. If formal EU DMA action starts to impair search economics materially, treat that as thesis damage rather than noise.

Bottom line: Buy GOOGL, aligned with the bull analyst, but do it as a staged accumulation rather than an aggressive one-shot entry.
