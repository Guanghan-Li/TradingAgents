# ALB Fundamental Research Report

## Company profile and market setup

ALB is Albemarle Corporation, a Basic Materials company in the Specialty Chemicals industry. Its fundamentals are heavily tied to lithium and specialty chemical demand, making earnings sensitive to commodity pricing, electric-vehicle battery supply chains, capital spending discipline, and global industrial activity.

As of the prefetched fundamentals data retrieved on 2026-04-16, ALB had a market capitalization of approximately $25.42 billion. The stock has sharply outperformed the Materials sector benchmark recently: ALB returned +25.11% over one week, +29.64% over one month, +21.74% over three months, +125.28% over six months, and +50.18% year to date. Relative to XLB, ALB generated alpha of +24.95 percentage points over one week, +24.67 points over one month, +15.12 points over three months, +108.01 points over six months, and +37.47 points YTD.

That performance is important context: the fundamentals show improving liquidity and positive free cash flow, but also negative trailing earnings, weak margins, impairment charges, and valuation expansion. Traders should treat ALB as a high-beta recovery/turnaround equity rather than a stable earnings compounder at this point.

## Valuation and basic financials

ALB trades at a forward P/E of 24.47x, based on forward EPS of $8.81. However, trailing EPS is negative at -$5.77, so the valuation case depends heavily on expected earnings recovery. Price-to-book is 3.48x, which is not distressed despite recent trailing losses. Dividend yield is modest at 0.87%, so the investment case is primarily capital appreciation and earnings normalization, not income.

The company reported TTM revenue of $5.14 billion, gross profit of $673.5 million, EBITDA of $737.8 million, and net income of -$677.4 million. Profitability remains pressured: profit margin is -9.93%, operating margin is only 2.26%, return on equity is -4.66%, and return on assets is 0.30%. These numbers indicate that the business is not yet producing attractive accounting returns despite recent market enthusiasm.

Liquidity is stronger. The current ratio is 2.23, free cash flow is positive at $453.3 million, and cash and equivalents were $1.62 billion at 2025-12-31. Debt-to-equity is 33.95, which is moderate for an asset-heavy chemicals business, and balance sheet risk has improved as net debt declined through 2025.

## Income statement analysis

Quarterly revenue improved through 2025. Revenue was $1.08 billion in Q1 2025, $1.33 billion in Q2, $1.31 billion in Q3, and $1.43 billion in Q4. Q4 2025 revenue was also above Q4 2024 revenue of $1.23 billion, suggesting top-line stabilization or recovery.

Gross profit was volatile but improved sequentially in Q4 2025. Gross profit was $156.3 million in Q1, $196.9 million in Q2, $117.6 million in Q3, and $197.9 million in Q4. The Q4 gross margin was approximately 13.9%, calculated from $197.9 million gross profit on $1.43 billion revenue. That is better than Q3 2025, where gross margin was approximately 9.0%, but still not strong for a company valued on recovery expectations.

Operating income remained weak and inconsistent. Operating income was $18.7 million in Q1, $52.0 million in Q2, -$33.6 million in Q3, and $30.3 million in Q4. Total operating income as reported was worse in Q4 at -$217.4 million due to unusual items and charges. This gap matters because adjusted operating performance looks better than reported profitability, but reported losses are still consuming retained earnings.

Net income deteriorated sharply in the second half of 2025. ALB posted net income of $41.3 million in Q1, $22.9 million in Q2, -$160.7 million in Q3, and -$414.2 million in Q4. Diluted EPS followed the same pattern: $0.00 in Q1, -$0.16 in Q2, -$1.72 in Q3, and -$3.87 in Q4. Q4 included $247.6 million of unusual items, primarily $245.6 million of impairment of capital assets. Q3 also had $183.3 million of unusual items, including $181.1 million of impairment of capital assets.

Normalized income paints a less severe but still uneven picture. Normalized income was $40.5 million in Q1, $26.4 million in Q2, -$0.8 million in Q3, and -$218.5 million in Q4. This indicates that even after adjusting for unusual items, Q4 remained fundamentally weak. Traders should not dismiss all losses as one-time charges.

Interest expense is also rising year over year. Net interest expense was $44.7 million in Q4 2024, $49.0 million in Q1 2025, $49.9 million in Q2, $51.0 million in Q3, and $57.8 million in Q4. Higher interest expense reduces flexibility during an earnings recovery and makes cash generation more important.

## Balance sheet analysis

ALB’s balance sheet improved in liquidity and net debt despite weak reported earnings. Total assets were $16.37 billion at 2025-12-31, down from $17.29 billion at 2025-06-30 and $16.61 billion at 2024-12-31. The decline reflects lower net PPE, inventory reductions, and asset impairments.

Cash and equivalents were $1.62 billion at Q4 2025, up from $1.19 billion at Q4 2024, though down from $1.93 billion at Q3 2025. Current assets were $4.01 billion against current liabilities of $1.80 billion, producing working capital of $2.21 billion. This compares favorably with Q4 2024 working capital of $1.88 billion.

Total debt was $3.30 billion at Q4 2025, down from $3.62 billion at Q4 2024 and $3.74 billion at Q3 2025. Net debt declined to $1.58 billion at Q4 2025 from $2.32 billion at Q4 2024. This is a constructive development: management appears to be using cash flow and balance sheet actions to reduce leverage rather than allowing losses to pressure liquidity.

Inventory fell meaningfully to $1.18 billion at Q4 2025 from $1.53 billion at Q3 2025 and $1.50 billion at Q4 2024. Finished goods fell from $901.9 million in Q3 to $620.7 million in Q4, while raw materials fell from $630.8 million to $260.6 million. This may indicate working-capital discipline, destocking, write-downs, improved sales conversion, or a mix of these factors. For traders, inventory reduction supports cash flow, but it also requires monitoring because overly aggressive destocking could constrain revenue if demand rebounds.

Net PPE was $8.73 billion at Q4 2025, down from $9.45 billion at Q4 2024. Goodwill and intangible assets were $1.71 billion, down from $1.81 billion at Q4 2024 and $1.91 billion at Q2 2025. Tangible book value was $5.58 billion at Q4 2025 versus $5.91 billion at Q4 2024, reflecting the impact of losses and impairments.

Stockholders’ equity was $9.53 billion at Q4 2025, down from $9.96 billion at Q4 2024. Retained earnings fell from $5.48 billion at Q4 2024 to $4.61 billion at Q4 2025. This confirms that the recent losses are not merely headline noise; they are reducing accumulated equity.

## Cash flow analysis

Cash flow is the strongest part of the ALB fundamental story. Operating cash flow was $545.4 million in Q1 2025, -$7.2 million in Q2, $355.6 million in Q3, and $388.5 million in Q4. Free cash flow was $362.8 million in Q1, -$126.8 million in Q2, $223.4 million in Q3, and $233.1 million in Q4. For the last two reported quarters, ALB generated positive free cash flow despite GAAP net losses.

Capital expenditure moderated significantly. Capex was -$355.7 million in Q4 2024, -$182.6 million in Q1 2025, -$119.6 million in Q2, -$132.2 million in Q3, and -$155.4 million in Q4. Lower capex supports free cash flow and debt reduction, but traders should evaluate whether reduced investment is sustainable or whether future growth projects may require renewed spending.

Financing cash flow was negative in every reported quarter, including -$533.0 million in Q4 2025. The major Q4 driver was debt repayment of $459.8 million. ALB also continued paying dividends: cash dividends paid were approximately $89.3 million per quarter, split between preferred dividends of about $41.7 million and common dividends of about $47.6 million. The dividend is manageable while free cash flow remains positive, but it could become a pressure point if lithium pricing or margins weaken again.

The Q4 2025 cash balance declined from $1.93 billion to $1.62 billion despite positive free cash flow because of debt repayment, dividends, and investing outflows. This is not necessarily negative: the cash reduction was partly used to improve leverage.

## Relative performance and market expectations

ALB ranked 1st out of 7 in the provided 3-month peer performance set. It materially outperformed LIN, FCX, APD, NUE, XLB, NEM, and SHW over multiple periods. The stock’s +125.28% six-month return is the clearest signal that the market has already priced in a meaningful recovery.

This creates a tension for traders. Fundamentals are improving in cash flow and leverage, but reported earnings are still negative and the company is still absorbing impairment charges. A forward P/E of 24.47x is reasonable only if forward EPS of $8.81 is achievable. If forward estimates are revised down, the stock could be vulnerable because the price has already moved far above its 200-day average of $122.33 and near its 52-week high of $215.69.

ALB’s beta of 1.43 confirms elevated volatility. The current macro backdrop is mixed but not hostile: the effective fed funds rate is 3.64%, the yield curve is upward sloping with the 10-year at 4.29% and the 2-year at 3.76%, unemployment is 4.30%, and VIX is 18.17. Lower policy rates versus late 2025 may support cyclicals and long-duration recovery stories, but producer inflation rose 1.78% sequentially in the latest data, which could pressure input costs.

## Key actionable insights for traders

The bullish case for ALB rests on liquidity, free cash flow, debt reduction, and expectations of earnings normalization. The company generated positive free cash flow in Q3 and Q4 2025, reduced net debt to $1.58 billion, lowered total debt sequentially, and improved working capital compared with Q4 2024. If lithium and specialty chemical pricing stabilize, ALB has operating leverage that could make forward EPS recovery credible.

The bearish case rests on valuation, weak trailing profitability, and impairment-driven earnings risk. TTM EPS is -$5.77, TTM net income is -$677.4 million, profit margin is -9.93%, and Q4 net income was -$414.2 million. The company has now reported large impairment charges in both Q3 and Q4 2025. Even normalized income was deeply negative in Q4, so traders should avoid treating all losses as purely non-recurring.

The key near-term trading question is whether the recent share-price rally has outrun the pace of fundamental improvement. ALB is trading with strong momentum and major sector alpha, but after a +25.11% one-week move and +125.28% six-month move, the risk/reward is more dependent on forward guidance and commodity sentiment than on already-reported financials.

For position management, traders should monitor: upcoming earnings commentary on lithium pricing, Q1 2026 margin progression, any additional impairments, free cash flow sustainability, capex guidance, inventory trends, and whether debt repayment continues without sacrificing liquidity. A continuation of positive FCF and stable gross margin would support the recovery thesis. Another quarter of large impairments or negative normalized income would challenge the current valuation.

## Bottom line

ALB’s fundamentals show a company in recovery mode, not yet a clean earnings compounder. The balance sheet and cash flow profile improved materially in 2025, especially through lower net debt, positive free cash flow, and better working capital. However, reported profitability remains weak, trailing EPS is negative, normalized Q4 income was poor, and the stock has already rallied aggressively. Traders should treat ALB as a high-beta recovery trade where upside depends on forward earnings delivery and commodity-cycle improvement, while downside risk is elevated if margins or estimates disappoint.
