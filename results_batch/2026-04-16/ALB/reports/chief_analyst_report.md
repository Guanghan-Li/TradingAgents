Add the April 16, 2026 breakout above 205.49 on roughly 2.5x normal volume, positive free cash flow, and net debt improvement to $1.58B, and the recovery setup is credible enough to own.

The bear analyst makes the right risk argument, but not a strong enough directional argument to justify Sell or even a passive Hold. The important bear points are that ALB is extended, trailing EPS is still -5.77, normalized Q4 income was still negative, and the 45% Base case implies stabilization more than an immediate earnings boom. That means ALB is investable, not low-risk. The lesson I would apply here is not to mistake improving cash flow and sentiment for a fully proven cycle turn, which is why ALB should be bought with discipline rather than chased.

My plan for ALB is to start at the underwritten 1.0% weight, preferably on a controlled retest or tight consolidation around the 201 to 205 breakout zone rather than after a vertical spike, and only scale toward 2.5% if late-April to early-May earnings confirm stronger realized lithium pricing, positive free cash flow, and credible forward earnings. Keep the trade-level loss capped at 0.3% of portfolio, which requires smaller size because ATR is 10.52. If ALB loses the 201 area decisively, breaks momentum after earnings, or lithium prices roll back over, exit. If ALB holds support and the recovery thesis is confirmed, stay long and let ALB work toward the mid-220s.
- Trader plan: ALB is in a legitimate bullish breakout, but the setup is tactically stretched. ALB closed at 215.62 on 2026-04-16, well above the 10 EMA at 186.57, the 50 SMA at 172.62, and the 200 SMA at 122.58. MACD is accelerating higher, RSI at 72.44 confirms strong momentum, and the breakout cleared prior resistance near 205.49 on roughly 2.5x normal volume. News and sentiment are also supportive, with lithium-price strength, EV-demand optimism, and a modest Oppenheimer target increase reinforcing the move.

The problem is entry quality, not direction. ALB is now extended above its upper Bollinger Band, ATR has expanded to 10.52, and the stock has already priced in a meaningful portion of the recovery story. Fundamentals are improving through free cash flow, debt reduction, and better liquidity, but trailing EPS is still negative and normalized profitability remains weak. Macro is demand-supportive but rate-constrained, so valuation upside is not getting a clean tailwind from falling yields.

My decision is to HOLD ALB at current levels. Existing holders should stay with the trend while ALB holds the 201 to 205 breakout zone; that area is the key test of whether the breakout is real support. A decisive loss of 201 would weaken the immediate setup, and a break back toward 186 to 190, especially through the 10 EMA, would be a stronger warning that the move was climactic. For new capital, the better risk-adjusted approach is to wait for a controlled retest or consolidation rather than chase ALB immediately after a vertical expansion day.
