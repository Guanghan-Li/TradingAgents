## Segment View: ALB

ALB is still fundamentally a lithium-led company with partial diversification from bromine specialties and catalysts. Based on FY2025 reported segment results, **Energy Storage** generated about **52.7% of revenue** and roughly **62% of segment adjusted EBITDA**, so the stock remains primarily a lithium pricing and EV/ESS demand vehicle. **Specialties** is the more durable business-mix buffer, but its 2026 outlook is softer. **Ketjen** improved operationally in 2025, yet its strategic importance is falling because Albemarle expects the refining catalyst sale to make post-close earnings contribution immaterial and move most of it outside segment reporting. Primary evidence: [FY2025 results](https://investors.albemarle.com/news-and-events/news/news-details/2026/Albemarle-Reports-Fourth-Quarter-and-Full-Year-2025-Results/default.aspx), [2025 10-K](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/915913/000091591326000018/alb-20251231.htm).

Energy Storage has the best upside torque but also the weakest revenue durability because contracts are still heavily tied to lithium market pricing. 2025 sales fell to **$2.71B** from **$3.02B**, while adjusted EBITDA declined to **$697M** from **$758M**, despite higher volumes. The setup improved late in 2025 and recent news flow points to a lithium-price rebound, which matters disproportionately for ALB because management's 2026 scenario framework shows a very large earnings swing if lithium pricing holds closer to January 2026 levels. Specialties is a higher-quality stabilizer: 2025 sales rose to **$1.37B** and adjusted EBITDA to **$276M**, helped by cost and productivity gains, but 2026 guidance calls for flat-to-down sales and lower EBITDA because of weaker oil and gas demand, lower lithium-specialties pricing, and temporary disruption from the January 2026 JBC flooding event. Ketjen had a decent operating year, with **$1.07B** of sales and **$150M** of adjusted EBITDA, but the pending divestiture means investors should treat it more as a portfolio simplification event than a durable growth leg.

The main concentration risk is that ALB is becoming **more**, not less, dependent on lithium after the Ketjen transaction. That can improve strategic focus and lower capital intensity, but it also removes a diversification buffer just as Energy Storage remains the dominant profit pool. Business-mix quality is therefore improving on simplicity, but not on stability.
