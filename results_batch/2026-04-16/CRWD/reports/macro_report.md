The Fed has already eased meaningfully from 4.09% in October 2025 to 3.64% by March 2026, but the latest inflation prints look firm again while labor remains healthy. CPI and PPI both accelerated sequentially in March 2026, unemployment dipped to 4.3%, and nonfarm payrolls rose by 178k. That combination argues for a Fed pause with a slower easing path than equities would prefer.

The Treasury curve through 5 years is modestly upward sloping, not inverted: 3M is 3.71%, 2Y is 3.76%, and 5Y is 3.90%. That weakens the classic near-term recession signal and is more consistent with steady nominal growth plus policy near neutral. For `CRWD`, this is a mixed but still workable backdrop. On one hand, resilient labor and a non-inverted front-end curve support corporate IT budgets and keep cybersecurity spend relatively durable. On the other hand, a pause in Fed easing and any renewed rise in intermediate yields can pressure valuation for long-duration growth software names like `CRWD` even if fundamentals stay intact.

Base case: macro is not currently a direct bearish thesis against `CRWD`, but it is also not the kind of fast-disinflation / aggressive-cut environment that would maximize multiple expansion. The clean macro read is constructive on demand resilience, cautious on valuation expansion. Other analysts should incorporate that `CRWD` likely benefits more from stable growth and defensible security budgets than from rate relief. If the committee is leaning bullish on company-specific execution, macro is a secondary tailwind; if the thesis depends on multiple rerating alone, macro support is weaker.

Concrete risks for `CRWD`:
- Sticky inflation could keep the Fed on hold longer and lift 2Y-5Y yields, compressing software multiples.
- A hawkish repricing would matter more to `CRWD` than to lower-duration value sectors because its equity story is partly duration-sensitive.
- If labor softens abruptly after this report window, enterprise seat growth, deal timing, and upsell activity could weaken even if cybersecurity remains relatively non-discretionary.
- The missing GDP, ISM, confidence, and VIX series reduce visibility into cyclical breadth, so the macro call should be treated as moderately confident rather than complete.
