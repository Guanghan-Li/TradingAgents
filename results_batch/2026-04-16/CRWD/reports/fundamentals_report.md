## CRWD Fundamental Report

CRWD remains one of the strongest large-cap cybersecurity growth stories on a business-quality basis, but it is still trading like a premium-duration software name that must keep executing nearly perfectly. Using the supplied live fundamentals snapshot dated April 16, 2026, the key fundamental takeaway is that the business is showing a real profitability inflection while preserving very strong cash generation and balance-sheet flexibility. The key market takeaway is that the stock has materially underperformed both peers and the Technology sector despite that improvement, which suggests valuation sensitivity, positioning, or concern that the margin progress still needs to prove durable.

No separate new filing or major corporate event is included in the supplied one-week context, so the latest hard fundamental anchor for CRWD is the quarter ended January 31, 2026.

### Company profile and business quality

CRWD operates in `Software - Infrastructure` within the Technology sector, with a market cap of about `$106.1B`. This places it firmly in the large-cap, premium-multiple category. The business profile implied by the statements is consistent with a subscription-heavy cybersecurity platform: high gross margins, very large deferred revenue balances, strong operating cash flow, and meaningful stock-based compensation typical of scaled software platforms.

The company’s scale is now substantial:

- TTM revenue: about `$4.81B`
- Gross profit: about `$3.60B`
- Latest quarterly revenue: `$1.305B`
- Latest quarterly gross profit: `$994M`

That latest quarter implies about `23.3%` year-over-year revenue growth versus the comparable quarter one year earlier (`$1.305B` vs `$1.059B`). Sequentially, quarterly revenue has continued to rise at roughly `4%` to `6%` each quarter over the last five reported quarters, which is strong for a company already above a `$4B` annual revenue run rate.

### Income statement trends

The most important development in the CRWD income statement is margin improvement.

Revenue over the last five quarters has progressed from `$1.059B` to `$1.305B`, while gross profit rose from `$785M` to `$994M`. Gross margin improved from roughly `74.1%` one year ago to `76.1%` in the latest quarter. On an LTM basis, gross margin is about `74.7%`, which is strong even by high-quality software standards.

Operating profitability improved sharply:

- Latest-quarter operating income: `$13.8M`
- Prior quarter operating income: `-$69.4M`
- Quarter before that: `-$113.0M`
- Quarter before that: `-$124.7M`
- Year-ago quarter: `-$85.3M`

That means reported operating margin improved from about `-8.1%` a year ago to `+1.1%` in the latest quarter. This is a meaningful inflection. EBITDA also improved to `$142.9M` in the latest quarter from near breakeven or negative levels in prior periods.

Net income also flipped positive:

- Latest-quarter net income: `$59.4M`
- Prior four quarterly results: negative in each quarter

Latest-quarter net margin was about `4.6%`. That is still modest, but the direction matters more than the absolute level at this stage. The TTM picture remains negative, with TTM net income of about `-$162.5M` and TTM net margin of about `-3.4%`, but traders should recognize that the reported earnings profile has improved materially in the most recent quarter.

The supplied snapshot shows `EPS (TTM) = -0.66` but `Forward EPS = 6.17`. That gap is large and implies the market expects a significant profitability ramp from here. This is one reason valuation remains elevated: investors are not paying for current GAAP earnings, they are paying for expected forward earnings power.

### Expense structure and operating leverage

CRWD still spends aggressively, but the latest quarter suggests the model is beginning to scale.

Latest-quarter operating expenses were about `$980M`, composed primarily of:

- R&D: `$356.4M`
- SG&A: `$623.5M`
- Selling and marketing alone: `$463.6M`

This is still a very heavy expense base relative to revenue, and it explains why the company only recently reached positive operating income. However, revenue has been rising faster than total opex in the most recent quarter, creating the first clear sign of operating leverage.

The trader-grade interpretation is straightforward: if CRWD can keep revenue growth above `20%` while holding gross margin around `75%+` and slowing opex growth, earnings revisions can continue moving higher. If revenue growth slips meaningfully or sales efficiency weakens, the premium multiple becomes much harder to defend.

### Cash flow quality

Cash generation is a major strength.

Latest-quarter cash flow metrics were strong:

- Operating cash flow: `$497.9M`
- Free cash flow: `$378.1M`
- Free cash flow margin: about `29.0%`
- Operating cash flow margin: about `38.1%`

Over the latest four reported quarters, cumulative figures are approximately:

- Operating cash flow: `$1.61B`
- Free cash flow: `$1.24B`

The prefetched company snapshot separately lists free cash flow at about `$1.60B`, while summing the quarterly cash-flow table yields about `$1.24B` for the most recent four quarters shown. That looks like a vendor-definition or trailing-period mismatch rather than a deterioration signal, but it is worth normalizing in any valuation model.

Either way, the directional conclusion is clear: CRWD is a strong cash generator.

Capital intensity remains low for the scale of the business. Latest-quarter capex was about `$119.7M`, and capex has stayed manageable relative to operating cash flow. That is consistent with a software business that can fund growth internally.

### Balance sheet strength

The balance sheet is another major positive.

As of January 31, 2026:

- Cash and short-term investments: `$5.23B`
- Total debt: `$820M`
- Cash-to-debt ratio: about `6.4x`
- Current assets: `$7.42B`
- Current liabilities: `$4.18B`
- Current ratio: `1.77`
- Stockholders’ equity: `$4.43B`

This is a very liquid balance sheet. The debt burden is not a core risk at current scale. The supplied `Debt to Equity` value of `18.336` is best interpreted as roughly `18.3%`, which matches the absolute balances much better than an `18x` reading.

Deferred revenue is especially notable:

- Current deferred revenue: `$3.42B`
- Non-current deferred revenue: `$1.33B`
- Total deferred revenue: about `$4.75B`

That total deferred revenue is almost equal to one full year of TTM revenue, which is a strong signal for revenue visibility, customer prepayment behavior, and renewal support. For traders, this is one of the clearest pieces of evidence that CRWD still has a high-quality subscription engine underneath the headline valuation debate.

There are also a few balance-sheet items worth monitoring:

- Goodwill rose to about `$1.36B` from about `$913M` earlier in the series, reflecting acquisitions.
- The cash-flow statement shows business purchases of about `$380.9M` in the October 2025 quarter and `$213.9M` in the January 2025 quarter.
- Accounts receivable rose to `$1.36B` from `$1.01B` in the prior quarter.

The M&A activity is manageable given the cash balance, but it introduces integration and execution risk. The receivables increase likely reflects billing seasonality and growth rather than immediate stress, especially since deferred revenue also increased sharply, but it is still a metric to watch for collection efficiency.

### Stock-based compensation and dilution

This is the clearest fundamental caution flag.

Latest-quarter stock-based compensation was about `$274M`, and the last four quarters sum to about `$1.10B`. That is roughly `22.8%` of LTM revenue, which is high. Latest-quarter SBC alone was about `21.0%` of revenue.

Share count also continued to rise:

- Diluted average shares latest quarter: `258.1M`
- Diluted average shares year-ago quarter: `246.9M`
- YoY dilution: about `2.6%`

This means part of CRWD’s excellent cash generation is offset by meaningful equity compensation. Traders should not treat free cash flow as fully equivalent to owner earnings without adjusting for ongoing dilution.

### Valuation

CRWD is still expensive on conventional multiples:

- Forward P/E: `67.7`
- Price to book: `23.9`
- Market cap / TTM revenue: about `22x`

Those are premium multiples even for a best-in-class software franchise. The market is clearly underwriting a long runway of durable growth plus an expanding earnings base. That can work if the latest quarter marks the start of a sustained margin breakout. It becomes a problem if growth slows into the high teens while the multiple remains built for something better.

### Macro sensitivity

The macro backdrop is mixed but not hostile.

- Fed funds rate: `3.64%`, unchanged recently
- 2Y Treasury: `3.76%`
- 5Y Treasury: `3.90%`
- CPI and PPI both moved higher in the latest reading
- Unemployment eased to `4.3%`

For CRWD, the main macro issue is not demand collapse. Cybersecurity spending tends to hold up relatively well because it is mission-critical. The bigger issue is valuation sensitivity: a premium, long-duration growth stock is more vulnerable when rates are not falling aggressively. So the macro backdrop supports business resilience better than it supports multiple expansion.

### What matters most next for CRWD

The next checkpoints that matter most for traders are:

- Revenue growth staying above `20%` year over year.
- Gross margin holding around `75%` or better.
- Operating margin remaining positive or at least much less negative than in fiscal 2025.
- Free cash flow margin staying above `25%`.
- Stock-based compensation declining as a percentage of revenue.
- No major deterioration in receivables quality or sales efficiency.

### Bottom line

Fundamentally, CRWD looks strong and is getting stronger. The business now combines scale, high gross margins, improving operating leverage, strong free cash flow, heavy deferred revenue support, and a fortress balance sheet. That is the bullish case.

The bear case is not about financial distress or weak demand. It is about price and expectations. CRWD still carries a very rich valuation, and SBC/dilution remains high. The stock’s recent underperformance versus peers and sector benchmarks suggests the market wants more proof before rewarding the company’s improved fundamentals with a higher multiple again.

The practical trading read is that CRWD is fundamentally constructive but valuation-sensitive. If the next print confirms another quarter of `20%+` growth, positive operating income, and strong free cash flow, the current stock lag could reverse quickly. If growth or guide softens, the multiple leaves room for further compression even though the core business remains high quality.
