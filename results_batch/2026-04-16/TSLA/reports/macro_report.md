The Fed already eased from `4.09%` in October 2025 to `3.64%` by January 2026, then paused through March, which suggests policy has shifted from cutting to wait-and-see.

For `TSLA`, that is a mixed macro setup. Growth is not collapsing, which helps auto demand and broad equity risk appetite, but sticky inflation plus a paused Fed is not ideal for a long-duration growth stock. `TSLA` is exposed both through valuation duration and through end-market financing sensitivity: if rates stay near current levels for longer, auto affordability and incremental multiple expansion both stay constrained. The hot PPI print also matters for `TSLA` because producer inflation can pressure industrial and supply-chain costs at a time when EV pricing power is still competitive rather than dominant.

Policy direction for `TSLA`: the most likely near-term path is Fed hold, with cuts delayed unless inflation cools again. That makes the macro impulse neutral to slightly negative for `TSLA` over the next few months. A renewed disinflation trend would be a clear bullish macro catalyst for `TSLA`; another upside inflation surprise would raise the risk of higher real yields and renewed pressure on high-beta growth equities.

It is a higher-for-longer front-end message, not an imminent easing message. The provided curve data do not include intermediate or long maturities, so recession-signal confidence is limited here; other analysts should not overstate curve-steepening or inversion conclusions from this snapshot alone.
