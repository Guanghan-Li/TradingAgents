### TSLA Segment View
TSLA is still overwhelmingly an automotive company, with Energy Generation & Storage the only business line large enough to materially improve mix quality. The prefetched quarterly P&L shows consolidated gross margin recovering from about 16.3% in Q1 2025 to about 20.1% in Q4 2025, while operating margin improved from about 2.5% to about 6.3%. That points to better cost absorption and mix after an early-2025 trough, but earnings durability remains heavily concentrated in vehicle volume, pricing, and competitive intensity.

The highest-quality growth vector appears to be energy storage, which likely carries better backlog visibility and less consumer-financing sensitivity than autos. Services/other and leasing add ecosystem support, but they are too small to offset a real automotive downturn. Recent news around AI5 chips, Terafab, and Optimus strengthens long-duration optionality for TSLA, but those themes still look more like future call options than current profit anchors. A near-term concentration risk is that reported delivery support from affiliated/fleet buying would help volume optics without proving broad end-demand.
