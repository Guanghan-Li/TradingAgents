TSM fundamental report as of 2026-04-16, using the prefetched live fundamentals context as primary evidence.

TSM remains one of the strongest large-cap semiconductor fundamentals in the dataset. The core picture is accelerating revenue, expanding margins, very high returns on capital, massive ongoing fab investment, and enough liquidity to fund that investment while still paying dividends. The main debate for traders is no longer business quality; it is whether current valuation already discounts a large share of that strength.

One important normalization note: the financial statement line items appear to be reported in TWD based on scale and company reporting conventions, while market-based valuation fields such as market cap, EPS, PE, and book value appear ADR/USD-based. That is an inference from the dataset, and traders should avoid mixing those figures mechanically without FX and ADR-ratio normalization.

**Company Profile And Quality**

TSM is the dominant pure-play semiconductor foundry in this dataset, positioned at the center of AI, high-performance compute, smartphone, automotive, and industrial semiconductor demand. The quality signals are unusually strong for a manufacturer:

- TTM revenue: 3.81T
- TTM gross profit: 2.28T
- TTM EBITDA: 2.61T
- TTM net income: 1.72T
- Profit margin: 45.10%
- Operating margin: 53.92%
- ROE: 35.06%
- ROA: 16.56%
- Current ratio: 2.62
- Debt to equity: 19.57%
- Free cash flow: 643.45B

These are elite profitability and balance-sheet metrics. A 45% net margin and 35% ROE at this scale indicate exceptional pricing power, process leadership, and operating discipline.

**Income Statement Trend**

The latest quarter, 2025-12-31, was very strong.

- Revenue was 1.046T, up about 20.5% YoY from 868.5B in 2024-12-31.
- Revenue rose about 5.7% sequentially from 989.9B in 2025-09-30.
- Gross profit reached 652.0B, up about 27.2% YoY.
- Operating income was 564.9B, up about 32.7% YoY.
- Net income was 485.5B, up about 34.9% YoY.
- Diluted EPS was 97.5 versus 72.24 a year earlier, also up about 34.9%.

The shape of 2025 matters even more than the single quarter. Revenue progressed from 839.3B in Q1 to 933.8B in Q2, 989.9B in Q3, and 1.046T in Q4. Net income followed the same path: 360.7B, 398.3B, 452.3B, and 485.5B. That is not a one-off spike; it is sustained quarterly acceleration.

Margins also improved materially:

- Gross margin in 2025-12 was about 62.3%.
- Gross margin in 2024-12 was about 59.0%.
- Operating margin in 2025-12 was about 54.0%.
- Net margin in 2025-12 was about 46.4%.

This is the most bullish part of the report. TSM did not grow by sacrificing profitability. It grew while widening margins.

Another favorable detail is operating leverage. Operating expense in 2025-12 was 87.1B, nearly flat versus 86.7B in 2024-12, while revenue increased by more than 20%. R&D still increased to 64.9B from 57.2B, so TSM is still investing in technology leadership while keeping overhead disciplined.

**Balance Sheet And Liquidity**

TSM’s balance sheet is strong and improving.

- Total assets increased to 7.93T from 6.69T in 2024-12.
- Stockholders’ equity increased to 5.36T from 4.24T.
- Working capital increased to 2.29T from 1.78T.
- Cash and cash equivalents rose to 2.768T.
- Cash, equivalents, and short-term investments totaled 3.128T.
- Total debt was 1.065T.

On a simple cash-plus-short-term-investments minus total debt basis, TSM still appears to be in a very strong net cash position of roughly 2.06T. That matches the positive net interest income profile:

- Interest income in 2025-12: 29.5B
- Interest expense in 2025-12: 3.0B
- Net interest income: 26.5B

This is a major stabilizer for traders. TSM is not relying on aggressive leverage to support growth.

Asset composition also shows where the company is deploying capital:

- Net PPE rose to 3.736T from 3.275T in 2024-12.
- Construction in progress rose to 1.519T from 1.080T.

That large increase in construction in progress suggests aggressive fab buildout remains underway. This supports a long-duration growth view, but it also means execution and utilization remain key variables.

Working-capital quality looks healthy:

- Inventory was 288.1B, almost flat YoY.
- Receivables were 282.1B, up only modestly versus revenue growth.
- Payables rose to 877.9B, broadly consistent with scale expansion.

The fact that receivables and inventory are not exploding alongside revenue is a favorable sign for earnings quality.

**Cash Flow And Capital Allocation**

Cash generation remains very strong even under extreme capex.

Latest quarter, 2025-12-31:

- Operating cash flow: 725.5B
- Capex: 361.5B
- Free cash flow: 364.0B
- Cash dividends paid: 129.7B

That means TSM covered dividends about 2.8x with free cash flow and about 5.6x with operating cash flow in the quarter. The dividend yield is only 0.93%, so the payout is conservative relative to cash generation.

Across 2025, free cash flow stayed positive every quarter:

- Q1: 292.5B
- Q2: 197.5B
- Q3: 138.4B
- Q4: 364.0B

The volatility is mainly capex-driven, not because operations are weak. Capex stayed extremely high all year:

- Q1: 333.1B
- Q2: 299.6B
- Q3: 288.4B
- Q4: 361.5B

This is both a strength and a risk. It shows confidence and demand visibility, but it also makes the equity more sensitive to any future slowdown in utilization, pricing, or node-mix.

**What Matters Most For Traders**

- The bull case is straightforward: revenue is accelerating, margins are expanding, capex is being absorbed by very strong operating cash flow, and the balance sheet remains fortress-like.
- The strongest single datapoint is Q4 2025 net income growth of about 35% YoY on revenue growth of about 20.5% YoY. That is real operating leverage.
- The strongest balance-sheet datapoint is cash plus short-term investments of about 3.13T against total debt of about 1.06T.
- The strongest cash-flow datapoint is 725.5B of quarterly operating cash flow even while spending 361.5B on capex.
- The main risk is expectation risk, not financial stress. A 34.7x trailing PE and 55.6x price-to-book mean the stock already reflects a very high-quality narrative.
- The main operating risk in the numbers is capex intensity. If end-demand or pricing weakens, free cash flow can compress quickly because investment levels are very high.
- The short-term tape is softer than the medium-term fundamentals. That can either be a pause before continuation or an early sign of multiple compression. Traders should watch whether relative weakness persists without any deterioration in the next revenue or margin prints.

**Actionable Monitoring List For TSM**

- Watch whether quarterly revenue continues to compound above 1.0T. If revenue stalls while capex stays above 300B, sentiment could compress quickly.
- Watch gross margin. Holding near or above 60% is a key proof point that node mix and pricing power remain intact.
- Watch free cash flow, not just earnings. TSM’s investment cycle is so large that FCF resilience is a more important stress test than net income alone.
- Watch construction in progress and net PPE growth against future revenue realization. This is where execution risk sits.
- Watch relative performance versus XLK and the semiconductor peer group. If fundamentals stay elite and short-term underperformance persists, that may create tactical entry opportunities.
- Watch forward-earnings revisions. The current forward PE assumes a large earnings step-up; that assumption must keep being confirmed.
