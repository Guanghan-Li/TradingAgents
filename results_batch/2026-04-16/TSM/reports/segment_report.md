### Segment View

TSM's business-mix quality remains strong and is improving. The prefetched quarterly results show revenue rising from TWD 839.3bn in 2025-03 to TWD 1,046.1bn in 2025-12, while gross margin expanded from about 58.8% to 62.3% and operating margin from about 48.5% to 54.0%. That pattern is consistent with a richer mix of leading-edge wafers and advanced packaging, with recent news flow reinforcing that AI demand is the key near-term catalyst.

HPC is the core value driver and likely the largest segment by revenue now. It has the best durability because AI accelerators, custom silicon, and leading-edge compute workloads depend on TSM's process leadership and packaging ecosystem. Smartphone is still a large business, but it is more seasonal and customer-concentrated; it supports scale and node absorption, yet it is no longer the main quality driver. IoT and automotive add diversification and stickier long-tail demand, though they are smaller and less margin-accretive than HPC. DCE/consumer/other is the weakest bucket strategically and most exposed to cyclical swings.

The main concentration risk is that mix quality is increasingly tied to HPC/AI and a relatively small number of flagship customers and programs. That is positive while AI demand stays supply-constrained, but it raises sensitivity to any digestion cycle, packaging bottleneck, export restriction, or competitive change at the high end. Net/net, the segment mix still supports a bullish operating profile because the biggest segment also appears to be the most margin-accretive.
