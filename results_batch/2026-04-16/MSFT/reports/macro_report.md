Macro view for `MSFT`: the backdrop is a soft-landing but not clean disinflation regime. Growth and labor are still holding up enough to prevent an urgent easing cycle, while inflation has re-accelerated at the margin. That leaves the Fed in a likely `on hold / mildly hawkish` stance rather than a near-term cutting path.

Key read-through from the supplied data:
- Policy: effective fed funds is `3.64%` and unchanged for three straight monthly observations through March 2026 after cuts from late 2025. That argues the easing phase has paused.
- Inflation: CPI rose `+0.87%` sequential in March and PPI `+1.78%` sequential. Even allowing for index noise, both prints point to sticky pipeline inflation rather than continued clean cooling.
- Labor: unemployment improved to `4.3%` and nonfarm payrolls increased `+178k`, which is consistent with a still-resilient labor market, not recessionary deterioration.
- Curve: the available Treasury curve from `1M` to `3Y` is mildly upward sloping (`3.72%` at 1M, `3.71%` at 3M, `3.76%` at 2Y, `3.79%` at 3Y). That is not a classic near-term recession signal; it is more consistent with policy staying restrictive-to-neutral for longer.

Implications for `MSFT`:
- Baseline macro regime is supportive for top-line resilience because enterprise spending usually holds up better in a soft landing than in a recession.
- The main macro headwind is valuation, not immediate demand destruction. `MSFT` is a long-duration mega-cap, so a higher-for-longer rate path or renewed upward pressure in Treasury yields can compress multiples even if fundamentals remain solid.
- If inflation stays sticky, the market may reprice cuts farther out, which is typically less favorable for premium software and AI leaders like `MSFT` than for lower-duration value sectors.
- If the economy stays firm without overheating further, `MSFT` can still outperform many cyclicals because cash flow quality and balance-sheet strength matter more in a restrictive policy regime.

Likely policy direction:
- Near term: pause.
- Conditional bias: the next move is still probably easing over a longer horizon, but the immediate path has shifted toward delayed cuts because inflation is not cooperating.
- Market risk: any upside inflation surprise raises the probability of a longer hold, which is the key macro risk for `MSFT`.

Concrete risks for the committee to incorporate on `MSFT`:
- Rate sensitivity: sticky inflation could lift real yields and pressure `MSFT`'s earnings multiple.
- FX sensitivity: a relatively hawkish Fed can support the USD, creating translation headwinds for a globally exposed company like `MSFT`.
- Demand sensitivity: while current labor data are stable, a later policy mistake from staying tight too long could weaken enterprise IT budgets with a lag.
- Data quality gap: GDP, ISM, consumer confidence, and VIX were unavailable in the prefetched context, so conviction on broad-cycle acceleration vs slowdown is lower than usual.
