## NEE Scenario Summary

NEE screens as a high-quality regulated utility / renewables platform, but the setup is no longer cheap after a strong rerating: the stock is near its 52-week high, above both the 50-day and 200-day averages, and trades at about 27.8x TTM EPS and 21.0x forward EPS. The core bull case rests on incremental load growth from data-center demand, continued earnings durability, and a rate backdrop that is no longer tightening. The base case is that NEE remains a premium utility compounder, but upside is moderated because expectations have already improved and free cash flow remains deeply negative with leverage still material. The bear case is that valuation de-rates if earnings disappoint, financing needs stay elevated, or long-duration yields keep drifting higher.

Macro is mixed but not hostile for NEE. Fed funds are stable at 3.64%, the curve is positively sloped, and equity volatility is moderate, which helps duration-sensitive utilities. Offsetting that, CPI and PPI both re-accelerated in the latest print, while the 10-year Treasury at 4.29% and 30-year near 4.89% keep pressure on capital-intensive balance sheets. For NEE specifically, the key debate is whether data-center and power-demand optimism can outrun valuation compression risk and funding sensitivity.

### Invalidation Logic

- Bull case invalidates if NEE cannot translate data-center enthusiasm into measurable backlog, customer wins, or forward EPS revision support.
- Base case invalidates if earnings and guidance materially surprise in either direction, because current valuation leaves limited room for merely "fine" execution.
- Bear case invalidates if long-end yields stabilize or fall and NEE posts a clean quarter with confident funding and growth commentary, preserving the premium multiple.
