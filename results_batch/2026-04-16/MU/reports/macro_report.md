Macro view for `MU`: the backdrop is still consistent with a soft-landing / mid-cycle expansion rather than an imminent recession, but the inflation impulse has re-accelerated enough to keep the Fed cautious. Labor is holding up, with unemployment at 4.3% on 2026-03-01 and nonfarm payrolls up 178k, while the Treasury curve is positively sloped with a 2Y-10Y spread of +53 bps as of 2026-04-15. That combination argues against a near-term macro recession call and is generally supportive for cyclical equities like `MU`.

The main complication is inflation. CPI and PPI both showed strong sequential increases in the latest March 2026 readings, and the long end of the curve is notably elevated, with the 10Y at 4.29% and the 30Y at 4.89%. The Fed has already eased materially from 4.09% in October 2025 to 3.64% by January-March 2026, but the recent inflation prints make a fast follow-on easing cycle less likely. My regime read is: growth resilient, labor decent, inflation sticky, Fed near neutral but biased to wait. For `MU`, that is a mixed macro setup: supportive for semiconductor demand if growth stays intact, but a headwind for valuation if long yields keep rising.

Trading implications for `MU`: macro is not flashing recession risk, so the demand side is better than in a classic downturn scenario. A normal upward-sloping curve usually helps pro-cyclical sectors, and stable volatility around VIX 18 also supports risk appetite. However, `MU` is still sensitive to real-rate duration pressure, capex sentiment, and any renewed inflation scare that pushes the 10Y higher. In committee terms, macro is a modest positive for `MU` fundamentals but not an all-clear for multiple expansion. Other analysts should stress-test `MU` against two macro paths: 1) soft landing with delayed but eventual Fed easing, which is constructive; 2) sticky inflation with higher-for-longer long yields, which can cap upside even if earnings remain solid.

Key risks other analysts should incorporate for `MU`:
- Inflation re-acceleration forcing the Fed to stay on hold longer than equity markets expect.
- Rising term premium and long-end yields compressing valuation for growth/cyclical tech.
- Missing PMI / confidence data reduces visibility into manufacturing momentum and end-demand breadth.
- If labor stays too firm, the market may reprice out cuts, which is usually a near-term headwind for higher-beta semis.
