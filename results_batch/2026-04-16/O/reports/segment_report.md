## O Segment Summary
`O` has a high-quality but still concentrated revenue base. As of December 31, 2025, property-type mix was 79.1% retail, 15.4% industrial, 3.1% gaming, and 2.4% other by annualized base rent. That mix is durable because the portfolio ran at 98.9% occupancy, property expenses were only 1.6% of revenue, and adjusted EBITDA margin was about 94.4%, which is consistent with the triple-net lease model. Within retail, the mix is tilted toward defensive categories such as grocery, convenience, and home improvement, which helps stabilize cash flow.

Growth is positive across all major property types, but the best incremental momentum is outside legacy retail concentration. 2025 same-store rental revenue growth was 1.1% in retail, 2.0% in industrial, 1.8% in gaming, and 2.3% in other. New 2025 investments were 69.5% retail and 30.0% industrial by cash income, and Europe represented $3.7 billion of 2025 volume at a 7.4% initial cash yield, so `O` is diversifying faster at the margin than the current rent mix suggests.

The main margin debate for `O` is not property-level profitability but capital costs. Company revenue and normalized EBITDA improved through 2025, but interest expense remained elevated around $263 million to $287 million per quarter. March 2026 funding actions, including the Apollo retail JV and the $800 million 2033 notes issuance, improve liquidity and reduce refinancing pressure, which is supportive for the core retail engine. The key concentration risk is that `O` is still primarily a retail net-lease vehicle, so segment fundamentals look steady, but trading upside depends heavily on funding spreads and capital-market access.
