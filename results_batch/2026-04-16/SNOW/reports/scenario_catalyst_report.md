## SNOW Scenario Summary
SNOW screens as a high-duration software name where the debate is still centered on growth durability versus valuation compression. The fundamental setup is mixed: revenue scale is strong at about $4.68B TTM and free cash flow is positive at about $1.59B, but GAAP profitability remains negative, valuation is still rich at about 59.1x forward earnings and 25.7x book, and the stock is trading well below its 200-day average of 211.5. Recent company-specific news flow is modestly constructive around observability expansion and generally favorable sell-side tone, but not strong enough on its own to offset execution risk if product consumption or enterprise optimization worsens.

My framing is a **base case of controlled re-acceleration but continued multiple discipline**, a **bull case of platform expansion driving a cleaner growth re-rating**, and a **bear case where expensive valuation meets slower consumption growth**. Macro is not overtly hostile for software: the Fed backdrop has eased versus late 2025, the curve is positively sloped, and VIX is contained, but CPI and long-end yields still matter because SNOW remains sensitive to discount-rate moves.

## Key Signposts
- SNOW product and observability commentary translating into broader platform adoption rather than isolated feature messaging.
- Evidence that free cash flow strength remains intact even if GAAP losses persist.
- Any change in investor tolerance for premium software multiples if Treasury yields continue to rise from the current 10Y level of 4.29%.
- Whether the stock can reclaim and hold levels closer to the 200-day average; failure to do so keeps sentiment fragile.

## Thesis Invalidation Logic
- The constructive thesis weakens materially if SNOW shows slowing consumption trends without a compensating margin improvement story.
- The bear thesis is weakened if SNOW delivers clear cross-sell traction in observability/data platform expansion with sustained FCF conversion.
- The base case is invalidated if either growth clearly re-accelerates enough to justify premium multiple expansion, or fundamentals deteriorate enough to force a broad de-rating.
