That sizing output supports the conservative and neutral analysts: `SNOW` is at best a tightly risk-budgeted watchlist candidate, not a position the portfolio needs to carry today.

The technical setup reinforces that conclusion. The aggressive analyst is right that the equity story is not permanently broken, but the trader, conservative analyst, and neutral analyst are correct that the entry is unproven. `SNOW` at 143.55 remains below the 50 SMA of 164.41 and the 200 SMA of 211.10 after a sharp drop from 182.84 on March 9, 2026 to 121.11 on April 10, 2026. That is still a damaged trend. With negative MACD, RSI below 50, ATR around 9.45, and immediate resistance at 149 to 151, the stock has not earned fresh long exposure.

Fundamentals are strong enough to prevent a structural collapse call, but not strong enough to override timing and valuation risk. Revenue grew to $1.284B in the latest quarter, roughly 30% year over year, and gross margins held around 66% to 68%, which supports the aggressive analyst’s point that the platform still has quality and scale. But the conservative analyst has the more decision-useful evidence right now: TTM net income is about -$1.33B, operating margin is -33.2%, SBC was about $403.6M or roughly 31% of revenue, and dilution continued. Free cash flow is positive, but its quality is not yet clean because the strong quarter was helped by a roughly $541.2M working-capital benefit and deferred-revenue support.

The bullish narrative also lacks segment proof. In the structured business-unit work, `revenue_share_pct` is null for the key adjacencies, including observability and AI/application-layer workloads. That means the committee does not yet have hard evidence that newer products are large enough to diversify the core consumption engine. This is exactly why the neutral and conservative analysts argue that observability is still thematic rather than measurable.

Macro does not rescue the setup. The backdrop is soft-landing rather than recessionary, which limits fundamental collapse risk, but that is not enough for a premium software name like `SNOW`. With the 10-year yield at 4.29%, sticky inflation, and a Fed that is no longer clearly easing, multiple expansion remains constrained. That keeps the bar high for a stock that is still technically weak and fundamentally unprofitable.

Decision rule: stay out now, or reduce if already long. Upgrade `SNOW` only after three things happen together: price reclaims 149 to 151 and holds, the stock starts repairing toward the 50-day area near 164.41, and the next company update shows better consumption trends, less SBC intensity, and free cash flow that is less dependent on working-capital timing. Until then, the correct portfolio posture is **Sell / Underweight**.
