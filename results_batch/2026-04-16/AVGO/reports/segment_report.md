**AVGO Segment View**

Broadcom's business mix is now anchored by two durable platforms: Semiconductor Solutions, increasingly led by AI data-center silicon and networking, and Infrastructure Software, enlarged by VMware and reinforced by cybersecurity and enterprise software franchises. The reported quarterly trend is constructive: revenue rose from $14.9B in Jan. 2025 to $19.3B in Jan. 2026, while gross profit and operating income expanded, suggesting positive operating leverage despite elevated R&D and VMware-related integration costs.

Semiconductor Solutions has the stronger growth optionality because AVGO is positioned in custom AI accelerators, high-speed networking, switching, and connectivity for hyperscale data centers. Recent news around an expanded Meta AI chip partnership reinforces demand visibility, but it also raises customer concentration risk: a larger share of upside depends on a small number of hyperscale AI programs. Non-AI semiconductor lines remain more cyclical and exposed to enterprise, broadband, storage, and wireless demand normalization.

Infrastructure Software provides recurring revenue, high margins, and cash-flow durability. VMware integration should support margin expansion through portfolio rationalization and enterprise renewals, but customer pushback, renewal timing, and debt-service demands remain key risks. Overall, segment quality is high, with AI semiconductors driving upside and software improving revenue durability.

|---|---:|---|---|

| Legacy / non-AI semiconductor franchises | Mixed, tied to broadband, wireless, storage, and industrial cycles | Flattish to cyclical depending on end-market recovery | Neutral; less important to upside but can dilute AI-driven growth if demand weakens |
