AVGO fundamental report as of 2026-04-16

Broadcom Inc. (AVGO) remains one of the strongest large-cap fundamental stories in semiconductors on the numbers provided, with three things standing out immediately: revenue is still compounding at a high rate, margins remain unusually strong for a hardware-plus-infrastructure-software platform, and free cash flow is large enough to support both dividends and buybacks while leverage gradually improves. The main counterweights are a rich valuation on trailing earnings, a balance sheet dominated by goodwill/intangibles, and capital returns that currently exceed quarterly free cash flow when buybacks are elevated.

Company profile

AVGO operates in Technology, within Semiconductors, but the financial profile is not that of a pure commodity chip vendor. Its mix appears to benefit from scale, sticky enterprise exposure, and acquisition-driven software/intangible assets. That shows up in the balance sheet and profitability structure:

- Market cap: about $1.889 trillion
- TTM revenue: $68.28 billion
- TTM EBITDA: $37.22 billion
- TTM net income: $24.97 billion
- TTM operating margin: 44.94%
- TTM profit margin: 36.57%
- Free cash flow: $25.50 billion
- Dividend yield: 0.66%
- Beta: 1.253

This is a very high-quality earnings base in absolute dollar terms. Few companies globally produce this amount of cash with this level of margin.

Income statement quality and operating trend

The latest reported quarter, 2026-01-31, was strong on nearly every operating line.

Latest quarter vs prior quarter:

- Revenue: $19.311 billion vs $18.015 billion, up 7.2% sequentially
- Gross profit: $13.157 billion vs $12.249 billion, up 7.4%
- Operating income: $8.666 billion vs $7.654 billion, up 13.2%
- EBITDA: $11.149 billion vs $9.863 billion, up 13.0%
- Net income: $7.349 billion vs $8.518 billion, down 13.7%

That net income decline is not the right headline takeaway. Operating performance improved materially; the decline in reported net income was largely driven by tax normalization. In 2025-10-31, AVGO had a negative tax provision of $1.649 billion, while in 2026-01-31 it recorded a positive tax provision of $846 million. Pretax income actually rose to $8.195 billion from $6.869 billion.

Latest quarter year over year vs 2025-01-31:

- Revenue: $19.311 billion vs $14.916 billion, up about 29.5%
- Gross profit: $13.157 billion vs $10.145 billion, up about 29.7%
- Operating income: $8.666 billion vs $6.432 billion, up about 34.7%
- Net income: $7.349 billion vs $5.503 billion, up about 33.5%
- Diluted EPS: $1.50 vs $1.14, up about 31.6%

This is the profile traders want to see in a premium multiple name: strong top-line growth with operating leverage, not growth bought at the expense of margins.

Margin structure

Using the latest quarter:

- Gross margin: about 68.1% ($13.157B / $19.311B)
- Operating margin: about 44.9% ($8.666B / $19.311B)
- EBITDA margin: about 57.7% ($11.149B / $19.311B)
- Net margin: about 38.1% ($7.349B / $19.311B)

Those are elite margins. They indicate pricing power, favorable mix, and substantial fixed-cost leverage. R&D also remains large at $2.965 billion in the latest quarter, which suggests AVGO is not starving innovation to manufacture margins.

A notable nuance is that R&D is rising meaningfully:

- R&D: $2.965 billion in 2026-01-31 vs $2.253 billion in 2025-01-31, up about 31.6%

That is healthy because it roughly matches revenue growth rather than collapsing as a percentage of sales. AVGO is preserving investment intensity while expanding profits.

Cash flow and earnings conversion

AVGO’s cash generation is one of the strongest parts of the story.

Latest quarter cash flow:

- Operating cash flow: $8.260 billion
- Capex: $250 million
- Free cash flow: $8.010 billion

Year-over-year vs 2025-01-31:

- Operating cash flow: $8.260 billion vs $6.113 billion, up about 35.1%
- Free cash flow: $8.010 billion vs $6.013 billion, up about 33.2%

Cash conversion remains excellent:

- Latest quarter FCF / net income: about 109%
- TTM FCF / TTM net income: about 102%

That means reported earnings are backed by real cash, which is critical for a company with heavy acquisition accounting and large amortization. Capex is also modest relative to revenue:

- Capex as % of latest-quarter revenue: about 1.3%

That low capital intensity is favorable for equity holders because incremental revenue can translate into very high incremental free cash flow.

One caution inside cash flow is working capital drag:

- Change in working capital in latest quarter: negative $3.105 billion
- Receivables increased by $1.315 billion
- Inventory increased by $692 million

Even with that drag, AVGO still produced $8.26 billion of operating cash flow. That is a bullish signal. If working capital normalizes, cash generation could stay very strong.

Balance sheet, leverage, and capital structure

The balance sheet is mixed: liquid and improving on one hand, acquisition-heavy on the other.

Strengths:

- Cash and equivalents: $14.174 billion
- Current ratio: 1.902
- Working capital: $15.203 billion, versus only $80 million a year earlier
- Stockholders’ equity: $79.872 billion, up from $69.789 billion year over year
- Net debt: $51.883 billion, down from $57.250 billion year over year

Debt remains large in absolute terms:

- Total debt: $66.057 billion
- Long-term debt: $63.805 billion

But leverage looks manageable against EBITDA:

- Gross debt / TTM EBITDA: about 1.8x
- Net debt / TTM EBITDA: about 1.4x

That is not conservative, but it is reasonable for a company with AVGO’s cash generation.

The larger structural concern is asset quality:

- Goodwill and other intangibles: $128.103 billion
- Total assets: $169.903 billion
- Tangible book value: negative $48.231 billion

So roughly three quarters of total assets are goodwill or intangibles. That means book-based valuation metrics are not very informative, and it means AVGO is more exposed to impairment risk or acquisition-integration disappointment than a cleaner balance sheet company would be. Price-to-book at 23.63 should therefore not be treated as a primary valuation anchor.

Capital allocation

AVGO is returning a massive amount of capital to shareholders, but the mix deserves scrutiny.

Latest quarter capital returns:

- Dividends paid: $3.086 billion
- Share repurchases: $7.850 billion
- Total direct shareholder return: about $10.936 billion

Against:

- Free cash flow: $8.010 billion

So in the latest quarter, shareholder returns exceeded free cash flow by about $2.9 billion. That gap was not disastrous because AVGO still has balance sheet flexibility and also issued debt during the quarter, but it does matter. It implies that the latest pace of buybacks is more aggressive than internally funded cash generation alone would support on a steady-state basis.

Dividend quality looks solid:

- Dividend yield is only 0.66%, mostly because the stock has appreciated sharply
- Quarterly dividend cash outlay of $3.086 billion is covered about 2.6x by quarterly FCF

Buyback quality is more mixed:

- Repurchases were very large in the latest quarter at $7.85 billion
- Yet ordinary shares outstanding were 4.736 billion vs 4.702 billion a year earlier
- Diluted average shares were 4.888 billion vs 4.836 billion a year earlier

That suggests stock-based compensation and issuance are offsetting at least part of the buyback effect. Traders should not assume buybacks are translating one-for-one into per-share accretion.

Valuation

The valuation is expensive on trailing numbers but much more reasonable on forward earnings.

- TTM P/E: 77.83
- Forward P/E: 22.14
- TTM EPS: 5.12
- Forward EPS: 17.99684

That large gap between trailing and forward P/E implies the market is pricing in substantial earnings expansion, likely reflecting the view that reported trailing EPS understates the earnings power of the business because of amortization, tax effects, and growth acceleration.

The key read-through for traders is this:

- AVGO is not cheap on backward-looking GAAP optics
- AVGO is more defensible on forward operating power and cash flow
- If growth or margin delivery slips even modestly, the multiple can compress quickly because expectations are already elevated

Interpretation:

- Rate pressure has eased versus tighter-policy periods, which helps long-duration growth equities
- Inflation is not fully dead, so valuation-rich names still need execution
- A normal yield curve and contained VIX are supportive for momentum and cyclically sensitive technology leadership

For AVGO specifically, this backdrop supports continued premium valuation, but only if revenue growth and cash generation stay strong.

Key risks

1. Valuation risk. A forward P/E around 22 is reasonable only if AVGO keeps delivering high-20s to low-30s earnings growth and very high margins. If growth slows, the stock can rerate even if the business remains healthy.

2. Balance-sheet composition risk. Negative tangible book and $128.1 billion of goodwill/intangibles create accounting and perception risk in any integration stumble.

3. Capital return sustainability risk. The latest quarter’s combination of dividends plus buybacks exceeded free cash flow. That is fine occasionally, but not ideal as a lasting pattern.

4. Working-capital risk. Receivables and inventory both rose sharply, which may simply reflect growth, but traders should watch whether that trend persists faster than revenue.

5. Interest burden. Latest-quarter interest expense was $801 million. It is manageable, but not trivial.

What matters most in the next reports

For AVGO, traders should focus on these forward checkpoints:

- Revenue staying above the $18-19 billion quarterly range and continuing double-digit year-over-year growth
- Operating margin holding near or above 44%
- Free cash flow staying above roughly $7 billion per quarter
- Net debt continuing to trend down from the current $51.9 billion area
- Buybacks translating into actual share-count reduction, not just offsetting SBC
- No material deterioration in goodwill/intangible asset quality or impairment signals

Actionable fundamental interpretation for traders

Bullish interpretation:

- AVGO is producing elite margins, strong year-over-year revenue growth, and excellent free-cash-flow conversion.
- The latest quarter showed operating acceleration, not deterioration.
- Relative strength versus XLK and peers confirms institutions are rewarding the fundamentals.
- Net leverage is declining, and the dividend is very well covered.

Neutral-to-cautious interpretation:

- A lot of good news is already in the stock, especially after strong 1-month and 3-month outperformance.
- The balance sheet is acquisition-heavy, making downside psychology worse if any guidance slips.
- Buybacks are aggressive enough that traders should verify they are accretive rather than just optics.

Bottom line

Fundamentally, AVGO screens as a high-quality, cash-rich, high-margin compounder with strong recent operating momentum. The latest quarter supports the bull case: revenue growth was strong, operating leverage was real, free cash flow remained exceptional, and net debt improved year over year. The main reason not to be blindly aggressive is not business weakness; it is expectations. AVGO is now priced like a company that must keep executing at a very high level. For traders, that usually means dips on non-structural noise are more attractive than chasing parabolic extensions, unless future updates confirm another leg of earnings acceleration.
