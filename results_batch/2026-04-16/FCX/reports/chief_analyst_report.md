But FCX does not merit Overweight. The modal scenario is still the 45% base case, not the 30% bull case. That means FCX is trading more on expected recovery than on recently delivered results. The valuation split makes that clear: 44.9x trailing earnings versus about 17x forward earnings. If the next earnings report fails to validate the $4.02 forward EPS recovery, or if copper weakens, the stock can re-rate down quickly. That is why the conservative analyst is right about tight risk control and why the neutral analyst is right that this is economically closer to a conditional Buy than an all-clear bullish call.

So the committee should buy FCX now, but treat it as a disciplined starter. The debate resolves in favor of action because trend, macro, and underwriting support owning FCX; it resolves against Overweight because valuation, earnings-quality risk, and event risk still require proof before scaling.
