## FCX Scenario And Catalyst View

FCX is priced for a constructive copper cycle: the stock is near its 52-week high of $69.75, above its 50-day average of $61.89 and 200-day average of $49.64, with a high TTM P/E of 44.9x but a more cycle-normal forward P/E near 17.0x. The setup is therefore less about current trailing earnings and more about whether copper prices, volumes, and costs can deliver the forward EPS recovery implied by consensus. Recent analyst actions are broadly supportive, with Wells Fargo, Deutsche Bank, JPMorgan, HSBC, and Scotiabank maintaining positive ratings or raising price targets mostly into the low-to-high $70s.

Base case probability is highest: FCX consolidates near recent highs as copper fundamentals remain supportive but valuation leaves limited room for execution misses. Bull case requires copper strength and earnings revisions to continue, pushing investors to underwrite peak-cycle cash flow. Bear case centers on commodity reversal, higher real-rate pressure, cost inflation, or disappointment versus the expected earnings growth narrative.
