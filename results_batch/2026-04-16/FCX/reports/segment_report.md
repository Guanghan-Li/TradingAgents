### FCX Segment View

FCX is a high-quality but concentrated mining story: economically, it is still overwhelmingly a **copper** company, with **gold by-product exposure** materially improving unit economics at Indonesia and **molybdenum** acting as a smaller specialty metals contributor. Revenue durability is decent for a miner because FCX owns long-life, tier-one assets, but the mix remains exposed to copper price volatility, smelter/refining terms, grade timing, and Indonesia execution.

The key business-mix point is that **copper drives the thesis**, while **gold credits support margins and cash costs** rather than fully diversifying the model. That means upside is strongest when copper demand tightens on electrification/AI-data-center power buildout, but concentration risk is also high if copper prices soften or major mines see grade/throughput disruptions. The quarterly income statement supports this cyclicality: revenue and gross profit peaked in mid-2025, then fell sharply by Q4 2025, implying segment margins are highly volume/price sensitive rather than steadily recurring.

By segment, **Copper** remains the strategic core and primary value driver. **Gold** is strategically important because it lowers consolidated cash costs and boosts margin during strong by-product periods, especially from Indonesia-linked production. **Molybdenum** is smaller and less thesis-defining, but helps mix quality through specialty end-market exposure. Recent news flow is broadly supportive for the copper complex, with multiple analyst target increases and ongoing market narratives around AI/data-center electricity infrastructure, but the news set is more sentiment-supportive than a direct company-specific demand datapoint.

Biggest concentration risks are: (1) dependence on copper pricing for the bulk of EBITDA, (2) operational dependence on Indonesia/Grasberg for high-value copper-gold output, and (3) earnings sensitivity to quarterly grade timing, which likely explains the large sequential EBITDA/EBIT swing from 2025-06-30 to 2025-12-31.
