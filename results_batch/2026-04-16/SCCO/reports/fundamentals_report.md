SCCO fundamental report as of 2026-04-16

SCCO, Southern Copper Corporation, screens as a high-margin, cash-generative large-cap copper producer with unusually strong recent earnings momentum, a liquid balance sheet, and an equity story that is increasingly dependent on sustained copper-cycle strength. The current setup is fundamentally strong, but valuation is no longer cheap: SCCO trades at 36.27x trailing earnings, 30.52x forward earnings, and 14.09x book value after a major price run.

From a company profile standpoint, SCCO sits in the Basic Materials sector and Copper industry with a market capitalization of about $155.5 billion. The stock has been volatile enough to reflect commodity exposure, with a beta of 1.166. Price action has been powerful: SCCO is near the top of its 52-week range, with a 52-week high of $223.89 versus a low of $82.06. The 50-day average is $188.17 and the 200-day average is $140.71, confirming a strong medium-term uptrend.

Financial quality is the core bull case. On a trailing twelve-month basis, SCCO generated $13.42 billion of revenue, $8.17 billion of gross profit, $7.90 billion of EBITDA, and $4.33 billion of net income. That implies approximate margins of 60.9% gross, 58.9% EBITDA, 54.5% operating, and 32.3% net. Those are elite profitability levels for a cyclical materials company. Return on equity is 42.7% and return on assets is 21.8%, which indicates management is converting the asset base and equity capital into earnings at a very high rate.

The quarterly income statement shows a clear acceleration trend through 2025. Revenue increased sequentially from $3.12 billion in 2025-03-31 to $3.05 billion in 2025-06-30, then to $3.38 billion in 2025-09-30 and $3.87 billion in 2025-12-31. Gross profit rose from $1.58 billion in 2025-03-31 to $2.17 billion in 2025-12-31. EBITDA climbed from $1.79 billion to $2.36 billion over the same span. Net income improved from $945.9 million in 2025-03-31 to $1.31 billion in 2025-12-31. Diluted EPS moved from $1.147 in Q1 2025 to $1.547 in Q4 2025. That is a meaningful earnings ramp, not just a stable base business.

The year-over-year comparison versus 2024-12-31 is also strong. Q4 2025 revenue of $3.87 billion was up about 39.0% from $2.78 billion. Net income of $1.31 billion was up about 64.7% from $793.9 million. EBITDA of $2.36 billion was up about 52.7% from $1.55 billion. Diluted EPS of $1.547 was up about 60.1% from $0.966. This kind of operating leverage is exactly what traders usually want in a commodity producer during an improving price environment.

The balance sheet is solid and gives SCCO flexibility. At 2025-12-31, SCCO had $4.91 billion in cash, $7.41 billion in total debt, and $2.45 billion in net debt. Against trailing EBITDA of $7.90 billion, net debt is only about 0.31x EBITDA, which is conservative. Total equity was $11.10 billion, so debt-to-equity based on statement values is about 0.67x, consistent with the provided 66.76 figure. Liquidity is strong: current assets were $8.35 billion versus current liabilities of $2.15 billion, and working capital was $6.21 billion. The reported current ratio of 3.894 confirms low near-term refinancing stress.

Balance-sheet trends were favorable through 2025. Total assets increased from $18.71 billion at 2024-12-31 to $21.38 billion at 2025-12-31. Stockholders' equity rose from $9.17 billion to $11.04 billion. Working capital improved from $3.93 billion to $6.21 billion. Cash and short-term investments climbed from $3.50 billion to $4.91 billion. Net debt declined from $3.00 billion at 2024-12-31 to $2.45 billion at 2025-12-31 despite ongoing dividends and capital spending. That combination matters: SCCO is not just profitable on paper, it is translating earnings into a visibly stronger financial position.

Cash flow supports the earnings story. Quarterly operating cash flow was $721.4 million in 2025-03-31, $976.8 million in 2025-06-30, $1.56 billion in 2025-09-30, and $1.49 billion in 2025-12-31. Free cash flow was positive every reported quarter in 2025: $403.6 million, $741.1 million, $1.21 billion, and $1.07 billion respectively. The trailing free cash flow figure in the snapshot is $3.34 billion. This is important because SCCO is funding dividends and capex internally rather than relying on balance-sheet strain.

Capital allocation looks shareholder-friendly but needs monitoring. SCCO paid common dividends of $553.3 million in Q1 2025, $557.4 million in Q2, $643.3 million in Q3, and $731.1 million in Q4. That is a heavy cash return profile and broadly supported by free cash flow, though coverage in weaker quarters is thinner than in stronger quarters. Capital expenditures were moderate but steady at $317.8 million, $235.7 million, $349.2 million, and $422.6 million through 2025. Construction in progress of $1.82 billion on the year-end balance sheet suggests ongoing project investment, which can support future production and earnings but also raises execution risk if commodity prices weaken.

A subtle but positive signal is the share count trend. Treasury shares fell and common shares outstanding stayed roughly stable, while diluted average shares rose modestly during 2025. This is not a major repurchase story right now; the capital-return emphasis is clearly dividends. For traders, that means the equity case is more tied to earnings/copper leverage and income support than to aggressive buyback-driven EPS engineering.

Relative performance says the market already recognizes the story. SCCO was up 28.23% year-to-date and 47.96% over six months, outperforming the XLB materials ETF by 15.53 percentage points YTD and 30.69 points over six months. Over one month, SCCO also outperformed XLB by 1.38 points. The only softer spot is recent short-term relative momentum: over one week SCCO returned +0.11% versus XLB at +0.15%, and over three months SCCO lagged XLB by 0.85 points. That suggests the stock may be consolidating after a large move rather than freshly re-rating.

Macro context is mixed but still broadly supportive for SCCO. The Fed funds rate is 3.64% as of 2026-03-01, with an upward-sloping yield curve on 2026-04-15 and a 2Y-10Y spread of +0.53 percentage points. That usually aligns with a more normal growth backdrop than an imminent recession signal. Unemployment at 4.30% and rising payrolls also suggest demand resilience. The less supportive side is inflation: CPI rose 0.87% sequentially and PPI rose 1.78% sequentially in the latest data window. For SCCO, persistent producer inflation can pressure costs, though its current operating margins are wide enough to absorb a fair amount of inflation if copper pricing remains favorable.

What matters most for traders is the tension between fundamentals and valuation. Fundamentally, SCCO is executing very well: expanding revenue, faster earnings growth, strong free cash flow, improving liquidity, and manageable leverage. Valuation, however, is demanding for a cyclical miner. A 36.27x trailing P/E and 14.09x price-to-book multiple imply the market is capitalizing current strength aggressively and expects elevated earnings durability. If copper prices remain firm or improve further, SCCO can justify premium trading. If the copper cycle softens, multiple compression risk is real even if the company remains profitable.

Actionable trading insights:

1. The most defensible bullish argument on SCCO is earnings momentum plus balance-sheet strength. Revenue, EBITDA, net income, and EPS all rose materially through 2025, while net debt fell and cash increased.
2. The main tactical risk is valuation compression. SCCO has already dramatically outperformed over six months and now trades near its 52-week high with premium earnings and book multiples.
3. Dividend support is meaningful but not the whole thesis. The 2.11% yield helps, but traders should focus more on copper-cycle earnings sensitivity and free-cash-flow durability than on yield alone.
4. Liquidity risk is low. A current ratio of 3.894, $4.91 billion of cash, and low net leverage reduce downside associated with funding stress.
5. Watch future quarters for one issue above all: whether Q4 2025 profitability was a new base or a peak. If revenue and EBITDA continue near Q4 levels, the premium multiple becomes easier to defend. If they fade, the stock could de-rate quickly.

Bottom line: SCCO’s fundamentals are strong enough to support a constructive stance on the business, but the stock is no longer a pure value play. It now looks like a high-quality cyclical compounder priced for continued copper strength. Traders should treat SCCO as fundamentally bullish but valuation-sensitive.
