PLTR fundamental report as of 2026-04-16

PLTR is a large-cap software infrastructure company in the Technology sector with a market cap of about $341.4B. The core fundamental picture is unusually strong on growth, margins, liquidity, and cash generation, but the stock is priced at an extreme premium. That combination matters: the business quality is improving rapidly, while the equity already discounts a lot of future execution.

Core fundamental view

1. Growth is still very strong
PLTR reported Q4 2025 revenue of $1.407B, up from $827.5M in Q4 2024, which is roughly 70% year over year. Revenue also climbed sequentially each quarter in 2025:
- Q1 2025: $883.9M
- Q2 2025: $1.004B
- Q3 2025: $1.181B
- Q4 2025: $1.407B

That progression shows both sustained demand and strong operating leverage. For traders, this is the most important bullish fundamental fact in the dataset.

2. Margin expansion is dramatic
Quarterly profitability improved sharply through 2025:
- Q4 2025 gross margin was about 84.7% ($1.191B gross profit on $1.407B revenue)
- Q4 2025 operating margin was about 40.9%
- Q4 2025 net margin was about 43.3%

Net income rose from $79.0M in Q4 2024 to $608.7M in Q4 2025. Operating income rose from just $11.0M to $575.4M over the same period. This is not just revenue growth; it is a step-change in profitability.

3. EPS trend is improving quickly
Diluted EPS across 2025:
- Q1 2025: $0.080
- Q2 2025: $0.130
- Q3 2025: $0.180
- Q4 2025: $0.240

That is a clear upward staircase. Forward EPS of 1.86248 versus TTM EPS of 0.63 implies the market expects much higher earnings power ahead. If execution slips, valuation risk rises quickly.

4. Cash generation is high quality
PLTR generated positive operating cash flow and free cash flow in every quarter shown:
- Q1 2025 OCF / FCF: $310.3M / $304.1M
- Q2 2025 OCF / FCF: $539.3M / $531.6M
- Q3 2025 OCF / FCF: $507.7M / $500.9M
- Q4 2025 OCF / FCF: $777.3M / $764.0M

Capital expenditures remain very low relative to operating cash flow, which is typical of a software business and supports very high FCF conversion. This strengthens the case that profitability is not purely accounting-driven.

5. Balance sheet is exceptionally liquid
PLTR ended Q4 2025 with:
- Total assets: $8.90B
- Cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments: $7.18B
- Current assets: $8.36B
- Current liabilities: $1.18B
- Working capital: $7.18B
- Total debt: only $229.3M

This is a fortress-style balance sheet. Liquidity is not a concern. The company has substantial flexibility for R&D, M&A, buybacks, and downside protection.

6. Equity base is improving, but dilution still matters
Common stock equity increased from $5.00B at 2024-12-31 to $7.39B at 2025-12-31. Retained earnings remain negative at -$3.56B, but the deficit narrowed materially from -$5.19B a year earlier, reflecting rapid earnings growth.

Share count continues to rise:
- 2024-12-31 shares issued: 2.3388B
- 2025-12-31 shares issued: 2.3912B

Dilution is therefore still present. PLTR is repurchasing stock, but buybacks are small relative to the scale of equity issuance and stock-based compensation.

7. Stock-based compensation remains a real watch item
Quarterly stock-based compensation was:
- Q1 2025: $155.3M
- Q2 2025: $160.0M
- Q3 2025: $172.3M
- Q4 2025: $196.4M

That is sizable, even though the company is strongly profitable. Traders should treat SBC as an ongoing drag on per-share compounding unless buybacks scale materially.

8. Deferred revenue and receivables suggest strong commercial activity, but require monitoring
Current deferred revenue increased from $524.9M at 2024-12-31 to $766.0M at 2025-12-31. That is usually supportive because it implies billings and cash collection ahead of revenue recognition.

Accounts receivable also rose from $575.0M to $1.042B over the same period. Some of that is consistent with growth, but it should be watched for signs that revenue is outpacing collections. In the cash flow statement, receivables were a recurring working-capital headwind in 2025.

9. Expense structure shows operating leverage, but SG&A is still heavy
Q4 2025 operating expense was $615.4M, versus gross profit of $1.191B. SG&A alone was $471.9M. The business is scaling well, but PLTR still spends aggressively to support growth. If revenue momentum slows, market tolerance for this cost structure may fall.

10. Valuation is the main fundamental risk
Even after strong execution, valuation remains stretched:
- P/E: 226.6x TTM
- Forward P/E: 76.7x
- Price to book: 46.2x

Those multiples require continued exceptional growth and margin delivery. Fundamental quality is high, but the stock is not cheap on any conventional basis.

Financial history and trajectory
- Revenue, operating income, net income, EPS, and free cash flow all improved materially throughout 2025.
- EBITDA moved from just $18.0M in Q4 2024 to $582.4M in Q4 2025.
- Equity increased steadily while debt stayed low.
- Cash and investments expanded to more than $7B.
- The company appears to have crossed from “high-growth software story” into “high-growth, high-margin, cash-rich platform” status.

Market context and trading implications
- PLTR outperformed XLK over 1 week: +9.40% vs +7.00%
- But it badly underperformed over 1 month, 3 months, 6 months, and YTD
- 3-month alpha vs XLK: -25.17%
- 6-month alpha vs XLK: -28.88%

This creates an important setup: fundamentals have strengthened, but price performance has lagged the sector over medium horizons. That can mean either a catch-up opportunity or a sign that valuation compression is still underway.

Macro backdrop
- Fed funds rate: 3.64% as of 2026-03-01, unchanged recently
- CPI and PPI both increased sequentially in March 2026
- Unemployment: 4.3%
- Yield curve out to 3 years is around 3.70% to 3.79%

This is a relatively neutral-to-moderately restrictive rate backdrop. High-multiple software names like PLTR can still trade well, but they remain sensitive to discount-rate changes and growth expectation resets.

Actionable insights for traders
- Bullish fundamental case: PLTR is showing rare combination strength in revenue growth, margin expansion, FCF generation, and balance-sheet quality.
- Risk case: valuation remains extreme, so even strong quarters may not fully protect the stock if growth normalizes.
- Best confirming indicators next quarter: revenue growth above current trajectory, sustained operating margin above 35%, continued FCF above $500M per quarter, and evidence that receivables growth is not outrunning collections.
- Main warning indicators: decelerating sequential revenue growth, rising SBC without offsetting buybacks, receivables expansion, or any compression in deferred revenue growth.
- Tactical interpretation: fundamentally, PLTR looks like a high-quality operator; on the stock, the debate is almost entirely about whether business momentum can outrun multiple compression.

Bottom line
PLTR fundamentals are strong and improving rapidly. The company has elite gross margins, strong operating leverage, large and growing free cash flow, minimal debt, and exceptional liquidity. The weak point is not business quality; it is valuation and per-share dilution. For traders, PLTR currently looks fundamentally robust but highly expectation-sensitive.
