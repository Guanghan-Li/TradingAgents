# SMCI Fundamental Research Report

## Company Overview

SMCI refers to Super Micro Computer, Inc., a Technology-sector company in the Computer Hardware industry. The company is a server and high-performance computing hardware provider with strong exposure to AI infrastructure, accelerated computing, data-center buildouts, and enterprise/server demand cycles.

As of the prefetched fundamentals data retrieved on 2026-04-16, SMCI has a market capitalization of approximately $17.05 billion. The valuation profile shows a trailing P/E of 20.73, a forward P/E of 9.51, price-to-book of 2.43, trailing EPS of $1.37, and forward EPS of $2.99. This implies that the market is pricing in a meaningful earnings rebound or continuation of growth, but the discount between trailing and forward P/E also means execution risk is central to the SMCI investment case.

SMCI is a high-beta equity, with beta of 1.629. This indicates elevated sensitivity to market and sector movements, especially relevant given SMCI’s AI-infrastructure narrative and the Technology-sector risk environment.

## Revenue and Profitability

SMCI’s TTM revenue is $28.06 billion, with TTM gross profit of $2.25 billion, EBITDA of $1.10 billion, and net income of $872.78 million. The reported TTM profit margin is 3.11%, operating margin is 3.74%, return on equity is 13.19%, and return on assets is 3.42%.

The profitability profile is mixed. Revenue scale is very large relative to market capitalization, producing an approximate price-to-sales ratio of 0.61. However, SMCI operates with thin margins. TTM gross margin is approximately 8.02%, EBITDA margin approximately 3.91%, and net margin 3.11%. For a hardware business, these margin levels are not unusual, but they leave limited room for execution errors, pricing pressure, inventory missteps, customer concentration issues, or supply-chain cost shocks.

The latest quarterly income statement shows a major revenue acceleration in the quarter ended 2025-12-31. SMCI reported quarterly revenue of $12.68 billion, up from $5.02 billion in 2025-09-30 and $5.76 billion in 2025-06-30. Gross profit rose to $798.57 million from $467.37 million in the prior quarter. Operating income rose to $474.30 million from $182.26 million, and net income rose to $400.56 million from $168.29 million.

The latest quarter therefore shows strong sequential growth and improved absolute profitability. However, the gross margin in the 2025-12-31 quarter was approximately 6.30%, below the TTM gross margin estimate of about 8.02%. This suggests that growth may be coming with margin compression, possibly due to product mix, competitive pricing, large-scale AI server deployments, or supply-chain costs. Traders should monitor whether SMCI can convert high revenue volume into durable operating leverage.

Basic average shares were much lower at 598.00 million, so traders should pay attention to the difference between basic and diluted share counts when modeling EPS.

## Balance Sheet Quality

SMCI’s balance sheet expanded dramatically in the latest quarter. Total assets increased to $28.00 billion as of 2025-12-31 from $14.39 billion as of 2025-09-30. Current assets rose to $26.12 billion from $12.66 billion, while total liabilities increased to $21.01 billion from $7.86 billion. Stockholders’ equity rose more modestly to $6.99 billion from $6.52 billion.

The most important balance-sheet signal is the large working-capital expansion. Accounts receivable increased to $11.00 billion from $2.53 billion sequentially. Inventory increased to $10.60 billion from $5.73 billion. Accounts payable increased to $13.75 billion from $1.28 billion. These are unusually large sequential moves and should be central to any SMCI fundamental assessment.

The current ratio is 1.697, which indicates adequate near-term liquidity on the surface. Working capital was $10.73 billion at 2025-12-31. Cash and cash equivalents were $4.09 billion, down slightly from $4.20 billion at 2025-09-30 but still substantial. However, current liabilities surged to $15.40 billion, driven largely by payables and accrued expenses. This creates a working-capital and vendor-financing risk: SMCI’s liquidity is tied to collecting receivables, managing inventory, and maintaining supplier terms.

Total debt was $4.91 billion at 2025-12-31, with net debt of $786.75 million. Debt-to-equity is reported at 75.28. The debt burden is significant but partly offset by the large cash position. Long-term debt was $4.68 billion, and current debt was $201.78 million. Net debt is manageable relative to the company’s revenue base, but thin margins and working-capital swings increase risk.

Book value per share is $11.674, and price-to-book is 2.43. Tangible book value was $6.99 billion at 2025-12-31, nearly equal to stockholders’ equity, suggesting limited intangible asset distortion in book equity.

## Cash Flow and Working Capital

Cash flow is the weakest part of the current SMCI fundamental profile. TTM free cash flow is reported at $103.48 million, modest relative to market capitalization and net income. In the latest quarter ended 2025-12-31, operating cash flow was negative $23.90 million and free cash flow was negative $45.12 million despite net income of $400.56 million.

The primary cause was working-capital consumption. Change in working capital was negative $637.60 million in the latest quarter. Inventory consumed $5.00 billion of cash, and receivables consumed $8.47 billion. These outflows were partly offset by a $12.75 billion increase in payables and accrued expenses. This means SMCI’s latest quarter earnings were not strongly cash-backed, and cash conversion is a key risk.

The prior quarter, 2025-09-30, was even weaker on cash flow: operating cash flow was negative $917.52 million and free cash flow was negative $949.79 million. The 2025-06-30 and 2025-03-31 quarters were positive, with free cash flow of $840.94 million and $594.06 million respectively. This volatility suggests SMCI’s cash generation is highly sensitive to order timing, customer collections, inventory build, and supplier credit terms.

Capital expenditures remain modest relative to revenue, at $21.22 million in the latest quarter and $32.27 million in the prior quarter. The business is not highly capex-intensive compared with revenue scale, but working capital functions as the real capital requirement.

## Valuation

SMCI’s valuation looks inexpensive on forward earnings and revenue, but not obviously cheap when adjusted for margin quality, cash-flow volatility, and balance-sheet expansion.

The trailing P/E is 20.73, while the forward P/E is 9.51. That gap implies significant expected earnings growth. If SMCI delivers on forward EPS of $2.99, the equity could screen attractively versus many AI infrastructure and Technology peers. However, the forward multiple depends heavily on revenue durability and margin stabilization.

Price-to-sales is approximately 0.61, based on market cap of $17.05 billion and TTM revenue of $28.06 billion. That is low for a Technology company but more reasonable for a lower-margin hardware business. Price-to-book is 2.43, and estimated enterprise value is approximately $17.84 billion using market cap plus net debt. EV/EBITDA is therefore roughly 16.3x based on TTM EBITDA of $1.10 billion. That EV/EBITDA multiple is less clearly cheap, especially given EBITDA margin below 4%.

The valuation setup is therefore asymmetric but risky: SMCI may look undervalued if the latest quarter’s revenue scale is sustainable and cash conversion improves, but the stock can remain pressured if investors discount revenue quality, working-capital strain, or margin compression.

## Key Fundamental Strengths

SMCI’s latest quarter showed exceptional revenue acceleration, with revenue reaching $12.68 billion versus $5.02 billion in the prior quarter. Net income also improved meaningfully to $400.56 million, and diluted EPS rose to $0.60. This supports the bull case that SMCI remains exposed to major AI infrastructure demand.

The company has substantial liquidity, with $4.09 billion in cash and cash equivalents, $10.73 billion of working capital, and a current ratio of 1.697. Net debt of $786.75 million is manageable relative to revenue scale.

The forward valuation is attractive if estimates are achievable. A forward P/E of 9.51 and forward EPS of $2.99 suggest the market is not assigning a premium multiple despite AI infrastructure exposure.

## Key Fundamental Risks

Cash conversion is weak. The latest quarter produced negative free cash flow of $45.12 million despite $400.56 million of net income. TTM free cash flow of $103.48 million is small relative to net income and market capitalization.

Working-capital expansion is extreme. Accounts receivable rose to $11.00 billion, inventory rose to $10.60 billion, and accounts payable rose to $13.75 billion. These moves indicate that SMCI’s revenue growth is tied to heavy balance-sheet usage. Traders should watch receivable collection, inventory turnover, customer concentration, and supplier payment terms.

Margins remain thin. TTM net margin is 3.11%, operating margin is 3.74%, and the latest quarterly gross margin was approximately 6.30%. Thin margins magnify the impact of pricing pressure, cost inflation, execution errors, and product mix changes.

Dilution is also relevant. Diluted average shares increased to 693.99 million in the latest quarter, while basic average shares were 598.00 million. This gap affects per-share economics and should be incorporated into valuation models.

## Actionable Trading Insights

For bullish traders, the strongest argument is that SMCI may be transitioning back into high-growth mode, supported by the latest quarter’s revenue surge and low forward P/E. A sustained move above the 50-day average and improvement toward the 200-day average would be more convincing if paired with evidence of better free cash flow and stable gross margin.

For cautious or bearish traders, the main argument is that SMCI’s latest growth came with large working-capital strain and weak cash conversion. The equity may remain volatile until investors gain confidence that receivables are collectible, inventory is not excessive, and supplier financing is sustainable.

The highest-priority metrics to monitor in the next SMCI update are gross margin, operating margin, free cash flow, accounts receivable, inventory, accounts payable, net debt, and diluted share count. In particular, traders should look for operating cash flow turning positive while revenue remains elevated. Without that, earnings quality may remain a concern.

## Bottom Line

SMCI presents a high-upside but high-risk fundamental profile. The latest quarter showed explosive revenue growth and a major rebound in profitability, while forward valuation appears inexpensive. However, the quality of that growth is not yet fully confirmed because free cash flow is weak, working capital has expanded dramatically, and margins remain thin. SMCI is suitable for traders who can tolerate volatility and closely monitor execution, but the fundamental risk/reward depends on whether SMCI can convert AI-driven revenue growth into durable cash flow and stable margins.
