Meta Platforms, Inc. (`META`) remains a two-segment story with one dominant profit engine and one strategic option. `Family of Apps` is the core business by a very wide margin: advertising across Facebook, Instagram, Messenger, WhatsApp, and increasingly Threads drives essentially all current revenue and nearly all operating profit. The quarterly revenue trend in the prefetched statements is strong, moving from $42.3B in 2025-03 to $59.9B in 2025-12, while operating income also expanded from $17.6B to $24.7B despite materially higher R&D and SG&A. That points to continued ad demand, healthy pricing/engagement, and durable scale economics in the core ad stack.

`Reality Labs` remains strategically important but economically weak. Recent news on Quest price increases tied to higher component and RAM costs suggests hardware margins are under pressure and that Meta is attempting to offset cost inflation rather than riding strong standalone pricing power. The Broadcom AI chip partnership is more relevant to the core ad/AI ecosystem and long-run compute advantage than to near-term Reality Labs profitability. Threads product updates improve Meta's monetization surface inside the core ecosystem, but do not change the fact that business-mix concentration is very high: `META` is still overwhelmingly dependent on digital advertising monetized through `Family of Apps`.

The key concentration risk is therefore not segment fragmentation but segment dependence. If ad demand, targeting efficiency, regulatory constraints, or platform engagement weaken, there is no second segment large enough to cushion the revenue base. Conversely, AI infrastructure and recommendation improvements likely reinforce the moat of the core segment, while Reality Labs remains a long-duration call option with negative near-term margin impact.
