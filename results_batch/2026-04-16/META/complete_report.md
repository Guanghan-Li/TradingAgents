Those are unusually strong profitability metrics for a company also investing at hyperscaler levels in capex.

From a valuation standpoint, META is no longer cheap on simple book-value measures, but it is not obviously stretched relative to its earnings power. The market cap is about $1.71T, trailing P/E is 28.79, forward P/E is 19.00, and forward EPS is $35.62 versus trailing EPS of $23.51. That gap between trailing and forward earnings implies the market expects a meaningful earnings rebound or normalization from recent quarterly noise, especially after the tax-distorted September 2025 quarter. Price-to-book is 7.88, which is elevated in absolute terms, but ROE of 30.24% supports paying a premium to book for this business.

**Business quality and profitability**

The strongest part of the META fundamental story is still operating quality. Gross profit is $164.8B on $201.0B of revenue, implying a very high gross margin business even before considering scale benefits. Operating margin at 41.31% and ROA at 16.21% confirm that META continues to monetize its asset base efficiently despite heavy expansion spending. Beta is 1.309, so the stock is not defensive, but the underlying business economics are strong enough that volatility is mostly market-driven rather than balance-sheet-driven.

Dividend yield is only 0.31%, so traders should not treat META as an income vehicle. The dividend is better understood as a signal of financial maturity and confidence rather than a major part of the shareholder return case. The main equity story remains earnings growth, AI monetization, and capital allocation.

**Income statement trends**

Quarterly revenue trends are strong and accelerating into year-end 2025:

- 2025-03-31: $42.314B
- 2025-06-30: $47.516B
- 2025-09-30: $51.242B
- 2025-12-31: $59.893B

That is a clear upward progression through the year. Gross profit followed the same direction, reaching $48.987B in the December 2025 quarter, while operating income rose to $24.745B and EBIT to $25.810B. Q4 2025 net income was $22.768B, with diluted EPS of $8.88, which is a very strong print.

The major distortion in the quarterly history is 2025-09-30. Revenue and operating income were both solid in that quarter, but reported net income fell to just $2.709B because tax provision spiked to $18.954B on pretax income of $21.663B. That means the apparent earnings collapse in that quarter was not caused by a collapse in the operating business. For traders, this matters because the trailing P/E may overstate how expensive META is if one focuses too literally on GAAP trailing earnings that include this tax event.

This confirms META is still in an investment-heavy phase, particularly around AI and infrastructure. The key point is that revenue and gross profit growth have been large enough to absorb those cost increases while preserving elite margins.

- Q1 2025: $24.026B
- Q2 2025: $25.561B
- Q3 2025: $29.999B
- Q4 2025: $36.214B
