In other words, the range is stabilizing, but it has stabilized at a lower level.

`ATR` at `5.69` is still elevated relative to the stock price. That is a critical risk-management signal. An ATR near `5.9%` of price means `NOW` is still moving too violently to treat as a low-risk trend name. Position sizing, stop placement, and time horizon matter much more than usual here. A trader buying this rebound without accounting for ATR is likely to be shaken out even if the short-term direction ends up right.

The constructive part is that the bounce from `83.00` on April 10 to `96.44` on April 16 happened quickly, and some of those rebound sessions also carried strong volume. The cautionary part is that rebound volume has not clearly overwhelmed the liquidation volume that caused the breakdown in the first place. That matters because genuine trend repairs usually show sustained accumulation, not just a reflex rally.
