FINAL TRANSACTION PROPOSAL: **HOLD**

`NOW` fundamental report as of 2026-04-16

`NOW` remains a fundamentally strong large-cap enterprise software company, but the current setup is mixed. The business still shows durable revenue growth, high gross margins, strong free cash flow, and a net cash position. The problem is that the stock has dramatically underperformed peers and the sector while valuation still assumes a meaningful earnings ramp. That makes `NOW` more of a high-quality name in a de-rating/integration phase than a clean fundamental momentum long.

**Company Profile**

ServiceNow, Inc. (`NOW`) operates in Technology / Software - Application. Market cap is about $100.9B. The business model is consistent with a high-visibility subscription software platform: large deferred revenue balances, strong gross profit generation, and recurring cash flow. Beta is 1.005, so the stock is roughly market-like in sensitivity, but the recent drawdown shows that valuation compression can still hit hard.

**Core Financial Quality**

TTM revenue is $13.278B, gross profit is $10.295B, EBITDA is $2.738B, net income is $1.748B, and free cash flow is $4.952B. That implies very strong software economics:

- Gross margin: about 77.5%
- EBITDA margin: about 20.6%
- Operating margin: 16.5%
- Net margin: 13.2%
- ROE: 15.5%
- ROA: 5.4%

These are solid metrics for a scaled software platform. `NOW` is not a low-margin growth story; it is already profitable and strongly cash generative.

**Income Statement Trends**

Quarterly revenue has remained consistently upward:

- 2025-03-31: $3.088B
- 2025-06-30: $3.215B
- 2025-09-30: $3.407B
- 2025-12-31: $3.568B

Q4 2025 was strong on growth:

- Revenue: $3.568B, up 20.7% YoY from $2.957B
- Gross profit: $2.734B, up 17.5% YoY
- Operating income: $443M, up 18.4% YoY
- Net income: $401M, up 4.4% YoY
- Diluted EPS: $0.38 vs $0.366 YoY

The caution is in sequential profitability. Q4 2025 revenue rose 4.7% vs Q3 2025, but operating income fell from $572M to $443M and net income fell from $502M to $401M. That was driven by a noticeable expense ramp:

- Operating expense: $2.291B in Q4 vs $2.061B in Q3
- R&D: $773M vs $750M
- Selling and marketing: $1.150B vs $1.056B
- G&A: $368M vs $255M

So the business is still growing well, but recent operating leverage weakened. Traders should watch whether this was temporary investment/M&A-related pressure or the start of a lower-margin phase.

**Balance Sheet Strength**

`NOW` has a healthy balance sheet overall.

- Cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments: $6.284B
- Total debt: $2.403B
- Net cash vs debt: about $3.881B
- Stockholders' equity: $12.964B
- Long-term debt: $1.491B
- Current debt and lease obligations: $112M

This is not a balance-sheet-stressed company. The reported debt-to-equity figure of 18.536 is best read as about 18.5%, which is reasonable.

Two balance-sheet details matter a lot:

1. Current deferred revenue is $8.314B. That is a major sign of revenue visibility and prepaid customer commitments.
2. Goodwill and other intangibles jumped to $4.699B in Q4 2025 from $2.211B in Q3 2025. That is a major step-up and likely reflects acquisition activity.

Working capital fell sharply to just $28M and the current ratio is 1.003. On the surface that looks tight, but for a subscription software company with very large deferred revenue, this is not automatically a liquidity problem. The cash balance and FCF profile matter more here than the headline current ratio.

**Cash Flow and Capital Allocation**

Cash generation is one of the strongest parts of the `NOW` story.

Q4 2025 cash flow:

- Operating cash flow: $2.238B
- Capex: $238M
- Free cash flow: $2.000B

That compares very favorably with Q4 2024:

- OCF: $1.635B to $2.238B, up 36.9%
- FCF: $1.372B to $2.000B, up 45.8%

Quarterly free cash flow has been robust across the last five reported quarters:

- 2024-12-31: $1.372B
- 2025-03-31: $1.438B
- 2025-06-30: $526M
- 2025-09-30: $569M
- 2025-12-31: $2.000B

This shows that `NOW` has meaningful FCF seasonality, but the full-year/TTM cash picture is very strong.

Capital allocation is active:

- Share repurchases in Q4 2025: $597M
- Share repurchases across 2025: about $1.84B
- Business acquisition spend in Q4 2025: $869M

This is the key strategic tension in the quarter: management is returning capital via buybacks while also deploying meaningful cash into acquisitions. That can be bullish if acquisitions accelerate platform growth, but it raises integration and amortization risk.

A further quality issue to watch is stock-based compensation, which stayed elevated around $470M-$499M per quarter. Buybacks are helping offset dilution, but SBC remains material.

**Valuation**

Valuation is still not obviously cheap despite the stock decline:

- TTM P/E: 57.75
- Forward P/E: 19.23
- Price/book: 7.79
- EPS (TTM): 1.67
- Forward EPS: 5.02

The huge gap between TTM and forward P/E suggests the market expects a strong earnings expansion. That expectation may be reasonable if margin pressure fades and acquired assets contribute, but it also means `NOW` still carries execution risk. If the earnings ramp slips, the valuation can compress further.

**Actionable Trading Insights**

- `NOW` still has strong operating quality. Revenue growth around 20% YoY, gross margins near 78%, and nearly $5B of TTM FCF are not distressed-company metrics.
- The major near-term risk is not solvency or business-model weakness; it is execution and multiple risk. Q4 2025 showed sequential margin compression despite higher revenue.
- The Q4 acquisition signal is important. Goodwill/intangibles rose sharply and business purchase cash outflow hit $869M. The next report needs to show that this deal activity supports growth rather than just inflating balance-sheet intangibles.
- Deferred revenue is one of the best leading indicators here. At $8.314B current deferred revenue, the core platform still has strong backlog/renewal support. If that starts to flatten, the bull case weakens quickly.
- SBC remains high. If buybacks slow while SBC stays elevated, per-share economics may not improve as fast as headline earnings suggest.
- Because the stock has already been repriced hard, `NOW` could rally aggressively on even a modestly clean quarter. But traders need confirmation in operating margin, deferred revenue growth, and post-acquisition integration.

**Bottom Line**

Fundamentally, `NOW` is still a strong software franchise with durable growth, high-quality recurring revenue, substantial deferred revenue, and elite cash generation. The reason not to move to an outright bullish transaction call yet is that recent operating leverage weakened, acquisition-related balance-sheet changes were large, and the stock's relative performance is extremely poor despite these otherwise solid fundamentals. That combination argues for `HOLD`, not because the business is weak, but because the market still needs proof that earnings quality and integration are improving.
