# Final Allocation: 2026-04-16

## Executive Decision

- Summary: Allocate the $200 paper portfolio across AMZN, JPM, and ANET. These three won because each already carries an explicit Buy in the supplied evidence, each has strong underlying business quality, and together they offer a better mix of durable earnings power and still-manageable entry conditions than the closest rejected Buy alternatives. AMZN is the anchor because its profit engine is broad and the underwrite supports a staged Overweight. JPM is the second-largest position because franchise quality, earnings breadth, and a constructive soft-landing setup create a favorable risk-adjusted entry. ANET remains the smallest of the three because its business quality is elite but its entry is the most extended and valuation-sensitive of the selected names.
- Why this portfolio: This portfolio deliberately avoids concentrating all $200 in the most crowded AI hardware trades. AMZN adds cloud and consumer-platform compounding with diversified revenue and visible margin leverage. JPM adds a high-quality financial compounder whose return path depends less on multiple expansion and more on resilient net interest income, fee mix, and buybacks. ANET supplies direct exposure to the AI infrastructure cycle, but in a smaller size because the packet itself argues for staged accumulation rather than full-size chasing. The combination is stronger than a pure semiconductor basket because it preserves upside to AI and enterprise spending while reducing reliance on a single valuation regime or one catalyst window.
- Weighting principle: Weighting follows two criteria: business durability first, then entry quality. AMZN receives the largest weight because it combines very large earnings power, diversified revenue streams, and an explicit staged Overweight recommendation despite a stretched chart. JPM receives a slightly smaller but still large weight because its business quality is comparable on durability and its entry is cleaner than most growth names, though upside is somewhat more base-case than bull-case. ANET receives the smallest weight because its operating quality is excellent, but the packet is clear that valuation, RSI, and distance from moving averages require tighter staging and lower starting conviction than the other two.
- Total allocated: $200

## Position Memos

### AMZN

- Role: Core compounder and portfolio anchor
- Core thesis: AMZN is the best mix of business quality and acceptable entry among the explicit Buy names. AWS remains the key profit engine, retail margins are improving, and forward EPS of $9.40 versus trailing EPS of $7.18 supports continued earnings growth rather than a purely valuation-driven move.
- Supporting evidence: The packet supports Buy / Overweight with a 2.0% initial weight and 4.0% target. TTM revenue is $716.9B, net income is $77.7B, operating margin is 10.53%, and Q4 2025 showed operating leverage with $213.4B revenue and $25.0B operating income. Revenue diversification across North America, International, and AWS reduces single-engine risk.
- Disconfirming evidence: Entry is not clean. AMZN closed at $249.70 with RSI above 70, about 6.0% above the 10 EMA and 16.8% above the 50 SMA. Valuation is not cheap at 34.78x trailing and 26.57x forward earnings, and capex reached $39.5B in Q4 2025.
- Entry quality: Good enough for a starter, not good enough for an undisciplined chase. The evidence explicitly favors buying now only as staged overweight exposure, with better adds on a pullback to $240-$235 or a confirmed move above $250-$253.
- Why it beat a close rejected Buy: AMZN beats GOOGL for inclusion because both are high-quality megacaps, but AMZN has cleaner near-term operating leverage in the supplied evidence and less explicit monetization skepticism. GOOGL still depends on proving Gemini and Cloud AI monetization while managing EU DMA and UK scrutiny, and its packet highlights cash-conversion pressure from capex. AMZN also beats META on entry discipline because META is pressing major resistance near 680-683 and the packet warns not to move toward full size before earnings.
- Risk controls and invalidation: Do not add aggressively unless AMZN either pulls back constructively into $240-$235 or breaks $250-$253 on confirmation. Use $234-$235 as the first risk-control zone, and treat a loss of $225, weaker AWS growth commentary, retail margin slippage, or forward EPS deterioration below the $9.40 path as thesis failure.
- Confidence: high

### JPM

- Role: Quality financial ballast with cyclical upside
- Core thesis: JPM earns the second-largest weight because it combines premium franchise quality with a more balanced entry profile than most of the eligible tech names. The business has broad earnings drivers across consumer banking, investment banking, commercial banking, and wealth, and the current macro setup still supports NII resilience and capital-markets activity.
- Supporting evidence: The packet is Buy / Overweight with a 3.5% initial weight and 7.0% target. JPM posted about 16.5% ROE, 1.27% ROA, 33.9% net margin, and about $34.6B of buybacks over the last four quarters. Net interest income improved from about $23.3B in 2025-03 to $25.0B in 2025-12, while SG&A fell from about $15.7B to $14.6B. The stock closed above its 10 EMA, 50 SMA, and 200 SMA.

Only 30% of the scenario map is bull, valuation is premium for a bank at about 2.41x book and 14.8x trailing earnings, ATR near 6.98 means support can be retested quickly, and the evidence still demands confirmation from clean credit and benign regulatory language.
- Entry quality: Better than most growth alternatives because JPM is not described as extremely overbought, and the packet gives specific constructive pullback zones at 305-306 or 298-301. The setup is not cheap enough for complacency, but it is less extended than names like ANET, AVGO, or MU.
- Why it beat a close rejected Buy: JPM beats XLE because both have explicit Overweight destinations, but JPM offers stronger underlying business quality and more controllable company-level earnings power. XLE is still below its 50-day area and 10 EMA with negative MACD, so its entry depends more on technical repair and flow persistence. JPM also beats VYM because VYM's appeal is mainly range-bound dividend carry while JPM still has identifiable upside from fee mix, NII, and buybacks without requiring multiple expansion alone.
- Risk controls and invalidation: Add only on constructive pullbacks into 305-306 or 298-301 and only if provisions, charge-offs, and regulatory-capital commentary stay benign. Reduce or exit on a decisive break below the 200-day area around 300.72 or if reserve builds, charge-offs, or regulatory language worsen materially.

## Portfolio Risks

- Growth-stock entry risk remains real because AMZN and ANET are both extended into catalyst windows, so valuation compression can occur even if business quality remains intact.
- The portfolio still carries meaningful macro sensitivity: hotter CPI or PPI, higher Treasury yields, or hawkish Fed communication would pressure AMZN and ANET multiples and could also weigh on JPM through risk appetite and capital-markets sentiment.
- AI infrastructure spending is an important shared driver because AMZN depends partly on AWS and ANET depends directly on hyperscale networking demand; a broad enterprise or cloud digestion period would hurt both names simultaneously.
- JPM adds diversification, but a deterioration in credit, reserve builds, or harsher regulatory-capital language would weaken the portfolio's intended ballast function.
- Concentration: The portfolio is intentionally concentrated in three names, with 65% in AMZN and JPM and 25% in ANET. Sector exposure is tilted toward technology and AI-linked spending, though JPM reduces pure technology concentration. This is still a growth-and-quality portfolio, not a defensive barbell.
- Macro: The supplied evidence describes a soft-landing backdrop, but also sticky inflation and a 10-year Treasury yield near 4.29%. That is supportive enough for ownership, but not supportive enough to ignore entry discipline. The portfolio therefore favors high-quality franchises that can still work without needing an aggressive multiple rerating.
- Timing: All three positions sit ahead of important validation windows. AMZN needs earnings and AWS/margin confirmation, JPM needs clean credit and regulatory read-through, and ANET needs either technical follow-through above 162 or better entry on a reset. The portfolio is therefore sized for staged participation, not all-clear aggression.

## Decision Quality and Key Assumptions

- Evidence quality: Good but uneven. The supplied packets provide detailed, decision-grade business, valuation, technical, and risk-control evidence for each selected and rejected Buy name. The weakest point is that some entries are clearly extended, so the distinction between business quality and entry quality materially affects the decision.
- Assumption: AWS growth and retail margin improvement remain intact enough for AMZN to justify its staged Overweight recommendation.
- Assumption: JPM's credit costs and regulatory environment remain stable enough that current earnings breadth and buyback capacity persist.
- Assumption: ANET's AI and hyperscale networking demand remains healthy and forward EPS revisions stay at least stable, even if the stock needs a better add point.
- Assumption: The macro backdrop stays soft-landing rather than shifting to a higher-for-longer shock that would compress growth multiples more aggressively.
- Known weak point: Two of the three selections, AMZN and ANET, are being bought with explicitly stretched technical conditions rather than ideal entry resets.
- Known weak point: The portfolio rejected some very strong AI names, especially NVDA, largely on entry discipline rather than business quality, so a clean breakout in those names could create relative underperformance.
- Known weak point: Because only supplied evidence can be used, the committee cannot resolve some open questions such as exact near-term earnings dates or fresh post-packet developments.
- Internal consistency: The portfolio is internally consistent with the supplied evidence because all three selected names are explicit Buy decisions, all weights are positive and sum to $200, and the largest allocations go to the names with the best combination of durable business quality and more acceptable entry structure. Rejected alternatives were not dismissed generically; they lost because the supplied packets showed either less favorable entry quality, greater unresolved monetization questions, or weaker technical repair than the selected three.
