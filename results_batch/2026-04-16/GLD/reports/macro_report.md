GLD macro view: the backdrop is late-cycle soft landing, not recession. Labor is still resilient for GLD, with unemployment at 4.3% and nonfarm payrolls up 178k, while the Treasury curve is positively sloped with 2Y-10Y at +53 bps. That removes the strongest recession-safe-haven support for GLD.

The inflation side is more supportive for GLD. March 2026 CPI and PPI both showed firm sequential reacceleration, while the Fed has already cut from 4.09% in October 2025 to 3.64% by January 2026 and then paused through March 2026. That suggests policy is near neutral, but sticky inflation limits the case for aggressive further easing. For GLD, this creates a mixed regime: inflation uncertainty is supportive, but a higher long end with the 10Y at 4.29% and 30Y at 4.89% raises opportunity cost and can cap upside if real yields stay elevated.

Base case for GLD: modestly constructive medium term, tactically mixed near term. If inflation stays sticky while growth remains intact, GLD can retain support as a hedge against policy error and inflation persistence. If the market instead reprices to higher real yields and a firmer policy hold, GLD faces valuation pressure. The clean bullish macro catalyst for GLD would be either renewed disinflation that reopens Fed cuts, or a sharper growth/risk shock that revives safe-haven demand.

Cross-asset implications for GLD:
- A normal curve and subdued VIX around 18 imply no acute panic bid for GLD.
- Sticky inflation with a paused Fed is better for inflation hedging demand than for duration.
- Rising long-end yields are a near-term headwind for GLD unless inflation expectations rise faster than real yields.
- The macro setup argues for treating GLD more as a hedge and regime diversifier than as a clean momentum macro long right here.

Key risks other analysts should incorporate for GLD:
- Inflation persistence could push nominal and real yields higher, which is a direct macro headwind for GLD.
- A surprise growth slowdown would likely help GLD via safe-haven demand and renewed easing expectations.
- A hawkish Fed re-pricing after the recent pause would likely pressure GLD.
- Data confidence is incomplete because ISM manufacturing and consumer confidence were unavailable, and GDP is stale versus the rest of the dataset.
