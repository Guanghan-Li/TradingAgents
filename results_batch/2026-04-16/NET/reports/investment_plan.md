I am not choosing Hold because the numeric underwriting does not support standing flat; it supports a small, risk-capped long. I am not choosing Sell because the business quality, growth durability, liquidity, and the May 7, 2026 earnings and Investor Day catalyst are strong enough to justify initial exposure.

The investment plan is straightforward. Start NET at 0.75% now. Do not add just because the stock bounced; add only if NET either reclaims and holds above the 200-day area near 202, or if the May 7 update shows a clear beat-and-raise with better margin or billings commentary. Cap total NET exposure at 2.0%. Size the trade so total portfolio damage cannot exceed 0.5%; with ATR around 13.84, that means respecting volatility and not averaging down mechanically. If NET loses the 50-day area around 194.4 and momentum rolls over, freeze adds. If earnings are merely in line, guidance softens, or operating leverage does not improve, exit NET rather than rationalize the premium. The mistake to avoid in names like NET is binary thinking: premium valuation alone is not a reason to stay out, but early technical repair is also not a reason to go full size.
