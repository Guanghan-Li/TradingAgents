Momentum has improved materially, not just marginally. `macd` moved from -3.04 on 2026-03-30 to +9.04 on 2026-04-16. In a weak tape, that would often argue for an imminent reversal. In a strong tape, especially one with a fresh breakout and positive macro breadth, it more often means momentum is powerful but increasingly vulnerable to consolidation, a shallow pullback, or a few days of sideways digestion. The important nuance is that overbought is not bearish by itself. It becomes more problematic when price stops making progress or when momentum diverges. That divergence is not present yet.

The Bollinger profile reinforces the “strong but stretched” interpretation. The 2026-04-16 close of 249.70 is just below `boll_ub` at 252.90 and far above `boll_lb` at 185.59. AMZN has been riding the upper band since the breakout accelerated, which is exactly what strong trend continuation often looks like. But proximity to the upper band also means incremental upside may become harder without a pause. When a stock stays pinned near the upper band while RSI remains above 70, the usual next phase is either a brief mean reversion toward the 10 EMA or a sideways range that lets the averages catch up.

`atr` at 6.60 shows volatility has expanded, but not in a disorderly way. ATR rose from 5.70 on 2026-03-30 to the current 6.60, which makes sense given AMZN just ran nearly 25% from the 2026-03-27 low at 199.34 to the 2026-04-16 close at 249.70. Higher ATR in this context reflects price expansion, not necessarily distribution. For trade management, though, it matters a lot: entries taken at current levels need wider stops, and that weakens immediate reward-to-risk unless the trader is explicitly playing continuation with smaller size.

From a regime standpoint, AMZN is well-positioned. A risk-on macro overlay, resilient labor, still-positive payroll growth, and a non-recessionary backdrop all support top-line durability and ongoing appetite for growth leadership. That is the bullish case. The offset is that CPI and PPI are running hot while the Fed pause remains intact, so the market is unlikely to hand AMZN effortless multiple expansion if growth execution slips. In practical terms, that means technical breakouts should still work, but they will be more earnings- and execution-sensitive than they would be in an aggressively dovish easing cycle.
