## ASML Scenario And Catalyst Framing

ASML enters the next catalyst window with strong fundamental momentum but limited valuation margin for error. The bull case is anchored in EUV demand exceeding supply, AI-related foundry and logic spending resilience, and a valuation gap versus U.S. semiconductor peers that recent news says has compressed to a decade low. The bear case is less about balance-sheet stress and more about multiple compression, export-control friction, China demand limits, and execution risk from supply-chain constraints.

| Scenario | Probability | Core Thesis | Key Signposts |
| --- | ---: | --- | --- |
| Bull | 35% | AI-driven leading-edge capacity additions keep EUV demand above supply into 2027, supporting backlog quality, pricing power, and premium valuation durability. | Continued order strength, RBC-style upward estimate revisions, TSMC/foundry capex resilience, easing supply-chain bottlenecks |
| Base | 45% | ASML compounds earnings at a high rate, but valuation already discounts much of the 2026-2027 AI/EUV recovery, creating range-bound upside unless estimates move higher. | Q2/Q3 bookings match expectations, gross margin stable, China exposure manageable, rates remain near current levels |
| Bear | 20% | Export controls, supply constraints, or AI capex disappointment pressure bookings while elevated P/E and beta amplify downside. | China restrictions tighten, order pushouts appear, long yields rise, gross margin or delivery guidance disappoints |
