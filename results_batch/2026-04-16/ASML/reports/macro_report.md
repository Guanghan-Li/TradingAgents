Macro view for ASML: the U.S. backdrop is a soft-landing regime, not a recession regime. Labor is still intact with unemployment at 4.3% and nonfarm payrolls up 178k in March 2026, while the Treasury curve is positively sloped with 2Y-10Y at +53 bps, which argues against an imminent contraction signal. The Fed has already eased from 4.09% in October 2025 to 3.64% by March 2026, then paused, so policy is no longer overtight but also not clearly turning stimulative.

The main macro complication for ASML is inflation re-acceleration. March CPI rose 0.87% m/m and PPI rose 1.78% m/m, both too hot for a clean disinflation narrative. That makes the likely Fed path a longer pause with a higher bar for additional cuts, especially because the long end is already elevated with the 10Y at 4.29% and 30Y at 4.89%. For ASML, that is a mixed setup: still-firm growth and a normal curve support semiconductor capex demand, but sticky inflation and higher long-duration discount rates can cap valuation expansion for a high-quality growth name like ASML.

Cross-asset implication for ASML: macro is modestly supportive for fundamentals, but only conditionally supportive for multiple expansion. If the market keeps believing in soft landing plus AI capex, ASML should hold up fundamentally; if inflation stays hot and the long end reprices higher, ASML becomes vulnerable to a rates-driven derating even without a growth scare. The key risk other analysts should incorporate for ASML is that the macro threat is not recession first, but a higher-for-longer nominal-rate regime that pressures equity duration.
