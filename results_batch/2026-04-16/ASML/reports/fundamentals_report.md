ASML fundamental report for `ASML` as of 2026-04-16

`ASML` remains a high-quality, capital-equipment franchise with strong profitability, net cash, and clear earnings power, but the stock is priced for continued execution. The main fundamental tension is straightforward: operating margins and net income remain excellent, while quarterly cash flow is volatile because of working-capital swings tied to inventory, receivables, and deferred revenue. That makes `ASML` fundamentally strong, but not cheap.

Company profile

`ASML Holding N.V.` operates in Semiconductor Equipment & Materials and is one of the critical suppliers to the semiconductor manufacturing ecosystem. Its size and quality are reflected in a market cap of about $554.0B, TTM revenue of $33.69B, TTM net income of $10.01B, and TTM free cash flow of $8.24B. Reported profitability is elite for industrial equipment: profit margin is 29.7%, operating margin is 36.0%, ROE is 52.2%, and ROA is 15.7%.

The stock has also already rerated materially. `ASML` trades at 46.44x TTM earnings, 29.57x forward earnings, and 23.96x book value. That forward/TTM PE compression implies the market expects a major earnings step-up, with forward EPS of 47.72 versus trailing EPS of 30.38. In other words, the market is not paying for current conditions alone; it is paying for continued growth and successful conversion of demand into shipments, margins, and cash.

Balance sheet analysis

The balance sheet remains one of the stronger parts of the `ASML` thesis.

At 2026-03-31:
- Total assets were $48.06B.
- Stockholders' equity was $20.83B.
- Total liabilities were $27.23B.
- Total debt was $2.71B.
- Cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments were $8.38B.
- Working capital was $7.30B.
- Tangible book value was $15.71B.

The simplest balance-sheet takeaway is that `ASML` is in a net cash position. Cash and short-term investments of $8.38B versus total debt of $2.71B implies net cash of roughly $5.67B. That materially lowers financial risk.

The prefetched debt-to-equity figure is 12.989, but the raw balance-sheet numbers imply debt is roughly 13% of equity, not 13x equity. Interpreted correctly, leverage is low.

Liquidity is adequate rather than excessive:
- Current ratio is 1.36.
- Current assets were $27.58B versus current liabilities of $20.29B.

This is healthy, though not so large that working-capital swings are irrelevant. That matters because `ASML`'s quarter-to-quarter cash generation can move sharply even when profitability remains strong.

Working-capital composition is worth watching closely:
- Inventory rose to $11.71B from $11.43B in the prior quarter and from $11.02B a year earlier.
- Receivables rose to $5.91B from $4.62B in the prior quarter.
- Cash fell sharply to $8.38B from $13.32B in the prior quarter.

That pattern suggests capital is being tied up in the operating cycle. For a company like `ASML`, this is not automatically bearish, because build cycles, milestone timing, and customer delivery schedules can distort quarter-end working capital. But for traders, it is the main short-term fundamental pressure point in the current dataset.

Cash flow analysis

Cash flow is the weakest part of the most recent quarter, but it needs to be interpreted carefully.

For 2026-03-31:
- Operating cash flow was negative $2.19B.
- Capex was $422.3M.
- Free cash flow was negative $2.61B.
- Change in working capital was negative $5.16B.

That weak cash quarter was driven overwhelmingly by working capital rather than an earnings collapse. Net income was still $2.76B, so the disconnect between earnings and cash was timing/cycle-driven.

This is visible in the recent quarterly sequence:
- Q1 2026 free cash flow: negative $2.61B.
- Q4 2025 free cash flow: positive $10.94B.
- Q3 2025 free cash flow: positive $243.7M.
- Q2 2025 free cash flow: positive $357.7M.
- Q1 2025 free cash flow: negative $475.1M.

That is a highly volatile cash pattern, but not an unusual one for a company with large customer milestones and inventory commitments. Traders should distinguish between structural cash impairment and timing noise. The current data looks more like timing noise, but if negative working-capital pressure persists for multiple quarters, the market may start to question conversion quality.

Capital allocation remains shareholder-friendly:
- `ASML` repurchased $1.00B of stock in Q1 2026.
- It paid $617.0M in dividends in Q1 2026.
- It also reduced debt by $692.9M.

This combination is important. Management was willing to return capital and reduce debt despite a weak cash quarter, which suggests confidence in the medium-term cash engine.

Financial history and trend view

Over the last several reported quarters, `ASML` has shown three stable fundamental traits.

First, earnings power is consistently high.
- Revenue has stayed in a high range, mostly between about $7.5B and $9.7B per quarter.
- Net income has remained above $2.1B in each listed quarter.
- Operating income has generally stayed in a $2.47B to $3.43B range.

Second, margin durability is strong.
- Gross profit has remained robust even with quarter-to-quarter shipment variation.
- Operating margin has generally stayed in the mid-30% range.
- EBITDA has stayed near $2.7B to $3.7B per quarter.

Third, the balance sheet has steadily strengthened.
- Equity increased from $17.50B in 2025-03-31 to $20.83B in 2026-03-31.
- Tangible book value rose from $12.32B to $15.71B over the same span.
- Long-term debt is manageable and did not expand meaningfully.

These are the marks of a fundamentally strong franchise rather than a cyclical company under stress.

Valuation analysis

On valuation, `ASML` screens as expensive on current fundamentals but more reasonable on forward expectations.

Key points:
- TTM PE of 46.44x is high even for a premier semiconductor-enablement asset.
- Forward PE of 29.57x is still premium, but much easier to defend if earnings growth materializes.
- Price/book of 23.96x is very high, but book value is not the best anchor for a high-IP, high-margin equipment leader.
- TTM free cash flow yield is only about 1.5%, which is not cheap.
- Dividend yield of 0.59% does not provide much valuation support.

So the valuation case depends heavily on continued earnings expansion rather than on mean-reversion value. Traders should treat `ASML` as a quality growth compounder, not as a discounted cyclical bargain.

Supportive factors:

- The yield curve is upward sloping, which usually aligns with normal growth expectations.

For `ASML`, the macro setup is good enough to support semiconductor capital spending sentiment, but not so easy that investors will ignore valuation misses or cash-conversion concerns.

Actionable trader takeaways

1. The core fundamental setup is still bullish on business quality. `ASML` has elite margins, high returns on capital, net cash, and year-over-year earnings growth.

2. The immediate watch item is cash conversion, not profitability. Q1 2026 negative free cash flow came from a $5.16B working-capital outflow. If that normalizes, the bearish read fades quickly. If it repeats, the market may start discounting weaker conversion quality.

3. Valuation leaves less room for disappointment. A 46.44x trailing PE means even strong companies can be vulnerable if order timing, deliveries, or margins wobble.

4. The stock's recent underperformance versus XLK over 1 week and 1 month likely reflects digestion after a major 6-month run, not a collapse in fundamentals. That argues for separating tactical momentum weakness from business deterioration.

5. Inventory and receivables should be monitored closely next quarter. Rising inventory and receivables alongside negative operating cash flow is acceptable for one quarter, but not ideal if it becomes a trend.

6. Balance-sheet risk is low. With roughly $5.67B of net cash and modest debt, `ASML` is not facing financing stress.

Bottom line

Fundamentally, `ASML` still looks like a best-in-class semiconductor equipment franchise with strong revenue scale, exceptional margins, and a very healthy balance sheet. The main caution is not solvency or earnings weakness; it is that the stock already reflects a lot of optimism, while the latest quarter showed a meaningful working-capital drag and negative free cash flow. For traders, that supports a view of `ASML` as fundamentally strong but tactically sensitive to cash-conversion follow-through, valuation tolerance, and next-quarter working-capital normalization.
