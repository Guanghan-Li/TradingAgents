AMD fundamental report as of 2026-04-16

AMD remains one of the strongest fundamental momentum stories in large-cap semiconductors, but the stock is no longer cheap. The key setup is this: operating performance improved sharply through 2025, liquidity is strong, cash generation is healthy, and the market has already re-rated AMD aggressively in anticipation of much higher forward earnings. For traders, AMD currently looks like a high-quality growth name with improving fundamentals, but also one where execution risk matters more because valuation is elevated and the stock has already moved hard.

AMD is a $453.7B technology semiconductor company. The balance of evidence in the provided fundamentals context points to a business that exited 2025 with accelerating scale. TTM revenue is $34.64B, gross profit is $18.18B, EBITDA is $6.74B, net income is $4.27B, EPS is $2.61, and free cash flow is listed at $4.59B in the headline snapshot. AMD also trades with high market sensitivity: beta is 1.963, which means macro and sentiment moves are likely to be amplified in the stock.

The most important fundamental development is the quarterly revenue ramp. AMD quarterly revenue moved from $7.44B in 2025-03-31 to $7.69B in 2025-06-30, then accelerated to $9.25B in 2025-09-30 and $10.27B in 2025-12-31. That is a major step-up in scale in the back half of 2025. Gross profit followed the same pattern, rising from $3.74B in Q1 2025 to $5.58B in Q4 2025. Gross margin also recovered strongly after a mid-year dip: roughly 50.2% in Q1 2025, 39.8% in Q2 2025, 51.7% in Q3 2025, and 54.3% in Q4 2025. That Q4 result is especially important because it suggests AMD ended the year with stronger product mix and better operating leverage.

Profitability improved materially as the year progressed. Reported operating income was $806M in Q1 2025, fell to -$134M in Q2 2025, then rebounded to $1.27B in Q3 and $1.75B in Q4. Net income showed a similar progression: $709M, $872M, $1.24B, and $1.51B across 2025. Diluted EPS climbed from $0.44 in Q1 to $0.92 in Q4. Net margin improved from about 9.5% in Q1 to 14.7% in Q4. Normalized EBITDA rose to $2.86B in Q4 from $1.59B in Q1 and only $721M in Q2. Traders should read this as a business that had a real mid-year wobble but finished 2025 at a much higher earnings power level than it started.

There is one quality-of-earnings nuance worth flagging. Q2 2025 included a pretax loss of $74M but still showed positive net income, helped by a large tax benefit. That means part of the 2025 earnings progression was not purely clean operating expansion early in the year. However, the second-half rebound looks much healthier because Q3 and Q4 profitability was supported by materially better revenue and operating income, not just tax effects.

AMD is investing aggressively. Research and development expense increased from $1.71B in Q4 2024 to $2.33B in Q4 2025, while SG&A increased from $792M to $1.20B over the same period. That is a large cost step-up, but so far the company appears to be absorbing it well because revenue and gross profit have scaled faster in recent quarters. For traders, that supports the view that AMD is still in expansion mode rather than harvest mode.

The balance sheet is strong. Total assets reached $76.93B at 2025-12-31, with stockholders' equity of $63.00B and total liabilities of $13.93B. Cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments rose to $10.55B from $5.13B a year earlier. Working capital increased to $17.49B from $11.77B. The current ratio is 2.85, which is comfortably healthy. Total debt was $3.85B at year-end 2025, up from $2.21B at 2024-12-31, but still modest relative to equity and liquidity. The headline debt-to-equity figure in the prefetched snapshot appears consistent with a low-single-digit percentage leverage profile rather than heavy balance-sheet stress. In practical terms, AMD has ample flexibility to keep funding R&D, buybacks, and selective strategic activity.

A more mixed balance-sheet item is inventory. Inventory climbed from $5.73B at 2024-12-31 to $7.92B at 2025-12-31, an increase of about 38%. Some of that can be justified by the revenue ramp, but it is still a metric traders should watch closely. In semiconductors, rising inventory can mean anticipated demand and product build, but if end demand softens it can also become a margin headwind. Receivables ended 2025 at $6.32B, broadly manageable relative to revenue. Inventory is the more important watch item than receivables here.

AMD's asset mix still reflects a major acquisition legacy. Goodwill and other intangible assets remain very large at $41.83B, including $25.13B of goodwill. Tangible book value was $21.17B. The positive angle is that intangible balances have been gradually stepping down, which reduces amortization pressure over time. The caution is that AMD's book value quality is still meaningfully influenced by acquired intangibles rather than purely hard operating assets.

Cash generation was solid and improved through 2025. Operating cash flow was $939M in Q1, $2.01B in Q2, $2.16B in Q3, and $2.60B in Q4. Free cash flow was $727M, $1.73B, $1.90B, and $2.38B across those quarters. Capital expenditures stayed moderate, running between $212M and $282M per quarter. That means most of the cash flow improvement translated into real flexibility rather than being consumed by heavy fixed-capex needs. AMD also repurchased stock throughout 2025, with buybacks totaling about $1.92B across the four quarters. Even so, share count reduction was limited, which suggests buybacks are partly offsetting stock compensation and issuance rather than driving major per-share shrinkage.

Valuation is the main constraint on the AMD bull case. TTM P/E is 106.61, which is extremely rich on trailing earnings. Forward P/E is 25.46, which is much more reasonable but implies the market expects a major jump in EPS from $2.61 TTM to $10.93 forward. That is a very large embedded growth assumption. Price-to-book is 7.20. Return on equity is 7.08% and return on assets is 3.2%, both still modest relative to the headline valuation. The market is clearly paying for future earnings power, not current balance-sheet efficiency.

That valuation setup creates a specific trading implication: AMD likely needs continued clean execution to justify the multiple. If AMD keeps revenue near or above the $10B quarterly level and sustains 50%+ gross margin with double-digit operating leverage, the stock can continue to work. If growth slows, margins soften, or inventory converts poorly, the valuation leaves room for compression.

The past week price action confirms that the market is leaning into the bullish interpretation. AMD returned +17.59% over 1 week versus +7.00% for XLK, and it ranked 1st out of 8 peers over 3 months. AMD also outperformed XLK by +32.67 percentage points over 1 month and +19.26 points over 3 months. That relative strength is not a fundamental data point by itself, but it tells traders that the market is rapidly repricing AMD's earnings outlook higher. Strong fundamentals are now being recognized rather than ignored.

Macro context is broadly constructive for AMD. The effective Fed funds rate has fallen to 3.64% as of 2026-03-01 from 4.09% in 2025-10-01, the yield curve is upward sloping, unemployment is 4.3%, and VIX is 18.17. That backdrop is generally supportive for high-beta growth and semiconductor names. The main macro caveat is that CPI and PPI both ticked higher in the latest reading, so if inflation re-accelerates, long-duration growth multiples like AMD's could face pressure even if company fundamentals remain solid.

Actionable trader read-through for AMD:

- Bullish fundamental signal: Q3-Q4 2025 show a real acceleration in revenue, gross profit, EBITDA, and net income.
- Balance-sheet support: AMD has enough liquidity to absorb volatility and continue investing.
- Cash-flow support: free cash flow and operating cash flow improved meaningfully into year-end.
- Valuation risk: the stock is pricing in strong forward EPS delivery already.
- Execution watchpoints: inventory, gross margin durability, and whether Q4 2025 revenue scale holds into the next prints.
- Quality watchpoint: Q2 2025 earnings were helped by tax dynamics, so second-half operating strength matters more than full-year headline EPS alone.

Bottom line: AMD fundamentals improved substantially into late 2025 and justify a constructive medium-term view, but the easy rerating may already have happened. For trading purposes, AMD looks fundamentally strong enough to stay on the long side during pullbacks and trend continuation setups, but it is no longer a low-expectation story. The next leg will depend less on narrative and more on AMD proving it can sustain high revenue growth, 50%+ gross margins, and strong cash conversion without inventory becoming a problem.
