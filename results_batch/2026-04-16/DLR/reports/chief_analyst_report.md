- Thesis: The correct committee decision for DLR is Hold / Neutral because the business is credible, the stock is technically strong, and the current entry is still unattractive. The strongest evidence supporting DLR is the business mix and operating structure. About 58% of revenue comes from the more stable base franchise when combining 50% core colocation with 8% interconnection, while 30% hyperscale / AI leasing and 12% development / Asia expansion provide genuine growth optionality. That is why the aggressive case is right that DLR is not just a generic REIT momentum trade. It has recurring cash flow plus a real AI-linked upside sleeve.

But the conservative and neutral analysts are right on the stock-level decision. The structured scenario map makes the key point: DLR is not being underwritten as a high-conviction breakout buy from here. The most likely path is the 50% base case, where DLR remains a premium AI-linked REIT but trades sideways to modestly higher unless fundamentals accelerate further. That matters because DLR is already at 199.05, its 52-week high, with RSI at 79.23 and price above the upper Bollinger Band. The tape is strong, but the near-term reward-to-risk is weak.

The technical setup supports holding, not selling. DLR is above the 10 EMA at 190.08, the 50 SMA at 178.83, and the 200 SMA at 167.67, and MACD has expanded sharply to 5.06. That confirms an established uptrend. The aggressive analyst is right that this strength should not be faded mechanically, especially since the bear case is only 20% and is framed mainly as multiple compression rather than operational collapse. That lowers the probability of a thesis failure severe enough to justify an outright Sell today.

The reason DLR is not a Buy is valuation, financing sensitivity, and incomplete proof. DLR trades around 55.6x trailing earnings and 61.5x forward earnings, while leverage and financing burden remain meaningful, with total debt around $19.66B and net debt around $14.95B in the provided context. At the same time, the macro backdrop is only mixed: the Fed has eased, but long-end rates remain elevated with the 10Y at 4.29% and the 30Y at 4.89%. For a capital-intensive REIT, that keeps pressure on funding economics and limits how much additional multiple expansion should be assumed. This is exactly why the conservative analyst is right that “multiple compression” is not a trivial risk for DLR at current levels.

The missing proof points also matter. The bullish narrative for DLR depends on AI demand converting into measurable leasing velocity, backlog growth, utilization strength, pricing power, and FFO/AFFO improvement. The analyst debate repeatedly notes that recent support has come more from target-price revisions and AI thematics than from fresh operating proof. Until DLR shows that the 30% hyperscale / AI leasing segment and 12% development / Asia expansion sleeve are translating into returns without damaging capital efficiency, the committee should not move from Hold to Buy.

That leads directly to implementation. For DLR, maintain current exposure or a starter-sized position around the 1.0% initial weight. Do not scale aggressively above that unless one of two things happens: first, DLR pulls back into the 195 to 190 zone and holds; second, the next company update confirms leasing acceleration, backlog visibility, and FFO improvement. If either condition is met, moving toward the 3.0% target weight becomes reasonable. If DLR breaks the 189.6 risk line, the impulse thesis is weakening and exposure should be reduced to respect the 0.5% max-loss discipline.

In short, DLR deserves respect, not chase capital. The business quality and trend strength are good enough to avoid Sell, but the base-case probability, stretched technicals, premium valuation, and still-unproven AI monetization are enough to block a Buy. Hold DLR, keep stance Neutral, and wait for either better price or better proof.
