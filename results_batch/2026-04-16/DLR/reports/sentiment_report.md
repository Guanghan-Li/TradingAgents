### Company-Specific News Analysis

The most important development in the recent news set is the cluster of analyst actions on `DLR`. Jefferies raised its price target to $223 from $189 while maintaining a Buy rating. Morgan Stanley raised its target to $210 from $195 while keeping an Equalweight rating. Barclays raised its target to $189 from $182 while maintaining Equalweight. Cantor Fitzgerald initiated coverage at Overweight with a $211 price target.

This mix is important. The target increases indicate that sell-side expectations are moving upward, likely reflecting stronger demand assumptions, better leasing visibility, or higher confidence in AI-driven data-center growth. But the continued Equalweight ratings from Barclays and Morgan Stanley suggest not all analysts view the risk/reward as clearly compelling at current valuation levels. In other words, the news flow is positive, but not uniformly aggressive.

The AI narrative is also central. Insider Monkey described `DLR` as one of the best AI pick-and-shovel stocks to buy, reinforcing the view that Digital Realty Trust benefits from AI infrastructure spending without needing to own model, software, or chip-cycle risk directly. Simply Wall St. also highlighted `DLR`'s Asia AI expansion and the potential implications of a S$7 billion Asia AI expansion. This supports a bullish long-term thesis: if demand for AI compute continues rising, data-center capacity, interconnection, power availability, and geographic reach become increasingly valuable.

Still, the Asia expansion also introduces execution risk. Large-scale infrastructure expansion can create value if leasing demand, power access, pricing, and development yields are strong. But it can pressure free cash flow, leverage metrics, and investor confidence if capital spending rises faster than returns. Traders should distinguish between "AI expansion headline bullishness" and actual evidence of accretive leasing, pre-leasing, margins, and return on invested capital.
