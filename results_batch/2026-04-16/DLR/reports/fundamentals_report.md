# DLR Fundamental Research Report

## Company Overview

Digital Realty Trust, Inc. (`DLR`) is a specialty REIT focused on data center real estate and related infrastructure. The company sits at the intersection of real estate, cloud infrastructure, enterprise IT outsourcing, AI compute demand, and network connectivity. Its fundamental profile is therefore different from traditional office, retail, or industrial REITs: demand is driven less by local real estate cycles and more by hyperscale cloud, enterprise colocation, interconnection, and AI-related data growth.

As of the prefetched fundamentals data retrieved on 2026-04-16, `DLR` had a market capitalization of approximately **$69.63 billion**, placing it among the larger publicly traded real estate companies. The stock has materially outperformed the broader real estate sector recently, with strong relative performance versus the XLRE real estate ETF across 1-week, 1-month, 3-month, 6-month, and YTD periods.

## Valuation Snapshot

`DLR` trades at a premium valuation relative to many traditional REITs. Key valuation metrics include:

- **Market cap:** $69.63B
- **Trailing P/E:** 55.6x
- **Forward P/E:** 61.5x
- **Price/book:** 3.08x
- **Book value per share:** $64.60
- **EPS TTM:** $3.58
- **Forward EPS:** $3.24
- **Dividend yield:** 2.49%
- **Beta:** 1.091
- **52-week high:** $199.05
- **52-week low:** $143.83
- **50-day average:** $178.80
- **200-day average:** $169.63

The valuation implies that investors are paying for structural growth, data center scarcity value, and AI/cloud demand. However, the high P/E and elevated price/book multiple leave less room for execution disappointment, margin compression, or higher-for-longer rates.

A key caveat: for REITs, net income and P/E are often less informative than FFO/AFFO because depreciation, property gains/losses, and asset sales can heavily distort GAAP earnings. The provided data does not include FFO or AFFO, so traders should avoid relying solely on the P/E ratio when evaluating `DLR`.

## Profitability and Operating Performance

TTM fundamentals show a business with meaningful scale and positive profitability:

- **Revenue TTM:** $6.08B
- **Gross profit:** $3.35B
- **EBITDA:** $2.78B
- **Net income:** $1.27B
- **Profit margin:** 21.52%
- **Operating margin:** 14.15%
- **ROE:** 5.47%
- **ROA:** 1.18%
- **Free cash flow:** $1.96B

Quarterly revenue growth was strong through 2025. Revenue rose from **$1.408B in Q1 2025** to **$1.635B in Q4 2025**, representing approximately **16.1% growth over the year**. On a year-over-year basis, Q4 2025 revenue increased from **$1.436B in Q4 2024** to **$1.635B in Q4 2025**, or roughly **13.8% YoY**.

Gross profit also improved from **$773.2M in Q4 2024** to **$885.0M in Q4 2025**, an increase of approximately **14.5% YoY**. This indicates that revenue growth is flowing through to gross profit, although gross margin has been somewhat uneven across quarters.

Operating income improved from **$179.0M in Q4 2024** to **$227.3M in Q4 2025**, a gain of approximately **27.0% YoY**. Sequentially, operating income was relatively stable in 2025, ranging from about **$224.98M to $235.65M** across Q1 through Q4, suggesting that much of the revenue growth was offset by depreciation, SG&A, and operating cost increases.

Higher interest expense matters because `DLR` is capital-intensive and debt-funded growth is central to the data center REIT model. In a rate environment with the 10-year Treasury at **4.29%** and the 30-year at **4.89%**, refinancing and development capital costs remain important watch items.

## Balance Sheet and Leverage

`DLR` has a large and leveraged balance sheet, typical for an infrastructure-heavy REIT but still important for risk assessment.

Key balance sheet figures as of Q4 2025:

- **Total assets:** $49.41B
- **Total liabilities:** $24.56B
- **Total equity including minority interest:** $24.85B
- **Stockholders’ equity:** $22.93B
- **Common stock equity:** $22.19B
- **Total debt:** $19.66B
- **Net debt:** $14.95B
- **Cash and cash equivalents:** $3.45B
- **Working capital:** $476.9M
- **Current ratio:** 1.30
- **Debt/equity:** 81.655

Total debt rose from **$18.01B at Q4 2024** to **$19.66B at Q4 2025**, an increase of about **$1.65B**, or roughly **9.1%**. Net debt increased from **$12.84B** to **$14.95B**, an increase of approximately **16.4%**. This indicates that the company is continuing to invest and expand, but it is also adding leverage.

The company’s cash position remains substantial at **$3.45B**, providing liquidity for development, refinancing, and corporate needs. However, current liabilities of **$4.68B** exceed working capital by a wide margin, leaving the company dependent on continued capital markets access, operating cash flow, and debt management.

Investment properties were **$26.43B** at Q4 2025. Goodwill and other intangible assets totaled **$11.85B**, a large component of the asset base. Tangible book value was **$10.35B**, while stockholders’ equity was **$22.93B**, highlighting the importance of intangible assets and acquisition history in the balance sheet.

## Cash Flow and Capital Allocation

Quarterly operating cash flow was solid:

- **Q4 2025 operating cash flow:** $719.0M
- **Q3 2025:** $652.9M
- **Q2 2025:** $641.2M
- **Q1 2025:** $399.1M
- **Q4 2024:** $769.5M

The provided data lists free cash flow equal to operating cash flow in each quarter, but traders should be careful with that interpretation. `DLR` reports large investment property purchases in investing cash flow, and for a data center REIT these outlays are economically important even if they are not captured in a simple FCF field.

Investment property purchases were large throughout 2025:

- **Q4 2025:** -$1.002B
- **Q3 2025:** -$687.4M
- **Q2 2025:** -$704.3M
- **Q1 2025:** -$787.3M

That totals roughly **$3.18B** of investment property purchases during 2025. This confirms that `DLR` remains in growth-investment mode. While this supports future capacity and revenue growth, it also means the company requires significant external funding and/or asset recycling.

Financing activity shows active capital markets usage. In 2025, the company issued debt, repaid debt, and issued common stock. Common stock issuance was especially notable in Q2 and Q3 2025, at **$604.2M** and **$424.0M**, respectively. This supports balance sheet flexibility, but also creates potential dilution.

Cash dividends paid were significant, particularly in Q1 through Q3 2025. The company’s dividend yield of **2.49%** is moderate for a REIT, suggesting the market values `DLR` more as a growth/infrastructure compounder than as a pure income vehicle.

## Macro Backdrop

The macro backdrop is mixed but manageable for `DLR`.

The latest effective federal funds rate was **3.64%** as of 2026-03-01 and unchanged sequentially. The Fed policy path has eased from **4.09% in October 2025** to **3.64% in March 2026**, which is supportive for REIT valuations relative to a tightening environment.

However, long-term yields remain material:

- **2-year Treasury:** 3.76%
- **10-year Treasury:** 4.29%
- **30-year Treasury:** 4.89%
- **2Y-10Y spread:** +0.53 percentage points

An upward-sloping yield curve suggests more normal growth expectations, but long-end yields near 4.9% keep pressure on cap rates, financing costs, and equity REIT valuation multiples. For `DLR`, this is partly offset by strong secular demand from cloud and AI infrastructure.

Inflation readings show some sequential pressure, with CPI up **0.87%** and PPI up **1.78%** in the latest macro window. Construction, power infrastructure, equipment, and labor costs are important for data center development economics, so inflation should be monitored as a margin and return-on-invested-capital risk.

## Fundamental Strengths

`DLR` has several clear strengths:

- Strong top-line growth, with sequential revenue growth across all reported 2025 quarters.
- Exposure to durable secular demand from data centers, cloud, AI workloads, enterprise digitization, and connectivity.
- Large asset base and global scale, which can support customer relationships and capital access.
- Substantial liquidity, with Q4 2025 cash and equivalents of **$3.45B**.
- Positive operating cash flow, with Q4 2025 operating cash flow of **$719.0M**.
- Strong relative stock performance versus both XLRE and most real estate peers.

## Fundamental Risks

Key risks include:

- High valuation, with trailing P/E of **55.6x**, forward P/E of **61.5x**, and price/book of **3.08x**.
- Significant leverage, with total debt of **$19.66B** and net debt of **$14.95B** at Q4 2025.
- Rising interest expense, which increased to **$116.5M** in Q4 2025.
- Heavy capital intensity, with approximately **$3.18B** of investment property purchases in 2025.
- GAAP earnings volatility due to unusual items, asset sales, impairments, and gains on securities.
- Potential equity dilution from common stock issuance used to finance growth and preserve balance sheet strength.
- Sensitivity to power availability, construction costs, permitting, supply-chain constraints, and hyperscale customer demand cycles.

## Actionable Trader Insights

For bullish traders, the fundamental case rests on sustained revenue growth, data center demand, improving normalized income versus prior-year levels, strong liquidity, and market leadership within a favored real estate subsector. `DLR` is outperforming XLRE materially, and the market is treating it as a growth infrastructure REIT rather than a conventional yield REIT.

For cautious or valuation-sensitive traders, the key issue is that the stock already reflects a high degree of optimism. The stock is near its 52-week high, trades at a premium book multiple, and has already delivered strong 1-month, 3-month, and YTD outperformance. Any signs of leasing slowdown, development cost inflation, higher financing costs, or weaker normalized earnings could trigger multiple compression.

The most important items to monitor next are: leasing demand and renewal spreads, development pipeline economics, FFO/AFFO growth, net debt/EBITDA, debt maturity schedule, interest-rate sensitivity, capital recycling activity, dividend coverage, and whether revenue growth continues to translate into operating income growth.

## Bottom Line

`DLR` presents a fundamentally strong but expensive growth-REIT profile. The company is growing revenue, producing meaningful operating cash flow, and benefiting from powerful secular demand for data center capacity. However, the balance sheet is leveraged, capital requirements are high, and GAAP earnings are noisy due to unusual gains and charges. The stock’s recent outperformance confirms strong investor sponsorship, but also increases the risk that expectations are elevated.

For trading context, `DLR` looks fundamentally supported by growth and sector leadership, but risk/reward depends heavily on whether future FFO/AFFO and leasing momentum can justify the premium valuation.
