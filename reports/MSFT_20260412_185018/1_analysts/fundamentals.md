# MICROSOFT CORPORATION (MSFT) - COMPREHENSIVE FUNDAMENTAL ANALYSIS REPORT
Data as of: 2026-04-12

## Executive Summary

Microsoft Corporation demonstrates exceptional financial strength with robust revenue growth, expanding profitability, and substantial free cash flow generation. The company is well-positioned in the high-growth technology infrastructure sector, with strong balance sheet metrics and operational efficiency improvements that support continued shareholder value creation.

## Company Profile

**Name:** Microsoft Corporation  
**Sector:** Technology  
**Industry:** Software - Infrastructure  
**Market Capitalization:** $2.76 trillion  
**Headquarters:** Redmond, Washington

Microsoft is a dominant global technology leader providing cloud computing infrastructure, software solutions, and enterprise services through its Azure, Office 365, and gaming divisions.

## Financial Performance Analysis

### Revenue & Growth Trajectory

Microsoft's trailing twelve-month (TTM) revenue stands at $305.5 billion, demonstrating consistent quarterly growth momentum:

- **Q1 2025 (Mar 31):** $70.1 billion
- **Q2 2025 (Jun 30):** $76.4 billion
- **Q3 2025 (Sep 30):** $77.7 billion
- **Q4 2025 (Dec 31):** $81.3 billion

The quarter-over-quarter acceleration, particularly the $3.6 billion jump from Q3 to Q4 2025, indicates strong end-of-fiscal-year demand and positions the company favorably for FY2026.

### Profitability Metrics

**Net Income (TTM):** $119.3 billion  
**Profit Margin:** 39.04%  
**Operating Margin:** 47.09%

The exceptional 39% net profit margin reflects Microsoft's software-as-a-service business model economics, where incremental scaling generates disproportionate earnings growth. Operating margins near 47% indicate exceptional operational leverage and pricing power.

**Quarterly Net Income Progression:**
- Q4 2025: $38.5 billion (most recent, representing 5.16 EPS)
- Q3 2025: $27.7 billion (3.72 EPS)
- Q2 2025: $27.2 billion (3.65 EPS)
- Q1 2025: $25.8 billion (3.46 EPS)

The Q4 spike of $38.5 billion net income (39% higher than Q3) reflects typical seasonal strength in technology spending and demonstrates Microsoft's ability to convert higher revenue into substantial bottom-line earnings.

### Cash Flow Analysis

**Operating Cash Flow (TTM):** $160.5 billion (Q1-Q4 2025 combined)  
**Free Cash Flow (TTM):** $77.4 billion  
**FCF Margin:** 25.3% of revenue

Microsoft's free cash flow generation is exceptional:

- **Q4 2025:** $5.9 billion FCF
- **Q3 2025:** $25.7 billion FCF
- **Q2 2025:** $25.6 billion FCF
- **Q1 2025:** $20.3 billion FCF

**Capital Expenditure Intensity:** Microsoft is investing heavily in infrastructure ($29.9 billion in Q4 2025 alone, $83.1 billion annualized), reflecting its commitment to Azure cloud capacity expansion and AI infrastructure development. This CapEx represents 27% of revenue, which is elevated versus historical norms but justified by the AI computing buildout cycle.

### Return Metrics

- **Return on Equity (ROE):** 34.4%
- **Return on Assets (ROA):** 14.9%
- **EBITDA (TTM):** $175.3 billion
- **EBITDA Margin:** 57.4%

These returns significantly exceed the cost of capital and reflect the high-quality nature of Microsoft's competitive moat.

## Balance Sheet Strength

**Working Capital:** $50.2 billion

**Asset Composition:**
- Net Property, Plant & Equipment: $286.2 billion (43% of assets) — substantial cloud infrastructure

- Current Assets: $180.2 billion, including $89.5 billion in cash and short-term investments

### Leverage Profile

**Total Debt:** $57.6 billion  
**Net Debt:** $16.0 billion (after cash adjustment)  
**Debt-to-Equity:** 31.5x (appears high due to operating lease accounting)
**Stockholders' Equity:** $390.9 billion

The high nominal debt-to-equity ratio is misleading; Microsoft has substantial equity and manages debt conservatively. The company maintained debt repayment discipline in Q1 2025 ($3.0 billion repayment) while continuing shareholder returns.

### Capital Allocation

Microsoft returned $7.4 billion to shareholders via buybacks in Q4 2025 and $6.8 billion in dividends (TTM dividend payout: $25.3 billion). Combined shareholder returns of $33.3 billion demonstrate confidence in intrinsic value and commitment to capital returns.

## Investment Thesis

Microsoft is a fortress balance sheet company with exceptional profitability, industry-leading market positions, and strong free cash flow generation. The elevated capital expenditure ($83B annually) reflects the secular AI and cloud infrastructure opportunity, and management's confidence is evident in aggressive shareholder returns.

**Near-term considerations:**
- Stock is trading 17% below 200-day average, suggesting oversold conditions
- Forward P/E of 19.7x is reasonable for 18% EPS growth visibility
- Dividend yield of 0.98% provides modest income, but capital appreciation is primary driver
- Macro backdrop is supportive (neutral rates, low unemployment, normal yield curve)

**Risk factors to monitor:**
- CapEx ROI validation (AI infrastructure must drive revenue growth)
- Enterprise IT spending trends in Q2/Q3 2026 (early cycle visibility critical)
- Competitive dynamics in cloud infrastructure
- Regulatory outcomes (antitrust scrutiny)

The company's scale, market dominance, and balance sheet strength provide downside protection, while AI infrastructure adoption and cloud migration offer multi-year growth runways. Suitable as both growth and quality dividend holding.
