
Bull Analyst: The bull case for BABA is not that every problem is solved today. It is that the market is still pricing Alibaba like a low-trust, slow-growth commerce conglomerate while the evidence increasingly supports a higher-quality platform rerating driven by AI, cloud, and international commerce. Start with the structured underwriting: the scenario map gives only a 20% bear probability versus 80% combined base-plus-bull, and the bull case specifically allows forward valuation to move from about 17.7x toward the low-20s if the market treats BABA as an AI infrastructure and application platform. That is the key asymmetry. You are not paying an extreme multiple here. You are paying a mid-to-high teens forward multiple for a company with roughly RMB 1.02 trillion of trailing revenue, RMB 92.8 billion of net income, RMB 338.1 billion of cash plus short-term investments, RMB 649.2 billion of long-term investments, and over RMB 1.1 trillion of equity. That is not a fragile story stock.

If the bear says, “this is just AI narrative without proof,” I think that misses the highest-quality part of the evidence. The strongest positive theme in the research is not retail excitement or robotics headlines. It is enterprise AI adoption feeding Alibaba Cloud stickiness through Qwen. That matters because cloud is the cleanest rerating lever in the whole business mix. The structured value-driver map explicitly calls Cloud Intelligence / AI the highest strategic rerating lever, and the scenario framework tells us the market is watching whether cloud can sustain growth above 30% with AI product revenue continuing triple-digit growth. In other words, the upside driver is not vague. It is identifiable, measurable, and directly linked to valuation expansion.

The second bear point is usually margin pressure: SG&A and marketing rose hard, operating income fell, and free cash flow has been uneven. True, but the wrong conclusion is being drawn. This is exactly what an investment phase looks like when a dominant platform defends the core profit pool and funds new growth vectors at the same time. China commerce remains the largest segment and primary cash-flow engine. International commerce is a meaningful minority with above-group growth. Cloud/AI has improving demand outlook. Logistics supports both. The bear frames this as dilution. I frame it as a portfolio of real operating assets where the mature cash engine is financing the segments most likely to earn a higher multiple later.

And importantly, the balance sheet gives management time to execute. With total debt around RMB 262.7 billion against enormous cash and investment balances, this is not a solvency debate. Even the fundamental report, which is fairly cautious, admits the balance sheet is a major support and that BABA is not distressed. The latest quarter also showed positive free cash flow of RMB 36.0 billion after prior lumpiness. So yes, cash conversion needs to normalize, but no, this is not a business running out of room.

On valuation, the bear case is also weaker than it sounds. If growth were collapsing, a forward PE of 17.7x might still be too high. But revenue is resilient, December-quarter revenue rose to RMB 284.8 billion, and forward EPS of 7.51 versus trailing EPS of 5.76 implies the market already expects some recovery. The real question is whether that recovery is too optimistic. Given the AI/cloud catalyst path, I do not think it is. If anything, the stock still looks discounted relative to what even moderate execution could justify.

Technically, the chart actually fits a bull-in-formation setup better than a finished move. Price at 133.28 is above the 10 EMA of 127.96, MACD improved sharply from about -6.84 to -2.44, RSI recovered to 54.49, and the stock is pressing the upper Bollinger band near 133.82. Yes, it is still below the 50 SMA at 139.24 and 200 SMA at 146.76. But that is exactly why the opportunity still exists. If the stock had already reclaimed all of those levels, the rerating would be less interesting. The current setup says early recovery, not late-cycle euphoria.

Macro is a headwind to multiple expansion, but only a modest one. Higher-for-longer rates argue against reckless position sizing, not against owning a financially strong platform with internal catalysts. That is why the structured sizing output is sensible: medium conviction, 2.0% initial weight, 4.0% target weight, max portfolio loss of 0.25%. The lesson from similar situations is not “wait until everything is obvious.” It is “size the uncertainty correctly when the payoff is asymmetric.” BABA fits that template.

So my response to the bear is straightforward: you are right that this is not yet a clean, fully confirmed compounder. But that is not the bar for a good investment. The bar is whether the market is underpricing a credible improvement path. Here, the largest profit pool is still intact, the balance sheet is strong, cloud/AI is the clearest rerating lever, the news flow is supportive, the technicals are repairing, and the scenario math favors non-bear outcomes 80% to 20%. That is enough to justify a long bias and a starter position now, with room to add on confirmation above the mid-130s and especially through the 50-day area near 139. In committee terms: this is not a table-pounding full-size buy, but it is absolutely an investable bullish setup.
