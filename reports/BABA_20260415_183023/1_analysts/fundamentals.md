**BABA Fundamental Report (as of April 15, 2026)**

Alibaba Group Holding Limited (`BABA`) remains a very large-scale internet retail and platform business with a market capitalization of about $318.8 billion, trailing twelve month revenue of roughly 1.02 trillion, gross profit of 414.4 billion, EBITDA of 138.7 billion, and net income of 92.8 billion. On headline valuation, the stock is not expensive versus its own earnings power: `PE 23.1x`, `forward PE 17.7x`, `price/book 2.05x`, `EPS 5.76`, and `forward EPS 7.51`. That combination implies the market is still discounting execution risk, margin volatility, and recent share-price underperformance rather than pricing BABA as a clean growth compounder.

**Company Profile And Quality Snapshot**

`BABA` operates in `Consumer Cyclical` / `Internet Retail`, but its financial profile looks more like a scaled platform ecosystem than a traditional retailer. The business has a low reported `beta of 0.489`, a modest `dividend yield of 0.8%`, and substantial balance sheet resources. Profitability is positive but not elite on current trailing numbers: `profit margin 8.9%`, `operating margin 7.1%`, `ROE 8.23%`, and `ROA 3.13%`. Those returns are acceptable for a business of this scale, but they are below what traders usually want to see from a top-tier internet platform in a clean operating environment.

**Income Statement Trends**

The latest reported quarter in the dataset is `December 31, 2025`.

Quarterly revenue has been relatively stable with a strong seasonal lift in the December quarter:
- `2024-12-31`: 280.154 billion
- `2025-03-31`: 236.454 billion
- `2025-06-30`: 247.652 billion
- `2025-09-30`: 247.795 billion
- `2025-12-31`: 284.843 billion

That means December 2025 revenue was up about `1.7% YoY` versus December 2024 and up about `14.9% QoQ` versus September 2025. Revenue is holding up, but it is not showing the kind of breakout acceleration that would usually justify aggressive multiple expansion.

Profitability was much more volatile than revenue. In the `2025-12-31` quarter:
- Gross profit was `115.309 billion`
- Operating income was `20.160 billion`
- EBITDA was `27.273 billion`
- Net income was `16.383 billion`

Against `2024-12-31`, that is a notable deterioration:
- Gross profit fell from `117.630 billion` to `115.309 billion`
- Operating income fell from `47.376 billion` to `20.160 billion`
- Net income fell from `49.127 billion` to `16.383 billion`

A key driver was `9.515 billion` of unusual items / impairment-related charges in the December 2025 quarter. Even after adjusting for unusual items, normalized income still declined materially from the prior-year period. This matters because it suggests the weakness was not purely accounting noise.

Operating expense intensity is also worth watching. Selling, marketing, and SG&A remain very large absolute cost centers. In the December 2025 quarter:
- R&D: `15.480 billion`
- SG&A: `80.289 billion`
- Selling and marketing: `71.934 billion`

That cost structure can support long-term ecosystem defense, but in the near term it limits operating leverage unless revenue growth improves.

As of `2025-12-31`:
- Total assets: `1.878 trillion`
- Total equity gross minority interest: `1.106 trillion`
- Common stock equity: `1.040 trillion`
- Tangible book value: `775.595 billion`
- Working capital: `152.405 billion`
- Cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments: `338.110 billion`
- Long-term investments and advances: `649.216 billion`
- Total debt: `262.740 billion`
- Net debt: `134.566 billion`
- Current ratio: `1.331`

This is a liquid, asset-rich balance sheet. Even though reported debt is sizable, it is offset by substantial cash and investment holdings. Retained earnings also rose from `636.392 billion` at `2024-12-31` to `682.830 billion` at `2025-12-31`, which signals that the business is still accumulating capital over time despite earnings volatility.

One caution: goodwill and other intangibles remain large at `264.060 billion`. That is manageable relative to the asset base, but it increases the risk of future impairment charges if portfolio assets or acquired businesses are marked down again.

**Cash Flow And Capital Allocation**

Cash generation has been uneven.

Quarterly free cash flow:
- `2024-12-31`: `70.915 billion`
- `2025-03-31`: `-58.452 billion`
- `2025-06-30`: `20.672 billion`
- `2025-09-30`: `10.099 billion`
- `2025-12-31`: `36.032 billion`

The latest quarter was healthy, with `36.032 billion` of operating cash flow and free cash flow, but the March 2025 quarter was deeply negative. The prefetched overview also shows trailing `free cash flow` of `-26.12 billion`, which does not fully reconcile with the quarterly series shown here. Traders should treat cash flow as directionally positive but operationally lumpy, and they should verify whether timing, investment purchases, or data normalization explain the mismatch.

Cash balances have trended down from `453.278 billion` at `2024-12-31` to `338.110 billion` at `2025-12-31`, while investment balances increased. That looks more like capital redeployment than liquidity stress, but it still means traders should watch whether management is shifting toward lower-cash, more investment-heavy balance sheet positioning.

**Financial History And Trend Read-Through**

The most important historical read is this:
- Revenue is resilient.
- Gross profit is relatively stable.
- Earnings are volatile.
- Cash flow is uneven.
- Equity and retained earnings are still growing.
- The balance sheet remains strong enough to absorb shocks.

This is not the profile of a distressed company. It is the profile of a very large platform company going through a period where valuation, margin stability, and investor confidence are all below their best historical levels.

**Actionable Insights For Traders**

1. `BABA` looks more attractive on forward earnings than trailing earnings. The drop from `23.1x` trailing PE to `17.7x` forward PE implies expected EPS recovery. If that recovery starts to show up in clean quarterly results, the stock has rerating potential.
2. The balance sheet is a real support. With `338.110 billion` in cash/short-term investments and `649.216 billion` in long-term investments, solvency is not the core issue.
3. The main fundamental risk is not top-line collapse; it is margin inconsistency. Revenue held up, but operating and net income were much weaker year over year in the latest December quarter.
4. Special items matter. The `9.515 billion` impairment in the December 2025 quarter hurt reported profitability, and the size of goodwill/intangibles means future charges cannot be ruled out.
5. Price weakness versus peers and sector suggests the market still doubts earnings quality. Traders should not treat the stock as a pure value story unless margins and cash conversion improve.
6. A more constructive setup would be: stable revenue above `~250 billion` quarterly, fewer unusual charges, stronger operating margin conversion, and confirmation that free cash flow remains positive for consecutive quarters.

**Bottom Line**

Fundamentally, `BABA` is financially solid, liquid, and still profitable, but it is not currently producing the clean, predictable earnings profile that would justify strong market leadership. The bull case rests on undervaluation, asset strength, and forward EPS recovery. The bear case rests on weak sentiment, poor relative performance, earnings volatility, and inconsistent cash flow. On fundamentals alone, the stock looks more like a recovery / rerating candidate than a fully confirmed high-conviction quality compounder.
