### Bull Researcher

Bull Analyst: The bull case for BABA is not that every problem is solved today. It is that the market is still pricing Alibaba like a low-trust, slow-growth commerce conglomerate while the evidence increasingly supports a higher-quality platform rerating driven by AI, cloud, and international commerce. Start with the structured underwriting: the scenario map gives only a 20% bear probability versus 80% combined base-plus-bull, and the bull case specifically allows forward valuation to move from about 17.7x toward the low-20s if the market treats BABA as an AI infrastructure and application platform. That is the key asymmetry. You are not paying an extreme multiple here. You are paying a mid-to-high teens forward multiple for a company with roughly RMB 1.02 trillion of trailing revenue, RMB 92.8 billion of net income, RMB 338.1 billion of cash plus short-term investments, RMB 649.2 billion of long-term investments, and over RMB 1.1 trillion of equity. That is not a fragile story stock.

If the bear says, “this is just AI narrative without proof,” I think that misses the highest-quality part of the evidence. The strongest positive theme in the research is not retail excitement or robotics headlines. It is enterprise AI adoption feeding Alibaba Cloud stickiness through Qwen. That matters because cloud is the cleanest rerating lever in the whole business mix. The structured value-driver map explicitly calls Cloud Intelligence / AI the highest strategic rerating lever, and the scenario framework tells us the market is watching whether cloud can sustain growth above 30% with AI product revenue continuing triple-digit growth. In other words, the upside driver is not vague. It is identifiable, measurable, and directly linked to valuation expansion.

The second bear point is usually margin pressure: SG&A and marketing rose hard, operating income fell, and free cash flow has been uneven. True, but the wrong conclusion is being drawn. This is exactly what an investment phase looks like when a dominant platform defends the core profit pool and funds new growth vectors at the same time. China commerce remains the largest segment and primary cash-flow engine. International commerce is a meaningful minority with above-group growth. Cloud/AI has improving demand outlook. Logistics supports both. The bear frames this as dilution. I frame it as a portfolio of real operating assets where the mature cash engine is financing the segments most likely to earn a higher multiple later.
