# MSFT Technical Analysis Report
**Date: 2026-04-13**
**Macro Regime: RISK-ON (+3/6, Medium Confidence)**

## Executive Summary

MSFT is in a confirmed intermediate-term downtrend with price trading significantly below key moving averages. The stock has declined from ~$410 in early March to ~$371 currently (-9.5%), showing persistent selling pressure. While RSI suggests the selloff may be approaching exhaustion, momentum indicators have not yet confirmed a reversal. The risk-on macro backdrop provides a supportive tailwind for potential recovery, but elevated Treasury yields (10Y at 4.29%) continue to pressure mega-cap tech valuations.

## Trend Analysis: Bearish Structure Intact

**Price vs. Moving Averages:**
- Current price: $370.87 (as of 2026-04-10)
- 10 EMA: $372.66 - Price just below, indicating recent stabilization attempt
- 50 SMA: $393.62 - Price 5.8% below, confirming intermediate downtrend
- 200 SMA: $472.59 - Price 21.5% below, confirming long-term bearish structure

The death cross configuration (10 EMA < 50 SMA < 200 SMA) signals entrenched bearish momentum. The 50 SMA has been declining steadily (from 414 on 2026-03-25 to 393.62 currently), acting as dynamic resistance. Any rally attempt will face overhead supply at the $393-395 zone.

**Key Observation:** The 10 EMA has flattened and is converging with price, suggesting the rate of decline is slowing. This is the first technical hint of potential stabilization, but confirmation requires price to reclaim the 10 EMA decisively.

## Momentum: Oversold Conditions Building

**RSI Analysis:**
- Current RSI: 38.37 (neutral-to-weak zone)
- Recent low: 22.29 on 2026-03-27 (deeply oversold)
- Trend: RSI has recovered from extreme oversold levels but remains below 50

The RSI bottomed at 22.29 during the late-March capitulation and has since climbed to 38.37, indicating some buying interest at lower levels. However, RSI has not yet broken above 50, which would signal a momentum shift from bearish to bullish. The current reading suggests MSFT is in a consolidation phase after a sharp decline.

**Actionable Insight:** Watch for RSI to break above 45-50 on rising volume. This would confirm momentum is shifting and could precede a test of the 50 SMA. Conversely, if RSI rolls over below 35, expect another leg down toward the $360 support zone.

**MACD Analysis:**
- Current MACD: -8.32
- Trend: Improving from -13.08 on 2026-03-30
- Interpretation: Still negative but diverging positively

The MACD histogram has been compressing since late March, indicating bearish momentum is waning. The MACD line is rising toward the signal line, suggesting a potential bullish crossover in the coming sessions if buying pressure continues. However, the MACD remains deeply negative, confirming the downtrend is still in force.

**Actionable Insight:** A MACD bullish crossover above the signal line would be the first momentum-based buy signal. Traders should wait for this confirmation before initiating long positions, as premature entries risk catching a falling knife.

## Volatility & Range: Bollinger Band Compression

**Bollinger Bands:**
- Upper Band: $401.93
- Lower Band: $352.32
- Current Price: $370.87 (37% of band width from lower band)
- Band Width: $49.61

Price is trading in the lower third of the Bollinger Band range, indicating the stock is closer to oversold than overbought territory. The bands have widened significantly from mid-March (when width was ~$46) to current levels, reflecting elevated volatility during the selloff.

**Key Pattern:** The lower band has been rising (from $350.55 on 2026-04-06 to $352.32 currently), while the upper band has been falling (from $416.83 to $401.93). This band compression suggests volatility is beginning to contract, which often precedes a directional breakout.

**Actionable Insight:** A break above $380 (mid-band zone) would signal a move toward the upper band and potential trend reversal. Conversely, a break below $365 would target the lower band at $352, where strong support is expected.

**ATR Analysis:**
- Current ATR: 8.47
- Recent Peak: 9.12 on 2026-03-31
- Interpretation: Volatility remains elevated but declining

The ATR peaked during the late-March selloff and has since declined, indicating the panic phase may be over. Current ATR of 8.47 suggests daily ranges of ~$8-9, which traders should use for stop-loss placement. A reasonable stop for long positions would be 1.5x ATR below entry (~$12-13 below entry).

## Volume Analysis

Volume has been elevated during the decline, with spikes on down days (e.g., 50.8M on 2026-03-20, 44.8M on 2026-03-30), confirming distribution. Recent sessions show declining volume (28M on 2026-04-10), which is typical during consolidation phases. A breakout above $380 on volume >35M would confirm renewed buying interest.

## Macro Integration: Rates vs. Growth

The macro backdrop presents a mixed picture for MSFT:

**Supportive Factors:**
- Risk-on regime with falling VIX (19.49, down 7.4% sequentially)
- Resilient labor market (unemployment 4.3%, payrolls +178k) supports enterprise IT spending
- No recession signal from yield curve (2s10s at +51 bp)

**Headwinds:**
- 10Y Treasury at 4.29% pressures duration-heavy tech valuations
- Re-accelerating inflation (CPI +0.87%, PPI +2.08%) reduces Fed easing probability
- Fed on hold since January 2026, limiting rate-cut catalyst

**Net Assessment:** The macro environment supports MSFT's fundamental earnings power (cloud, AI capex) but limits multiple expansion. This suggests any rally will be driven by operational execution rather than valuation re-rating. Traders should focus on technical entry points rather than expecting a V-shaped recovery.

## Trading Recommendations

**For Swing Traders (3-10 day horizon):**
1. **Wait for confirmation:** Do not chase current levels. Wait for RSI >45 and MACD bullish crossover before entering long positions.
2. **Entry zone:** $368-372 on a successful retest with declining volume. Stop-loss at $360 (below lower Bollinger Band).
3. **Target:** Initial target $383-385 (mid-March support), extended target $393-395 (50 SMA).
4. **Risk/Reward:** 2:1 minimum. Entry at $370 with stop at $360 and target at $385 offers 1.5:1, which is acceptable given macro support.

**For Day Traders:**
- Trade the $365-375 range until a breakout occurs. Fade moves to range extremes with tight stops (1x ATR = $8.47).
- Watch for volume spikes above 30M as confirmation of directional moves.

**For Position Traders (weeks to months):**
- Current levels offer a reasonable accumulation zone for long-term holders, but expect choppiness. Dollar-cost average into weakness rather than deploying full capital immediately.
- Major support at $356-358 is the line in the sand. A break below on high volume would signal a deeper correction toward $330-340.

## Risk Factors

1. **Breakdown Risk:** A close below $365 would negate the stabilization thesis and target $350-355.
2. **Macro Shock:** Any surprise hawkish Fed pivot or inflation re-acceleration could pressure tech broadly.
3. **Earnings Risk:** MSFT's next earnings report (likely late April) could be a catalyst. Weak cloud growth or AI monetization concerns could trigger another leg down.
4. **Sector Rotation:** If the risk-on regime strengthens further, capital may rotate from mega-cap tech into cyclicals, limiting MSFT upside.

## Conclusion

MSFT is in a technical no-man's land: oversold enough to attract dip-buyers but not yet showing confirmed reversal signals. The risk-on macro regime and improving momentum indicators (RSI recovery, MACD compression) suggest the worst of the selloff may be over, but traders should wait for technical confirmation before committing capital. The $370-375 zone is a reasonable accumulation area for patient buyers, with stops below $360. A break above $380 on volume would confirm a trend reversal and open the door to a test of the 50 SMA at $393.
