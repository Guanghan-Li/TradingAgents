## Macroeconomic Regime Assessment – April 13, 2026

**Regime: Late-Cycle Normalization with Sticky Inflation Risks**

The Federal Reserve has delivered 45 basis points of cumulative easing since October 2025, bringing the effective Fed Funds rate to 3.64% and holding steady since January 2026. The pause signals the FOMC believes it has reached a neutral-to-slightly-restrictive stance. However, the inflation backdrop remains uneven:

- **CPI** rose 0.87% month-over-month in March, a pace that annualizes above the Fed's 2% target.
- **PPI** surged 2.08% sequentially in February, the most concerning data point in this snapshot. Producer-level price pressures often lead consumer inflation by 1–2 quarters, raising the risk that disinflation has stalled.
- **Labor market** remains resilient: unemployment ticked down to 4.30%, and payrolls added 178k in March. This is consistent with a soft landing, but also removes urgency for further cuts.

**Yield Curve & Recession Signal**

The 2Y-10Y spread is +51 basis points, a normal upward slope that reflects growth expectations and no imminent recession signal. The curve has re-steepened from prior inversion, consistent with the Fed's easing cycle. However, the 10Y yield at 4.29% and 30Y at 4.90% suggest bond markets are pricing persistent term premium or inflation risk, not a dovish Fed pivot.

**Policy Path & Cross-Asset Implications**

- **Fed on hold**: With inflation sticky and labor strong, the next move is more likely a hike than a cut if PPI acceleration continues into CPI. The market should not price aggressive easing.
- **Equity risk**: A pause-then-hike scenario would pressure valuations, especially growth/tech names with long-duration cash flows (e.g., AMZN). VIX at 19.49 is below long-term average, suggesting complacency.
- **Rates risk**: If the Fed signals hawkish patience, the front end (1Y-2Y) could reprice higher, flattening the curve and tightening financial conditions.
- **Credit/corporate**: Resilient growth supports corporate earnings, but higher-for-longer rates increase refinancing risk for leveraged issuers.

**Key Risks for Trading Desks**

1. **PPI-to-CPI pass-through**: If March CPI (due soon) confirms acceleration, Fed rhetoric will shift hawkish. Watch core services inflation.
2. **Labor market inflection**: Payrolls remain positive, but any sharp deterioration could force the Fed to ease despite inflation, creating a stagflation risk.
3. **Curve dynamics**: A bear steepener (long-end selling) would hurt both bonds and equities. A bull flattener (front-end rally on growth fears) would favor defensives.
4. **Geopolitical/supply shocks**: PPI surge could reflect supply-side pressures (energy, trade). Monitor commodity inputs and trade policy.
