## Macroeconomic Analysis – April 12, 2026

**Regime: Late-cycle normalization with sticky producer inflation**

The Fed has paused at 3.64% after a 45bp easing cycle from October 2025 through January 2026. The 2Y-10Y spread sits at +51bp—a normal, upward-sloping curve signaling no imminent recession risk. Growth remains resilient: nonfarm payrolls added 178k in March, unemployment ticked down to 4.3%, and the VIX closed at 19.49 (down 7% week-over-week), indicating subdued volatility and risk-on sentiment.

**Inflation divergence is the key risk.** Headline CPI rose 0.87% month-over-month, but PPI surged 2.08%—a sharp acceleration that suggests upstream cost pressures have not yet passed through to consumers. If this persists, the Fed's pause could shift to a hawkish hold or even a resumption of hikes, particularly if core services inflation re-accelerates.

**Policy path:** The Fed is likely on hold through mid-2026 unless PPI inflation feeds into CPI or labor markets overheat. The curve pricing reflects confidence in a soft landing, but the producer-price spike is a yellow flag for bond and equity volatility.

**Cross-asset implications:**
- **Equities (especially growth/tech):** The stable Fed and low VIX support risk assets. However, NVDA and other high-duration names remain vulnerable to any hawkish Fed pivot if inflation surprises persist.
- **Rates:** The curve is fairly priced for a hold, but watch 5Y-10Y steepening if PPI inflation proves sticky—long-end yields could rise.
- **Credit:** Benign for now, but a Fed re-hike scenario would pressure spreads.

**Concrete risks for the committee:**
1. PPI-to-CPI pass-through accelerates → Fed hikes → growth stocks reprice lower.
2. Labor market cools faster than expected → Fed cuts sooner → curve bull-steepens, growth stocks rally.
3. Geopolitical or supply-chain shock → stagflation risk re-emerges.
