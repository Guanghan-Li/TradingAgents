**Macro Regime: Soft Landing, Fed on Hold**

The current macro backdrop supports risk assets, particularly growth-sensitive equities like DDOG:

**Growth & Policy**: Fed funds stable at 3.64% after a 45bp cutting cycle (Oct 2025–Jan 2026). The pause signals the Fed believes it has reached a neutral-to-slightly-restrictive stance. GDP data is stale (Q4 2025), but payrolls remain constructive (+178k March), and unemployment improved to 4.30%. The economy is expanding at a moderate pace without overheating.

**Inflation**: Headline CPI rose 0.87% month-over-month in March, while PPI surged 1.78%. The producer-level spike suggests pipeline pressures that may feed through to consumer prices, keeping the Fed cautious. However, the sequential CPI gain is not yet alarming, and the Fed is likely to tolerate near-target inflation while monitoring labor and growth.

**Yield Curve**: The 2Y-10Y spread is +50bp, a normal upward slope consistent with growth expectations and no imminent recession signal. The 10Y at 4.26% reflects a "higher-for-longer" regime relative to 2020–2021, but the curve shape is healthy. Long-end yields (30Y at 4.87%) suggest bond markets are pricing modest term premium and stable long-run inflation expectations.

**Volatility**: VIX at 18.36 (down 3.97%) indicates calm equity markets and low systemic stress. This environment favors risk-taking and supports equity multiples.

**Cross-Asset Implications for DDOG**:
- **Equities**: Soft landing + Fed pause = constructive for growth stocks. Cloud/SaaS names benefit from stable rates and continued enterprise IT spending.
- **Rates**: 10Y at 4.26% is a headwind vs. the zero-rate era, but the stabilization and curve shape are less punitive than an inverted or steepening-from-inversion scenario.
- **Credit**: Low VIX and stable growth support corporate credit spreads, reducing financing risk for high-growth tech.
- **FX/Commodities**: Not directly relevant to DDOG, but a stable dollar and moderate commodity prices support corporate margins.

**Key Risks**:
1. **PPI pass-through**: If March's 1.78% PPI gain feeds into CPI, the Fed may hike again, pressuring growth multiples.
2. **Growth slowdown**: Payrolls or consumer spending weakness could trigger a risk-off rotation out of high-multiple tech.
3. **Curve inversion**: A return to inversion (2Y > 10Y) would signal recession fears and hurt DDOG's growth narrative.
4. **Geopolitical/fiscal shock**: Not visible in the data, but tail risk for volatility spikes.

The primary risk is a PPI-driven inflation resurgence forcing the Fed back into tightening mode. Absent that, the backdrop is constructive.
