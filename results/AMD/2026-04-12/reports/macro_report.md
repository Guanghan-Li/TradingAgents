## Macroeconomic Analysis for AMD Trading Decision (2026-04-12)

### Regime Identification: Reflationary Pause with Inflation Risk

The macro backdrop has shifted into a **reflationary regime with reaccelerating inflation pressure**. The Fed cut 45 bps from October 2025 (4.09%) to December 2025 (3.72%), then held at 3.64% through March 2026. That pause now looks prescient:

- **CPI** rose 0.87% month-over-month in March (~11% annualized), a sharp reacceleration.
- **PPI** surged 2.08% MoM (~28% annualized), signaling upstream price pressure.
- **Labor market** tightened further: unemployment fell to 4.30% (-10 bps) with payrolls adding 178k.
- **VIX** at 19.49 suggests markets are not pricing acute stress, but the -7% drop masks complacency.

### Yield Curve & Policy Path

The **2Y-10Y spread is +51 bps**, a normal upward slope consistent with growth expectations. However, the curve's steepness (30Y at 4.90% vs 2Y at 3.78%) reflects bond vigilantes pricing persistent inflation or future Fed hikes. The Fed is **on hold at 3.64%**, but if CPI/PPI prints continue hot, the next move is a hike, not a cut. The market has not fully priced this risk.

### Cross-Asset Implications for AMD

**Semiconductors (AMD) are cyclical growth stocks sensitive to:**

1. **Real rates**: 10Y Treasury at 4.29% with CPI running hot means real yields are compressing but nominal rates are elevated. This caps valuation multiples for long-duration growth stocks like AMD.
2. **Growth expectations**: Payrolls and tight labor support demand, but inflation eats into real consumer spending and corporate margins. AMD's data-center and AI tailwinds remain intact, but PC/gaming segments face headwinds.
3. **Dollar strength**: Reflationary + Fed hawkish tilt = stronger USD, a headwind for AMD's ~50% international revenue.
4. **Volatility regime**: VIX at 19 is calm, but PPI/CPI surprises could spike vol quickly, punishing high-beta names.

### Concrete Risks for Trading Committee

- **Upside scenario**: If the Fed holds and inflation moderates (next CPI print <0.3% MoM), risk assets rally. AMD benefits from AI capex cycle.
- **Downside scenario**: If April CPI prints hot again (>0.5% MoM), Fed hikes 25 bps in May/June. Tech multiples compress; AMD could fall 10-15% on rate-hike repricing.
- **Base case**: Fed holds through Q2 2026, but forward guidance turns hawkish. AMD trades rangebound with 10-12% downside risk if macro data deteriorates.

**Recommendation for AMD**: The macro setup is **neutral-to-bearish** for high-multiple cyclicals. Inflation reacceleration and Fed pause (not pivot) argue for **HOLD or reduce exposure**. If the committee is long AMD, consider trimming into strength or hedging with puts. Do not add aggressively until inflation trajectory clarifies.
