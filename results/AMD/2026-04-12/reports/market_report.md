## Macro-Technical Synthesis

The technical picture is unambiguously bullish: AMD has broken out above key moving averages, MACD is surging, and the trend structure is intact. However, the overbought RSI, price above the upper Bollinger Band, and extreme MACD readings all warn of near-term exhaustion.

Overlaying the macro context complicates the bullish case:

1. **Inflation Reacceleration:** CPI at +0.87% MoM and PPI at +2.08% MoM suggest the Fed's cutting cycle is over. If inflation persists, the next move is a hike, not a cut. This would compress valuation multiples for high-duration growth stocks like AMD.

2. **Elevated Nominal Rates:** The 10Y Treasury at 4.29% is a headwind for tech valuations. AMD's forward P/E is likely in the 30-40x range (based on historical norms), making it sensitive to rate moves.

3. **RISK-ON Regime:** The +3/6 RISK-ON score supports cyclicals and growth, but the "medium confidence" rating and neutral VIX (19.49) suggest the regime is fragile. A hot CPI print in April could flip sentiment quickly.

4. **Dollar Strength:** Reflationary + hawkish Fed = stronger USD, a headwind for AMD's ~50% international revenue.

**Bottom Line:** The technical momentum is strong enough to carry AMD higher in the very short term (1-3 days), but the macro setup argues against aggressive buying. The risk/reward is skewed toward taking profits or waiting for a pullback to the $230-235 zone.
