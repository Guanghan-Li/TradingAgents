Macro view for `BABA`: the U.S. backdrop is not recessionary right now. Labor remains firm with unemployment at 4.3% and payrolls still expanding, while inflation re-accelerated in the latest print with CPI up 0.87% m/m and PPI up 1.78% m/m in March 2026. That combination points to a late-cycle, growth-still-positive but inflation-sticky regime. For markets, this is a "higher-for-longer" rates setup rather than a clean easing cycle.

The Treasury curve has normalized from prior inversion into a bear-steep shape: front-end yields are around 3.7%-3.8%, while the 20Y-30Y is near 4.8%-4.9%. That usually means near-term recession fears have eased, but long-duration discount rates and term premium are rising. For `BABA`, that is mixed: less global recession risk helps broad risk appetite and trade-sensitive growth expectations, but higher long-end yields pressure equity multiples, especially ADRs and duration-sensitive tech/internet names.

The Fed proxy path shows effective fed funds down to 3.64% by March 2026 after several cuts from late 2025, but the latest inflation data argues for a pause rather than imminent further easing. Base case: the Fed is likely on hold until inflation momentum cools again. That keeps real rates restrictive enough to limit valuation expansion. `BABA` therefore faces a macro regime where fundamentals matter more than multiple re-rating. If China-specific demand, stimulus, and platform monetization improve, `BABA` can still work, but U.S. macro is not currently providing a strong tailwind.

Concrete implications for `BABA`: macro is a modest headwind to valuation, not an outright collapse signal. Key risks other analysts should incorporate are: 1) renewed U.S. inflation keeping rates high, 2) further steepening via higher long yields compressing internet/ADR multiples, 3) USD strength tightening external financial conditions, and 4) the fact that `BABA` is driven more by China growth/regulation than by U.S. macro alone. Net macro stance on `BABA`: neutral to slightly negative for valuation, but not recessionary enough to create a broad risk-off macro short thesis by itself.
